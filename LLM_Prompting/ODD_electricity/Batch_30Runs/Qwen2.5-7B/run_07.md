### Title: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game

### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Coordination Dilemma

### Matrix:
|               | Farmer B: No Capacitor | Farmer B: Capacitor |
|---------------|-----------------------|--------------------|
| **Farmer A: No Capacitor** | (2, 2)                | (1, 3)             |
| **Farmer A: Capacitor**    | (3, 1)                | (4, 4)             |

### Justification:
This action situation represents the coordination dilemma faced by two neighboring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing capacitors. Mutual investment in capacitors improves voltage stability and overall irrigation reliability, but a single farmer investing while the other does not reaps no private benefit. The payoff matrix reflects the following:

- **(2, 2)**: Both farmers do not invest, resulting in low voltage stability and moderate reliability.
- **(1, 3)**: Farmer A invests in a capacitor while Farmer B does not, leading to a moderate improvement in reliability for Farmer A but no private benefit.
- **(3, 1)**: Farmer B invests in a capacitor while Farmer A does not, leading to a moderate improvement in reliability for Farmer B but no private benefit.
- **(4, 4)**: Both farmers invest in capacitors, leading to the highest reliability and most stable voltage for both.

The coordination problem is evident as mutual cooperation (both farmers investing) is Pareto-dominant but risky due to the potential for unilateral defection.

| **Farmer: Contribution**    | (1, 1)                 | (2, 0)              |

### Justification:
This action situation represents the dilemma faced by a farmer deciding whether to make a formal contribution to transformer capacity and the staff deciding whether to authorize the connection. Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but a farmer's unilateral contribution can lead to free-riding by other farmers.

- **(1, 1)**: Neither farmer contributes, and staff do not authorize, resulting in low reliability.
- **(0, 1)**: Farmer contributes, but staff do not authorize, leading to no benefit for the farmer.
- **(1, 1)**: Staff do not authorize, but the farmer contributes, leading to no benefit for the farmer.
- **(2, 0)**: Farmer contributes and staff authorize, leading to high reliability for the farmer but a cost for the staff.
