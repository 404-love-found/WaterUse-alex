### Title: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)

### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Coordination Dilemma
Farmers must decide whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing capacitors to improve irrigation efficiency. Mutual investment yields shared improvement, but unilateral investment offers no private benefit, creating a coordination problem.

#### Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|                  | Farmer 2: No Capacitor | Farmer 2: Capacitor |
|------------------|------------------------|---------------------|
| **Farmer 1: No Capacitor** | (0, 0)                 | (1, -1)             |
| **Farmer 1: Capacitor**     | (-1, 1)                | (2, 2)              |

#### Justification
- **Farmer 1 and Farmer 2**: If both farmers invest in capacitors, they share the benefits, leading to a mutual improvement in voltage stability and irrigation efficiency, resulting in a payoff of (2, 2).
- **Farmer 1: No Capacitor, Farmer 2: Capacitor**: If only one farmer invests, the investor incurs a cost without a private benefit, resulting in a payoff of (-1, 1).
- **Farmer 1: Capacitor, Farmer 2: No Capacitor**: If only one farmer invests, the non-investing farmer benefits from improved voltage stability without incurring costs, resulting in a payoff of (1, -1).
- **Farmer 1 and Farmer 2: No Capacitor**: If neither farmer invests, there is no improvement in voltage stability, resulting in a payoff of (0, 0).

### Title: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma (AS6)

### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Coordination Dilemma
Two farmers drawing from the same aquifer must decide whether to extract groundwater for irrigation. Mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain at the cost of accelerated depletion.

#### Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|                  | Farmer 2: Restrain | Farmer 2: Over-extract |
|------------------|--------------------|------------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Restrain** | (3, 3)              | (1, 5)                 |
| **Farmer 1: Over-extract** | (5, 1)            | (2, 2)                 |

#### Justification
- **Farmer 1 and Farmer 2: Restrain**: Both farmers maintain sustainable groundwater extraction, resulting in a payoff of (3, 3).
- **Farmer 1: Restrain, Farmer 2: Over-extract**: The over-extracting farmer benefits in the short term, while the restraining farmer suffers, resulting in a payoff of (1, 5).
- **Farmer 1: Over-extract, Farmer 2: Restrain**: The over-extracting farmer benefits in the short term, while the restraining farmer suffers, resulting in a payoff of (5, 1).
- **Farmer 1 and Farmer 2: Over-extract**: Both farmers over-extract, leading to accelerated depletion and lower future yields, resulting in a payoff of (2, 2).

### Title: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)

### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Free-Rider Dilemma
One farmer’s authorization or investment in capacitor equipment benefits both by improving voltage quality, but the costs fall solely on the authorizer, creating a free-rider incentive.

#### Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|                  | Farmer 2: No Investment | Farmer 2: Investment |
|------------------|-------------------------|----------------------|
| **Farmer 1: No Investment** | (0, 0)                  | (1, 2)               |
| **Farmer 1: Investment**     | (2, 1)                  | (2, 2)               |

#### Justification
- **Farmer 1 and Farmer 2: No Investment**: Both farmers remain at a low but non-zero baseline of voltage quality, resulting in a payoff of (0, 0).
- **Farmer 1: No Investment, Farmer 2: Investment**: The investing farmer bears the cost, while the non-investing farmer benefits, resulting in a payoff of (1, 2).
- **Farmer 1: Investment, Farmer 2: No Investment**: The investing farmer benefits, while the non-investing farmer remains at a low baseline, resulting in a payoff of (2, 1).
- **Farmer 1 and Farmer 2: Investment**: Both farmers benefit from improved voltage quality, resulting in a payoff of (2, 2).

### Title: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)

### Tension: Farmer-Sub-station Staff Coordination Dilemma
Reciprocal benefit arises only when both parties engage in informal exchange, but if either party abstains, the offerer bears a loss.

#### Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|                  | Sub-station Staff: No Exchange | Sub-station Staff: Exchange |
|------------------|--------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Farmer: No Exchange** | (0, 0)                         | (1, 1)                      |
| **Farmer: Exchange**     | (1, 1)                         | (2, 2)                      |

#### Justification
- **Farmer and Sub-station Staff: No Exchange**: Both parties remain at a baseline, resulting in a payoff of (0, 0).
- **Farmer: No Exchange, Sub-station Staff: Exchange**: The exchanging party benefits, while the non-exchanging party remains at a baseline, resulting in a payoff of (1, 1).
- **Farmer: Exchange, Sub-station Staff: No Exchange**: The exchanging party bears a loss, while the non-exchanging party remains at a baseline, resulting in a payoff of (1, 1).
- **Farmer and Sub-station Staff: Exchange**: Both parties benefit from mutual exchange, resulting in a payoff of (2, 2).

### Title: Authorization and Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game (AS5)

### Tension: Farmer-Sub-station Staff Asymmetric Coordination Dilemma
Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but if the farmer makes a formal request and the staff withhold, the farmer incurs a loss, and vice versa.

#### Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|                  | Sub-station Staff: Withhold | Sub-station Staff: Invest |
|------------------|-----------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Farmer: Formal Request** | (0, 1)                      | (2, 2)                   |
| **Farmer: Informal Request** | (1, 0)                      | (2, 2)                   |

#### Justification
- **Farmer: Formal Request, Sub-station Staff: Withhold**: The farmer incurs a loss, while the staff save effort or cost, resulting in a payoff of (0, 1).
- **Farmer: Formal Request, Sub-station Staff: Invest**: Both parties benefit from mutual formal cooperation, resulting in a payoff of (2, 2).
- **Farmer: Informal Request, Sub-station Staff: Withhold**: The staff bear the cost without the formal fee, while the farmer gains more, resulting in a payoff of (1, 0).
- **Farmer: Informal Request, Sub-station Staff: Invest**: Both parties benefit from mutual informal exchange, resulting in a payoff of (2, 2).
