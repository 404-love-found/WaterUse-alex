### Title: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)

### Tension: Coordination vs. Free-Rider Incentives

### Matrix:
|                | Farmer 2: Invest | Farmer 2: Not Invest |
|----------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Invest** | (B, B)          | (A, C)              |
| **Farmer 1: Not Invest** | (C, A)         | (D, D)              |

### Justification:
This action situation captures the coordination problem where mutual investment in capacitors improves local voltage stability and pump efficiency, but unilateral investment provides no private benefit. The ordinal payoffs reflect:
- **B (Best):** Both farmers invest, improving local service quality.
- **A (Worse):** Only one farmer invests, providing no added benefit.
- **C (Worse):** Only one farmer invests, incurring the cost without benefit.
- **D (Worst):** Neither farmer invests, maintaining the current (potentially poor) service quality.
