### Title: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)

### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Coordination in Capacitor Investment

### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|               | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
|---------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | (1, 1)          | (0, 0)                  |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | (0, 0)          | (0.5, 0.5)              |

### Justification:
This action situation captures the strategic tension between two neighboring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing capacitors. Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit, creating a coordination problem. The payoff matrix reflects the benefits of mutual cooperation (1, 1) and the lack of benefit for either party if one invests alone (0, 0) or if both do not invest (0.5, 0.5).

### Title: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)

### Tension: Farmer-Staff Interaction in Capacity Contribution

### Sequential Representation (Game Tree):

```
            [Farmer 1]
             / \
            /   \
[Staff]  [Farmer 1 Invests]  [Farmer 1 Does Not Invest]
          /                 \
         /                   \
[Staff Invests]  [Staff Withholds]
   /                 \
  /                   \
[Reliability 1]  [Reliability 2]
```

### Justification:
This action situation involves the decision of a farmer to invest in capacity improvement and the staff's decision to invest or withhold effort. The sequential structure captures the strategic tension where mutual investment is collectively optimal, but unilateral investment by the farmer can lead to a loss if the staff withholds effort, while the staff can save effort if the farmer withholds.

### Title: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma (AS6)

### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Groundwater Over-Extraction

### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|               | Farmer 2 Extracts | Farmer 2 Does Not Extract |
|---------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Extracts** | (1, 1)          | (0, 2)                  |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Extract** | (2, 0)          | (1.5, 1.5)              |

### Justification:
This action situation captures the strategic tension between two farmers drawing from the same aquifer. Mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion, creating a prisoner's dilemma. The payoff matrix reflects the benefits of mutual restraint (1.5, 1.5) and the short-term gain for the over-extractor at the cost of the other (2, 0).

### Title: Farmer-Farmer Social Learning (AS2)

### Tension: Diffusion of Capacitor Adoption

### Sequential Representation (Game Tree):

```
            [Farmer]
             / \
            /   \
[Neighbor]  [Neighbor Adopts Capacitor]  [Neighbor Does Not Adopt]
           /                 \
          /                   \
[Farmer Imitates]  [Farmer Does Not Imitate]
```

### Justification:
This action situation captures the strategic tension between a farmer deciding whether to adopt a capacitor based on the observed success of a neighboring farmer. The sequential structure reflects the path-dependent diffusion of technology, where early failures can discourage later uptake, while successful coordinated adoption can spread through the social network.
