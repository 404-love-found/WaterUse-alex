### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)
#### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Coordination
**Matrix:**
|                | Farmer B Invests | Farmer B Does Not Invest |
|----------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer A Invests** | (2, 2)            | (1, 0)                   |
| **Farmer A Does Not Invest** | (0, 1)            | (1, 1)                   |

**Justification:**
This game models the coordination between two neighboring farmers to invest in voltage-stabilizing capacitors. Mutual investment improves local electricity quality, but unilateral investment does not yield a private benefit. The payoff matrix reflects the mutual cooperation being Pareto-dominant but risky due to the potential for free-riding.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process (AS2)
#### Tension: Information Diffusion and Adoption
**Matrix:**
|                | Farmer B Adopts Capacitor | Farmer B Does Not Adopt Capacitor |
|----------------|--------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Farmer A Adopts Capacitor** | (2, 2)                    | (1, 1)                           |
| **Farmer A Does Not Adopt Capacitor** | (1, 1)                    | (0, 0)                           |

**Justification:**
This game models the sequential social-learning process where a farmer decides whether to adopt a capacitor based on the observed success of a neighboring farmer. The payoff matrix reflects the need for a successful coordinated trial to initiate diffusion, as individual adoption without prior success does not yield a higher payoff.

### Action Situation 3: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)
#### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Free-Rider Incentives
**Matrix:**
|                | Farmer B Invests | Farmer B Does Not Invest |
|----------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer A Invests** | (3, 3)            | (2, 2)                   |
| **Farmer A Does Not Invest** | (2, 2)            | (1, 1)                   |

**Justification:**
This game models the dilemma where one farmer invests in transformer capacity improvements, benefiting both parties, but incurring the full cost. The payoff matrix reflects the free-rider incentive where both parties benefit from the investment, but the contributor bears the full cost, leading to a lower payoff if only one invests.

### Action Situation 4: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)
#### Tension: Farmer-Staff Informal Exchange
**Matrix:**
|                | Sub-station Staff Invests | Sub-station Staff Does Not Invest |
|----------------|---------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Farmer Invests** | (2, 2)                    | (1, 0)                           |
| **Farmer Does Not Invest** | (0, 1)                    | (1, 1)                           |

**Justification:**
This game models the mutual exchange between a farmer and sub-station staff where both parties gain only if they engage in informal exchange. The payoff matrix reflects the need for mutual cooperation to yield a higher payoff, but unilateral investment by the farmer incurs a loss if the staff does not reciprocate.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization-And-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game (AS5)
#### Tension: Legal vs. Informal Cooperation
**Matrix:**
|                | Sub-station Staff Invests | Sub-station Staff Withholds |
|----------------|---------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Farmer Makes Formal Request** | (3, 3)                    | (1, 0)                      |
| **Farmer Makes Informal Request** | (2, 1)                    | (2, 2)                      |

**Justification:**
This game models the asymmetric coordination game between a farmer and sub-station staff where mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal. The payoff matrix reflects the different outcomes based on the farmer's request type and staff response, with formal cooperation yielding a higher payoff for both parties but informal cooperation yielding a lower payoff for the staff due to the lack of formal fee.

### Action Situation 6: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma (AS6)
#### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Extraction Coordination
**Matrix:**
|                | Farmer B Restraints | Farmer B Over-Extracts |
|----------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Farmer A Restraints** | (3, 3)              | (2, 1)                 |
| **Farmer A Over-Extracts** | (1, 2)              | (1, 1)                 |

**Justification:**
This game models the extraction of groundwater where mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain but accelerates depletion. The payoff matrix reflects the need for mutual restraint to sustain yields but the temptation for individual farmers to over-extract for short-term benefits.
