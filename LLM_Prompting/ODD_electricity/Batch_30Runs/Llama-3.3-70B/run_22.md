### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Coordination Dilemma in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared improvement.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two farmers who must decide whether to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment yields a better outcome (3, 3) than unilateral investment (1, 2) or no investment (2, 2), illustrating a coordination dilemma.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma
#### Tension: One farmer's authorization or investment in transformer capacity benefits both, but costs fall solely on the authorizer.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 2, 3 | 1, 3 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 3, 2 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the asymmetric authorization dilemma where one farmer's contribution to transformer capacity benefits both farmers, but the contributor bears the cost, creating an incentive for the other farmer to free-ride.

### Action Situation 3: Farmer-Staff Mutual Exchange
#### Title: Informal Exchange Dilemma
#### Tension: Reciprocal benefit arises only when both the farmer and staff engage in informal exchange; otherwise, one side bears a loss.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer offers informal cooperation.
2. Staff decides to tolerate or enforce.
   - If staff tolerates, farmer gains, staff loses slightly.
   - If staff enforces, farmer loses, staff gains slightly.
#### Justification: This action situation represents the informal exchange between farmers and staff, where mutual cooperation yields a better outcome, but mismatched expectations lead to losses for one party.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction
#### Title: Common-Pool Resource Dilemma
#### Tension: Individual high extraction dominates in the short run, but mutual high extraction accelerates depletion and raises future costs.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Extracts High | Farmer 2 Extracts Low |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts High** | 3, 3 | 4, 1 |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts Low** | 1, 4 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the common-pool resource dilemma in groundwater extraction, where individual short-term gains lead to long-term losses due to over-extraction.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Compliance Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to seek formal authorization, while staff must decide how much to enforce, with mutual formal cooperation being collectively optimal but individually costly.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer decides to seek formal or informal access.
2. Staff decides to enforce or tolerate.
   - If farmer seeks formal access and staff enforces, both comply, but staff bears effort costs.
   - If farmer seeks informal access and staff tolerates, farmer gains, but system reliability may suffer.
#### Justification: This action situation represents the compliance dilemma between farmers and staff, where formal cooperation is optimal but involves individual costs, leading to incentives for informal access or tolerance.
