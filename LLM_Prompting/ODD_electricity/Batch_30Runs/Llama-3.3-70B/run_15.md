### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Coordination Dilemma in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared improvement.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to invest in capacitors. If both invest, they both benefit (payoff 3), but if only one invests, the investor does not see a significant benefit (payoff 1), while the non-investor sees a slight improvement (payoff 2) due to the other's investment.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma
#### Tension: One farmer's authorization or investment in transformer capacity benefits both, but costs fall solely on the authorizer.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 2, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 3, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This situation highlights the asymmetric payoff structure where one farmer's contribution to transformer capacity upgrades benefits both farmers, but the contributor bears the cost, creating a free-rider incentive.

### Action Situation 3: Farmer-Staff Informal Exchange
#### Title: Mutual Exchange Coordination
#### Tension: Reciprocal benefit arises only when both the farmer and staff engage in informal exchange; otherwise, one party loses while the other reverts to baseline.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Staff Engages in Informal Exchange | Staff Does Not Engage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Engages in Informal Exchange** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer Does Not Engage in Informal Exchange** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation captures the mutual benefit from informal exchange between farmers and staff, where both parties must participate to gain, reflecting a coordination dilemma.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction
#### Title: Common-Pool Resource Extraction Dilemma
#### Tension: Mutual restraint in groundwater extraction sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 3, 3 | 2, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 2 | 1, 1 |
#### Justification: This situation represents the strategic tension in managing a common-pool resource, where individual short-term gains from over-extraction compromise long-term sustainability and mutual benefits from restraint.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization-Enforcement Dilemma
#### Tension: The farmer's formal request and staff's decision to invest or withhold capacity create asymmetric incentives, with the staff gaining modestly even under formal cooperation.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer decides to request formal access or seek informal access.
2. Staff decides to invest in capacity, enforce rules, or tolerate informal access.
#### Justification: This sequential action situation reflects the asymmetric incentives and decisions between farmers and staff regarding formal authorization, enforcement, and informal tolerance, affecting grid reliability and access to electricity.

### Action Situation 6: Capacitor Adoption through Social Learning
#### Title: Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful peers, but diffusion is path-dependent, and early failures can discourage later adoption.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts a capacitor and observes the outcome.
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome and decides to adopt or not.
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the process of social learning in the adoption of capacitors, where the decision to adopt is influenced by observing the outcomes of other farmers, reflecting a sequential decision-making process.
