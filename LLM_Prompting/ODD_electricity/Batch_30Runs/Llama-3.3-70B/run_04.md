# Run 4 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared improvement.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage stability, but unilateral investment yields no added private benefit, creating a coordination problem.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Asymmetric Contribution to Transformer Capacity
#### Tension: One farmer's authorization or investment benefits both farmers but costs fall solely on the authorizer, generating a free-rider incentive.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 2, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 3, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation captures the asymmetric incentives in contributing to transformer capacity upgrades, where one farmer's contribution benefits both but at a personal cost.

### Action Situation 3: Mutual Exchange Coordination
#### Title: Informal Exchange Between Farmer and Staff
#### Tension: Reciprocal benefit arises only when both the farmer and staff engage in informal exchange; if either abstains, neither gains.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Staff Engages in Informal Exchange | Staff Does Not Engage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Engages in Informal Exchange** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer Does Not Engage in Informal Exchange** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation represents the mutual exchange between farmers and staff, where both must engage in informal exchange to benefit, reflecting the tension between formal compliance and informal reciprocity.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Title: Over-Extraction of Groundwater
#### Tension: Mutual restraint sustains groundwater yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension in groundwater extraction, where individual short-term gains lead to collective long-term loss, a classic prisoner's dilemma scenario.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement Dilemma
#### Title: Formal vs. Informal Access to Electricity
#### Tension: Farmers decide whether to seek formal access (with costs) or informal access (with risks), while staff decide on enforcement effort, affecting grid reliability and personal benefits.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Access or Informal Access
2. Staff observes choice and decides: Enforce or Not Enforce
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff Enforces, outcome is (2, 3)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff does not Enforce, outcome is (1, 2)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff Enforces, outcome is (4, 1)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff does not Enforce, outcome is (3, 2)
#### Justification: This sequential action situation captures the tension between formal and informal access to electricity, and the staff's decision on enforcement, influencing outcomes for both farmers and staff.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Imitation and Diffusion of Capacitor Technology
#### Tension: Farmers observe neighbors' outcomes and decide whether to adopt capacitor technology, with successful coordinated adoption spreading through the social network.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Neighbor adopts capacitor and observes outcome
2. Farmer observes neighbor's outcome and decides: Adopt or Not Adopt
- If Neighbor's outcome is successful and Farmer adopts, outcome is (3, 3)
- If Neighbor's outcome is unsuccessful or Farmer does not adopt, outcome is (2, 2)
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the process of social learning in the adoption of capacitor technology, where the visibility of successful adoption influences the decision to adopt, leading to diffusion through the social network.
