### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Coordination Dilemma in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with benefits strongest when adoption is coordinated.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts Capacitor | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt Capacitor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts Capacitor** | 3, 3 (Improved voltage stability for both) | 1, 2 (Farmer 1 bears cost, Farmer 2 free-rides) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt Capacitor** | 2, 1 (Farmer 2 bears cost, Farmer 1 free-rides) | 0, 0 (No improvement in voltage stability) |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers who must decide whether to invest in capacitors. The payoff matrix shows that mutual adoption yields the highest payoff, but unilateral adoption results in a lower payoff for the adopting farmer.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction
#### Title: Common-Pool Resource Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmers must decide how much groundwater to extract, with individual high extraction dominating in the short run, but mutual high extraction accelerating depletion.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 chooses extraction level (High or Low).
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's choice and chooses extraction level (High or Low).
3. Payoffs are determined by the combination of extraction levels:
   - Both High: Depletion accelerates, payoffs decrease.
   - One High, One Low: High extractor benefits, low extractor loses.
   - Both Low: Sustainable use, payoffs stable.
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers who must decide how much groundwater to extract. The sequential representation shows that the payoff depends on the combination of extraction levels, with mutual high extraction leading to depletion and decreased payoffs.
