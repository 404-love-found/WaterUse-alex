### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Coordination Dilemma in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors, which improve voltage stability, but the benefits are strongest when adoption is coordinated among neighboring farmers.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts Capacitor | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt Capacitor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts Capacitor** | 3, 3 (Improved voltage stability for both) | 1, 2 (Farmer 1 bears cost, Farmer 2 benefits slightly) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt Capacitor** | 2, 1 (Farmer 2 bears cost, Farmer 1 benefits slightly) | 0, 0 (No improvement in voltage stability) |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers who must decide whether to adopt capacitors. The payoff matrix shows that mutual adoption yields the highest payoff, but unilateral adoption can result in a lower payoff for the adopting farmer.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction
#### Title: Common-Pool Resource Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmers must decide how much groundwater to extract, knowing that individual high extraction can dominate in the short run, but mutual high extraction accelerates depletion and raises future pumping costs.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Extracts High | Farmer 2 Extracts Low |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts High** | 2, 2 (Short-term gain, long-term loss) | 3, 1 (Farmer 1 gains, Farmer 2 loses) |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts Low** | 1, 3 (Farmer 1 loses, Farmer 2 gains) | 2, 2 (Sustainable extraction) |
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the common-pool resource dilemma, where individual high extraction can yield short-term gains, but mutual high extraction leads to long-term losses.
