### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Tension: 
Coordination problem among farmers to adopt capacitors for voltage stabilization.
#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopt | Farmer 2 Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopt** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two farmers deciding whether to adopt capacitors. Mutual adoption (3, 3) is the best outcome for both, as it improves voltage stability. If one adopts and the other does not (1, 2 or 2, 1), the adopter bears the cost without full benefit, and the non-adopter may not see an improvement in voltage stability.

| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contribute** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Contribute** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Tension: 
Conflict between individual short-term gain and collective long-term sustainability in groundwater extraction.
#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer 2 Restrain | Farmer 2 Over-extract |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restrain** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-extract** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
This action situation illustrates the classic prisoner's dilemma where mutual restraint (3, 3) is collectively optimal but individually dominated by the temptation to over-extract (4, 1 or 1, 4), leading to a suboptimal outcome of mutual over-extraction (2, 2) and accelerated depletion.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement Asymmetric Coordination
#### Tension: 
Asymmetric incentives for formal authorization and enforcement between farmers and staff.
#### Matrix:
|  | Staff Enforce/Formal | Staff Not Enforce/Informal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Seek Formal** | 3, 2 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer Seek Informal** | 4, 1 | 2, 3 |
#### Justification: 
This action situation reflects the tension where a farmer seeking formal authorization and a staff member enforcing formally (3, 2) can lead to a better outcome for the farmer but at a cost to the staff. If the farmer seeks informal access and the staff enforce (1, 4), the farmer is penalized, and the staff benefit. If both choose informal (2, 3), the farmer may gain temporary benefits, but the staff's reputation and formal compliance suffer.
