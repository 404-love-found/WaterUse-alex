### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Coordination Dilemma in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual cooperation being Pareto-dominant but risky.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two neighboring farmers deciding whether to adopt capacitors. The payoff matrix shows that mutual adoption (3, 3) is the best outcome, but if one farmer adopts and the other does not, the adopter gets a lower payoff (1, 2), illustrating the risk of unilateral investment.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma
#### Tension: One farmer's authorization or investment in transformer capacity benefits both farmers, but the costs fall solely on the authorizer, creating a free-rider incentive.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 2, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 3, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the asymmetric payoff structure when one farmer contributes to transformer capacity, benefiting both, but bearing the cost alone, while the other farmer can free-ride.

### Action Situation 3: Farmer-Staff Informal Exchange
#### Title: Mutual Exchange Coordination
#### Tension: Farmers and staff engage in informal exchange, where reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage, but if either abstains, the other loses.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Staff Cooperates | Staff Does Not Cooperate |
| --- | --- | --- |

#### Justification: This action situation represents the mutual exchange between farmers and staff, where cooperation from both sides yields a higher payoff, but lack of cooperation from either side results in a lower payoff for the cooperating party.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Title: Common-Pool Resource Extraction
#### Tension: Farmers decide how much groundwater to extract, with mutual restraint sustaining yields, but unilateral over-extraction offering short-term gain.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation illustrates the classic prisoner's dilemma in the context of groundwater extraction, where mutual cooperation (restraint) leads to a sustainable outcome, but the temptation to over-extract for short-term gain can dominate, leading to a worse outcome for both.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Asymmetric Enforcement Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmers decide whether to seek formal authorization, and staff decide whether to enforce rules, with staff bearing effort costs and farmers facing penalty risks.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Request or Informal Access
2. Staff chooses: Enforce or Tolerate
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Enforce, payoff is (2, 1)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Tolerate, payoff is (3, 2)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Enforce, payoff is (1, 3)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Tolerate, payoff is (2, 2)
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the sequential decision-making between farmers and staff regarding authorization and enforcement, with payoffs depending on the combination of choices made by both parties.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Sequential Social Learning
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful neighbors, with diffusion of capacitor adoption dependent on visible outcomes and social networks.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Neighbor adopts capacitor and observes outcome
2. Farmer observes neighbor's outcome and decides to adopt or not
- If Neighbor's outcome is successful, Farmer is more likely to adopt
- If Neighbor's outcome is unsuccessful, Farmer is less likely to adopt
#### Justification: This action situation represents the process of social learning in the context of capacitor adoption, where farmers make decisions based on observing the outcomes of their neighbors, leading to a potential diffusion of the technology through the social network.
