### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Farmer-Farmer Coordination on Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination vs. Free-Riding on Capacitor Investment
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts Capacitor | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt Capacitor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts Capacitor** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt Capacitor** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: The matrix reflects the strategic tension between two farmers deciding whether to adopt a capacitor. Mutual adoption (3, 3) yields the best outcome due to improved voltage stability, while unilateral adoption (1, 2 or 2, 1) is less beneficial due to the cost and uncertain attribution of improvements.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Farmer-Staff Interaction on Authorization and Enforcement
#### Tension: Formal Compliance vs. Informal Access
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Request or Informal Access
2. Staff chooses: Enforce or Tolerate
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff Enforces, outcome is (3, 2)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff Tolerates, outcome is (2, 3)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff Enforces, outcome is (1, 1)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff Tolerates, outcome is (2, 2)
#### Justification: This sequential game reflects the interaction between a farmer's choice of authorization method and the staff's decision on enforcement. The outcomes depend on the combination of these choices, with mutual formal compliance (3, 2) and mutual informal tolerance (2, 2) being more stable than mixed strategies.

### Action Situation 6: Capacitor Adoption with Social Learning
#### Title: Sequential Adoption of Capacitors with Social Learning
#### Tension: Adoption Based on Observed Success
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor or not
2. Farmer 2 observes outcome and decides to adopt or not
- If Farmer 1 adopts and succeeds, Farmer 2 adopts: outcome (3, 3)
- If Farmer 1 adopts and fails, Farmer 2 does not adopt: outcome (1, 2)
- If Farmer 1 does not adopt, Farmer 2 does not adopt: outcome (2, 1)
#### Justification: This situation models the sequential decision-making process of capacitor adoption influenced by social learning. The adoption decision by the second farmer depends on the observed outcome of the first farmer's decision, reflecting the path-dependent nature of technology diffusion.
