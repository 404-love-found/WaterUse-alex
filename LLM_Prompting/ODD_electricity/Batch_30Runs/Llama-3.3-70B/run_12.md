# Run 12 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations

1. **Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game**
	* Title: Capacitor Adoption
	* Tension: Coordination vs. Free-Riding
	* Matrix/Sequential Representation:
		|  | Adopt Capacitor | Not Adopt |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Adopt Capacitor | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
		| Not Adopt | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
	* Justification: Farmers must decide whether to adopt capacitors to improve voltage stability. Mutual adoption yields the highest payoff, but unilateral adoption provides little benefit.

2. **Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma**
	* Title: Transformer Capacity
	* Tension: Contribution vs. Free-Riding
	* Matrix/Sequential Representation:
		|  | Authorize | Not Authorize |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Authorize | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
		| Not Authorize | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
	* Justification: Farmers must decide whether to authorize and pay for transformer capacity upgrades. Contributing farmers bear the cost, while non-contributing farmers may still benefit from improved reliability.

3. **Farmer-Staff Informal Exchange Game**
	* Title: Informal Exchange
	* Tension: Reciprocity vs. Exploitation
	* Matrix/Sequential Representation:
		|  | Cooperate | Defect |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Cooperate | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
		| Defect | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
	* Justification: Farmers and staff engage in informal exchanges, where mutual cooperation yields benefits, but exploitation can occur if one party defects.

4. **Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma**
	* Title: Groundwater Extraction
	* Tension: Over-Extraction vs. Conservation
	* Matrix/Sequential Representation:
		|  | Extract | Conserve |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Extract | 3, 3 | 4, 1 |
		| Conserve | 1, 4 | 2, 2 |
	* Justification: Farmers must decide how much groundwater to extract. Over-extraction yields short-term benefits, but mutual conservation is necessary to sustain the resource.

5. **Authorization and Enforcement Sequential Game**
	* Title: Authorization and Enforcement
	* Tension: Compliance vs. Evasion
	* Sequential Representation:
		1. Farmer requests formal access.
		2. Staff decides to enforce or tolerate.
		3. Farmer decides to comply or evade.
	* Justification: The sequence of actions reflects the farmer's decision to seek formal access, the staff's decision to enforce or tolerate, and the farmer's subsequent decision to comply or evade.

6. **Capacitor Adoption and Social Learning**
	* Title: Social Learning
	* Tension: Adoption vs. Non-Adoption
	* Sequential Representation:
		1. Farmer observes neighbor's adoption outcome.
		2. Farmer decides to adopt or not adopt based on observed outcome.
	* Justification: Farmers learn from their neighbors' experiences with capacitor adoption, influencing their own decisions to adopt or not adopt.
