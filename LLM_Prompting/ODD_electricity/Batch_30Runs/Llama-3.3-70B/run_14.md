### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Coordination Dilemma in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared improvement but unilateral investment yielding no added private benefit.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers who must decide whether to invest in capacitors. The payoff matrix shows that mutual investment (3, 3) is the best outcome, but if one farmer invests and the other does not, the investing farmer gets a lower payoff (1, 2).

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction
#### Title: Common-Pool Resource Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmers must decide how much groundwater to extract, with individual high extraction dominating in the short run, but mutual high extraction accelerating depletion and raising future costs.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Extracts High | Farmer 2 Extracts Low |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts High** | 3, 3 | 4, 1 |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts Low** | 1, 4 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the common-pool resource dilemma, where individual high extraction is beneficial in the short run, but mutual high extraction leads to depletion and increased costs.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Asymmetric Enforcement Dilemma

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the asymmetric enforcement dilemma, where farmers and staff have different preferences and incentives regarding formal authorization and enforcement.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Sequential Social Learning
#### Tension: Farmers observe neighbors' capacitor adoption outcomes and decide whether to imitate, with diffusion occurring only after a successful coordinated trial.
#### Sequential Representation:

2. Farmer observes outcome

#### Justification: This action situation highlights the sequential social learning process, where farmers observe neighbors' outcomes and decide whether to imitate, leading to the diffusion of capacitor adoption.
