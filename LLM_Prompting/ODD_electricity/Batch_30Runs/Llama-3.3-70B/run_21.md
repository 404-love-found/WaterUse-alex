### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Coordination Dilemma in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual cooperation yielding the best outcome but unilateral investment providing little benefit.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two farmers deciding whether to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage stability for both, but if only one invests, the benefit is minimal.

#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma

| --- | --- | --- |

| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 3, 2 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This situation highlights the tension where one farmer's contribution to transformer capacity upgrades benefits both, but the contributor bears the cost, leading to a free-rider problem.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction
#### Title: Common-Pool Resource Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmers must decide how much groundwater to extract, with individual high extraction dominating in the short run but mutual high extraction leading to depletion and increased costs.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Extracts High | Farmer 2 Extracts Low |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts High** | 3, 3 | 4, 1 |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts Low** | 1, 4 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This situation reflects the classic common-pool resource dilemma, where individual farmers have an incentive to extract more groundwater, but collective over-extraction leads to resource depletion and increased costs.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Asymmetric Enforcement Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmers decide whether to seek formal authorization, and staff decide whether to enforce rules, with formal compliance providing legitimacy but informal access offering cheaper electricity.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Request or Informal Access
2. Staff chooses: Enforce or Tolerate
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Enforce, outcome is (2, 3)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Tolerate, outcome is (4, 1)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Enforce, outcome is (1, 4)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Tolerate, outcome is (3, 2)
#### Justification: This action situation represents the sequential decision-making process between farmers and staff regarding authorization and enforcement, with outcomes depending on the combination of choices made by both parties.

### Action Situation 6: Capacitor Adoption through Social Learning
#### Title: Sequential Social Learning
#### Tension: Farmers observe neighbors' outcomes from capacitor adoption and decide whether to imitate, with successful coordinated adoption spreading through the social network.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Neighbor adopts capacitor and observes outcome
2. Farmer observes neighbor's outcome and decides: Adopt or Not Adopt
- If Neighbor's outcome is successful, Farmer is more likely to adopt
- If Neighbor's outcome is unsuccessful, Farmer is less likely to adopt
#### Justification: This situation captures the process of social learning in capacitor adoption, where farmers observe the outcomes of their neighbors and make decisions based on that information, leading to the potential spread of adoption through the social network.
