# Run 21 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Tension: 
Coordination problem among farmers to invest in voltage-stabilizing equipment.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Doesn't Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Doesn't Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Mutual adoption yields shared improvement (3, 3), unilateral adoption yields no added private benefit (1, 2), and no adoption results in baseline payoffs (2, 2).

### Action Situation 2: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization
#### Tension: 
Free-rider problem where one farmer's investment benefits both but costs fall solely on the investor.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Doesn't Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 2, 3 | 1, 3 |
| **Farmer 1 Doesn't Contribute** | 3, 2 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Contributor bears cost while non-contributor benefits more if only one invests (1, 3), but mutual contribution is collectively beneficial (2, 3).

### Action Situation 3: Mutual-Exchange Coordination between Farmer and Staff
#### Tension: 
Reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage in informal exchange.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Staff Offers Exchange | Staff Doesn't Offer Exchange |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Offers Exchange** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer Doesn't Offer Exchange** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Matched cooperation yields mutual gain (3, 3), but mismatched expectations create losses (1, 2 or 2, 1).

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Tension: 
Individual high extraction dominates in the short run, but mutual high extraction accelerates depletion.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Farmer 2 Restrains | Farmer 2 Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts** | 3, 1 | 4, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Restrains** | 1, 3 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Mutual restraint sustains yields (2, 2), unilateral extraction offers short-term gain (4, 1 or 1, 4), and mutual extraction accelerates depletion (4, 4).

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement Asymmetric Coordination
#### Tension: 
Staff may withhold effort to avoid maintenance burden, while farmers may prefer informal access.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Staff Invests | Staff Doesn't Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Seeks Formal Access** | 2, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer Seeks Informal Access** | 3, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal (2, 3), but if farmer makes formal request and staff withhold, farmer incurs loss (1, 2).
