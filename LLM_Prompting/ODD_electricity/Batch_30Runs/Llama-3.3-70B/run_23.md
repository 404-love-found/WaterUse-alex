### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with benefits strongest when adoption is coordinated.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | 3, 3 (Improved stability) | 1, 2 (Weak improvement) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt** | 2, 1 (No improvement) | 0, 0 (No change) |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to adopt capacitors. The payoff matrix shows that mutual adoption yields the best outcome for both farmers, but unilateral adoption provides little benefit, creating a coordination problem.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization
#### Title: Authorization Dilemma for Transformer Capacity
#### Tension: One farmer's authorization or investment in transformer capacity benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer, creating a free-rider incentive.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 2, 3 (Improved reliability) | 1, 2 (Cost borne by Farmer 1) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 3, 1 (Free-rider benefit) | 0, 0 (No improvement) |
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the asymmetric authorization dilemma. The matrix illustrates that while mutual contribution improves reliability, one farmer contributing while the other does not creates an incentive for free-riding.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction
#### Title: Groundwater Extraction Dilemma
#### Tension: Individual high extraction can dominate in the short run, but mutual high extraction accelerates depletion and raises future costs.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Extracts High | Farmer 2 Extracts Low |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts High** | 2, 2 (Short-term gain, long-term loss) | 3, 1 (Farmer 1 gains, Farmer 2 loses) |

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension in groundwater extraction. The payoff matrix shows that while high extraction provides short-term benefits, mutual conservation is necessary for long-term sustainability.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Formal Authorization and Enforcement
#### Tension: Farmers decide whether to seek formal authorization, and staff decide whether to enforce rules or tolerate informal access.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Request or Informal Access
2. Staff chooses: Enforce or Tolerate
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff Enforces, outcome is (2, 1)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff Tolerates, outcome is (3, 2)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff Enforces, outcome is (1, 3)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff Tolerates, outcome is (0, 0)
#### Justification: This action situation represents the sequential game of formal authorization and enforcement. The outcome depends on the farmer's choice and the staff's subsequent action, reflecting the tension between formal compliance and informal exchange.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Learning from Visible Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers observe neighbors' adoption of capacitors and decide whether to imitate, with diffusion path-dependent on visible success.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Neighbor adopts capacitor and outcome is visible (success or failure)
2. Farmer decides: Adopt or Not Adopt based on observed outcome
- If Neighbor's adoption is successful, Farmer adopts with probability p
- If Neighbor's adoption fails, Farmer does not adopt with probability 1-p
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the social learning process in capacitor adoption. The sequential representation captures how farmers learn from their neighbors' experiences and make decisions based on observed outcomes.
