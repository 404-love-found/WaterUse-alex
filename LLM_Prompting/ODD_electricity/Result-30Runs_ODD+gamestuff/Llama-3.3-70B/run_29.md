### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Coordination Dilemma in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared improvement.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two neighboring farmers deciding whether to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage stability, but unilateral investment yields little private benefit.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization-Enforcement Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmer's formal request and staff's investment in capacity or maintenance improve reliability, but staff bear effort costs and farmers bear formal fees.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer requests formal access (or seeks informal access).
2. Staff decide to invest in capacity/maintenance (or withhold effort).
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the sequential strategic tension between a farmer and sub-station staff, where the farmer's formal request and staff's investment improve reliability, but create asymmetric incentives.

### Action Situation 6: Capacitor Adoption with Social Learning
#### Title: Social Learning and Coordination
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful neighbors, but diffusion is path-dependent and affected by visible adoption outcomes.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor (or does not adopt).
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome and decides to adopt (or not).
#### Justification: This action situation captures the sequential strategic tension between two farmers, where social learning and visible adoption outcomes influence the diffusion of capacitor adoption.
