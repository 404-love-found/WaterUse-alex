4. Collusion Exchange (Battle of the Sexes):
Location: Between farmer and staff with potential tie.
Players: Farmer, Staff.
Roles: Farmer – potential colluder; Staff – potential colluder.
Actions: Farmer: Offer collusion (Collude) or Not Offer (Formal). Staff: Accept collusion (Collude) or Reject (Formal). Here we frame as choice between informal collusion and formal interaction.
Control rules: If both choose Collude, informal exchange occurs: farmer gets benefits (e.g., unauthorized use tolerance, capacity) without formal fees, staff gets bribes but risk. If both choose Formal, formal process: farmer pays fee, gets service, staff gets official fee, no risk. If farmer Collude and staff Formal, farmer's offer rejected, may face penalty (low), staff gets nothing (medium). If farmer Formal and staff Collude, staff offers informal but farmer insists on formal, no deal, farmer gets formal process maybe delayed, staff gets nothing. So outcomes reflect coordination on mode.
Information: They know each other's past ties.
Payoffs: Farmer prefers informal collusion (Collude, Collude) because no fee, best (3). Next prefers formal (Formal, Formal) gets service with fee (2). If mismatch, farmer gets worse: if farmer offers collusion but staff refuses, farmer may be reported or get no service, worst (0). If farmer wants formal but staff offers collusion, farmer may still get formal but with delay or conflict, so low (1). Staff prefers formal (Formal, Formal) because official fee and no risk, best (3). Next prefers collusion (Collude, Collude) gets bribe but risk, second (2). If mismatch: staff rejects collusion (Formal) when farmer offers collusion, staff gets nothing (1). If staff offers collusion but farmer wants formal, staff's offer rejected, maybe reputation loss, worst (0). So payoff matrix:
        Staff
        Collude  Formal
Farmer Collude (3,2)  (0,1)
       Formal  (1,0)  (2,3)
This is a Battle of the Sexes: two pure Nash equilibria: (Collude, Collude) and (Formal, Formal), with each preferring a different one. Strategic tension: coordination with conflict. Temporal: repeated annually.

5. Groundwater Extraction (Prisoner's Dilemma):
Location: Shared aquifer among farmers on same transformer or district.
Players: Two farmers.
Roles: groundwater users.
Actions: Pump Full (Defect) or Restrain (Cooperate).
Control rules: Both pumping full depletes aquifer, increasing pumping costs for both. Both restraining conserves water, lower costs. If one pumps full and other restrains, the full pumper gets high extraction with some cost, the restrainee suffers from depletion and low extraction.
Information: They observe water table depth, past costs.
Outcomes: water extraction, energy costs.
Payoffs: Best for individual is to pump full while other restrains (free-ride on conservation). Second best is both restrain (sustainable). Third is both pump full (depletion, high costs). Worst is to restrain while other pumps full (suffer depletion and low extraction). So ordering: DC > CC > DD > CD. So payoff matrix: (Restrain, Restrain) = (2,2); (Pump Full, Pump Full) = (1,1); (Restrain, Pump Full) = (0,3); (Pump Full, Restrain) = (3,0). That's PD. Strategic tension: Prisoner's Dilemma. Temporal: repeated annually.
