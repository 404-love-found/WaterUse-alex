- Control Rules: If a farmer authorizes, they pay a fee and get a formal connection, and the transformer's authorized capacity increases, improving voltage quality and reliability for all connected farmers. If a farmer does not authorize, they may remain informal (if collusion tie exists) or face penalties, but they benefit from improved capacity if others authorize. The benefit is non-excludable and non-rival (public good). The cost is private.
- Information: Farmers know the cost of authorization and the general benefit of improved capacity, but may not know others' choices.
- Outcomes: Total capacity increases with each authorization; reliability improves incrementally.
