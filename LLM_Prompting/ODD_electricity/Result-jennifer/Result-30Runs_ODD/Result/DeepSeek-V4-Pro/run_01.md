Now, Regularization Offer Game:
Title: Regularization Offer Game
Location: Transformer area, between staff and connected free-rider
Players: Substation staff, Connected free-rider farmer
Roles: Staff: enforcer/regularizer; Farmer: informal user
Actions: Staff: Offer regularization (O) or Not (N). Farmer: Accept (A) or Reject (R) (only meaningful if offered).
Control Rules: If staff offers and farmer accepts, farmer pays fee, gets formal connection, staff achieves compliance. If staff offers and farmer rejects, staff may impose penalty, farmer worse off. If staff doesn't offer, farmer remains informal.
Information: Staff knows farmer's free-rider status, workload. Farmer knows own preference for informal.
Outcomes: Formalization or continued informal use, penalties, fees.
Payoffs: Farmer: N (status quo) = 3; O,A = 2 (formal, pays fee); O,R = 0 (penalty). Staff: O,A = 3 (compliance), N = 2 (status quo), O,R = 1 (effort wasted, conflict). Matrix (Farmer, Staff):
        A     R
O    (2,3) (0,1)
N    (3,2) (3,2)
Strategic Tension: Coercion/ultimatum. Staff can force acceptance by threatening penalty. The equilibrium is (O,A) even though farmer prefers N. This reflects power asymmetry.
Temporal: Annual or as needed.
Relevant Rules: Boundary: staff and connected free-rider with tie. Choice rules: staff willingness declines with workload; farmer's willingness to accept is low but forced by penalty threat.
