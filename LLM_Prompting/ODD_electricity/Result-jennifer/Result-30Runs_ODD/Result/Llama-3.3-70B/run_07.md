# Run 7 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

Here are the distinct action situations extracted from the model:

1. **Capacity Provision Game**
	* Title: Capacity Provision Game
	* Location: Transformer group level
	* Players: Farmers, Substation staff
	* Roles: Electricity consumers, Service providers
	* Actions: Farmers - invest in capacity, free-ride; Substation staff - invest in capacity, not invest
	* Control Rules: Farmers' investments lead to increased capacity, but free-riding farmers benefit without contributing
	* Information: Farmers know their capacity needs and costs, substation staff know capacity requirements and investment costs
	* Outcomes: Increased capacity, reduced overload, improved voltage quality
	* Payoffs: 
		|  | Invest | Not Invest |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Invest | 3, 2 | 1, 3 |
		| Not Invest | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
	* Strategic Tension: Public Goods Game, where farmers must decide whether to contribute to shared capacity
	* Temporal Structure: Repeated annually
	* Relevant Rules: Choice rules - farmers decide whether to invest, control rules - investments lead to increased capacity

2. **Collusion Exchange Game**
	* Title: Collusion Exchange Game
	* Location: Substation level
	* Players: Farmers, Substation staff
	* Roles: Electricity consumers, Enforcers
	* Actions: Farmers - offer bribe, not offer bribe; Substation staff - accept bribe, not accept bribe
	* Control Rules: Bribe offers lead to informal connections, but detection leads to penalties
	* Information: Farmers know their connection needs and costs, substation staff know detection risks and bribe benefits
	* Outcomes: Informal connections, reduced enforcement, improved access
	* Payoffs: 
		|  | Offer Bribe | Not Offer Bribe |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Accept Bribe | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
		| Not Accept Bribe | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
	* Strategic Tension: Game of Trust, where farmers and substation staff must decide whether to engage in informal exchanges
	* Temporal Structure: Repeated annually
	* Relevant Rules: Choice rules - farmers decide whether to offer bribe, control rules - bribe offers lead to informal connections

3. **Groundwater Extraction Game**
	* Title: Groundwater Extraction Game
	* Location: Aquifer level
	* Players: Farmers
	* Roles: Groundwater extractors
	* Actions: Farmers - extract, restrain
	* Control Rules: Extraction leads to aquifer depletion, but restraint reduces energy costs
	* Information: Farmers know their extraction needs and costs, aquifer depletion rates
	* Outcomes: Aquifer depletion, reduced energy costs, improved water table
	* Payoffs: 
		|  | Extract | Restrain |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Extract | 2, 2 | 1, 3 |
		| Restrain | 3, 1 | 0, 0 |
	* Strategic Tension: Common Pool Resource Game, where farmers must decide whether to extract or restrain
	* Temporal Structure: Repeated annually
	* Relevant Rules: Choice rules - farmers decide whether to extract, control rules - extraction leads to aquifer depletion, restraint reduces energy costs

4. **Authorization Game**
	* Title: Authorization Game
	* Location: Substation level
	* Players: Farmers, Substation staff
	* Roles: Electricity consumers, Enforcers
	* Actions: Farmers - seek authorization, not seek authorization; Substation staff - invest in service, not invest
	* Control Rules: Authorization leads to formal connections, but investment costs are borne by substation staff
	* Information: Farmers know their connection needs and costs, substation staff know investment costs and authorization benefits
	* Outcomes: Formal connections, improved access, reduced informal connections
	* Payoffs: 
		|  | Seek Authorization | Not Seek Authorization |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Invest | 3, 2 | 1, 3 |
		| Not Invest | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
	* Strategic Tension: Authorization Game, where farmers and substation staff must decide whether to invest in formal connections
	* Temporal Structure: Repeated annually
	* Relevant Rules: Choice rules - farmers decide whether to seek authorization, control rules - authorization leads to formal connections

5. **DSM Coordination Game**
	* Title: DSM Coordination Game
	* Location: Transformer group level
	* Players: Farmers
	* Roles: Electricity consumers
	* Actions: Farmers - adopt DSM, not adopt DSM
	* Control Rules: Adoption leads to improved voltage quality, but benefits depend on neighbor adoption
	* Information: Farmers know their DSM needs and costs, neighbor adoption rates
	* Outcomes: Improved voltage quality, reduced energy costs, increased adoption
	* Payoffs: 
		|  | Adopt DSM | Not Adopt DSM |
		| --- | --- | --- |

		| Not Adopt DSM | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |

	* Temporal Structure: Repeated annually
	* Relevant Rules: Choice rules - farmers decide whether to adopt DSM, control rules - adoption leads to improved voltage quality
