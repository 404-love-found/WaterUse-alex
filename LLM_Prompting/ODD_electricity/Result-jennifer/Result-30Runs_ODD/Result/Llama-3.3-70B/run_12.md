### Action Situations in Electricity-Irrigation Governance

Based on the ODD+D description provided, the following action situations are identified, each representing a distinct governance interaction within the context of electricity-irrigation governance in Andhra Pradesh, India.

1. **Capacity Provision Game**
   - **Title**: Investment in Shared Electrical Infrastructure
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumers
   - **Actions**: Invest in transformer capacity, Do not invest
   - **Control Rules**: Farmers decide simultaneously; investment leads to improved electricity reliability
   - **Information**: Past experiences of electricity service quality, observed neighbor investments
   - **Outcomes**: Improved or degraded electricity reliability, financial gain or loss
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Invest | Do Not Invest |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Invest** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Do Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Public Goods Game - The dilemma of contributing to a public good (electricity infrastructure) where individual contributions are costly but the benefit is shared.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Boundary rules defining who can invest, choice rules governing investment decisions

2. **Collusion Exchange Game**
   - **Title**: Informal Exchanges between Farmers and Substation Staff
   - **Location**: Substation level
   - **Players**: Farmers, Substation Staff
   - **Roles**: Service seekers, Service providers
   - **Actions**: Engage in collusion, Do not engage
   - **Control Rules**: Decisions are made simultaneously; collusion leads to favorable treatment
   - **Information**: Past experiences with staff, perceived risk of detection
   - **Outcomes**: Favorable or unfavorable treatment, financial gain or loss
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Engage | Do Not Engage |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Engage** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Do Not Engage** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Game of Trust - The dilemma of trusting another party to reciprocate in an informal exchange.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules governing engagement in collusion, control rules defining outcomes of collusion

3. **Groundwater Extraction Game**
   - **Title**: Groundwater Extraction Decisions
   - **Location**: Individual farm level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Water extractors
   - **Actions**: Extract at full rate, Restrict extraction
   - **Control Rules**: Decisions are made sequentially; extraction affects aquifer level and pumping costs
   - **Information**: Current aquifer level, past extraction costs
   - **Outcomes**: Increased or decreased pumping costs, aquifer depletion
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Extract Full | Restrict |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Extract Full** | 2, 2 | 3, 1 |
     | **Restrict** | 1, 3 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Common Pool Resource Game - The dilemma of over-extracting a shared resource (groundwater) due to individual self-interest.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Control rules governing extraction outcomes, boundary rules defining access to groundwater
