### Action Situations in Electricity-Irrigation Governance

1. **Capacity Provision Game**
   - **Title**: Investment in Shared Electrical Infrastructure
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumers, investors in shared infrastructure
   - **Actions**: Invest in transformer capacity, Free-ride on others' investments
   - **Control Rules**: Investments lead to improved voltage quality and reliability; free-riding may lead to overloads and decreased service quality
   - **Information**: Past voltage quality, peer investment decisions
   - **Outcomes**: Improved or degraded grid quality, increased or decreased reliability
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Invest | Free-ride |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Invest** | 2, 2 | 1, 3 |
     | **Free-ride** | 3, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Public Goods Game - The dilemma between individual cost-saving and collective reliability
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding investment decisions, control rules governing grid quality and reliability

2. **Groundwater Extraction Game**
   - **Title**: Groundwater Use Management
   - **Location**: Aquifer level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Groundwater extractors
   - **Actions**: Restrict extraction, Extract at full capacity
   - **Control Rules**: Extraction levels affect aquifer depletion and energy costs for pumping
   - **Information**: Current aquifer level, past extraction rates
   - **Outcomes**: Aquifer depletion, increased energy costs for pumping
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Restrict | Extract Full |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Restrict** | 2, 2 | 1, 3 |
     | **Extract Full** | 3, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Common Pool Resource Game - The dilemma of over-extraction
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding extraction decisions, control rules governing aquifer depletion and energy costs

3. **Collusion Exchange Game**
   - **Title**: Informal Exchanges between Farmers and Staff
   - **Location**: Substation level
   - **Players**: Farmers, Substation staff
   - **Roles**: Service seekers, service providers
   - **Actions**: Engage in collusion, Comply with formal rules
   - **Control Rules**: Collusion can lead to unauthorized connections or service; compliance ensures formal authorization but may involve costs and delays
   - **Information**: Risk of detection, benefits of informal connections
   - **Outcomes**: Unauthorized connections, improved service, or detection and penalties
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Collude | Comply |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Collude** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Comply** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Collusion Game - The dilemma between mutual benefit through informal exchanges and the risk of detection
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding engagement in collusion, control rules governing detection and service provision

4. **Authorization Game**
   - **Title**: Formal Authorization for Electricity Connections
   - **Location**: Substation level
   - **Players**: Farmers, Substation staff
   - **Roles**: Service seekers, service providers
   - **Actions**: Seek formal authorization, Pursue informal connection
   - **Control Rules**: Formal authorization ensures legal and reliable service; informal connections risk detection and penalties
   - **Information**: Costs and benefits of formal authorization, risk of detection for informal connections
   - **Outcomes**: Authorized or unauthorized connections, reliability of service
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Formal | Informal |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Formal** | 2, 2 | 1, 3 |
     | **Informal** | 3, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Authorization Game - The dilemma between seeking formal authorization and pursuing informal connections
   - **Temporal Structure**: One-shot
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding authorization decisions, control rules governing service provision and detection

5. **DSM Coordination Game**
   - **Title**: Adoption of Demand-Side Management Technologies
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Technology adopters
   - **Actions**: Adopt DSM technology, Do not adopt
   - **Control Rules**: Adoption leads to improved efficiency and potential for shared benefits; non-adoption may result in continued inefficiency
   - **Information**: Peer adoption rates, potential benefits of DSM technology
   - **Outcomes**: Improved efficiency, shared benefits, or continued inefficiency
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Adopt | Not Adopt |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Adopt** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Coordination Game - The dilemma between adopting DSM technology for shared benefits and not adopting due to individual costs
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding adoption decisions, control rules governing efficiency and shared benefits

6. **Social Learning Game**
   - **Title**: Observation and Imitation of Technology Adoption
   - **Location**: Village level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Observers, adopters
   - **Actions**: Observe and imitate, Do not observe or imitate
   - **Control Rules**: Observation and imitation can lead to widespread adoption of efficient technologies; lack of observation may result in missed opportunities
   - **Information**: Observable outcomes of peer technology adoption
   - **Outcomes**: Widespread adoption of efficient technologies, or continued use of inefficient technologies
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Imitate | Not Imitate |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Imitate** | 2, 2 | 1, 1 |
     | **Not Imitate** | 1, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Non-strategic sequential process - The process of observing and imitating technology adoption decisions
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Control rules governing observation and imitation, outcome rules regarding technology adoption

Each of these action situations reflects a distinct governance interaction within the context of electricity-irrigation governance, involving strategic dilemmas and non-strategic sequential processes that influence the outcomes for farmers and substation staff. The payoff matrices are designed to capture the essence of the strategic tensions involved, using ordinal ranks to represent preference orders without implying numeric utilities. The games comply with the ODD+D description by incorporating the institutional logic, environmental feedback, and asymmetries present in the model.
