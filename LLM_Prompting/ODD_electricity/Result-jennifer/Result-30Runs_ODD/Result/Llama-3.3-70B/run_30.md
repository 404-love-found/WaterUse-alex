# Run 30 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situations

1. **Capacity Provision Game**
   - **Title**: Deciding on Shared Electrical Infrastructure Capacity
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Farmers, Substation Staff
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumers, Service providers
   - **Actions**: Contribute to capacity, Free-ride
   - **Control Rules**: Contribution leads to better grid quality, free-riding may lead to penalties or reduced service quality
   - **Information**: Past grid quality, peer contribution rates
   - **Outcomes**: Grid quality improvement, penalty or service reduction
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Contribute | Free-ride |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Contribute** | 2, 2 | 0, 3 |
     | **Free-ride** | 3, 0 | 1, 1 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Public Goods Game - contributing to shared infrastructure benefits all, but individual costs may deter contribution.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding contribution, control rules on grid quality and penalties

2. **Groundwater Extraction Game**
   - **Title**: Managing Groundwater Extraction
   - **Location**: Aquifer level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Groundwater extractors
   - **Actions**: Extract at full rate, Restrain extraction
   - **Control Rules**: Extraction affects aquifer level and pumping costs
   - **Information**: Current aquifer level, past extraction costs
   - **Outcomes**: Aquifer level change, pumping cost change
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Extract Full | Restrain |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Extract Full** | 2, 2 | 0, 3 |
     | **Restrain** | 3, 0 | 1, 1 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Common Pool Resource Game - individual extraction benefits the farmer, but collective over-extraction depletes the resource.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules on extraction rates, control rules on aquifer depletion and cost impacts

   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules on collusion, control rules on detection and service quality

### Analysis of Strategic Core and Comparison

- The **Capacity Provision Game** and **Groundwater Extraction Game** both involve public goods and common pool resource dilemmas, respectively, but they are distinct due to their different contexts (electrical infrastructure vs. groundwater) and player roles.
- The **Collusion Exchange Game** and **Authorization Game** involve different strategic tensions (game of trust vs. authorization game), despite both being between farmers and staff, due to their different focuses (informal exchanges vs. formal authorization).
- The **DSM Coordination Game** is distinct from the **Capacity Provision Game** because it focuses on the coordination of technology adoption rather than infrastructure contribution.
- The **Social Learning Game** is a non-strategic process and thus differs fundamentally from the other games.

Each action situation reflects a different governance interaction, ensuring strategic diversity across the model. The payoff matrices are grounded in the context, reflecting the consequences of actions in terms of ordinal ranks, and comply with the ODD+D description.
