### Action Situations in the Electricity-Irrigation Governance Model

1. **Capacity Provision Game**
   - **Title**: Investment in Shared Electrical Infrastructure
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Farmers, Substation Staff
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumers, Service providers
   - **Actions**: Invest in capacity, Do not invest
   - **Control Rules**: Investment decisions affect grid reliability and voltage quality
   - **Information**: Past voltage quality, peer investment decisions
   - **Outcomes**: Improved or degraded grid quality, budget changes
   - **Payoffs**: Reflect reliability, cost savings, and potential for future investments
   - **Strategic Tension**: Public Goods Game - Farmers must decide whether to contribute to shared infrastructure, knowing that all will benefit but individual costs may outweigh personal benefits.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding investment, control rules linking investments to outcomes

   **Game Description**:
   - **Players**: Farmer, Substation Staff
   - **Actions**: Invest, Not Invest
   - **Payoff Matrix**:
     |  | Farmer Invests | Farmer Does Not Invest |

     | **Staff Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |

6. **Social Learning Game**
   - **Title**: Observational Learning among Farmers
   - **Location**: Village level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Observers, Adopters
   - **Actions**: Observe and adopt, Do not observe or adopt
   - **Control Rules**: Observational learning affects technology adoption decisions
   - **Information**: Observations of peer adoption decisions and outcomes
   - **Outcomes**: Changes in adoption rates, improved energy efficiency
   - **Payoffs**: Reflect adoption rates, energy savings, and social influence

   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding observation and adoption, control rules linking observation to outcomes

### Analysis of Strategic Core and Comparison

Each action situation represents a distinct governance interaction with its strategic core analyzed based on the type of game it resembles. The comparison across situations highlights different strategic tensions and payoffs, ensuring diversity in governance interactions.

- **Capacity Provision Game** and **DSM Coordination Game** both involve public goods dilemmas but differ in their focus on infrastructure investment versus technology adoption.
- **Collusion Exchange Game** and **Authorization Game** involve trust and institutional compliance but are distinct in their focus on informal exchanges versus formal connection decisions.
- **Groundwater Extraction Game** stands out as a common pool resource game, emphasizing the sustainability of groundwater use.
- **Social Learning Game** is unique as a non-strategic sequential process, focusing on observational learning and its impact on adoption decisions.

These distinctions ensure that each action situation offers a unique perspective on the governance interactions within the electricity-irrigation system, complying with the ODD+D description and providing a comprehensive understanding of the strategic tensions at play.
