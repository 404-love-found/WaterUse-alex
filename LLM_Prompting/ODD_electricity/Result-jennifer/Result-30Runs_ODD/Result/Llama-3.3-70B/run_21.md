### Action Situations in the Electricity-Irrigation Governance Model

1. **Capacity Provision Game**
   - **Title**: Investing in Shared Electrical Infrastructure
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumers
   - **Actions**: Invest in capacity, Free-ride
   - **Control Rules**: Collective investment leads to improved grid reliability; free-riding may lead to overload and reduced reliability
   - **Information**: Past voltage quality, peer investment decisions
   - **Outcomes**: Grid reliability change, Budget change
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Invest | Free-ride |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Invest** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Free-ride** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Public Goods Game - The dilemma between contributing to shared infrastructure for collective benefit versus free-riding for individual cost savings.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding investment decisions, control rules governing grid reliability

2. **Collusion Exchange Game**
   - **Title**: Informal Exchanges Between Farmers and Staff
   - **Location**: Substation level
   - **Players**: Farmer, Substation Staff
   - **Roles**: Service seeker, Service provider
   - **Actions**: Engage in collusion, Comply formally
   - **Control Rules**: Collusion leads to mutual benefits but risks detection; formal compliance ensures legality but may lack benefits of collusion
   - **Information**: Local collusion density, trust networks
   - **Outcomes**: Service quality change, Risk of detection
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Collude | Comply |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Collude** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Comply** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Game of Trust - The dilemma of trusting in mutual benefits from informal exchanges versus the safety of formal compliance.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding engagement in collusion, control rules governing detection risk

3. **Groundwater Extraction Game**
   - **Title**: Managing Groundwater Resources
   - **Location**: Aquifer level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Resource extractors
   - **Actions**: Extract at full rate, Restrain extraction
   - **Control Rules**: Over-extraction depletes the aquifer, increasing pumping costs; restraint conserves the resource but may reduce immediate benefits
   - **Information**: Aquifer stress, peer extraction decisions
   - **Outcomes**: Aquifer level change, Pumping cost change
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Extract | Restrain |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Extract** | 3, 3 | 2, 1 |
     | **Restrain** | 1, 2 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Common Pool Resource Game - The dilemma between extracting a shared resource for immediate benefits versus conserving it for long-term sustainability.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding extraction decisions, control rules governing aquifer depletion

4. **DSM Coordination Game**
   - **Title**: Adopting Demand-Side Management Technologies
   - **Location**: Farmer level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Technology adopters
   - **Actions**: Adopt DSM technology, Do not adopt
   - **Control Rules**: Collective adoption leads to improved energy efficiency; individual adoption may not yield full benefits without widespread adoption
   - **Information**: Peer adoption decisions, technology efficacy
   - **Outcomes**: Energy efficiency change, Budget change
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Adopt | Not Adopt |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Adopt** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Coordination Game - The dilemma between adopting a technology for collective benefit versus waiting for others to adopt first.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding adoption decisions, control rules governing technology efficacy

5. **Authorization Game**
   - **Title**: Seeking Formal Authorization for Electricity Connections
   - **Location**: Substation level
   - **Players**: Farmer, Substation Staff
   - **Roles**: Service seeker, Service provider
   - **Actions**: Seek formal authorization, Seek informal connection
   - **Control Rules**: Formal authorization ensures legal connection but may involve costs and bureaucracy; informal connections may be cheaper but risk detection and service quality issues
   - **Information**: Local authorization processes, informal connection risks
   - **Outcomes**: Service quality change, Risk of detection
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Seek Formal | Seek Informal |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Seek Formal** | 3, 3 | 2, 1 |
     | **Seek Informal** | 1, 2 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Authorization Game - The dilemma between seeking formal authorization for a legal and reliable connection versus opting for an informal connection for potential cost savings and convenience.
   - **Temporal Structure**: One-shot
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules regarding authorization decisions, control rules governing service quality and detection risk

6. **Social Learning Game**
   - **Title**: Observing and Learning from Neighbors' Technology Adoption
   - **Location**: Farmer level
   - **Players**: Farmers
   - **Roles**: Observers, Adopters
   - **Actions**: Observe and learn, Do not observe
   - **Control Rules**: Observing neighbors' adoption decisions and outcomes can inform own adoption decisions; not observing may lead to missed opportunities for learning
   - **Information**: Neighbors' adoption decisions and outcomes
   - **Outcomes**: Knowledge change, Adoption decision change
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Observe | Not Observe |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Observe** | 3, 3 | 2, 1 |
     | **Not Observe** | 1, 2 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Non-strategic sequential process - The process of learning from others' experiences and adapting one's own decisions accordingly.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Control rules governing observation and learning processes

### Analysis of Strategic Core and Comparison

- **Capacity Provision Game** and **DSM Coordination Game** both involve collective action dilemmas but differ in context (infrastructure investment versus technology adoption).
- **Collusion Exchange Game** and **Authorization Game** involve interactions between farmers and staff but are distinct in their focus on informal exchanges versus formal authorization processes.
- **Groundwater Extraction Game** is unique in its focus on resource management and the tragedy of the commons.
- **Social Learning Game** stands out as a non-strategic process focusing on observation and learning.

Each game represents a distinct strategic tension or process within the electricity-irrigation governance model, ensuring a diverse set of governance interactions are captured. The payoffs and game structures are designed to reflect the complexities and challenges of managing shared resources and infrastructure, as well as the interactions between different stakeholders in the system.
