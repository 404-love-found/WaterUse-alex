# Run 2 — Qwen/Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
1. **Title**: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
2. **Location**: Transformer group level
3. **Players**: Farmers
4. **Roles**: Electricity consumers
5. **Actions**:
   - **Invest**: Install a capacitor.
   - **Do Not Invest**: Do not install a capacitor.
6. **Control Rules**: The outcome of each farmer’s decision affects the overall voltage stability of the transformer. If enough farmers invest, the transformer’s voltage quality improves. If only a few or none invest, the transformer’s voltage quality remains poor.
7. **Information**: Farmers observe the visible adoption rates and the resulting voltage quality improvements or failures.
8. **Outcomes**: 
   - Transformer voltage quality improves.
   - Transformer voltage quality remains poor.
9. **Payoffs**: 
   - **(3, 3)**: Both farmers benefit from improved voltage quality.
   - **(1, 1)**: Both farmers experience poor voltage quality.
10. **Strategic Tension**: This is a **Coordination Game**. Farmers face the dilemma of whether to invest in capacitors, knowing that the benefits are only realized if enough farmers also invest.
11. **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually.
12. **Relevant Rules**: Farmer social networks, past experiences with voltage quality, and perceived risks of voltage drops.

### Action Situation 2: Farmer-Staff Collusion Exchange
1. **Title**: Farmer-Staff Collusion Exchange
2. **Location**: Transformer group level
3. **Players**: Farmers, Sub-station staff
4. **Roles**: Electricity consumers, service providers
5. **Actions**:
   - **Collude**: Offer informal access to electricity.
   - **Do Not Collude**: Enforce formal rules.
6. **Control Rules**: If both parties collude, the farmer obtains cheaper, informal access, and the staff avoids enforcement costs. If one party colludes and the other does not, the farmer faces penalties or the staff incurs reputational risk.
7. **Information**: Farmers observe the local collusion density and the staff’s enforcement history. Staff observe the farmer’s willingness to pay and the local risk of detection.
8. **Outcomes**: 
   - Farmer obtains informal access, staff avoids enforcement costs.
   - Strict enforcement with penalties or reputational risk.
9. **Payoffs**: 
   - **(2, 2)**: Farmer obtains informal access, staff avoids enforcement costs.
   - **(0, 0)**: Strict enforcement with penalties or reputational risk.
10. **Strategic Tension**: This is an **Authorization Game**. Farmers and staff face the dilemma of whether to engage in informal exchanges, knowing that mutual cooperation is required for benefits but that unilateral cooperation can lead to losses.
11. **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually.
12. **Relevant Rules**: Social networks, enforcement costs, and reputational risk.

### Action Situation 3: Groundwater Extraction
1. **Title**: Groundwater Extraction
2. **Location**: Groundwater basin level
3. **Players**: Farmers
4. **Roles**: Water users
5. **Actions**:
   - **Extract**: Pump groundwater for irrigation.
   - **Restrain**: Restrain extraction to conserve water.
6. **Control Rules**: Groundwater extraction affects the aquifer level and the cost of extraction. Over-extraction leads to higher pumping costs and faster aquifer depletion.
7. **Information**: Farmers observe the local groundwater level and the visible outcomes of neighbors’ extraction decisions.
8. **Outcomes**: 
   - Groundwater level remains stable.
   - Groundwater level depletes.
9. **Payoffs**: 
   - **(3, 3)**: Farmers benefit from stable groundwater levels and lower pumping costs.
   - **(1, 1)**: Farmers face higher pumping costs and risk depletion.
10. **Strategic Tension**: This is a **Common Pool Resource Game**. Farmers face the dilemma of whether to extract water for immediate benefits or to restrain extraction to avoid depletion and higher costs.
11. **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time.
12. **Relevant Rules**: Aquifer recharge rates, rainfall, and farmer social networks.

### Action Situation 4: Farmer Decision on Formal Authorization
1. **Title**: Farmer Decision on Formal Authorization
2. **Location**: Transformer group level
3. **Players**: Farmers
4. **Roles**: Electricity consumers
5. **Actions**:
   - **Apply for Formal Authorization**: Pay for formal connection.
   - **Do Not Apply**: Seek informal access.
6. **Control Rules**: Formal authorization improves reliability but requires higher costs. Informal access is cheaper but may lead to penalties or service interruptions.
7. **Information**: Farmers observe the local formal connection density and the cost of formal authorization.
8. **Outcomes**: 
   - Reliable electricity with higher costs.
   - Unreliable electricity with lower costs.
9. **Payoffs**: 
   - **(2, 2)**: Reliable electricity with higher costs.
   - **(1, 1)**: Unreliable electricity with lower costs.
10. **Strategic Tension**: This is a **Capacity Provision Game**. Farmers face the dilemma of whether to invest in formal authorization, knowing that benefits spill over to non-contributors.
11. **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually.
12. **Relevant Rules**: Cost of formal authorization, local formal connection density, and perceived risk of service interruptions.

### Action Situation 5: Sub-station Staff Decision on Enforcement
1. **Title**: Sub-station Staff Decision on Enforcement
2. **Location**: Transformer group level
3. **Players**: Sub-station staff
4. **Roles**: Service providers
5. **Actions**:
   - **Enforce Formal Rules**: Invest in enforcement.
   - **Tolerate Informal Access**: Avoid enforcement costs.
6. **Control Rules**: Enforcement reduces unauthorized connections but incurs costs and reputational risk. Tolerating informal access saves costs but risks penalties or service interruptions.
7. **Information**: Staff observe the local enforcement history and the cost of enforcement.
8. **Outcomes**: 
   - Reliable enforcement with costs.
   - Tolerant enforcement with risks.
9. **Payoffs**: 
   - **(2, 2)**: Reliable enforcement with costs.
   - **(1, 1)**: Tolerant enforcement with risks.
10. **Strategic Tension**: This is an **Authorization Game**. Staff face the dilemma of whether to enforce formal rules, knowing that mutual cooperation is required for reliable service but that unilateral enforcement can lead to losses.
11. **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually.
12. **Relevant Rules**: Enforcement costs, reputational risk, and local enforcement history.

### Strategic Core Analysis
1. **Capacitor Adoption Coordination**: This is a **Coordination Game** where both farmers must coordinate to achieve the most preferred outcome. The tension arises from the need for mutual cooperation to improve voltage quality.
2. **Farmer-Staff Collusion Exchange**: This is an **Authorization Game** where both parties must cooperate to achieve the most preferred outcome. The tension arises from the need for mutual cooperation to avoid losses.
3. **Groundwater Extraction**: This is a **Common Pool Resource Game** where mutual restraint is required to achieve the most preferred outcome. The tension arises from the need to balance immediate benefits with long-term sustainability.
4. **Farmer Decision on Formal Authorization**: This is a **Capacity Provision Game** where the most preferred outcome is reliable electricity. The tension arises from the need to balance the benefits of formal authorization with the costs.
5. **Sub-station Staff Decision on Enforcement**: This is an **Authorization Game** where the most preferred outcome is reliable enforcement. The tension arises from the need to balance the costs of enforcement with the risks of tolerating informal access.
