### Action Situations in the Electricity-Irrigation Governance Model

Based on the provided ODD+D description, the following action situations are identified, reflecting distinct governance interactions in the context of electricity-irrigation in Andhra Pradesh, India.

1. **Capacitor Adoption Game**
   - **Title**: Coordination on Capacitor Adoption
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Farmer 1, Farmer 2
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumers
   - **Actions**: Adopt Capacitor, Do Not Adopt
   - **Control Rules**: Outcome depends on both farmers' decisions
   - **Information**: Partial, based on observed neighbors' adoption and perceived voltage quality
   - **Outcomes**: Improved voltage quality if both adopt, no improvement if only one adopts
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Adopt Capacitor | Do Not Adopt |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Adopt Capacitor** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Do Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Coordination dilemma, resembling a **DSM Coordination Game**. Farmers prefer mutual adoption for better voltage quality but may hesitate to adopt unilaterally due to costs.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules based on perceived benefits and costs of capacitor adoption

2. **Groundwater Extraction Game**
   - **Title**: Groundwater Extraction Dilemma
   - **Location**: Aquifer level
   - **Players**: Farmer 1, Farmer 2
   - **Roles**: Groundwater extractors
   - **Actions**: Extract, Conserve
   - **Control Rules**: Outcome depends on both farmers' extraction decisions
   - **Information**: Partial, based on observed water table levels and pumping costs
   - **Outcomes**: Sustainable aquifer if both conserve, depletion if both extract heavily
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Extract | Conserve |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Extract** | 2, 2 | 3, 0 |
     | **Conserve** | 0, 3 | 1, 1 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Tragedy of the Commons, resembling a **Common Pool Resource Game**. Farmers face a dilemma between short-term gains from extraction and long-term sustainability.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules based on extraction costs and benefits, considering aquifer recharge rates

3. **Authorization Game**
   - **Title**: Seeking Formal Authorization
   - **Location**: Substation level
   - **Players**: Farmer, Substation Staff
   - **Roles**: Service seeker, Service provider
   - **Actions**: Seek Authorization, Bypass Authorization
   - **Control Rules**: Outcome depends on both farmer's and staff's decisions
   - **Information**: Asymmetric, staff has more information about connection processes
   - **Outcomes**: Legitimate connection if authorized, potential penalties if bypassed
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Seek Authorization | Bypass Authorization |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Seek Authorization** | 3, 2 | 1, 3 |
     | **Bypass Authorization** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Dilemma of seeking formal authorization versus bypassing it, resembling an **Authorization Game**. The farmer weighs the costs and benefits of formal authorization against the risks of bypassing it.
   - **Temporal Structure**: One-shot or repeated based on connection needs
   - **Relevant Rules**: Boundary rules defining eligibility for authorization, choice rules based on costs and benefits of formal connection

4. **Collusion Exchange Game**
   - **Title**: Informal Exchange between Farmer and Staff
   - **Location**: Substation level
   - **Players**: Farmer, Substation Staff
   - **Roles**: Reciprocal parties
   - **Actions**: Offer Reciprocity, Do Not Offer
   - **Control Rules**: Outcome depends on mutual reciprocity
   - **Information**: Private, based on trust and past interactions
   - **Outcomes**: Mutual benefit if both offer reciprocity, loss if one offers and the other does not
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Offer Reciprocity | Do Not Offer |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Offer Reciprocity** | 3, 3 | 0, 2 |
     | **Do Not Offer** | 2, 0 | 1, 1 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Coordination dilemma in informal exchange, resembling a **Collusion Exchange Game**. Both parties must trust each other to offer reciprocity for mutual benefit.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated over time based on ongoing interactions
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules based on trust, past interactions, and perceived benefits of reciprocity

5. **Capacity Provision Game**
   - **Title**: Contributing to Transformer Capacity
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Farmer 1, Farmer 2
   - **Roles**: Contributors to shared infrastructure
   - **Actions**: Contribute, Free-Ride
   - **Control Rules**: Outcome depends on contributions to capacity
   - **Information**: Partial, based on observed contributions and transformer performance
   - **Outcomes**: Improved transformer reliability if both contribute, decreased reliability if one free-rides
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Contribute | Free-Ride |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Contribute** | 2, 2 | 1, 3 |
     | **Free-Ride** | 3, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Public Goods dilemma, resembling a **Public Goods Game**. Farmers face a dilemma between contributing to shared capacity for collective benefit and free-riding on others' contributions.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually based on capacity needs
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules based on perceived benefits and costs of contributing to transformer capacity

6. **Social Learning Game**
   - **Title**: Observing and Imitating Neighbors
   - **Location**: Village level
   - **Players**: Farmer, Neighbor Farmer
   - **Roles**: Observer, Observed
   - **Actions**: Imitate, Do Not Imitate
   - **Control Rules**: Outcome depends on the observed neighbor's actions and their outcomes
   - **Information**: Observational, based on visible adoption and outcomes
   - **Outcomes**: Adoption of effective practices if imitation is successful, no change if not imitated
   - **Payoffs**: This is a non-strategic sequential process, not a simultaneous game. The payoff is based on the outcome of the observed action.
   - **Strategic Tension**: Non-strategic, resembling a **Social Learning Game**. Farmers learn from observing their neighbors' actions and outcomes, influencing their own decisions.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time as farmers observe and learn from each other
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules based on observed neighbor outcomes and perceived benefits of imitation

### Analysis of Strategic Core and Comparison

- **Capacitor Adoption Game** and **Capacity Provision Game** both involve coordination dilemmas but differ in context (capacitor adoption versus contributing to transformer capacity). They reflect different strategic tensions despite similar game structures.
- **Groundwater Extraction Game** and **Common Pool Resource Game** share similarities in the tragedy of the commons dilemma but are distinct in their focus on groundwater extraction versus general common pool resources.
- **Authorization Game** and **Collusion Exchange Game** involve different forms of interaction between farmers and substation staff, highlighting the complexities of formal and informal relationships in the governance model.

### Revision for Strategic Diversity

To ensure strategic diversity, each action situation is distinct and reflects a different governance interaction. No two situations are merged, and each represents a unique strategic tension or non-strategic process within the electricity-irrigation governance context. The payoff matrices are designed to reflect the specific context and strategic tensions of each situation, ensuring that the games comply with the ODD+D description and provide a comprehensive view of the governance dynamics at play.
