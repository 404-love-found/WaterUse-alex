### Action Situations in Electricity-Irrigation Governance

1. **Capacitor Adoption Game**
   - **Title**: Capacitor Adoption
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Two farmers sharing the same transformer
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumers
   - **Actions**: Invest in capacitor, Do not invest
   - **Control Rules**: Adoption leads to improved voltage stability if both invest; otherwise, the benefit is weak or unattributable.
   - **Information**: Past voltage quality, peer adoption rates
   - **Outcomes**: Improved or unchanged voltage stability
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Farmer 1 Invests | Farmer 1 Does Not Invest |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Farmer 2 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Farmer 2 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Coordination dilemma, akin to a **DSM Coordination Game**. Farmers need to coordinate their investments to achieve the best outcome.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules depend on perceived benefits and costs of capacitor adoption.

2. **Groundwater Extraction Game**
   - **Title**: Groundwater Extraction
   - **Location**: Aquifer level
   - **Players**: Two farmers sharing the same aquifer
   - **Roles**: Groundwater extractors
   - **Actions**: Extract at high rate, Extract at low rate
   - **Control Rules**: High extraction rates deplete the aquifer faster, increasing pumping costs.
   - **Information**: Current aquifer level, past extraction rates
   - **Outcomes**: Aquifer depletion rate, pumping costs
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Farmer 1 Extracts High | Farmer 1 Extracts Low |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Farmer 2 Extracts High** | 2, 2 | 1, 3 |
     | **Farmer 2 Extracts Low** | 3, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: **Common Pool Resource Game** dilemma. Individual high extraction is beneficial in the short term but leads to over-depletion.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules depend on short-term benefits versus long-term sustainability.

3. **Authorization Game**
   - **Title**: Electricity Connection Authorization
   - **Location**: Substation level
   - **Players**: Farmer, Substation staff
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumer, Service provider
   - **Actions**: Farmer: Seek formal connection, Seek informal connection; Staff: Authorize, Do not authorize
   - **Control Rules**: Formal authorization leads to reliable service but at a cost; informal connections are cheaper but risk penalties.
   - **Information**: Past service reliability, penalty risks
   - **Outcomes**: Service reliability, penalty risk
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Staff Authorizes | Staff Does Not Authorize |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Farmer Seeks Formal** | 3, 2 | 0, 1 |
     | **Farmer Seeks Informal** | 1, 3 | 2, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: **Authorization Game** dilemma. The farmer weighs the cost of formal authorization against the risk of informal connections.
   - **Temporal Structure**: One-shot, with potential for repeated interactions
   - **Relevant Rules**: Boundary rules define who can seek authorization; choice rules depend on perceived risks and benefits.

4. **Capacity Provision Game**
   - **Title**: Transformer Capacity Contribution
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Two farmers sharing the same transformer
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumers
   - **Actions**: Contribute to capacity upgrade, Do not contribute
   - **Control Rules**: Contribution improves transformer reliability but is costly; free-riding benefits from others' contributions.
   - **Information**: Current transformer capacity, past contribution rates
   - **Outcomes**: Transformer reliability, contribution costs
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Farmer 1 Contributes | Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Farmer 2 Contributes** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: **Public Goods Game** dilemma. Contribution is a public good; individual farmers may prefer to free-ride.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules depend on perceived benefits and costs of contribution.

5. **Collusion Exchange Game**
   - **Title**: Informal Exchange
   - **Location**: Substation level
   - **Players**: Farmer, Substation staff
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumer, Service provider
   - **Actions**: Farmer: Offer informal cooperation, Do not offer; Staff: Accept cooperation, Do not accept
   - **Control Rules**: Mutual cooperation leads to benefits for both; one-sided cooperation leads to losses.
   - **Information**: Past cooperation outcomes, trust levels
   - **Outcomes**: Mutual benefit or loss
   - **Payoffs**:
     |  | Staff Accepts | Staff Does Not Accept |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Farmer Offers Cooperation** | 3, 3 | 0, 2 |
     | **Farmer Does Not Offer** | 2, 0 | 1, 1 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: **Game of Trust** dilemma. Mutual trust is necessary for beneficial outcomes.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules depend on trust and expected reciprocity.

6. **Social Learning Game**
   - **Title**: Technology Adoption
   - **Location**: Village level
   - **Players**: Farmer, Neighboring farmers
   - **Roles**: Technology adopters
   - **Actions**: Adopt new technology, Do not adopt
   - **Control Rules**: Adoption decisions are influenced by observing neighbors' outcomes.
   - **Information**: Visible adoption and outcomes of neighbors
   - **Outcomes**: Adoption rate, perceived technology effectiveness
   - **Payoffs**: Not applicable in a traditional sense; outcomes influence future adoption decisions.
   - **Strategic Tension**: Non-strategic sequential process. Farmers update their strategies based on observed outcomes.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules depend on social learning and perceived benefits of technology adoption.

### Analysis of Strategic Core and Comparison

- **Capacitor Adoption Game** and **Capacity Provision Game** both involve coordination dilemmas but differ in the specific action (capacitor adoption vs. contributing to transformer capacity).
- **Groundwater Extraction Game** is a common pool resource dilemma, distinct from the coordination and public goods dilemmas.
- **Authorization Game** and **Collusion Exchange Game** involve strategic interactions between farmers and substation staff but with different focuses (formal authorization vs. informal cooperation).
- **Social Learning Game** is a non-strategic process, focusing on how farmers learn from each other's technology adoption decisions.

Each action situation presents a distinct governance interaction, reflecting different strategic tensions and decision-making processes within the electricity-irrigation governance context.
