# Run 19 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situations

1. **Capacitor Adoption Game**
   - **Title**: Capacitor Adoption
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Two farmers
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumers
   - **Actions**: Invest in capacitor, Do not invest
   - **Control Rules**: Coordination required for maximum benefit
   - **Information**: Past voltage quality, peer adoption rates
   - **Outcomes**: Improved voltage quality, increased cost
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Invest | Do not invest |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Invest** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Do not invest** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Coordination game, similar to the DSM Coordination Game. Farmers prefer to invest if the other also invests, but not if alone.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Boundary rules defining who can invest, choice rules on investment decisions

2. **Groundwater Extraction Game**
   - **Title**: Groundwater Extraction
   - **Location**: Groundwater basin level
   - **Players**: Two farmers
   - **Roles**: Groundwater extractors
   - **Actions**: Extract heavily, Extract moderately
   - **Control Rules**: Over-extraction depletes aquifer, increasing pumping costs
   - **Information**: Current aquifer level, past extraction rates
   - **Outcomes**: Increased pumping costs, decreased aquifer level
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Extract heavily | Extract moderately |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Extract heavily** | 2, 2 | 3, 0 |
     | **Extract moderately** | 0, 3 | 1, 1 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Common Pool Resource game. Farmers are tempted to extract heavily, but this depletes the aquifer, harming both.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules on extraction rates, control rules on aquifer depletion

3. **Authorization Game**
   - **Title**: Electricity Connection Authorization
   - **Location**: Substation level
   - **Players**: Farmer, Substation staff
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumer, Service provider
   - **Actions**: Seek formal authorization, Seek informal connection
   - **Control Rules**: Formal authorization requires payment, informal connection risks penalty
   - **Information**: Past connection requests, staff workload
   - **Outcomes**: Authorized connection, informal connection, penalty
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Seek formal | Seek informal |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Seek formal** | 3, 2 | 0, 3 |
     | **Seek informal** | 2, 0 | 1, 1 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Authorization game. The farmer prefers formal authorization for reliability, but may seek informal connections to save costs.
   - **Temporal Structure**: One-shot
   - **Relevant Rules**: Boundary rules defining eligibility, choice rules on connection types

4. **Collusion Exchange Game**
   - **Title**: Informal Exchange
   - **Location**: Substation level
   - **Players**: Farmer, Substation staff
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumer, Service provider
   - **Actions**: Offer informal cooperation, Do not offer
   - **Control Rules**: Mutual benefit from informal exchange, risk of detection
   - **Information**: Past cooperation, trust level
   - **Outcomes**: Mutual benefit, detection and penalty
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Offer cooperation | Do not offer |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Offer cooperation** | 3, 3 | 0, 2 |
     | **Do not offer** | 2, 0 | 1, 1 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Game of Trust. Both parties benefit from cooperation, but risk loss if the other does not reciprocate.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules on cooperation, control rules on detection risk

5. **Capacity Provision Game**
   - **Title**: Transformer Capacity Contribution
   - **Location**: Transformer group level
   - **Players**: Two farmers
   - **Roles**: Electricity consumers
   - **Actions**: Contribute to capacity, Do not contribute
   - **Control Rules**: Contribution improves transformer reliability
   - **Information**: Current transformer capacity, past contributions
   - **Outcomes**: Improved reliability, increased cost
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Contribute | Do not contribute |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Contribute** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
     | **Do not contribute** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Public Goods game. Farmers prefer to contribute if others also contribute, but may free-ride if others bear the cost.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Repeated annually
   - **Relevant Rules**: Boundary rules defining who can contribute, choice rules on contribution decisions

6. **Social Learning Game**
   - **Title**: Technology Adoption
   - **Location**: Village level
   - **Players**: Farmer, Neighboring farmer
   - **Roles**: Technology adopter, Technology observer
   - **Actions**: Adopt technology, Do not adopt
   - **Control Rules**: Observation of neighbor's outcomes influences adoption decision
   - **Information**: Neighbor's past adoption decisions and outcomes
   - **Outcomes**: Adoption of technology, improved outcomes
   - **Payoffs**: 
     |  | Adopt | Do not adopt |
     | --- | --- | --- |
     | **Adopt** | 3, 2 | 1, 1 |
     | **Do not adopt** | 2, 1 | 0, 0 |
   - **Strategic Tension**: Non-strategic sequential process. Farmers learn from neighbors' experiences and adjust their technology adoption decisions accordingly.
   - **Temporal Structure**: Continuous over time
   - **Relevant Rules**: Choice rules on adoption decisions, control rules on information flow

### Analysis and Comparison
Each action situation represents a distinct governance interaction with its unique strategic tension, reflecting various sustainability games such as coordination, public goods, common pool resource, and trust games. The payoffs are grounded in the context, reflecting the consequences of actions such as improved voltage quality, increased costs, aquifer depletion, and mutual benefits from cooperation. The games are designed to capture the essence of the electricity-irrigation governance model, including the challenges of coordination, free-riding, over-extraction, and the importance of trust and cooperation.

The strategic core of each situation varies, with some being coordination games (Capacitor Adoption, Capacity Provision), others being public goods games (Capacity Provision), common pool resource games (Groundwater Extraction), or games of trust (Collusion Exchange). Each situation is distinct, with different player roles, actions, and payoff structures, ensuring strategic diversity.

The comparison across action situations highlights the complexities of governance interactions in the electricity-irrigation context. While some situations involve similar player types (e.g., farmers in Capacitor Adoption and Groundwater Extraction), the strategic tensions and payoff structures are distinct, reflecting different challenges and incentives. The analysis underscores the importance of considering these diverse interactions when designing governance structures and policies for sustainable electricity-irrigation management.
