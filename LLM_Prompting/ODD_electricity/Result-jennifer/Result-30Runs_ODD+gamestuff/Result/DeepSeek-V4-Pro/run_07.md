- Actions: Farmer chooses Formal (apply for authorized connection, pay fees) or Informal (seek unauthorized access, rely on informal tolerance). Staff chooses Enforce (invest effort in providing formal service, enforce rules) or Tolerate (withhold formal service, tolerate informal connections).
- Control Rules: If Farmer Formal and Staff Enforce, the connection is authorized, reliability improves, farmer pays fees, staff bears effort but fulfills duty. If Farmer Informal and Staff Tolerate, farmer gets cheap access without fees, staff gains informal benefit (e.g., bribes) but system records degrade. If Farmer Formal and Staff Tolerate, farmer pays but receives no service, staff fails duty and may be blamed. If Farmer Informal and Staff Enforce, farmer is caught and penalized, staff avoids blame but incurs effort.
- Information: Both know the local oversight intensity, social ties, and past reliability. They have imperfect information about the other’s exact choice but can form expectations.
- Outcomes: Connection status, fee payment, penalty, reliability, staff reputation, informal benefit.
- Payoffs: Ordinal ranks: Farmer’s best (3) = Informal + Tolerate (cheap, no penalty); worst (0) = Formal + Tolerate (pay but no service) or Informal + Enforce (caught). Staff’s best (3) = Formal + Enforce (compliance, no blame); worst (0) = Formal + Tolerate (failure). (Informal, Tolerate) gives Farmer 3, Staff 2 (informal benefit but less preferred than formal compliance). (Formal, Enforce) gives Farmer 2, Staff 3.
- Strategic Tension: This is an asymmetric coordination game with conflicting preferences (Battle of the Sexes). Both want to coordinate on either formal compliance or informal collusion, but the farmer prefers the informal equilibrium while the staff prefers the formal one. Miscoordination yields the worst outcomes for both. Type: Battle of the Sexes.
- Temporal Structure: Repeated annually; each year farmer and staff make their choices, and outcomes are realized.
- Relevant Rules: Boundary: farmer and assigned staff. Choice rules as above. Control rules: authorization and enforcement outcomes.

Normal form:
Players: Farmer (row), Staff (col)
Actions: Farmer: Formal (F), Informal (I); Staff: Enforce (E), Tolerate (T)
Payoff matrix:
        E     T
F   (2,3) (0,0)
I   (0,0) (3,2)

Explanation: (F,E): farmer gets reliable connection but pays fee (2), staff fulfills duty (3). (I,T): farmer cheap access (3), staff gets informal benefit (2). Off-diagonals: miscoordination leads to worst for both (0,0).

### 4. Collusion Exchange Game (Trust Game)
- Title: Collusion Tie Formation
- Location: Informal interaction between a farmer and a sub-station staff member within the transformer service area
- Players: One farmer, one sub-station staff member
- Roles: Farmer – potential bribe-giver; Staff – potential favor-granter
- Actions: The game is sequential: first, Farmer decides to Trust (offer an informal payment/gift to establish a collusive tie) or Not Trust (abstain). If Farmer chooses Trust, then Staff decides to Reciprocate (accept the offer, provide favors such as tolerance of unauthorized use or priority service) or Defect (take the payment but then enforce rules or report the farmer). In normal form representation, Staff’s strategy is a complete plan: Reciprocate if trusted, or Defect if trusted.
- Control Rules: A collusive tie forms only if Farmer trusts and Staff reciprocates. If Farmer trusts and Staff defects, Farmer loses the payment and faces penalties, Staff gains the payment and possibly a reward. If Farmer does not trust, the status quo prevails regardless of Staff’s planned response.
- Information: Farmer knows the staff’s general corruption level and past behavior; Staff knows the farmer’s financial strain. Both perceive the local detection risk. However, the farmer cannot be certain of staff’s response.
- Outcomes: Collusive tie status, informal payment, detection risk, access to unauthorized electricity, staff illicit income.
- Payoffs: Ordinal ranks: (Trust, Reciprocate): both get 2 (mutual benefit from collusion). (Trust, Defect): Farmer gets 0 (loses payment, penalized), Staff gets 3 (gain from payment and possible reward). (Not Trust, …): both get 1 (status quo, no collusion).
- Strategic Tension: This is a trust dilemma. The farmer must decide whether to trust the staff, knowing that the staff has a dominant incentive to defect once trusted. The unique subgame-perfect equilibrium is no trust, resulting in the Pareto-inferior status quo. Type: Trust Game (social dilemma).
- Temporal Structure: Repeated annually; each year a new trust decision can be made.
- Relevant Rules: Choice rules: Farmer – Trust or Not; Staff – Reciprocate or Defect. Control rules: collusion tie only
