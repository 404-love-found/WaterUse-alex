### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Farmer-Farmer Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination problem where mutual investment in capacitors yields shared improvement.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the coordination problem between two neighboring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing equipment (capacitors). Mutual investment yields a higher payoff for both, but unilateral investment yields no added private benefit, making cooperation risky.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Farmer Social Learning
#### Tension: Sequential decision-making where a farmer observes a peer's outcome and imitates only if that outcome ranks higher.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
- Farmer 1 adopts capacitor and observes outcome.
- Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome. If outcome is better, Farmer 2 adopts; otherwise, does not adopt.
#### Justification: This sequential action situation represents how farmers learn from each other's experiences with capacitor adoption, adopting the technology only if they observe a better outcome from their peers.

### Action Situation 3: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Farmer-Farmer Transformer Capacity
#### Tension: Asymmetric incentives where one farmer's authorization or investment benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 2, 4 | 1, 3 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 3, 2 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the dilemma where one farmer's decision to invest in transformer capacity benefits both farmers, but the cost is borne only by the investing farmer, creating an uneven payoff structure.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game
#### Title: Farmer-Staff Formal Cooperation
#### Tension: Asymmetric incentives between formal cooperation and opportunism.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Staff Invests | Staff Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Makes Formal Request** | 3, 2 | 1, 3 |

### Action Situation 6: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Title: Farmer-Farmer Groundwater Extraction
#### Tension: Mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation represents the classic prisoner's dilemma faced by two farmers extracting groundwater from the same aquifer, where mutual cooperation (restraint) leads to a sustainable yield, but the temptation for unilateral over-extraction can lead to short-term gains at the expense of long-term sustainability.
