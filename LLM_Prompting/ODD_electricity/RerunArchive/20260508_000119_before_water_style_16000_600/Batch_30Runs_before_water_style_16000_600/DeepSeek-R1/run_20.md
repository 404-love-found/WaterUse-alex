### Distinct Action Situations

**1. Capacitor-Adoption Assurance Game**  
Tension: Mutual investment in capacitors yields shared voltage stability benefits, but unilateral investment provides no private benefit and incurs cost, creating a coordination risk where mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant yet vulnerable to miscoordination.  
Matrix:  
| Farmer1 \ Farmer2 | Invest | Not Invest |
|-------------------|--------|------------|
| **Invest**        | (3, 3) | (1, 2)     |
| **Not Invest**    | (2, 1) | (2, 2)     |  
Justification: Based on III.iv.a: "Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit... mutual cooperation Pareto-dominant but risky."  

**2. Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma**  
Tension: One farmer’s contribution to transformer capacity benefits all connected farmers, but non-contributors free-ride, creating asymmetric costs where contributors bear private expenses while non-contributors gain higher net benefits.  
Matrix:  
| Farmer1 \ Farmer2 | Contribute | Not Contribute |
|-------------------|------------|----------------|
| **Contribute**    | (3, 3)     | (1, 4)         |
| **Not Contribute**| (4, 1)     | (2, 2)         |  
Justification: Based on III.iv.a: "One farmer’s authorization or investment benefits both... if only one invests, the contributor bears cost while the non-investor benefits more."  

**3. Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game**  
Tension: Reciprocal informal exchanges between farmers and staff yield mutual gains only if both engage; mismatched actions (e.g., one offers exchange while the other abstains) impose losses on the cooperator, reverting the abstainer to baseline.  
Matrix:  
| Farmer \ Staff | Engage | Abstain |
|----------------|---------|---------|
| **Engage**     | (3, 3)  | (1, 2)  |
| **Abstain**    | (2, 1)  | (2, 2)  |  
Justification: Based on III.iv.a: "Reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage... if either abstains while the other offers exchange, the offerer bears a loss."  

**4. Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game**  
Tension: Formal cooperation (farmer’s request + staff’s investment) is collectively optimal but staff gain modestly due to effort costs; mismatched actions (e.g., formal request met with withheld investment) create asymmetric losses favoring opportunistic behavior.  
Matrix:  
| Farmer \ Staff | Invest | Withhold |
|----------------|---------|----------|
| **Formal**     | (3, 2)  | (1, 4)   |
| **Informal**   | (4, 1)  | (2, 2)   |  
Justification: Based on III.iv.a: "Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal... staff gain modestly... if farmer makes formal request and staff withhold, farmer incurs a loss."  

**5. Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma**  
Tension: Mutual restraint sustains groundwater yields, but unilateral over-extraction provides short-term gains at the expense of collective depletion, creating incentives for defection that undermine long-term sustainability.  
Matrix:  
| Farmer1 \ Farmer2 | Restrain | Over-Extract |
|-------------------|----------|--------------|
| **Restrain**      | (3, 3)   | (1, 4)       |
| **Over-Extract**  | (4, 1)   | (2, 2)       |  
Justification: Based on III.iv.a: "Mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain... mutual high extraction accelerates depletion."
