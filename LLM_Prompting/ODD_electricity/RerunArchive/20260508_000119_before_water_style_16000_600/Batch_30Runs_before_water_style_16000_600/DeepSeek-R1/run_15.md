Based on the ODD+D description, here are the distinct action situations with their strategic tensions, payoff matrices, and justifications:

1. **Title**: Capacitor Adoption Assurance  
   **Tension**: Mutual investment required for voltage stability benefits; unilateral action yields no private gain.  
   **Matrix**:  
   ```
           Farmer B
           | Invest | Not Invest |
   --------|--------|------------|
   Invest  | 4,4    | 2,3        |
   Farmer A|--------|------------|
   Not Inv | 3,2    | 1,1        |
   ```  
   **Justification**: "Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit" (III.iv.a). Pareto-dominant mutual cooperation requires coordination.

2. **Title**: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma  
   **Tension**: Asymmetric free-riding where one farmer's contribution benefits all, but costs are private.  
   **Matrix**:  
   ```
           Farmer B
           | Contribute | Free-ride |
   --------|------------|-----------|
   Contrb  | 3,3       | 2,4       |
   Farmer A|------------|-----------|
   Free-rd | 4,2        | 1,1       |
   ```  
   **Justification**: "One farmer’s authorization benefits both... but costs fall solely on the authorizer" (III.iv.a). Non-contributors gain more when others invest ("free-rider incentive").

3. **Title**: Informal Exchange Coordination  
   **Tension**: Reciprocal benefit only if both engage; mismatched actions create losses for the cooperator.  
   **Matrix**:  
   ```
           Staff
           | Tolerate | Enforce |
   --------|----------|---------|
   Cooper  | 4,4      | 1,3     |
   Farmer  |----------|---------|
   Abstain | 3,1      | 2,2     |
   ```  
   **Justification**: "Reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage in informal exchange; if either abstains, the offerer bears a loss" (III.iv.a).

4. **Title**: Authorization-Investment Asymmetric Coordination  
   **Tension**: Conflict between formal compliance (collectively optimal) and opportunistic behaviors favoring short-term gains.  
   **Matrix**:  
   ```
           Staff
           | Invest | Withhold |
   --------|--------|----------|
   Formal  | 3,2    | 1,3      |

   Informal| 4,1    | 2,4      |
   ```  
   **Justification**: "Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but... staff gain modestly under formal cooperation due to investment burden" (III.iv.a). Staff prefer withholding effort.

5. **Title**: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma  
   **Tension**: Short-term individual gain vs. long-term collective sustainability.  
   **Matrix**:
