# Run 1 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Tension: 
Coordination problem between two neighboring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing equipment.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Farmer 2 Adopt | Farmer 2 Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopt** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Mutual adoption yields shared improvement (3, 3), while unilateral adoption yields no added private benefit (1, 2), creating a coordination problem.

### Action Situation 2: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization
#### Tension: 
Free-rider incentive between two farmers where one farmer’s authorization benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Farmer 2 Contribute | Farmer 2 Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contribute** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Contribute** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Contributor bears cost while non-contributor benefits more if only one invests (1, 4), creating an asymmetric free-rider incentive.

### Action Situation 3: Mutual-Exchange Coordination between Farmer and Staff
#### Tension: 
Reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage in informal exchange, with mismatched expectations leading to losses.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Staff Reciprocate | Staff Not Reciprocate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Reciprocate** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer Not Reciprocate** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Mutual reciprocity yields mutual gain (3, 3), but mismatched expectations create losses for the cooperating party (1, 2).

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Tension: 
Individual high extraction dominates in the short run, but mutual high extraction accelerates depletion.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Farmer 2 Extract High | Farmer 2 Extract Low |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Extract High** | 4, 4 | 5, 1 |
| **Farmer 1 Extract Low** | 1, 5 | 3, 3 |
#### Justification: 
Unilateral high extraction offers short-term gain (5, 1), but mutual high extraction accelerates depletion, making mutual restraint collectively optimal (3, 3).

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement between Farmer and Staff
#### Tension: 
Asymmetric incentives between formal cooperation and informal exchange, with staff bearing effort costs and farmers bearing formal fees.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Staff Enforce/Formal | Staff Not Enforce/Informal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Formal/Comply** | 3, 2 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer Informal/Not Comply** | 4, 1 | 2, 3 |
#### Justification: 
Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal (3, 2), but staff may prefer informal tolerance to avoid effort costs, and farmers may prefer informal access to reduce costs (2, 3).
