### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual cooperation being Pareto-dominant but risky.
#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This matrix reflects the strategic tension where both farmers benefit from adopting capacitors (3, 3), but if only one adopts, the benefit is less (1, 2 or 2, 1), and not adopting at all results in a mediocre outcome (2, 2).

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Title: Sustainable Groundwater Use
#### Tension: Mutual restraint in groundwater extraction sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain, accelerating depletion.
#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This matrix represents the classic prisoner's dilemma, where mutual cooperation (restraint) leads to a good outcome (3, 3), but the temptation to over-extract for short-term gain (4, 1 or 1, 4) can dominate, leading to a worse outcome for both if both over-extract (2, 2).
