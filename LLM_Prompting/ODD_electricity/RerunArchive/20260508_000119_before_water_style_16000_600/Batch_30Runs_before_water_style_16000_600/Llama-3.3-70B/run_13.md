# Run 13 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Tension: 
Coordination problem among farmers to adopt capacitors for voltage stabilization.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Farmer 2 Adopt | Farmer 2 Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopt** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Both farmers benefit from mutual adoption (3, 3), but if only one adopts, the benefit is less (1, 2 or 2, 1), and if neither adopts, they both get a moderate payoff (2, 2).

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Tension: 
Asymmetric incentives for farmers to contribute to transformer capacity upgrades.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Farmer 2 Contribute | Farmer 2 Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contribute** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Contribute** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Mutual contribution yields a high payoff for both (3, 3), but if one contributes and! the other does not, the contributor gets a low payoff (1, 4 or 4, 1), and if neither contributes, they both get a moderate payoff (2, 2).

### Action Situation 3: Mutual Exchange Coordination between Farmer and Staff
#### Tension: 
Reciprocal benefit from informal exchange versus the risk of detection and penalty.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Staff Reciprocate | Staff Do Not Reciprocate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Reciprocate** | 4, 4 | 1, 3 |
| **Farmer Do Not Reciprocate** | 3, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Both benefit from mutual reciprocity (4, 4), but if one reciprocates and the other does not, the reciprocator gets a low payoff (1, 3 or 3, 1), and if neither reciprocates, they both get a moderate payoff (2, 2).

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Tension: 
Individual incentive to over-extract groundwater versus the collective benefit of restraint.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Farmer 2 Restrain | Farmer 2 Over-extract |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restrain** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-extract** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Mutual restraint yields a high payoff for both (3, 3), but if one over-extracts and the other restrains, the over-extractor gets a high payoff (4, 1), and if both over-extract, they both get a moderate payoff (2, 2).

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement Asymmetric Coordination
#### Tension: 
Farmer’s decision to seek formal authorization versus staff’s decision to enforce or tolerate informal access.
#### Matrix: 
|  | Staff Enforce | Staff Tolerate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Seek Formal** | 3, 2 | 4, 3 |
| **Farmer Seek Informal** | 1, 4 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: 
Mutual formal compliance yields a moderate to high payoff (3, 2), but if the farmer seeks formal access and staff tolerate informal, the farmer gets a high payoff (4, 3), and if the farmer seeks informal access, staff enforcement yields a low payoff for the farmer (1, 4), while staff tolerance yields a moderate payoff for both (2, 2).
