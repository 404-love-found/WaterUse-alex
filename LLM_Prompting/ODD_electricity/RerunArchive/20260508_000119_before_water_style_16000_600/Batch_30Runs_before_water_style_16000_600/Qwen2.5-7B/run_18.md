### Title: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)

### Tension: Capacitor Coordination Dilemma

#### 2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|                       | Farmer B: Invest | Farmer B: No Invest |
|-----------------------|------------------|--------------------|
| **Farmer A: Invest**  | (3, 3)           | (2, 4)             |
| **Farmer A: No Invest** | (4, 2)          | (1, 1)             |

#### Justification

**Capacitor Coordination Dilemma**:
- **Farmer A and Farmer B** must decide whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage stability and pump efficiency, but unilateral investment does not provide a private benefit. This creates a coordination problem where mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant but risky.

**Payoff Explanation**:
- **(3, 3)**: Both farmers invest, sharing the benefits of improved voltage stability and pump efficiency. This is the cooperative outcome, where both parties benefit.
- **(2, 4)**: Farmer A invests while Farmer B does not. Farmer A sees a benefit but Farmer B does not. This creates a free-rider incentive.
- **(4, 2)**: Farmer B invests while Farmer A does not. Similar to the previous scenario, Farmer B sees a benefit but Farmer A does not.
- **(1, 1)**: Neither farmer invests, resulting in no improvement in voltage stability or pump efficiency. This is the least beneficial outcome.

### Title: Asymmetric Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)

### Tension: Free-Rider Dilemma

#### 2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|                       | Farmer B: Invest | Farmer B: No Invest |
|-----------------------|------------------|--------------------|
| **Farmer A: Invest**  | (2, 2)           | (1, 3)             |
| **Farmer A: No Invest** | (3, 1)          | (0, 0)             |

#### Justification

**Asymmetric Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma**:
- **Farmer A and Farmer B** must decide whether to invest in transformer capacity. If only one farmer invests, the other benefits from improved voltage quality without bearing the cost. This creates a free-rider incentive, where the non-investing farmer benefits more than the investing farmer.

**Payoff Explanation**:
- **(2, 2)**: Both farmers invest, sharing the benefits of improved voltage quality. This is the cooperative outcome but requires mutual investment.
- **(1, 3)**: Farmer A invests while Farmer B does not. Farmer A bears the cost while Farmer B benefits more.
- **(3, 1)**: Farmer B invests while Farmer A does not. Similar to the previous scenario, Farmer B bears the cost while Farmer A benefits less.
- **(0, 0)**: Neither farmer invests, resulting in no improvement in voltage quality. This is the least beneficial outcome.

### Title: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)

### Tension: Reciprocal Benefit Dilemma

#### 2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|                       | Farmer B: Exchange | Farmer B: No Exchange |
|-----------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Farmer A: Exchange** | (2, 2)            | (1, 0)               |
| **Farmer A: No Exchange** | (0, 1)         | (1, 1)               |

#### Justification

**Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game**:
- **Farmer A and Farmer B** must decide whether to engage in informal exchanges with sub-station personnel. Mutual engagement in informal exchanges yields reciprocal benefits, but unilateral engagement offers no private benefit and can result in losses if the other party does not reciprocate.

**Payoff Explanation**:
- **(2, 2)**: Both farmers engage in informal exchanges. This yields reciprocal benefits for both, creating a stable cooperative outcome.
- **(1, 0)**: Farmer A engages in informal exchanges while Farmer B does not. Farmer A loses because the other party does not reciprocate.
- **(0, 1)**: Farmer B engages in informal exchanges while Farmer A does not. Similar to the previous scenario, Farmer B loses because the other party does not reciprocate.
- **(1, 1)**: Neither farmer engages in informal exchanges, resulting in no additional benefits. This is the least beneficial outcome.

### Title: Authorization and Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game (AS5)

### Tension: Legal vs. Opportunistic Behavior

#### 2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|                       | Staff: Invest | Staff: No Invest |
|-----------------------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Farmer: Formal Request**  | (2, -1)       | (1, 0)          |
| **Farmer: Informal Request** | (3, -2)      | (0, -1)         |

#### Justification

**Authorization and Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game**:
- **Farmer and Staff** must decide whether to make a formal or informal request for transformer capacity and whether to invest in capacity. Formal requests and investments yield collective benefits but require formal fees. Informal requests and investments offer private benefits but avoid formal fees.

**Payoff Explanation**:
- **(2, -1)**: Farmer makes a formal request and staff invest. Farmer gains more while staff bear the cost of investment without the formal fee.
- **(1, 0)**: Farmer makes a formal request and staff do not invest. Farmer incurs a loss while staff save effort or cost.
- **(3, -2)**: Farmer makes an informal request and staff invest. Farmer gains more while staff bear the cost.
- **(0, -1)**: Farmer makes an informal request and staff do not invest. Staff gain modestly due to investment burden, but Farmer reverts to baseline.

### Title: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma (AS6)

### Tension: Short-Term Gain vs. Long-Term Sustainability

#### 2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|                       | Farmer B: Restrain | Farmer B: Over-Extract |
|-----------------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| **Farmer A: Restrain**  | (2, 2)             | (1, 3)                |
| **Farmer A: Over-Extract** | (3, 1)          | (0, 0)                |

#### Justification

**Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma**:
- **Farmer A and Farmer B** must decide whether to restrain or over-extract groundwater. Mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gains but risks accelerating depletion.

**Payoff Explanation**:
- **(2, 2)**: Both farmers restrain groundwater extraction. This sustains yields and is the collectively optimal outcome.
- **(1, 3)**: Farmer A over-extracts while Farmer B restrains. Farmer A gains more while Farmer B loses.
- **(3, 1)**: Farmer B over-extracts while Farmer A restrains. Similar to the previous scenario, Farmer B gains more while Farmer A loses.
- **(0, 0)**: Both farmers over-extract groundwater. This accelerates depletion and raises future pumping and electricity costs.

These action situations capture the strategic tensions in the irrigation electricity governance model, reflecting the complexities of farmer-farmer coordination, farmer-staff interaction, transformer capacity, capacitor adoption, authorization/enforcement, informal exchange, groundwater extraction, social learning, and bounded rationality.
