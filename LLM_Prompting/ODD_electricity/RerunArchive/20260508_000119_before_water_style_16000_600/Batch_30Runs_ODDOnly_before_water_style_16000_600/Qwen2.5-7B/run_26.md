### Title: Distinct Action Situations in the Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. **Farmer-Farmer Coordination on Capacitor Adoption (AS1)**
   - **Tension:** Mutual cooperation vs. unilateral defection in capacitor adoption.
   - **Matrix:**
     ```
     |             | Farmer 2 Adopt | Farmer 2 Defect |
     |-------------|----------------|-----------------|
     | **Adopt**   | (3, 3)         | (1, 4)          |
     | **Defect**  | (4, 1)         | (2, 2)          |
     ```
   - **Justification:** This action situation captures the mutual benefit of both farmers investing in voltage-stabilizing equipment, but the risk of defection where one farmer invests while the other does not, gaining a higher private benefit.

#### 2. **Farmer-Staff Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization (AS3)**
   - **Tension:** Farmer’s investment in transformer capacity vs. staff’s authorization.
   - **Matrix:**
     ```
     |             | Staff Authorize | Staff Do Not Authorize |
     |-------------|-----------------|------------------------|
     | **Invest**  | (2, 2)          | (3, 1)                 |
     | **Do Not Invest** | (1, 3)         | (0, 0)                 |
     ```
   - **Justification:** This situation represents the free-rider dilemma where the farmer bears the cost of transformer capacity improvement, while staff authorization provides a public good but creates an uneven cost-benefit scenario due to the absence of formal fees.

#### 3. **Farmer-Farmer Groundwater Extraction (AS6)**
   - **Tension:** Mutual restraint vs. unilateral over-extraction.
   - **Matrix:**
     ```
     |             | Farmer 2 Restrain | Farmer 2 Over-Extract |
     |-------------|-------------------|-----------------------|
     | **Restrain** | (3, 3)            | (1, 4)                |
     | **Over-Extract** | (4, 1)           | (2, 2)                |
     ```
   - **Justification:** This action situation models the common-pool resource extraction dilemma where mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and leads to resource depletion.

#### 4. **Farmer-Farmer Social Learning on Capacitor Adoption (AS2)**
   - **Tension:** Imitation of successful outcomes vs. maintaining the status quo.
   - **Matrix:**
     ```
     |             | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
     |-------------|-----------------|------------------------|
     | **Adopt**   | (3, 3)          | (1, 2)                 |
     | **Do Not Adopt** | (2, 1)         | (2, 2)                 |
     ```
   - **Justification:** This situation reflects the social learning process where farmers imitate successful outcomes to improve their yields but face the risk of adopting when others do not, thereby maintaining the status quo.

#### 5. **Farmer-Staff Mutual Exchange Coordination (AS4)**
   - **Tension:** Informal exchange vs. formal authorization.
   - **Matrix:**
     ```
     |             | Staff Exchange | Staff Do Not Exchange |
     |-------------|----------------|-----------------------|
     | **Exchange** | (3, 3)         | (2, 1)                |
     | **Do Not Exchange** | (1, 2)        | (1, 1)                |
     ```
   - **Justification:** This action situation captures the mutual benefit of informal exchanges between farmers and staff, but the risk of one party abstaining while the other offers, leading to a loss for the offerer and no gain for the abstainer.

#### 6. **Farmer-Farmer Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma (AS3, Detailed)**

#### 8. **Farmer-Staff Asymmetric Coordination on Capacitor Investment (AS5)**
   - **Tension:** Farmer’s formal request vs. staff’s withholding of capacity.

