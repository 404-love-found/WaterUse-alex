### Title: Distinct Action Situations in Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. Farmer-Farmer Coordination (Capacitor Adoption Assurance)
**Tension:** Mutual cooperation vs. unilateral defection
**Matrix:**
|       | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Defects |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | (5, 5) | (1, 4) |
| **Farmer 1 Defects** | (4, 1) | (2, 2) |

**Justification:** This action situation models the coordination problem where mutual investment in capacitors benefits both farmers by stabilizing voltage. Unilateral investment yields no added private benefit, making it a prisoner’s dilemma. The ordinal values are derived from the observed incentives and infrastructure conditions in Andhra Pradesh.

#### 2. Farmer-Staff Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma
**Tension:** Formal cooperation vs. informal exchange vs. mutual defection
**Matrix:**
|           | Staff Invest | Staff Withhold |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **Farmer Formal Request** | (3, 3) | (0, 0) |
| **Farmer Informal Request** | (4, 0) | (1, 2) |

**Justification:** This action situation captures the asymmetric incentives between legality and opportunism. Formal cooperation is collectively optimal but costly for the farmer. Informal exchange offers a gain for the farmer at no cost to the staff, while mutual defection yields the lowest payoff for both.
