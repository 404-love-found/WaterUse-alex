### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in the Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. Farmer-Farmer Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)
**Title**: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
**Tension**: Mutual Cooperation vs. Free-Rider Dilemma
**Matrix**:
```
            Farmer 2
            |   Invest   |  No Invest
--------------------------------
Farmer 1 | (1, 1)      | (0, 2)
--------------------------------
            |  (2, 0)     | (0, 0)
```
**Justification**: This game captures the mutual cooperation dilemma where both farmers benefit from investing in capacitors, but if one invests and the other does not, the investor incurs the full cost while the non-investor enjoys the benefits. The Pareto-dominant outcome is mutual investment, but the free-rider incentive makes unilateral investment risky.

#### 2. Farmer-Farmer Asymmetric Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)
**Title**: Asymmetric Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma
**Tension**: Mutual Benefit vs. Free-Rider Incentive
**Matrix**:
```
            Farmer 2
            |   Authorize   |  No Authorize
--------------------------------
Farmer 1 | (1, 1)        | (0, 2)
--------------------------------
            |  (2, 0)       | (0, 0)
```
**Justification**: This game represents the asymmetric authorization dilemma where authorizing an upgrade benefits both farmers, but the cost falls solely on the authorizer. If only one farmer invests, the non-investor enjoys the benefits without bearing the cost, leading to a free-rider incentive.

#### 3. Farmer-Staff Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)
**Title**: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game
**Tension**: Reciprocal Benefit vs. Unilateral Loss
**Matrix**:
```
            Staff
            |   Exchange   |  No Exchange
--------------------------------
Farmer    | (1, 1)       | (0, 2)
--------------------------------
            |  (2, 0)      | (0, 0)
```
**Justification**: This game models the need for reciprocal benefit in informal exchanges between farmers and staff. If both engage in exchange, they both gain. If one party engages while the other does not, the engaging party incurs a loss, while the non-engaging party reverts to the baseline.

#### 4. Farmer-Staff Asymmetric Coordination Game (AS5)
**Title**: Asymmetric Coordination Game
**Tension**: Formal Cooperation vs. Opportunistic Behavior
**Matrix**:
```
            Staff
            |   Formal Request   |  Informal Request
--------------------------------
Farmer    | (1, 1)           | (0, 2)
--------------------------------
            |  (2, 0)           | (0, 0)
```
**Justification**: This game reflects the asymmetric incentives between legality and opportunism. Formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but staff may withhold capacity if a formal request is made, incurring a loss for the farmer. Informal requests, if accepted, yield a higher benefit for the farmer but cost the staff without the formal fee.

#### 5. Farmer-Farmer Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma (AS6)
**Title**: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
**Tension**: Mutual Restraint vs. Short-Term Gain
**Matrix**:
```
            Farmer 2
            |   Restrain   |  Over-Extract
--------------------------------
Farmer 1 | (1, 1)       | (0, 2)
--------------------------------
            |  (2, 0)      | (0, 0)
```
**Justification**: This game represents the groundwater extraction dilemma where mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gains and accelerates depletion. The Pareto-dominant outcome is mutual restraint, but the short-term incentive for over-extraction makes cooperation risky.

These five action situations capture the key strategic tensions in the irrigation electricity governance model, reflecting the interplay between farmer-farmer coordination, farmer-staff interaction, transformer capacity, capacitor adoption, authorization/enforcement, informal exchange, groundwater extraction, social learning, and bounded rationality.
