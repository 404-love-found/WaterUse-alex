### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance (AS1)

**Title:** Capacitor Adoption Assurance

**Tension:** Mutual Cooperation vs. Free-Rider Dilemma

**Matrix:**
```
                  Farmer 2
                  Invest   Not Invest
Farmer 1 Invest    (3, 3)    (0, 2)
Not Invest         (2, 0)    (1, 1)
```

**Justification:** In this scenario, two neighboring farmers must decide whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing equipment. Mutual investment yields a shared improvement in power quality (3, 3), but unilateral investment provides no additional private benefit, leading to a free-rider incentive (2, 0) if only one invests. If neither invests, the situation remains at a low but non-zero baseline (1, 1).

### Action Situation 2: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)

**Title:** Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization

**Tension:** Formal Cooperation vs. Free-Rider Dilemma

**Matrix:**
```
                  Farmer 2
                  Authorize   Do Not Authorize
Farmer 1 Authorize    (2, 2)    (1, 3)
Do Not Authorize      (3, 1)    (1, 1)
```

**Justification:** This dilemma involves two farmers deciding whether to authorize and invest in transformer capacity. Mutual authorization benefits both by raising voltage quality (2, 2), but costs fall solely on the authorizer, leading to a free-rider incentive (3, 1) if only one invests. If neither invests, both remain at a low but non-zero baseline (1, 1).

### Action Situation 3: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)

**Title:** Mutual-Exchange Coordination

**Tension:** Reciprocal Benefit vs. Opportunism

**Matrix:**
```
                  Staff
                  Offer Exchange   Do Not Offer
Farmer           Offer Exchange    (2, 2)    (3, 1)
Do Not Offer     (1, 3)            (1, 1)
```

**Justification:** This game involves a farmer and staff engaging in informal exchanges. Mutual exchange yields reciprocal benefit (2, 2), but if only the farmer offers exchange (3, 1), the farmer gains more while the staff incurs a cost. If neither offers, the outcome is a baseline (1, 1).

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)

**Title:** Groundwater Extraction

**Tension:** Mutual Restraint vs. Short-Term Gain

**Matrix:**
```
                  Farmer 2
                  Restrain   Over-Extract
Farmer 1 Restrain    (2, 2)    (1, 3)
Over-Extract         (3, 1)    (1, 1)
```

**Justification:** Two farmers drawing from the same aquifer must decide whether to restrain groundwater extraction. Mutual restraint sustains yields (2, 2), but unilateral over-extraction offers a short-term gain (3, 1) and accelerates depletion. If neither restrains, both face a non-zero baseline (1, 1).

### Action Situation 5: Capacitor Adoption Social Learning (AS2)

**Title:** Capacitor Adoption Social Learning

**Tension:** Diffusion of Innovations

**Matrix:**
```
                  Farmer 2
                  Adopt   Not Adopt
Farmer 1 Adopt    (3, 3)    (2, 0)
Not Adopt         (0, 2)    (1, 1)
```

**Justification:** This scenario involves the social learning process where a farmer observes the outcome of a neighboring farmer's capacitor adoption. Diffusion occurs only after a successful coordinated trial (3, 3), while unilateral adoption (2, 0) does not provide additional private benefits, and no adoption (1, 1) remains at a baseline.

### Action Situation 6: Asymmetric Authorization-Enforcement Dilemma (AS5)

**Title:** Asymmetric Authorization-Enforcement

**Tension:** Formal Cooperation vs. Opportunism

**Matrix:**
```
                  Staff
                  Formal Cooperation   Opportunism
Farmer           Formal Cooperation    (2, 2)    (3, 1)
Opportunism       (1, 3)              (1, 1)
```

**Justification:** This dilemma involves a farmer making a formal or informal request for authorization, and staff deciding whether to enforce formal rules or accept informal exchanges. Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal (2, 2), but if the farmer makes a formal request and staff withhold, the farmer incurs a loss (3, 1). If the farmer makes an informal request and staff invest, the farmer gains more (1, 3), but staff bear the cost without the formal fee (1, 1).
