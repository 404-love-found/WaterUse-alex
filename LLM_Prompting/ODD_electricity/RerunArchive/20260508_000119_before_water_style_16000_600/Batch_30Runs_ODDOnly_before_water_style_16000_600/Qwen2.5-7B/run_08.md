### Title: Action Situations in the Electricity Governance Model

### 1. Farmer-Farmer Coordination: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)

#### Tension: Mutual Cooperation vs. Free-Rider Dilemma
- **Players**: Two neighboring farmers (Farmer A and Farmer B)
- **Actions**: Invest in Capacitors (C) or Not Invest (NC)
- **Matrix:**
  ```
  |           | Farmer B: C | Farmer B: NC |
  |-----------|------------|-------------|
  | Farmer A: C | (2, 2)     | (3, 1)      |
  | Farmer A: NC| (1, 3)     | (1, 1)      |
  ```
- **Justification**: Mutual investment in capacitors improves voltage stability, but unilateral investment incurs costs without added benefit. This creates a coordination problem where mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant but risky.

### 2. Sequential Social-Learning Process: Capacitor Adoption (AS2)

#### Tension: Diffusion of Innovation
- **Players**: A single farmer (Farmer X) observing a peer's outcome
- **Actions**: Adopt Capacitor (A) or Not Adopt (NA)
- **Matrix:**
  ```
  |           | Peer: A | Peer: NA |
  |-----------|---------|----------|
  | X: A      | (2, 2)  | (1, 1)   |
  | X: NA     | (1, 1)  | (1, 1)   |
  ```
- **Justification**: Diffusion occurs only if a coordinated trial is observed successfully. This reflects the bounded rationality and social learning of farmers.

### 3. Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)

#### Tension: Free-Rider vs. Mutual Benefit
- **Players**: Two farmers (Farmer A and Farmer B)
- **Actions**: Authorize (A) or Not Authorize (NA)
- **Matrix:**
  ```
  |           | Farmer B: A | Farmer B: NA |
  |-----------|------------|-------------|
  | Farmer A: A | (2, 2)     | (1, 3)      |
  | Farmer A: NA| (3, 1)     | (1, 1)      |
  ```
- **Justification**: Authorizing a transformer upgrade benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer, creating a free-rider incentive and uneven payoffs.

### 4. Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game: Farmer-Sub-Station Staff Interaction (AS4)

#### Tension: Informal vs. Formal Exchange
- **Players**: A farmer (Farmer X) and sub-station staff (Staff Y)
- **Actions**: Formal Exchange (F) or Informal Exchange (I)
- **Matrix:**
  ```
  |           | Staff Y: F | Staff Y: I |
  |-----------|-----------|-----------|
  | X: F      | (2, 2)    | (1, 3)    |
  | X: I      | (3, 1)    | (1, 1)    |
  ```
- **Justification**: Reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage in informal exchange; unilateral exchange offers short-term gain but accelerates depletion of public resources.

### 5. Asymmetric Coordination Game: Formal vs. Informal Request (AS5)

#### Tension: Legality vs. Opportunism
- **Players**: A farmer (Farmer X) and sub-station staff (Staff Y)
- **Actions**: Formal Request (F) or Informal Request (I)
- **Matrix:**
  ```
  |           | Staff Y: F | Staff Y: I |
  |-----------|-----------|-----------|
  | X: F      | (2, 2)    | (1, 3)    |
  | X: I      | (3, 1)    | (1, 1)    |
  ```
- **Justification**: Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but if the farmer makes a formal request and the staff withhold, the farmer incurs a loss. If the farmer makes an informal request and staff invest, the farmer gains more.

### 6. Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma (AS6)

#### Tension: Mutual Restraint vs. Unilateral Over-Extraction
- **Players**: Two farmers (Farmer A and Farmer B)
- **Actions**: Restrain (R) or Over-Extract (O)
- **Matrix:**
  ```
  |           | Farmer B: R | Farmer B: O |
  |-----------|------------|-------------|
  | Farmer A: R | (2, 2)     | (1, 3)      |
  | Farmer A: O| (3, 1)     | (1, 1)      |
  ```
- **Justification**: Mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion, reflecting the common-pool resource dilemma.
