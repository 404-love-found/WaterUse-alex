**Title: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game**  
Tension: Mutual adoption yields shared voltage stability benefits, but unilateral adoption incurs costs without gains due to coordination failure. Non-adoption maintains baseline but prevents efficiency improvements.  
Matrix:  
```
          Farmer B
          Adopt    Not Adopt
Farmer A  Adopt    (3, 3)    (1, 2)
          Not Adopt (2, 1)    (2, 2)
```  
*Justification*: AS1 in III.iv.a. Mutual capacitor investment improves voltage stability (Pareto-dominant outcome), but unilateral investment provides no private benefit. Non-adopters retain baseline payoff (2), while adopters incur costs alone (1).  

**Title: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma**  
Tension: Authorization by one farmer improves shared grid reliability, but non-authorizers free-ride on benefits. Costs are borne unevenly, creating asymmetric free-riding incentives.  
Matrix:  
```
          Farmer B
          Authorize    Not Authorize
Farmer A  Authorize    (2, 2)      (1, 3)
          Not Authorize (3, 1)      (1, 1)
```  
*Justification*: AS3 in III.iv.a. One farmer’s authorization/investment raises voltage quality for all, but non-authorizers gain more (3) without cost. Authorizers bear private costs for collective benefit (1). Neither authorizing yields low baseline (1).  

**Title: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game**  
Tension: Reciprocal informal exchanges (e.g., favors, payments) between farmers and staff yield mutual gain only if both participate; unilateral engagement causes losses for the initiator.  
Matrix:  
```
          Staff
          Exchange    Not Exchange
Farmer    Exchange    (3, 3)      (1, 2)
          Not Exchange (2, 1)      (2, 2)
```  
*Justification*: AS4 in III.iv.a. Mutual exchange generates reciprocal benefits (3,3). If one engages unilaterally, the initiator incurs a loss (1) while the abstainer retains baseline (2). Mutual abstention maintains baseline.  

**Title: Authorization-Enforcement Asymmetric Game**  
Tension: Farmer authorization requests and staff investment create asymmetric payoffs. Formal cooperation is collectively optimal but burdens staff, while informal requests risk underinvestment.  
Matrix:  
```
          Staff
          Invest     Withhold
Farmer    Formal     (3, 2)      (1, 4)
          Informal   (4, 1)      (2, 3)
```  
*Justification*: AS5 in III.iv.a. Formal + Invest (3,2) is collectively optimal but staff gain modestly due to cost burden. Informal + Invest (4,1) benefits farmers disproportionately while penalizing staff. Staff prefer withholding (4) when farmers choose formal.  

**Title: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma**  
Tension: Mutual restraint sustains the aquifer, but individual incentives drive over-extraction for short-term gain, accelerating depletion and long-term losses.  
Matrix:  
```
          Farmer B
          Restrain    Over-Extract
Farmer A  Restrain    (3, 3)      (1, 4)
          Over-Extract (4, 1)      (2, 2)
```  
*Justification*: AS6 in III.iv.a. Mutual restraint maintains aquifer health (3,3). Unilateral over-extraction yields high short-term gains (4) for the defector but imposes losses (1) on the cooperator. Mutual over-extraction depletes resources (2,2).
