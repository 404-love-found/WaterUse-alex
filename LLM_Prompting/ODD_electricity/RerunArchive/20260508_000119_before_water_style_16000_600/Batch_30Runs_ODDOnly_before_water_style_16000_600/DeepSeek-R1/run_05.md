### Distinct Action Situations Analysis

**1. Title: Capacitor Adoption Assurance**  
**Tension**: Mutual investment in capacitors is required for voltage stability benefits; unilateral investment yields no private gain, creating a coordination risk despite mutual cooperation being Pareto-optimal.  
**Matrix**:  
```
          Farmer 2          
          Invest  Not Invest  
Farmer1 Invest   (1,1)    (3,2)  
        Not Invest (2,3)    (2,2)  
```  
*Ordinal Payoffs*: 1 (best) to 3 (worst).  
**Justification**: Grounded in AS1 description (III.iv.a) where mutual capacitor adoption ensures shared voltage stability improvement, but unilateral adoption is futile due to no added private benefit. Reflects coordination interdependence among farmers (I.iii.a, II.i.c).

**2. Title: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma**  
**Tension**: One farmer’s investment in transformer capacity benefits both, but costs are borne solely by the investor, creating free-riding incentives and asymmetric payoffs.  
**Matrix**:  
```
          Farmer 2          
          Authorize  Not Authorize  
Farmer1 Authorize   (4,4)      (4,1)  
        Not Authorize (1,4)      (3,3)  
```  
*Ordinal Payoffs*: 1 (best) to 4 (worst).  
**Justification**: From AS3 (III.iv.a), where a farmer’s authorization improves shared grid capacity, but non-contributors gain more. Aligns with asymmetric cost-sharing tensions in infrastructure under-investment (II.i.c, II.ii.a).

**3. Title: Informal Exchange Coordination**  
**Tension**: Reciprocal gains from informal farmer-staff exchanges (e.g., bribes for unauthorized connections) occur only if both engage; unilateral offers incur losses for the initiator.  
**Matrix**:  
```
          Staff          
          Engage  Abstain  
Farmer Engage   (1,1)    (3,2)  
        Abstain   (2,3)    (2,2)  
```  
*Ordinal Payoffs*: 1 (best) to 3 (worst).  
**Justification**: Matches AS4 (III.iv.a), where mutual exchange yields benefits, but defection by either party causes losses for the cooperator. Reflects relational governance and collusion norms (II.ii.e, II.ii.c).

**4. Title: Formal Authorization-Investment Coordination**  
**Tension**: Farmer and staff face asymmetric incentives: formal cooperation is collectively optimal but burdens staff, while informal exchanges or defection create unbalanced gains.  
**Matrix**:  
```
          Staff          
          Invest  Withhold  
Farmer Formal   (2,2)    (3,1)  
        Informal  (1,3)    (4,4)  
```  
*Ordinal Payoffs*: 1 (best) to 4 (worst).  
**Justification**: Based on AS5 (III.iv.a), where formal requests with staff investment yield modest gains, but staff withholding or informal requests lead to asymmetric outcomes. Captures legality-opportunism tensions (II.ii.a, II.i.c).

**5. Title: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma**  
**Tension**: Mutual groundwater restraint sustains long-term yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers immediate gains, accelerating depletion if both defect.  
**Matrix**:  
```
          Farmer 2          
          Restrain  Over-extract  
Farmer1 Restrain   (2,2)      (3,1)
```  

**Justification**: Derived from AS6 (III.iv.a) as a canonical prisoner’s dilemma. Aligns with common-pool resource extraction conflicts and energy inefficiency (II.i.a, II.ii.a).
