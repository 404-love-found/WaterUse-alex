### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in Electricity Governance Model

1. **Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game**
	* **Tension**: Coordination problem between two neighbouring farmers to invest in voltage-stabilising equipment.
	* **Matrix**:
		|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Farmer 1 Invests | 3, 3 | 2, 4 |
		| Farmer 1 Does Not Invest | 4, 2 | 1, 1 |
	* **Justification**: Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

2. **Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma**
	* **Tension**: Free-rider incentive between two farmers, where one farmer's authorization benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer.
	* **Matrix**:
		|  | Farmer 2 Authorizes | Farmer 2 Does Not Authorize |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Farmer 1 Authorizes | 4, 4 | 2, 5 |
		| Farmer 1 Does Not Authorize | 5, 2 | 1, 1 |
	* **Justification**: Uneven payoffs arise if only one farmer invests, creating a free-rider incentive.

3. **Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game**
	* **Tension**: Reciprocal benefit between a farmer and sub-station staff through informal exchange.
	* **Matrix**:
		|  | Staff Engages in Exchange | Staff Does Not Engage |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Farmer Engages in Exchange | 4, 4 | 2, 3 |
		| Farmer Does Not Engage | 3, 2 | 1, 1 |
	* **Justification**: Mutual cooperation yields mutual gain, while unilateral engagement results in a loss.

4. **Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game**
	* **Tension**: Asymmetric incentives between a farmer and staff regarding formal cooperation and opportunism.
	* **Matrix**:
		|  | Staff Invests | Staff Withholds |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Farmer Makes Formal Request | 4, 3 | 2, 4 |
		| Farmer Makes Informal Request | 5, 2 | 1, 1 |
	* **Justification**: Staff gain modestly even under formal cooperation, while the farmer incurs a loss if staff withhold capacity.

5. **Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma**
	* **Tension**: Conflict between two farmers drawing from the same aquifer, where mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain.
	* **Matrix**:
		|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Farmer 1 Restraints | 3, 3 | 2, 4 |
		| Farmer 1 Over-Extracts | 4, 2 | 1, 1 |
	* **Justification**: Mutual cooperation sustains yields, while unilateral over-extraction accelerates depletion.
