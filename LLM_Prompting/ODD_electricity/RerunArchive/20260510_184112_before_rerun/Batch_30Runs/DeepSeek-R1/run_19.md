# Run 19 — deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-R1

### Distinct Action Situations

1. **Title**: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game  
   **Tension**: Mutual investment required for voltage stability; unilateral investment yields no private benefit due to coordination failure.  
   **Matrix**:  
   | Farmer1 \ Farmer2 | Invest | Not Invest |  
   |-------------------|--------|------------|  
   | **Invest**        | (3, 3) | (1, 2)    |  
   | **Not Invest**    | (2, 1) | (2, 2)    |  
   **Justification**: AS1 in III.iv.a. Mutual investment creates shared voltage improvement, but unilateral investment incurs cost without benefit, leading to a coordination dilemma where mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant but risky.  

2. **Title**: Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption  
   **Tension**: Adoption depends on observing successful coordinated trials; failed or isolated attempts deter imitation.  
   **Sequential Representation**:  
   - **Stage 1**: Farmer A adopts capacitor → Outcome: Success (if coordinated) or Failure (if isolated).  
   - **Stage 2**: Farmer B observes outcome:  
     - If **Success** (payoff=3) → Farmer B adopts → Gets 3.  
     - If **Failure** (payoff=1) → Farmer B does not adopt → Gets 2 (baseline).  
   **Justification**: AS2 in III.iv.a. Farmers imitate peers only if outcomes rank higher; diffusion requires visible coordinated success.  

3. **Title**: Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma  
   **Tension**: One farmer’s authorization improves shared reliability but costs are borne unevenly, creating free-riding incentives.  
   **Matrix**:  
   | Farmer1 \ Farmer2 | Authorize | Not Authorize |  
   |-------------------|-----------|---------------|  
   | **Authorize**     | (3, 3)   | (1, 4)       |  
   | **Not Authorize** | (4, 1)   | (2, 2)       |  
   **Justification**: AS3 in III.iv.a. Authorizer bears private cost while non-authorizer benefits more, resulting in asymmetric payoffs and underinvestment.  

4. **Title**: Farmer-Staff Mutual Exchange Coordination  
   **Tension**: Informal reciprocity benefits both only if matched; mismatched actions cause losses for the cooperator.  
   **Matrix**:  
   | Farmer \ Staff | Engage | Abstain |  
   |---------------|--------|---------|  
   | **Engage**    | (3, 3) | (1, 2) |  
   | **Abstain**   | (2, 1) | (2, 2) |  
   **Justification**: AS4 in III.iv.a. Mutual informal exchange yields gains, but unilateral engagement incurs costs with no benefit (e.g., farmer offers bribe; staff enforces rules).  

5. **Title**: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination  
   **Tension**: Formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but asymmetric incentives favor opportunism (farmer: informal access; staff: withhold investment).  
   **Matrix**:  
   | Farmer \ Staff      | Invest         | Withhold       |  
   |---------------------|----------------|----------------|  
   | **Formal Request**  | (4, 3)        | (1, 4)        |  
   | **Informal Request**| (3, 1)        | (2, 2)        |  
   **Justification**: AS5 in III.iv.a. Farmer gains more from informal requests if staff invest, but staff bear costs without fees; mutual formal cooperation is efficient but burdensome.  

6. **Title**: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma  
   **Tension**: Mutual restraint sustains aquifer yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gains.  
   **Matrix**:  
   | Farmer1 \ Farmer2 | Restrain | Extract |  
   |-------------------|----------|---------|  
   | **Restrain**      | (3, 3)  | (1, 4) |  
   | **Extract**       | (4, 1)  | (2, 2) |  
   **Justification**: AS6 in III.iv.a. Individual incentives to over-extract dominate, accelerating depletion despite collective detriment (mutual extraction → lower long-term payoffs).

- **Heterogeneity**: Roles fixed in matrices (e.g., AS3 farmers differ in contribution history; AS5 staff face investment burden).  
- **Justifications**: Directly quoted from ODD+D submodels (III.iv.a) and linked to core tensions (coordination, free-riding, reciprocity).
