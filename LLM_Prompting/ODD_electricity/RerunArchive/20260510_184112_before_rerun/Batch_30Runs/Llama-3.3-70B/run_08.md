### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared benefits.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Asymmetric Coordination Game
#### Tension: Farmer's formal request and staff's investment yield collective benefits, but staff bear effort costs and farmer bears formal fees.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer requests formal access (or not).
2. Staff invests in capacity/maintenance (or not) if farmer requests formal access.
3. Payoffs:
	* Farmer: -fee (if formal), +reliability (if staff), -penalty (if informal).
	* Staff: -effort (if invest), +reputation (if formal), -oversight (if informal).

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the sequential strategic tension between the farmer and staff, where the farmer's request and staff's response influence the outcomes.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Sequential Social Learning
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful neighbors, with diffusion dependent on visible adoption and performance gains.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor (or not).
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome and decides to adopt (or not).
3. Payoffs:
	* Farmer 1: -investment (if adopt), +reliability (if successful).
	* Farmer 2: -investment (if adopt), +reliability (if successful), +learning (if Farmer 1 successful).

#### Justification: This action situation captures the sequential strategic tension between farmers, where social learning influences the diffusion of capacitor adoption.
