### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared improvement.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to adopt capacitors. Mutual adoption improves voltage stability, but unilateral adoption provides little benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Asymmetric Contribution to Transformer Capacity
#### Tension: One farmer's authorization or investment in transformer capacity benefits both, but costs fall solely on the authorizer.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This situation highlights the free-rider problem, where one farmer contributes to transformer capacity, benefiting both, but the other farmer does not contribute, creating an uneven cost-benefit distribution.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Title: Over-Extraction of Groundwater
#### Tension: Mutual restraint in groundwater extraction sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the classic prisoner's dilemma, where individual self-interest leads to over-extraction, despite the collective benefit of mutual restraint.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Formal vs. Informal Access to Electricity
#### Tension: Farmers decide between formal and informal access to electricity, while staff decide on enforcement and maintenance effort.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Request or Informal Access
2. Staff chooses: Enforce or Tolerate (if Informal) or Invest/Maintain (if Formal)
#### Justification: This sequential action situation captures the strategic interaction between farmers and staff, where farmers weigh the costs and benefits of formal vs. informal access, and staff respond with varying levels of enforcement and maintenance effort.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Diffusion of Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful neighbors, but failed adoption or unclear performance gains can slow diffusion.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer observes neighbor's adoption outcome
2. Farmer decides: Adopt or Not Adopt based on observed outcome
#### Justification: This action situation represents the social learning process, where farmers update their decisions based on observed outcomes of their neighbors, influencing the diffusion of capacitor adoption.
