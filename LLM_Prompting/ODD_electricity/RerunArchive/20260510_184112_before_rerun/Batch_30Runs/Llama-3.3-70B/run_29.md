#### Title: Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination vs. Free-Riding
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Adopts Capacitor | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt Capacitor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts Capacitor** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two farmers deciding whether to adopt capacitors to improve electricity quality. Mutual adoption yields the highest payoff, but unilateral adoption provides little benefit, creating a coordination problem.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement Game
#### Title: Formal Access and Staff Enforcement
#### Tension: Compliance vs. Evasion
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer requests formal access.
2. Staff decides to enforce or tolerate.
   - If staff enforces, farmer pays fee and gets reliable access.
   - If staff tolerates, farmer gets access without fee but risks penalties.

#### Justification: This action situation represents the sequential interaction between a farmer seeking formal access and staff deciding on enforcement. The farmer's decision to seek formal access is followed by the staff's decision to enforce rules or tolerate informal access, affecting the outcomes for both parties.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Diffusion of Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Adoption vs. Non-Adoption Based on Social Influence
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer observes neighbor's adoption outcome.
2. Farmer decides to adopt or not based on observed outcome.
   - If adoption is successful, farmer is more likely to adopt.
   - If adoption fails or is unclear, farmer may not adopt.

#### Justification: This action situation captures the process of social learning in the context of capacitor adoption. A farmer's decision to adopt a capacitor is influenced by observing the outcomes of neighboring farmers who have already adopted, reflecting the role of social influence in technology diffusion.
