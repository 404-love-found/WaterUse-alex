### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination vs. Free-Riding
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two farmers deciding whether to adopt capacitors. Mutual adoption (3, 3) is the best outcome, but if only one adopts, the adopter does not gain significantly (1, 2 or 2, 1), making unilateral adoption unattractive.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement Game
#### Title: Formal Access
#### Tension: Compliance vs. Evasion
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer requests formal access.
2. Staff decides to enforce or tolerate.
   - If staff enforces, farmer faces penalties.
   - If staff tolerates, farmer gains but staff bears reputational risk.

#### Justification: This sequential action situation captures the strategic interaction between farmers seeking formal access and staff deciding whether to enforce rules or tolerate informal access, considering the risks and benefits of each choice.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Social Learning
#### Tension: Adoption vs. Non-Adoption
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer observes neighbor's adoption outcome.
2. Farmer decides to adopt or not based on observed outcome.
   - If adoption is successful, farmer is more likely to adopt.
   - If adoption fails or outcome is unclear, farmer may not adopt.

#### Justification: This action situation represents the process of social learning in capacitor adoption. Farmers observe their neighbors' experiences and make decisions based on the perceived success or failure of adoption, influencing the diffusion of capacitors.
