### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination dilemma between two neighboring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing equipment.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 (Mutual improvement) | 1, 2 (Unilateral investment, no added private benefit) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 (No improvement) | 0, 0 (Baseline, no investment) |

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two neighboring farmers who must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability. Mutual investment yields shared improvement, but unilateral investment provides no added private benefit, creating a coordination problem.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization
#### Title: Transformer Capacity
#### Tension: Asymmetric dilemma between two farmers where one farmer’s authorization or investment benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 3, 3 (Mutual benefit) | 1, 4 (Farmer 1 bears cost, Farmer 2 benefits) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 4, 1 (Farmer 1 benefits, Farmer 2 bears cost) | 0, 0 (Baseline, no contribution) |

#### Justification: This action situation represents the asymmetric authorization dilemma where one farmer’s contribution to transformer capacity benefits both farmers, but the cost is borne solely by the contributor, creating an uneven incentive structure.

### Action Situation 3: Mutual Exchange Coordination
#### Title: Informal Exchange
#### Tension: Coordination dilemma between a farmer and sub-station staff where reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage in informal exchange.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Staff Engages in Informal Exchange | Staff Does Not Engage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Engages in Informal Exchange** | 3, 3 (Mutual benefit) | 1, 2 (Farmer loses, Staff gains) |
| **Farmer Does Not Engage in Informal Exchange** | 2, 1 (Staff loses, Farmer gains) | 0, 0 (Baseline, no exchange) |

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the mutual exchange coordination game where both the farmer and the sub-station staff must engage in informal exchange to receive reciprocal benefits, highlighting the importance of matched expectations for cooperation.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Title: Groundwater Extraction
#### Tension: Dilemma between two farmers extracting groundwater where mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 3, 3 (Mutual sustainability) | 1, 4 (Farmer 1 loses, Farmer 2 gains short-term) |
| **Farmer 1 Over-extracts** | 4, 1 (Farmer 1 gains short-term, Farmer 2 loses) | 2, 2 (Both lose in the long term) |

#### Justification: This action situation represents the groundwater extraction prisoner’s dilemma, where individual short-term gain from over-extraction conflicts with the mutual long-term sustainability of the resource, leading to potential depletion.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Formal Access
#### Tension: Sequential game between a farmer seeking formal access and sub-station staff deciding whether to invest in capacity or enforce rules.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer decides to seek formal access or not.
2. If farmer seeks formal access, staff decides to invest in capacity or enforce rules.
   - If staff invests, reliability improves, but staff bear effort costs.
   - If staff enforces without investment, farmer faces penalties.

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the sequential decision-making process between a farmer seeking formal electricity access and the sub-station staff’s response, involving considerations of enforcement, investment, and the farmer’s access strategy.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Social Learning
#### Tension: Sequential process where a farmer observes a peer’s outcome and decides whether to imitate the capacitor adoption decision.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor or not.
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1’s outcome.
3. Farmer 2 decides to adopt capacitor or not based on observed outcome.

#### Justification: This action situation represents the social learning process in capacitor adoption, where a farmer’s decision to adopt is influenced by observing the outcomes of neighboring farmers, reflecting the path-dependent nature of technology diffusion.
