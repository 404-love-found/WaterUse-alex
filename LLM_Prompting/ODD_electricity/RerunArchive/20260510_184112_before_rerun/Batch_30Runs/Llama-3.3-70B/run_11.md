# Run 11 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared benefits.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage stability, benefiting both, but unilateral investment offers little private benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Asymmetric Contribution to Transformer Capacity
#### Tension: One farmer's investment in transformer capacity benefits both, but costs are borne solely by the investor.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This dilemma arises from the asymmetry in contribution and benefit, where one farmer's investment in transformer capacity improves voltage quality for both but at a cost borne only by the contributor.

### Action Situation 4: Authorization and Enforcement Dilemma
#### Title: Formal Compliance vs. Informal Access
#### Tension: Farmers must decide between seeking formal authorization for electricity access and relying on informal connections, while staff decide on enforcement.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Request or Informal Access
2. Staff chooses: Enforce or Tolerate
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Enforce, outcome is (3, 2)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Tolerate, outcome is (4, 1)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Enforce, outcome is (1, 3)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Tolerate, outcome is (2, 4)
#### Justification: This sequential action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers seeking electricity access and staff deciding on enforcement, with outcomes depending on the combination of formal compliance and staff action.

### Action Situation 5: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Title: Over-extraction of Groundwater
#### Tension: Farmers must decide how much groundwater to extract, with individual over-extraction yielding short-term gains but accelerating depletion.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restrains | Farmer 2 Over-extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restrains** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation represents the classic prisoner's dilemma, where individual rationality leads to over-extraction and depletion of the groundwater resource, despite mutual restraint being collectively optimal.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Diffusion of Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful neighbors, with diffusion dependent on visible outcomes and social networks.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor or not
2. Farmer 2 observes outcome and decides to adopt or not
- Adoption by Farmer 1 and successful outcome leads to adoption by Farmer 2
- Non-adoption or failure by Farmer 1 leads to non-adoption by Farmer 2
#### Justification: This action situation captures the process of social learning in the adoption of capacitors, where the decision to adopt is influenced by the observed outcomes of neighboring farmers.
