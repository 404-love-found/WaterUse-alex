### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared benefits.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage stability, benefiting both, but unilateral investment provides little private benefit.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Title: Over-Extraction of Groundwater
#### Tension: Farmers face a dilemma in extracting groundwater, where unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gains but mutual over-extraction accelerates depletion.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts | Farmer 2 Restrains |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 2, 2 | 4, 1 |
| **Farmer 1 Restrains** | 1, 4 | 3, 3 |
#### Justification: This classic prisoner's dilemma reflects the tension between short-term individual gains from over-extraction and the long-term collective loss from depleted groundwater resources.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement Game
#### Title: Formal Access and Staff Effort
#### Tension: Farmers decide on formal access, and staff decide on effort, with mutual formal cooperation improving reliability but staff bearing effort costs and farmers bearing formal fees.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Request or Informal Access
2. Staff chooses: Invest Effort or Withhold Effort (depending on Farmer's choice)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff Invests: (3, 2)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff Withholds: (1, 1)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff Tolerates: (2, 3)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff Enforces: (0, 0)
#### Justification: This sequential game represents the interaction between farmers seeking electricity access and staff deciding on enforcement effort, with outcomes depending on the combination of formal access requests and staff responses.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Diffusion of Capacitor Technology
#### Tension: Farmers observe neighbors' outcomes and decide to adopt capacitors, with successful coordinated adoption spreading through social networks.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Neighbor adopts capacitor and observes outcome
2. Farmer observes neighbor's outcome and decides to adopt or not
- If Neighbor's outcome is successful and Farmer adopts: (3, 3)
- If Neighbor's outcome is unsuccessful or Farmer does not adopt: (2, 2)
#### Justification: This situation reflects the process of social learning in the adoption of capacitor technology, where the decision to adopt is influenced by the observed outcomes of neighboring farmers.
