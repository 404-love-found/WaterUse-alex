### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared improvement.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to invest in capacitors. The payoff matrix shows that mutual investment (3, 3) is the best outcome, but if one farmer invests and the other does not, the investing farmer gets a lower payoff (1, 2), illustrating the risk of unilateral investment.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma
#### Tension: One farmer's authorization or investment in transformer capacity benefits both, but costs fall solely on the authorizer.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the asymmetric incentives in contributing to transformer capacity. The matrix shows that if one farmer contributes and the other does not, the contributor bears the cost but the non-contributor benefits more (1, 4), creating a free-rider problem.
