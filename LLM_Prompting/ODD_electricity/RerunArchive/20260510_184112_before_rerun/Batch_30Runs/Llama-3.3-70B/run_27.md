# Run 27 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination problem among farmers to invest in voltage-stabilizing equipment
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: Farmers need to coordinate their investments in capacitors to improve voltage stability and pump efficiency. If both farmers adopt, they both benefit (payoff 3), but if only one adopts, the benefit is reduced (payoff 1 or 2).

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Transformer Capacity
#### Tension: Asymmetric authorization dilemma between farmers
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: One farmer's contribution to transformer capacity benefits both, but the cost falls solely on the contributor. This creates a free-rider incentive, making it difficult for farmers to coordinate their contributions.

### Action Situation 3: Mutual Exchange Coordination Game
#### Title: Informal Exchange
#### Tension: Reciprocal benefit between farmer and sub-station staff
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Staff Cooperates | Staff Does Not Cooperate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Cooperates** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer Does Not Cooperate** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: Farmer and staff engage in informal exchange, where reciprocal benefit arises only when both cooperate. If one party cooperates and the other does not, the cooperating party bears a loss.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Title: Groundwater Extraction
#### Tension: Over-extraction dilemma between farmers
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Extracts | Farmer 2 Does Not Extract |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts** | 3, 3 | 4, 1 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Extract** | 1, 4 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: Farmers face a prisoner's dilemma, where individual high extraction dominates in the short run, but mutual high extraction accelerates depletion and raises future pumping and electricity costs.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement Game
#### Title: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Tension: Asymmetric incentives between farmer and staff
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer requests formal access or seeks informal access.
2. Staff decides to enforce rules or tolerate informal access.
3. Payoffs:
	* Farmer (formal access, staff enforces): 2, 3
	* Farmer (informal access, staff tolerates): 3, 2
	* Farmer (formal access, staff does not enforce): 1, 1
	* Farmer (informal access, staff enforces): 0, 4
#### Justification: Farmer and staff have asymmetric incentives, where staff may prefer to withhold effort to avoid maintenance burden, and farmer may prefer informal access to reduce immediate costs.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Social Learning
#### Tension: Diffusion of capacitor adoption through social networks
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer observes neighbor's capacitor adoption and outcome.
2. Farmer decides to adopt or not based on observed outcome.
3. Payoffs:
	* Farmer adopts (successful outcome): 3
	* Farmer adopts (unsuccessful outcome): 1
	* Farmer does not adopt: 2
#### Justification: Farmers learn from each other's experiences and adopt capacitors based on observed outcomes, creating a path-dependent diffusion process.
