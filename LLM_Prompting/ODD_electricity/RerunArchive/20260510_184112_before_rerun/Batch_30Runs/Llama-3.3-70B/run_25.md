### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared benefits.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage stability, benefiting both, but unilateral investment yields little private benefit.

| --- | --- | --- |

#### Justification: This dilemma arises because one farmer's contribution to transformer capacity upgrades benefits both, but the contributor bears the cost, while the non-contributor enjoys the benefit without paying.

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Title: Over-Extraction of Groundwater
#### Tension: Farmers must decide how much groundwater to extract, with individual high extraction yielding short-term gains, but mutual high extraction leading to depletion and increased costs.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Extracts High | Farmer 2 Extracts Low |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts High** | 3, 3 | 4, 1 |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts Low** | 1, 4 | 2, 2 |

#### Justification: This dilemma represents the strategic tension in groundwater extraction. While high extraction by one farmer yields a short-term advantage, mutual high extraction accelerates depletion, increasing future pumping costs for both.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Formal vs. Informal Access to Electricity
#### Tension: Farmers decide whether to seek formal or informal access to electricity, while staff decide whether to enforce rules or tolerate informal access, affecting reliability and costs.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Access or Informal Access
2. Staff chooses: Enforce or Tolerate
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Enforce, outcome is (3, 2)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Tolerate, outcome is (4, 1)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Enforce, outcome is (1, 3)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Tolerate, outcome is (2, 2)

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the tension between farmers seeking access to electricity and staff deciding on enforcement. The outcome depends on the combination of farmer and staff choices, influencing electricity reliability and costs.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Diffusion of Capacitor Adoption through Social Learning
#### Tension: Farmers observe neighbors' adoption outcomes and decide whether to adopt capacitors, with successful coordinated adoption spreading through the social network.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Neighbor adopts capacitor and experiences outcome (success or failure)
2. Farmer observes neighbor's outcome
3. Farmer decides: Adopt Capacitor or Not
- If Neighbor's outcome is Success and Farmer adopts, outcome is (3, -)
- If Neighbor's outcome is Failure or Farmer does not adopt, outcome is (1, -)

#### Justification: This action situation captures the process of social learning in the context of capacitor adoption. Farmers are more likely to adopt if they observe successful outcomes among their neighbors, leading to the diffusion of capacitor adoption through the social network.
