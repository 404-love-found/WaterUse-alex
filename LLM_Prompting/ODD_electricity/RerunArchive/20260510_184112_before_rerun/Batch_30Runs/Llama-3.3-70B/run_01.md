### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared improvement.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage stability, but unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

#### Title: Over-Extraction of Groundwater

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Formal vs. Informal Access to Electricity
#### Tension: Farmers decide between formal and informal access to electricity, while staff decide on enforcement and maintenance effort.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Request or Informal Access
2. Staff chooses: Enforce or Tolerate (if Informal) or Invest/Maintain (if Formal)
#### Justification: This sequential action situation reflects the strategic interaction between farmers and staff, with farmers weighing the costs and benefits of formal vs. informal access and staff responding with varying levels of enforcement and maintenance effort.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Diffusion of Capacitor Technology
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful neighbors, with diffusion dependent on visible adoption and social learning.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer observes neighbor's capacitor adoption and outcome
2. Farmer decides: Adopt Capacitor or Not
#### Justification: This situation highlights the role of social learning in the diffusion of capacitor technology, where farmers are more likely to adopt if they observe successful neighbors.
