# Run 9 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations

1. **Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game**
	* Title: Capacitor Adoption
	* Tension: Coordination vs. Free-Riding
	* Matrix/Sequential Representation:
		|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Farmer 1 Adopts | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
		| Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
	* Justification: Farmers must decide whether to adopt capacitors to improve voltage stability. Mutual adoption yields the highest payoff, but unilateral adoption provides little benefit.

2. **Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma**
	* Title: Transformer Capacity
	* Tension: Contribution vs. Free-Riding
	* Matrix/Sequential Representation:
		|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Farmer 1 Contributes | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
		| Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
	* Justification: Farmers must decide whether to contribute to transformer capacity upgrades. Contribution improves reliability, but non-contributors can still benefit.

3. **Mutual Exchange Coordination Game**
	* Title: Informal Exchange
	* Tension: Reciprocity vs. Exploitation
	* Matrix/Sequential Representation:
		|  | Staff Reciprocates | Staff Does Not Reciprocate |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Farmer Cooperates | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
		| Farmer Does Not Cooperate | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
	* Justification: Farmers and staff engage in informal exchanges, where mutual cooperation yields benefits, but exploitation can occur if one side does not reciprocate.

4. **Authorization and Enforcement Game**
	* Title: Authorization and Enforcement
	* Tension: Formality vs. Informality
	* Sequential Representation:
		1. Farmer requests formal authorization.
		2. Staff decides to enforce or tolerate.
		* If staff enforces, farmer pays fee and receives reliable service.
		* If staff tolerates, farmer receives service without fee, but staff bears risk.
	* Justification: Farmers and staff interact through formal authorization and enforcement. Farmers weigh the costs and benefits of formal access, while staff consider the risks and rewards of enforcement.

5. **Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma**
	* Title: Groundwater Extraction
	* Tension: Over-Extraction vs. Conservation
	* Matrix/Sequential Representation:
		|  | Farmer 2 Extracts | Farmer 2 Conserves |
		| --- | --- | --- |
		| Farmer 1 Extracts | 2, 2 | 4, 1 |
		| Farmer 1 Conserves | 1, 4 | 3, 3 |
	* Justification: Farmers must decide how much groundwater to extract. Over-extraction yields short-term benefits, but mutual conservation sustains the resource.

6. **Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption**
	* Title: Social Learning
	* Tension: Imitation vs. Innovation
	* Sequential Representation:
		1. Farmer observes neighbor's capacitor adoption.
		2. Farmer decides to imitate or innovate.
		* If farmer imitates, they adopt capacitor if neighbor's outcome is successful.
		* If farmer innovates, they adopt capacitor based on their own assessment.
	* Justification: Farmers learn from their neighbors' experiences with capacitor adoption. Successful adoption can spread through social networks, but failed adoption can discourage further uptake.
