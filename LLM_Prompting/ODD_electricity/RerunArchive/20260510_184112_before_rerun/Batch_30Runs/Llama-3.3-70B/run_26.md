### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared improvement.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage stability, but unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Asymmetric Contribution to Transformer Capacity
#### Tension: One farmer's authorization or investment benefits both, but costs fall solely on the authorizer, creating a free-rider incentive.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation captures the asymmetric incentives in contributing to transformer capacity, where one farmer bears the cost while the other benefits without contributing.

### Action Situation 3: Mutual Exchange Coordination between Farmer and Staff
#### Title: Cooperative Informal Exchange
#### Tension: Reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage in informal exchange; if either abstains, the other loses.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

| --- | --- | --- |

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Title: Over-Extraction of Groundwater
#### Tension: Mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain, accelerating depletion.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the classic prisoner's dilemma in groundwater extraction, where individual short-term gains lead to mutual long-term loss due to over-extraction.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement Asymmetric Coordination
#### Title: Formal vs. Informal Access
#### Tension: Formal authorization increases legitimacy but requires costs; staff may withhold effort, while farmers may prefer informal access.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses Formal Request or Informal Access.
2. If Formal Request, Staff chooses Invest in Capacity or Withhold.
   - If Invest, outcome is (3, 3).
   - If Withhold, outcome is (1, 4).
3. If Informal Access, Staff chooses Tolerate or Enforce.
   - If Tolerate, outcome is (4, 1).
   - If Enforce, outcome is (2, 2).
#### Justification: This sequential action situation captures the asymmetric coordination between farmers and staff regarding authorization and enforcement, reflecting the strategic tension in choosing between formal and informal access to electricity.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Diffusion of Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful neighbors, but failed adoption can discourage later uptake.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts Capacitor or Not.
2. Farmer 2 observes outcome and decides to Adopt or Not.
   - If Farmer 1's adoption is successful, Farmer 2 is more likely to adopt.
   - If Farmer 1's adoption fails, Farmer 2 is less likely to adopt.
#### Justification: This action situation represents the social learning process in capacitor adoption, where the success or failure of early adopters influences the decisions of later farmers, reflecting the strategic tension in learning and imitation.
