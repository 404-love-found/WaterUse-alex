### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared improvement.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage stability, but unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization-Enforcement Dilemma
#### Tension: Staff may withhold effort to avoid maintenance burden, while farmers may prefer informal access to reduce immediate costs.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer requests formal access or seeks informal access.
2. Staff decides to invest in capacity/maintenance or withhold effort/enforce rules.
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the sequential strategic tension between farmers and staff in authorization and enforcement, with each side's optimal action depending on the other's monitoring or compliance effort.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Diffusion of Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful peers, but failed adoption or unclear performance gains can slow diffusion.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer observes neighbor's adoption outcome.
2. Farmer decides to adopt or not based on observed outcome.
#### Justification: This action situation captures the sequential process of social learning in capacitor adoption, where observation and imitation influence diffusion among farmers.
