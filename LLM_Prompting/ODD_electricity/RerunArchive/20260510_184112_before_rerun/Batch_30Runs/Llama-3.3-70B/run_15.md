### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual investment yielding shared benefits.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding whether to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage stability, benefiting both, but unilateral investment provides little private benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma
#### Tension: One farmer's authorization or investment in transformer capacity benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This situation highlights the free-rider problem where one farmer's contribution to transformer capacity benefits both, but the contributor bears the cost, creating an asymmetric incentive structure.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Asymmetric Coordination Game
#### Tension: Farmer's formal request for access and staff's decision to invest in capacity or enforce rules create asymmetric incentives.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Request or Informal Access
2. Staff chooses: Invest/Enforce or Withhold/Tolerate
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Invest, outcome is (3, 2)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Withhold, outcome is (1, 3)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Tolerate, outcome is (4, 1)
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Enforce, outcome is (2, 4)
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the sequential decision-making between farmers and staff regarding authorization and enforcement, with asymmetric payoffs based on the level of cooperation and compliance.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Sequential Social Learning
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful neighbors, but diffusion is path-dependent and influenced by initial successes or failures.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Initial Farmer adopts or not
2. Neighbor observes outcome and decides to adopt or not
- If Initial Farmer adopts and succeeds, Neighbor adopts with probability p
- If Initial Farmer adopts and fails, Neighbor does not adopt with probability q
- If Initial Farmer does not adopt, Neighbor does not adopt
#### Justification: This situation represents the process of social learning in capacitor adoption, where the decision to adopt is influenced by the observed outcomes of neighbors, introducing a path-dependent element to the diffusion process.
