### Title: Action Situations in the Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. **Action Situation: Capacitor Adoption Assurance (AS1)**
   - **Tension**: Mutual Coordination vs. Free-Rider Incentive
   - **Matrix**:
     ```
         Farmer A    Farmer B
         -------------------- 
     A   2, 2       1, 3
     B   3, 1       1, 1
     ```
   - **Justification**: Two neighboring farmers decide whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing equipment. Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit, creating a free-rider incentive for non-investing farmers.

#### 2. **Action Situation: Sequential Social-Learning in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)**
   - **Tension**: Learning from Success vs. Path Dependence
   - **Game Tree**:
     ```
         Farmer A
         /       \
        /         \
     A  (2)        (1)
     |            |
     \            /
     /            \
     B  (1)        (1)
     ```
   - **Justification**: Each farmer observes a peer's outcome and imitates only if that outcome ranks higher. Diffusion of capacitor adoption occurs only after a successful coordinated trial has been observed, creating path-dependent dynamics.

#### 3. **Action Situation: Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)**
   - **Tension**: Formal Cooperation vs. Free-Rider Incentive
   - **Matrix**:
     ```
         Farmer A    Farmer B
         -------------------- 
     A   2, 2       1, 1
     B   1, 1       1, 2
     ```
   - **Justification**: One farmer's authorization or investment benefits both by raising voltage quality, but costs fall solely on the authorizer, generating a free-rider incentive for non-investing farmers. If only one invests, the contributor bears cost while the non-investor benefits more.

#### 4. **Action Situation: Mutual-Exchange Coordination with Staff (AS4)**
   - **Tension**: Reciprocal Benefit vs. Opportunism
   - **Matrix**:
     ```
         Farmer A    Staff
         -------------------
     A   2, 2        1, 1
     B   1, 1        2, 1
     ```
   - **Justification**: Mutual benefit arises only when both engage in informal exchange; if either abstains, the offerer bears a loss while the abstainer reverts to baseline. Only matched cooperation yields mutual gain.

#### 5. **Action Situation: Formal vs. Informal Authorization (AS5)**
   - **Tension**: Legality vs. Opportunism
   - **Matrix**:
     ```
         Farmer A    Staff
         -------------------
     A   2, 2        1, 2
     B   1, 1        2, 1
     ```
   - **Justification**: Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but if the farmer makes a formal request and the staff withhold, the farmer incurs a loss while the staff save effort or cost. If the farmer requests informally and staff invest, the farmer gains while the staff bear the cost without the formal fee.

#### 6. **Action Situation: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma (AS6)**
   - **Tension**: Collective Restraint vs. Short-Term Gain
   - **Matrix**:
     ```
         Farmer A    Farmer B
         -------------------
     A   2, 2        1, 3
     B   3, 1        1, 1
     ```
   - **Justification**: Mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion. Short-term gain can lead to long-term resource depletion.

#### 7. **Action Situation: Farmer-Staff Interaction (AS7)**
   - **Tension**: Formal Compliance vs. Informal Exchange
   - **Matrix**:
     ```
         Farmer A    Staff
         -------------------
     A   2, 2        1, 1
     B   1, 1        2, 1
     ```
   - **Justification**: Formal compliance involves authorization fees and staff effort, while informal exchange involves tolerance of unauthorized access or reciprocal favors. Mutual benefit arises only when expectations are matched.

#### 8. **Action Situation: Transformer Reliability (AS8)**
   - **Tension**: Aggregate Load vs. Transformer Capacity
   - **Matrix**:
     ```
         Farmer A    Farmer B
         -------------------
     A   2, 2        1, 1
     B   1, 1        2, 2
     ```
   - **Justification**: Transformer reliability depends on effective capacity and aggregate load. Exceeding capacity increases service interruptions and repair delays, while coordinated contributions improve reliability.

#### 9. **Action Situation: Social Learning and Bounded Rationality (AS9)**
   - **Tension**: Local Experience vs. Perfect Knowledge
   - **Matrix**:
     ```
         Farmer A    Social Learning
         ---------------------------
     A   2, 2         1, 1
     B   1, 1         2, 2
     ```
   - **Justification**: Farmers rely on local experience and social learning rather than perfect technical knowledge. Misattributions and imperfect information can slow diffusion of efficient technologies.

#### 10. **Action Situation: Informal Staff-Farmer Networks (AS10)**
    - **Tension**: Trust and Reciprocal Benefit vs. Oversight and Penalties
    - **Matrix**:
      ```
          Farmer A    Staff
          -------------------
      A   2, 2        1, 1
      B   1, 1        2, 2
      ```
    - **Justification**: Informal networks can persist when both sides expect reciprocal benefit and low detection risk. However, mismatches in expectations can lead to losses for the party that offers cooperation while the other side abstains or enforces.

These action situations reflect the strategic tensions in the irrigation electricity governance model, focusing on farmer-farmer coordination, farmer-staff interaction, transformer capacity, capacitor adoption, authorization/enforcement, informal exchange, groundwater extraction, social learning, and bounded rationality.
