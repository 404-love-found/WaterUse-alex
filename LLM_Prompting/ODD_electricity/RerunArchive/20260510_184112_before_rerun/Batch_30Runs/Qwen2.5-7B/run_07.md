### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)

**Title:** Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game

**Tension:** Mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant but risky, while unilateral investment offers no private benefit.

**Matrix Representation:**

|       | Farmer B Invests | Farmer B Does Not Invest |
|-------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer A Invests** | (3, 3) | (1, 0) |
| **Farmer A Does Not Invest** | (0, 1) | (2, 2) |

**Justification:** This game captures the dilemma where mutual investment in capacitors yields shared improvement, but unilateral investment offers no private benefit, creating a coordination problem.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)

**Title:** Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption

**Sequential Representation (Game Tree):**

1. **Farmer A Decides**:
    - Invest (I)
    - Not Invest (N)

2. **Farmer B Observes and Decides**:
    - Invest (I) if Farmer A Invested (I) and Farmer A’s Outcome Ranks Higher
    - Not Invest (N) if Farmer A Invested (I) and Farmer A’s Outcome Ranks Lower or Not Invested (N)

**Justification:** This represents the process where farmers learn from each other’s outcomes and imitate successful peers, highlighting the path-dependent nature of diffusion.
