### Title: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)

### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Coordination Dilemma with Mutual Benefit

### Matrix/Sequential Representation

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| Farmer 2 \ Farmer 1 | No Capacitor | Capacitor |
|---------------------|--------------|-----------|
| No Capacitor        | (0, 0)       | (1, -1)   |
| Capacitor           | (-1, 1)      | (2, 2)    |

**Justification:**
This action situation represents the dilemma faced by two neighboring farmers deciding whether to invest in capacitors that improve electricity quality. The mutual investment yields a collective benefit, but unilateral investment offers no private benefit, creating a coordination problem. The payoff matrix reflects that mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant but risky, as it involves trust and the potential for free-riding.

### Title: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)

### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Free-Rider Dilemma

### Matrix/Sequential Representation

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| Farmer 2 \ Farmer 1 | No Authorization | Authorization |
|---------------------|-----------------|---------------|
| No Authorization    | (0, 0)          | (1, -1)       |
| Authorization       | (-1, 1)         | (2, 2)        |

**Justification:**
This action situation represents the dilemma faced by two farmers deciding whether to invest in transformer capacity, which benefits both but incurs a cost only on the investor. The matrix shows that mutual investment yields the highest benefit, but unilateral investment offers no private benefit, creating a free-rider problem. The payoff matrix reflects the asymmetric costs and benefits associated with capacity authorization.

### Title: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)

### Tension: Farmer-Farmer Over-Extraction

### Matrix/Sequential Representation

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| Farmer 2 \ Farmer 1 | No Extraction | Extraction |
|---------------------|---------------|------------|
| No Extraction       | (0, 0)        | (1, -1)    |
| Extraction          | (-1, 1)       | (2, 2)     |

**Justification:**
This action situation represents the dilemma faced by two farmers drawing from the same aquifer, where mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion. The matrix shows that mutual restraint is collectively optimal but that unilateral over-extraction can dominate in the short run, creating a prisoner’s dilemma. The payoff matrix reflects the trade-off between short-term benefit and long-term sustainability.
