### Title: Capacitor-Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)

#### Tension: Coordination vs. Free-Rider Dilemma

#### Matrix Representation:
|               | Farmer 2 (No Capacitor) | Farmer 2 (Capacitor) |
|---------------|-------------------------|----------------------|
| **Farmer 1 (No Capacitor)** | (0, 0)                  | (1, -1)              |
| **Farmer 1 (Capacitor)**    | (-1, 1)                 | (2, 2)               |

#### Justification:
This action situation represents the tension between mutual investment in capacitor adoption and the free-rider dilemma. If both farmers invest, voltage stability improves, and both benefit. However, if one farmer invests and the other does not, the investing farmer incurs a cost without benefiting. Mutual investment is Pareto-optimal but risky due to the possibility of the other farmer not contributing.

### Title: Sequential Social-Learning Process (AS2)

#### Tension: Social Learning vs. Path Dependence

#### Sequential Representation (Game Tree):
```
                Farmer 1 (Adopt)
                  /         \
                 /           \
            (0, 0)      (2, 2)
            /   \       /   \
           /     \     /     \
      Farmer 2 (Adopt) (0, 0)  (2, 2)
```

#### Justification:
This action situation models the sequential process of capacitor adoption, where Farmer 1 observes the outcome of Farmer 2’s adoption and decides whether to adopt based on the observed outcome. If Farmer 2 adopts and has a positive outcome, Farmer 1 is more likely to adopt. The path dependence arises from the fact that early isolated adoption can discourage later uptake, while successful coordinated adoption can spread through the social network.

### Title: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)

#### Tension: Contribution Imbalance vs. Free-Rider Incentive

#### Matrix Representation:
|               | Farmer 2 (No Contribution) | Farmer 2 (Contribution) |
|---------------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 (No Contribution)** | (0, 0)                    | (1, -1)                |
| **Farmer 1 (Contribution)**    | (-1, 1)                   | (2, 2)                 |

#### Justification:
This action situation captures the dilemma faced by farmers who must decide whether to invest in capacitor adoption or transformer capacity. If both farmers contribute, voltage quality improves, and both benefit. However, if only one farmer contributes, the contributor bears the cost, while the non-contributor benefits more. This creates an incentive for non-contributors to wait for others to pay first, leading to a free-rider problem.

### Title: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)

#### Tension: Reciprocal Benefit vs. Opportunism

#### Sequential Representation (Game Tree):
```
                Farmer (Request)
                  /         \
                 /           \
            (0, 0)      (2, 2)
            /   \       /   \
           /     \     /     \
      Staff (Exchange) (0, 0)  (2, 2)
```

#### Justification:
This action situation represents the interaction between a farmer and sub-station personnel, where a farmer can request formal or informal access to electricity. If both parties engage in an exchange, they benefit reciprocally. However, if only one party engages, the offering party incurs a cost while the other reverts to baseline. This creates a dilemma between mutual cooperation and opportunistic behavior.

### Title: Authorization and Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game (AS5)

#### Tension: Legal Cooperation vs. Opportunism

#### Matrix Representation:
|               | Staff (Withhold) | Staff (Invest) |
|---------------|------------------|----------------|
| **Farmer (Formal Request)** | (0, 0)           | (1, -1)        |
| **Farmer (Informal Request)** | (-1, 1)         | (2, 2)         |

#### Justification:
This action situation models the interaction between a farmer and sub-station personnel regarding formal and informal requests for transformer capacity. If both parties engage in formal cooperation, the farmer benefits while the staff incurs a cost. If the farmer makes a formal request and the staff withholds, the farmer incurs a loss. If the farmer makes an informal request and the staff invests, the farmer benefits more while the staff bears the cost without the formal fee. This creates a tension between legality and opportunism.

### Title: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)

#### Tension: Short-Term Gain vs. Long-Term Sustainability

#### Matrix Representation:
|               | Farmer 2 (Restrain) | Farmer 2 (Extract) |
|---------------|---------------------|--------------------|
| **Farmer 1 (Restrain)** | (1, 1)              | (0, 2)             |
| **Farmer 1 (Extract)**  | (2, 0)              | (0, 0)             |

#### Justification:
This action situation captures the dilemma faced by farmers who must decide whether to extract groundwater for irrigation. If both farmers restrain, groundwater levels are sustainable, and both benefit. However, if one farmer extracts and the other restrains, the extracting farmer gains short-term benefits, while the other suffers. If both extract, groundwater levels decline, and both face higher pumping costs in the future. This creates a tension between short-term gain and long-term sustainability.
