### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance (AS1)

**Title:** Capacitor Adoption Assurance

**Tension:** Mutual Cooperation vs. Free-Rider Problem

**Matrix Representation:**

|                | Farmer 1: Invest | Farmer 1: Don't Invest |
|----------------|------------------|-----------------------|
| **Farmer 2: Invest** | (2, 2) | (0, 1) |
| **Farmer 2: Don't Invest** | (1, 0) | (0, 0) |

**Justification:** This action situation represents the mutual investment in voltage-stabilizing equipment (capacitors) by two neighboring farmers. Mutual investment leads to shared benefits, but unilateral investment yields no added private benefit, creating a free-rider problem.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)

**Title:** Sequential Social-Learning in Capacitor Adoption

**Sequential Representation:**

```
1. Farmer 1 observes Farmer 2's outcome
2. Farmer 1 decides whether to adopt capacitors based on Farmer 2's outcome
```

**Justification:** This action situation involves a sequential process where each farmer observes a peer's outcome and imitates only if the outcome ranks higher. This creates a condition where diffusion occurs only after a successful coordinated trial has been observed.

### Action Situation 3: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)

**Title:** Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma

**Matrix Representation:**

|                | Farmer 1: Invest | Farmer 1: Don't Invest |
|----------------|------------------|-----------------------|
| **Farmer 2: Invest** | (2, 2) | (1, 0) |
| **Farmer 2: Don't Invest** | (0, 1) | (0, 0) |

**Justification:** This action situation involves two farmers deciding whether to authorize transformer capacity, with mutual investment raising voltage quality but costs falling solely on the authorizer. If only one invests, the contributor bears the cost while the non-investor benefits more. If neither invests, both remain at a low but non-zero baseline.

### Action Situation 4: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)

**Title:** Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game

**Matrix Representation:**

|                | Farmer: Exchange | Farmer: No Exchange |
|----------------|------------------|---------------------|
| **Staff: Exchange** | (1, 1) | (0, 0) |
| **Staff: No Exchange** | (0, 0) | (0, 0) |

**Justification:** This action situation involves a mutual-exchange coordination game between a farmer and sub-station staff, where reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage in informal exchange. If either party abstains, no extra benefit occurs.

**Title:** Authorization and Investment Asymmetric Coordination

**Title:** Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma

**Matrix Representation:**

|                | Farmer 1: Extract | Farmer 1: Don't Extract |
|----------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Farmer 2: Extract** | (-1, -1) | (0, 1) |
