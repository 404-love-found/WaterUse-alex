### Title: Action Situations in Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. Capillary Coordination Game (AS1)
**Tension:** Mutual Cooperation vs. Free-Rider Dilemma

**Matrix Representation:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}
 & \text{Farmer B: Invest} & \text{Farmer B: Do Not Invest} \\
\hline
\text{Farmer A: Invest} & (3, 3) & (0, 6) \\
\text{Farmer A: Do Not Invest} & (6, 0) & (2, 2) \\
\end{array}
\]

**Justification:** This game represents the coordination between two neighboring farmers to invest in capacitors, which stabilizes electricity quality. Mutual investment is Pareto-optimal, but one farmer can benefit from the other's investment (free-rider problem).

#### 2. Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)
**Tension:** Sequential Decision-Making and Social Learning

**Sequential Representation:**
```
1. Farmer A decides (Invest/Do Not Invest)
2. Farmer B observes and decides (Invest/Do Not Invest)
```

**Justification:** This game captures the sequential nature of capacitor adoption, where farmers observe the outcome of their peers and decide to invest based on the observed success or failure.

#### 3. Asymmetric Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)
**Tension:** Mutual Benefit vs. Free-Rider Dilemma

**Matrix Representation:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}
 & \text{Farmer B: Invest} & \text{Farmer B: Do Not Invest} \\
\hline
\text{Farmer A: Invest} & (3, 3) & (0, 5) \\
\text{Farmer A: Do Not Invest} & (5, 0) & (1, 1) \\
\end{array}
\]

**Justification:** This game models the decision of two farmers to invest in transformer capacity, where mutual investment improves voltage quality but incurs higher costs for the investor.

#### 4. Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)
**Tension:** Reciprocal Benefit vs. Opportunism

**Matrix Representation:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}
 & \text{Sub-station: Exchange} & \text{Sub-station: No Exchange} \\
\hline
\text{Farmer: Exchange} & (2, 2) & (1, 3) \\
\text{Farmer: No Exchange} & (3, 1) & (1, 1) \\
\end{array}
\]

**Justification:** This game represents the interaction between a farmer and sub-station staff, where mutual exchange yields reciprocal benefit but unilateral exchange can lead to opportunism.

#### 5. Formal vs. Informal Authorization Game (AS5)
**Tension:** Formal Compliance vs. Informal Opportunism

**Matrix Representation:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}
 & \text{Sub-station: Invest} & \text{Sub-station: Withhold} \\
\hline
\text{Farmer: Formal Request} & (2, 1) & (1, 2) \\
\text{Farmer: Informal Request} & (1, 2) & (2, 1) \\
\end{array}
\]

**Justification:** This game models the decision of a farmer to request formal or informal authorization from sub-station staff, where formal compliance incurs a fee but ensures legal benefits.

#### 6. Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma (AS6)
**Tension:** Mutual Restraint vs. Unilateral Over-Extraction

**Matrix Representation:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}
 & \text{Farmer B: Restrain} & \text{Farmer B: Over-Extract} \\
\hline
\text{Farmer A: Restrain} & (2, 2) & (3, 1) \\
\text{Farmer A: Over-Extract} & (1, 3) & (1, 1) \\
\end{array}
\]

**Justification:** This game represents the decision of two farmers to extract groundwater, where mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion.
