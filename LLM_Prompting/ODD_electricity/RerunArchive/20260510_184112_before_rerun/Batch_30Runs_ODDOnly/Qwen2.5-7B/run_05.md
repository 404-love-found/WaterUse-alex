### Title: Distinct Action Situations in Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. Farmer-Farmer Coordination on Capacitor Adoption (AS1)
**Tension:** Mutual coordination vs. free-riding on capacitor investment.
**Matrix:**
```
          Farmer 2
          |      No Capacitor | Capacitor
        --------------------------------
Farmer 1 | 1,1          0,2      | 2,0
         --------------------------------
```
**Justification:** This game captures the coordination dilemma where mutual investment in capacitors benefits both farmers, but unilateral investment provides no additional private benefit.

#### 2. Sequential Social-Learning in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)
**Tension:** Diffusion of capacitor adoption based on observed outcomes.
**Sequential Representation:**
```
                Farmer 2
                  | No Capacitor | Capacitor
                --------------------------------
Farmer 1: No Capacitor | (0,0)         | (1,1)
Farmer 1: Capacitor   | (2,0)         | (1,1)
```
**Justification:** This sequential game represents the process by which farmers learn from each other's outcomes and decide whether to adopt capacitors based on observed benefits.

#### 3. Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)
**Tension:** Farmer’s investment in transformer capacity vs. staff’s authorization.
**Matrix:**
```
          Farmer
          | No Investment | Investment
        --------------------------------
Staff: No Authorization | (0,0)         | (1,2)
Staff: Authorization   | (2,1)         | (1,1)
```
**Justification:** This game captures the asymmetric dilemma where a farmer’s investment benefits both, but the costs fall solely on the farmer, leading to free-rider incentives.

#### 4. Mutual-Exchange Coordination between Farmer and Staff (AS4)
**Tension:** Informal exchange of services vs. formal cooperation.
**Matrix:**
```
          Staff
          | Formal Cooperation | No Cooperation
        --------------------------------
Farmer: Formal Cooperation | (1,1)         | (0,0)
Farmer: No Cooperation    | (0,1)         | (0,0)
```
**Justification:** This game represents the mutual benefit arising only when both farmer and staff engage in informal exchange, with formal cooperation yielding only modest gains for staff.

#### 5. Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination (AS5)
**Tension:** Formal vs. informal request for authorization and investment.
**Matrix:**
```
          Farmer
          | Formal Request | Informal Request
        --------------------------------
Staff: Formal Cooperation | (1,1)         | (0,0)
Staff: No Cooperation    | (0,2)         | (0,1)
```
**Justification:** This game captures the asymmetric incentives where staff gain modestly even under formal cooperation due to the investment burden, leading to a preference for informal exchanges.

#### 6. Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)
**Tension:** Mutual restraint vs. unilateral over-extraction.
**Matrix:**
```
          Farmer 2
          | No Extraction | Extraction
        --------------------------------
Farmer 1: No Extraction | (1,1)         | (0,2)
Farmer 1: Extraction   | (2,0)         | (0,0)
```
**Justification:** This game represents the competition over groundwater resources where mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gains and accelerates depletion.

#### 7. Farmer-Farmer Coordination on Informal Connections (AS7)
**Tension:** Mutual authorization vs. unilateral unauthorized use.
**Matrix:**
```
          Farmer 2
          | Unauthorized | Authorized
        --------------------------------
Farmer 1: Unauthorized | (0,0)         | (1,1)
Farmer 1: Authorized   | (1,0)         | (0,1)
```
**Justification:** This game captures the coordination dilemma where mutual authorization benefits both, but unilateral unauthorized use offers short-term gains but risks detection and penalties.

#### 8. Staff’s Enforcement Effort (AS8)
**Tension:** Formal compliance vs. informal reciprocity.
**Matrix:**
```
          Farmer
          | Formal Compliance | Informal Reciprocity
        --------------------------------
Staff: Formal Compliance | (1,1)         | (0,0)
Staff: Informal Reciprocity | (0,1)         | (1,0)
```
**Justification:** This game represents the trade-off where staff balance formal compliance and informal reciprocity, seeking stable relations and personal gain.

#### 9. Social Learning and Bounded Rationality (AS9)
**Tension:** Learning from peers vs. individual decision-making.
**Matrix:**
```
          Neighbor
          | No Capacitor | Capacitor
        --------------------------------
Farmer: No Capacitor | (0,0)         | (1,1)
Farmer: Capacitor   | (2,0)         | (1,1)
```
**Justification:** This game captures the process of social learning where farmers learn from their neighbors' outcomes and decide whether to adopt capacitors based on observed benefits.

#### 10. Informal Collusion between Farmer and Staff (AS10)
**Tension:** Informal exchange vs. formal enforcement.
**Matrix:**
```
          Staff
          | Formal Enforcement | Informal Exchange
        --------------------------------
Farmer: Formal Enforcement | (0,0)         | (1,1)
Farmer: Informal Exchange   | (1,0)         | (0,0)
```
**Justification:** This game captures the mutual benefit arising from informal exchanges where both farmer and staff gain, but enforcement is costly and informal cooperation saves effort.
