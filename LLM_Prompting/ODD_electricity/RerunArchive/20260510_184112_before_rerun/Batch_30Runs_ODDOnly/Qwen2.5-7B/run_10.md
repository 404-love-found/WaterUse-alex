### Title: Distinct Action Situations in the Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. Farmer-Farmer Coordination on Capacitor Adoption (AS1)
**Tension:** Mutual investment vs. Free-rider dilemma

**Matrix Representation:**

|       | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
|-------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | (2, 2) | (1, 0) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | (0, 1) | (0, 0) |

**Justification:** Both farmers benefit from mutual capacitor investment, but if one invests and the other does not, the non-investing farmer gains without cost. This creates a mutual coordination problem with Pareto-dominant mutual cooperation, but the risk of free-riding.

#### 2. Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)
**Tension:** Diffusion vs. Hesitation

**Sequential Representation:**

```
       Farmer 1
         /   \
      (3,3)  (1,2)
        /     \
Farmer 2 (2,1)  (2,2)
```

**Justification:** Farmer 2 observes the outcome of Farmer 1's capacitor adoption. If the outcome is successful (3,3), Farmer 2 imitates with a higher payoff. If the outcome is less successful (2,2), Farmer 2 hesitates. This captures the social learning process and the risk of imitation based on uncertain outcomes.

#### 4. Mutual-Exchange Coordination between Farmer and Staff (AS4)
**Tension:** Informal exchange vs. Formal compliance

**Matrix Representation:**

|       | Staff Invests | Staff Does Not Invest |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Farmer Invests** | (2, 2) | (1, 0) |

**Justification:** Both parties benefit from mutual exchange, but if one party engages and the other does not, the non-engaging party reverts to baseline. This captures the mutual exchange coordination where both parties gain only if they cooperate.

#### 5. Asymmetric Coordination Game between Farmer and Staff (AS5)
**Tension:** Legal vs. Opportunistic behavior

**Matrix Representation:**

|       | Staff Invests | Staff Does Not Invest |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Farmer Invests** | (2, 1) | (1, 0) |
| **Farmer Does Not Invest** | (0, 2) | (1, 1) |

**Justification:** The farmer bears the cost of formal investment but gains a modest benefit, while the staff gains more from informal investment with no formal fee. This creates asymmetric incentives between legality and opportunism.

#### 6. Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)
**Tension:** Mutual restraint vs. Over-extraction

**Matrix Representation:**

|       | Farmer 2 Extracts | Farmer 2 Does Not Extract |
|-------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Extracts** | (1, 1) | (2, 0) |

**Justification:** Both farmers benefit from mutual restraint, but if one over-extracts, they gain short-term benefits at the expense of long-term depletion. This creates a mutual restraint dilemma.
