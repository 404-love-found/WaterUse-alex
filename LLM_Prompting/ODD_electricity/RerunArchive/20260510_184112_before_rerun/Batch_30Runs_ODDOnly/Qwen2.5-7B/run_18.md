### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance (AS1)
**Title**: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
**Tension**: Coordination vs. Free-Rider Dilemma
**Matrix Representation**:
```
                     Farmer 2
                     |   No Capacitor |   Capacitor
                  ---------------------------------
        No Capacitor |   (0, 0)       |   (0, 0)
Farmer 1       Capacitor |   (0, 0)       |   (1, 1)
```
**Justification**: This represents the coordination problem where mutual investment in capacitors is necessary to improve voltage quality, but unilateral investment yields no private benefit. The coordination is risky due to the potential for a free-rider dilemma.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)
**Title**: Sequential Social-Learning Process
**Tension**: Diffusion vs. Non-Diffusion of Innovations
**Sequential Representation**:
```
1. Farmer 1
   |--- A (Adopt) |--- R (Reject)
2. Farmer 2
   |--- A (Adopt) |--- R (Reject)
```
**Justification**: This game represents the sequential process where each farmer observes the outcome of a peer’s decision and imitates only if the outcome is better. Diffusion occurs only after a successful coordinated trial has been observed.

### Action Situation 3: Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)
**Title**: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma
**Tension**: Free-Rider Dilemma in Grid Upgrades
**Matrix Representation**:
```
                     Farmer 2
                     |   No Authorization |   Authorization
                  ---------------------------------
        No Authorization |   (0, 0)       |   (0, 0)
Farmer 1     Authorization |   (0, 0)       |   (1, 0)
```
**Justification**: This represents the situation where one farmer’s authorization or investment benefits both by raising voltage quality, but the cost falls solely on the authorizer, creating a free-rider incentive. Only mutual cooperation yields a positive outcome.

### Action Situation 4: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)
**Title**: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game
**Tension**: Reciprocity vs. Opportunism
**Matrix Representation**:
```
                     Staff
                     |   No Exchange |   Exchange
                  ---------------------------------
        No Exchange |   (0, 0)       |   (-1, 1)
Farmer     Exchange |   (1, -1)      |   (1, 1)
```
**Justification**: This game represents the situation where reciprocal benefit arises only when both the farmer and staff engage in informal exchange. If one party abstains, the offerer bears a loss while the abstainer reverts to baseline.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game (AS5)
**Title**: Authorization and Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game
**Tension**: Legal Cooperation vs. Opportunistic Behavior
**Matrix Representation**:
```
                     Staff
                     |   Withhold Capacity |   Invest Capacity
                  ---------------------------------
        Formal Request |   (-1, 1)       |   (0, 0)
Farmer     Informal Request |   (1, -1)      |   (1, 1)
```
**Justification**: This represents the asymmetric coordination game where mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but staff may withhold capacity for informal requests, leading to opportunistic behavior.

### Action Situation 6: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)
**Title**: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
**Tension**: Sustainable Resource Use vs. Short-Term Gain
**Matrix Representation**:
```
                     Farmer 2
                     |   No Extraction |   Extraction
                  ---------------------------------
        No Extraction |   (1, 1)       |   (0, 2)
Farmer 1     Extraction |   (2, 0)      |   (0, 0)
```
**Justification**: This represents the situation where mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion. The dilemma arises because individual incentives lead to over-extraction, even though it is not in the collective interest.
