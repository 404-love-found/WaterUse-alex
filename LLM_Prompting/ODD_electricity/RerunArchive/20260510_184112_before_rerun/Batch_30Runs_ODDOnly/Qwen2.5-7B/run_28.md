### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)

**Title:** Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game

**Tension:** Mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant, but there is a risk of free-riding.

**Matrix Representation:**

|                | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
|----------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | (2, 2)            | (0, 1)                  |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | (1, 0)            | (1, 1)                  |

**Justification:** Both farmers benefit from mutual investment in capacitors, which stabilizes the voltage and improves irrigation efficiency. However, if one farmer invests and the other does not, the investing farmer incurs a cost without additional private benefit, creating an incentive to free-ride.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)

**Title:** Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption

**Tension:** Diffusion occurs only after a successful coordinated trial has been observed, leading to potential delays in adoption.

**Sequential Representation (Game Tree):**

```
Farmer 1
  |
  - Invest (2)
  |
  - Do Not Invest (1)
  |
  - Observe Farmer 2
    |
    - Farmer 2 Invests
      |
      - Farmer 1 Invests (2, 2)
      |
      - Farmer 1 Does Not Invest (1, 1)
    |
    - Farmer 2 Does Not Invest
      |
      - Farmer 1 Invests (0, 2)
      |
      - Farmer 1 Does Not Invest (1, 1)
```

**Justification:** The game tree shows that Farmer 1 observes the outcome of Farmer 2's decision to invest in a capacitor. Diffusion only occurs if both farmers coordinate successfully, otherwise, adoption may be delayed.

**Title:** Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma

### Action Situation 5: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game (AS5)

**Title:** Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game

**Tension:** Formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but there are asymmetric incentives between legality and opportunism.

**Matrix Representation:**

|                | Sub-Station Personnel Invests | Sub-Station Personnel Withholds |
|----------------|-------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Farmer Makes Formal Request** | (2, 2)                        | (1, 2)                         |
| **Farmer Makes Informal Request** | (2, 2)                        | (2, 1)                         |

**Justification:** Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but if the farmer makes a formal request and the staff withhold, the farmer incurs a loss while the staff save effort. If the farmer makes an informal request and the staff invest, the farmer gains more while the staff bear the cost without the formal fee.
