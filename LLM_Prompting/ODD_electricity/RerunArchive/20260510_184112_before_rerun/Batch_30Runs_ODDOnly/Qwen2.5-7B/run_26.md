### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance (AS1)
#### Tension
Mutual coordination versus unilateral investment in voltage-stabilizing equipment.
#### Matrix
|          | Neighbour Invests | Neighbour Does Not Invest |
|----------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| **Farmer Invests** | (2, 2) | (0, 0) |
| **Farmer Does Not Invest** | (0, 0) | (1, 1) |

#### Justification
This action situation captures the coordination problem where mutual investment in capacitors benefits both farmers by improving voltage quality, but unilateral investment yields no private benefit. The payoff matrix reflects the mutual cooperation being Pareto-dominant but risky.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)
#### Tension
Diffusion of capacitor adoption through social learning.
#### Sequential Representation
```
1. Farmer 1 decides to adopt capacitor (C) or not (NC)
2. Farmer 2 observes outcome and decides to adopt (C) or not (NC)
```

|          | Farmer 1: C | Farmer 1: NC |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Farmer 2: C** | (2, 2) | (1, 1) |
| **Farmer 2: NC** | (1, 1) | (0, 0) |

#### Justification
This action situation models the process where diffusion occurs only after a successful coordinated trial has been observed. The sequential representation captures the learning process and the risk involved in imitating a successful peer.

### Action Situation 3: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)

### Action Situation 6: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)
#### Tension
Mutual restraint versus unilateral over-extraction of groundwater.
#### Matrix
|          | Farmer 1 Restraints | Farmer 1 Over-Extracts |
|----------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Farmer 2 Restraints** | (2, 2) | (0, 3) |
| **Farmer 2 Over-Extracts** | (3, 0) | (1, 1) |
