### Title: Distinct Action Situations in the Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. Farmer-Farmer Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)
**Tension**: Coordination vs. Free-Rider Dilemma
**Matrix**:
```
          Farmer 2
          |  Invest |  Do Not Invest
        --------------------------------
  Invest |  (3,3)  |  (0,3)
Farmer 1 --------------------------------
  Do Not Invest |  (3,0)  |  (1,1)
```
**Justification**: This game represents the mutual benefit of investing in voltage-stabilizing capacitors. Mutual investment leads to a Pareto-optimal outcome, but the first mover faces the risk of being a free-rider if the other farmer does not invest.

#### 2. Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)
**Tension**: Social Learning vs. Independent Decision-Making
**Sequential Representation**:
```
1. Farmer 1
   |
   v
2. Farmer 2
   |
   v
3. Farmer 1
   |
   v
4. Farmer 2
   |
   v
```
**Justification**: This sequential game illustrates how farmers learn from each other’s capacitor investments. The game tree shows that farmers observe each other’s outcomes and imitate only if the outcome is better than their own. This process can lead to slow diffusion of innovation.

#### 3. Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)
**Tension**: Investment vs. Free-Rider Dilemma
**Matrix**:
```
          Farmer 2
          |  Authorize |  Do Not Authorize
        --------------------------------
  Authorize |  (2,2)  |  (0,4)
Farmer 1 --------------------------------
  Do Not Authorize |  (4,0)  |  (1,1)
```
**Justification**: This game captures the dilemma where one farmer’s authorization benefits both, but the costs fall solely on the authorizer. The first mover (authorizer) faces a free-rider problem if the other farmer does not invest.

#### 4. Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)
**Tension**: Informal Exchange vs. Formal Compliance
**Matrix**:
```
          Sub-station Staff
          |  Exchange |  Do Not Exchange
        --------------------------------
  Farmer 1
  Exchange |  (2,2)  |  (0,4)
  --------------------------------
  Do Not Exchange |  (4,0)  |  (1,1)
```
**Justification**: This game models the interaction between a farmer and sub-station staff where mutual exchange of services benefits both, but the farmer faces the risk of not gaining if the staff do not reciprocate. The staff may save effort by not engaging in exchange.

#### 5. Asymmetric Coordination Game (AS5)
**Tension**: Formal vs. Informal Request for Transformer Capacity
**Matrix**:
```
          Sub-station Staff
          |  Invest |  Withhold
        -------------------------
  Formal Request |  (2,2)  |  (0,4)
Farmer 1 --------------------------------
  Informal Request |  (4,0)  |  (1,1)
```
**Justification**: This game shows the strategic tension between a farmer making a formal or informal request for transformer capacity. Formal requests lead to mutual cooperation if both comply, but informal requests may lead to one-sided gains or losses.

#### 6. Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)
**Tension**: Short-Term Gain vs. Long-Term Sustainability
**Matrix**:
```
          Farmer 2
          |  Restrain |  Over-Extract
        --------------------------------
  Restrain |  (2,2)  |  (0,5)
Farmer 1 --------------------------------
  Over-Extract |  (5,0)  |  (1,1)
```
**Justification**: This game represents the conflict between two farmers drawing from the same aquifer. Mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term benefits at the cost of long-term sustainability.
