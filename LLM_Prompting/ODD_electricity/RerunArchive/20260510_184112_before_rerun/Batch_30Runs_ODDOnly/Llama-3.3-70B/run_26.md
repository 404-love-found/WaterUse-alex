### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Improvement
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors, with mutual investment yielding shared benefits.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers deciding to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment improves voltage quality for both, while unilateral investment provides no added benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Observational Learning
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful peers who have adopted capacitors.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor.
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome.
   - If outcome is successful, Farmer 2 adopts capacitor.
   - If outcome is unsuccessful, Farmer 2 does not adopt capacitor.
#### Justification: This sequential action situation represents how farmers learn from each other's experiences with capacitor adoption, influencing their decision to invest.

### Action Situation 3: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Authorization and Investment

#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 2, 4 | 1, 3 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 3, 2 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the asymmetric incentives in transformer capacity investment, where one farmer bears the cost but both benefit.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game
#### Title: Formal vs. Informal Requests
#### Tension: The farmer decides between formal and informal requests for capacity, while staff decide whether to invest or withhold capacity.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer makes formal request.
   - Staff invests: Farmer gains, Staff bears cost.
   - Staff withholds: Farmer incurs loss, Staff saves effort.
2. Farmer makes informal request.
   - Staff invests: Farmer gains more, Staff bears cost without formal fee.
   - Staff does not invest: No extra benefit occurs.
#### Justification: This sequential action situation represents the strategic interaction between farmers and staff in authorization and investment decisions, with asymmetric incentives.

### Action Situation 6: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Title: Groundwater Over-Extraction
#### Tension: Mutual restraint in groundwater extraction sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gains.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the classic prisoner’s dilemma, where individual self-interest leads to over-extraction and depletion of the groundwater resource.
