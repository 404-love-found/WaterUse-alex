### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Improvement
#### Tension: Mutual cooperation for shared benefit vs. unilateral investment risk
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two neighbouring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilising equipment. Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Observational Learning for Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Imitation of successful peers vs. risk of unsuccessful investment
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
- Farmer 1 adopts capacitor
  - Success: Farmer 2 adopts
  - Failure: Farmer 2 does not adopt
- Farmer 1 does not adopt capacitor
  - Farmer 2 does not adopt
#### Justification: This sequential action situation represents the process by which farmers observe and imitate the successful outcomes of their peers in adopting capacitor measures, reflecting social learning and bounded rationality.

### Action Situation 3: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Uneven Costs for Shared Benefit
#### Tension: Contribution to shared capacity vs. free-riding on others' investment
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Authorizes | Farmer 2 Does Not Authorize |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Authorizes** | 4, 4 | 2, 5 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Authorize** | 5, 2 | 3, 3 |
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the asymmetric incentives between farmers when one farmer's authorization or investment benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer, generating a free-rider problem.
