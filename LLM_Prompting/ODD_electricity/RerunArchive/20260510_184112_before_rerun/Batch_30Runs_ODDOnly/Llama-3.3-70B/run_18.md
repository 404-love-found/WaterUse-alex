# Run 18 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Coordination for Voltage Stability
#### Tension: Farmers must coordinate to adopt capacitors for mutual benefit, as unilateral adoption yields no added private benefit.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopt | Farmer 2 Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopt** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between farmers who must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual cooperation being Pareto-dominant but risky.

### Action Situation 2: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Uneven Costs for Collective Benefit
#### Tension: One farmer's authorization or investment in transformer capacity benefits both farmers but costs fall solely on the authorizer.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invest | Farmer 2 Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invest** | 2, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Invest** | 3, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the free-rider incentive and uneven payoffs when one farmer invests in transformer capacity, benefiting both, but bearing the cost alone.

### Action Situation 3: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game
#### Title: Informal Exchange for Reciprocal Benefit
#### Tension: Reciprocal benefit arises only when both the farmer and sub-station staff engage in informal exchange; if either abstains, the offerer bears a loss.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Staff Engage | Staff Not Engage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Engage** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer Not Engage** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension in informal exchanges between farmers and staff, where mutual cooperation yields reciprocal benefits, but abstention by either party results in loss or baseline outcomes.

### Action Situation 4: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game
#### Title: Asymmetric Incentives for Legality and Opportunism
#### Tension: Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but staff gain modestly even under formal cooperation due to investment burden, producing asymmetric incentives.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer (Formal/Informal Request)
2. Staff (Invest/Withhold Capacity)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Invest, payoff is (2, 3).
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Withhold, payoff is (1, 2).
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Invest, payoff is (3, 1).
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Withhold, payoff is (2, 2).
#### Justification: This action situation captures the asymmetric incentives between legality and opportunism in the interaction between farmers and staff regarding authorization and investment in capacity.

### Action Situation 5: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Title: Unsustainable Extraction
#### Tension: Mutual restraint sustains groundwater yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restrain | Farmer 2 Over-Extract |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restrain** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extract** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the classic prisoner’s dilemma, where individual self-interest leads to over-extraction and depletion of the groundwater resource, despite the collective benefit of mutual restraint.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Sequential Adoption Based on Observations
#### Tension: Farmers observe a peer’s outcome and imitate only if that outcome ranks higher, leading to diffusion after a successful coordinated trial.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 (Adopt/Not Adopt)
2. Farmer 2 Observes Outcome
3. Farmer 2 (Adopt/Not Adopt based on observation)
- If Farmer 1 adopts and outcome is successful, Farmer 2 adopts.
- If Farmer 1 adopts and outcome is not successful, Farmer 2 does not adopt.
- If Farmer 1 does not adopt, Farmer 2 does not adopt.
#### Justification: This action situation captures the process of social learning in the adoption of capacitors, where farmers make decisions based on observing the outcomes of their peers, leading to the potential diffusion of capacitor adoption.
