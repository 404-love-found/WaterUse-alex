### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination problem between farmers to invest in voltage-stabilising equipment
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two neighbouring farmers deciding whether to invest in capacitor adoption. Mutual investment yields shared improvement (3, 3), while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit (1, 2), creating a coordination problem.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Sequential decision-making based on observed outcomes
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor, observes outcome (success/failure)
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome
   - If success, Farmer 2 adopts capacitor
   - If failure, Farmer 2 does not adopt capacitor

#### Justification: This action situation represents the sequential social-learning process where each farmer observes the outcome of the other's capacitor adoption decision and makes their decision based on that observation.

### Action Situation 3: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Transformer Capacity Authorization
#### Tension: Free-rider problem between farmers authorizing transformer capacity
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Authorizes | Farmer 2 Does Not Authorize |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Authorizes** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Authorize** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two farmers where one farmer's authorization benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer, generating a free-rider incentive.

### Action Situation 4: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game between Farmer and Staff
#### Title: Informal Exchange between Farmer and Staff

#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

| --- | --- | --- |

### Action Situation 6: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Title: Groundwater Extraction
#### Tension: Conflict between short-term gain and long-term sustainability
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Restraints Extraction | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints Extraction** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |

#### Justification: This action situation represents the groundwater-extraction prisoner’s dilemma where mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion.
