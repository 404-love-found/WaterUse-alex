### Action Situation 1: Capacitor-Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Farmer-Farmer Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination problem for mutual benefit
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopt | Farmer 2 Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopt** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two neighbouring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilising equipment (capacitors). Mutual investment yields a shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

#### Title: Farmer-Farmer Transformer Authorization

### Action Situation 4: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game
#### Title: Farmer-Staff Formal Cooperation
#### Tension: Asymmetric incentives between legality and opportunism
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer (Formal/Informal Request)
2. Staff (Invest/Withhold Capacity)
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Invest: Farmer gains 3, Staff gains 2
- If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff chooses Withhold: Farmer loses 1, Staff saves effort
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Invest: Farmer gains more, Staff bears cost
- If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff chooses Withhold: No extra benefit
#### Justification: This action situation represents the strategic tension between a farmer (formal vs. informal request) and staff (invest vs. withhold capacity) with asymmetric incentives.

### Action Situation 5: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Title: Farmer-Farmer Groundwater Extraction
#### Tension: Conflict between short-term gain and long-term sustainability
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restrain | Farmer 2 Over-Extract |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restrain** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extract** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two farmers drawing from the same aquifer, where mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion.

### Action Situation 6: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Farmer Social Learning
#### Tension: Adoption based on observed outcomes
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor or not
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome
3. Farmer 2 decides to adopt based on observed outcome
- If Farmer 1's outcome is successful, Farmer 2 adopts
- If Farmer 1's outcome is not successful, Farmer 2 does not adopt
#### Justification: This action situation represents the sequential process of social learning among farmers in adopting capacitor measures based on observed outcomes of their peers.
