### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination Problem
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Adopts Capacitor | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt Capacitor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts Capacitor** | 3, 3 | 0, 0 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt Capacitor** | 0, 0 | 1, 1 |

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two neighboring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing equipment (capacitors). Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Social Learning
#### Tension: Imitation and Coordination
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
- Farmer 1 adopts capacitor or not.
- Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome and decides to adopt or not based on the observed outcome.

#### Justification: This action situation represents the process of social learning where farmers observe their peers' outcomes and imitate them if the outcome is better, leading to the diffusion of capacitor adoption.

### Action Situation 3: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Transformer Capacity
#### Tension: Free-Rider Problem
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 2, 4 | 1, 3 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 3, 2 | 0, 0 |

#### Justification: This dilemma arises when one farmer's investment in transformer capacity benefits both farmers, but the costs fall solely on the investor, creating an incentive for the other farmer to free-ride.

### Action Situation 6: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Title: Groundwater Extraction
#### Tension: Over-Extraction
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Restraints Extraction | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints Extraction** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |

#### Justification: This dilemma represents the strategic tension between two farmers extracting groundwater from the same aquifer, where mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain, leading to the depletion of the groundwater.
