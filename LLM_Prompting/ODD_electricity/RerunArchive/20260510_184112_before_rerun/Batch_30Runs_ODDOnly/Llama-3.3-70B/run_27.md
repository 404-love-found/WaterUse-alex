### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Tension: Coordination Problem
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two neighbouring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilising equipment (capacitors). Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Imitation and Learning
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts or does not adopt a capacitor.
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome and decides whether to adopt based on the observed outcome.

#### Justification: This sequential action situation represents the process by which farmers learn from each other's experiences with capacitor adoption. Farmer 2 imitates Farmer 1 only if the observed outcome ranks higher.
