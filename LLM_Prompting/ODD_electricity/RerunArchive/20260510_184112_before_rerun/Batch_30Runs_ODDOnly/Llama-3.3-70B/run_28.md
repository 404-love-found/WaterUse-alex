### Action Situation 1: Capacitor-Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Farmer-Farmer Coordination
#### Tension: Mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant but risky
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the coordination problem between two neighboring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing equipment. Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Farmer Social Learning
#### Tension: Imitation of successful peers
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome
3. Farmer 2 imitates if outcome ranks higher
#### Justification: This action situation represents the sequential social-learning process in capacitor adoption, where each farmer observes a peer's outcome and imitates only if that outcome ranks higher.

#### Title: Farmer-Farmer Authorization

### Action Situation 5: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game
#### Title: Farmer-Staff Formal Cooperation
#### Tension: Asymmetric incentives between legality and opportunism
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer makes formal request
2. Staff decides to invest or withhold capacity
3. Payoffs: (Farmer, Staff) = (2, 3) if both cooperate, (1, 4) if farmer cooperates and staff does not, (3, 1) if farmer does not cooperate and staff does, (2, 2) if neither cooperates
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the authorization-and-investment asymmetric coordination game between a farmer and staff, where mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal but staff gain modestly even under formal cooperation due to investment burden.

### Action Situation 6: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Title: Farmer-Farmer Groundwater Extraction
#### Tension: Mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation represents the groundwater-extraction prisoner's dilemma between two farmers drawing from the same aquifer, where mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion.
