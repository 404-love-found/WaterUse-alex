### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Farmer-Farmer Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination vs. Free-Riding
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Adopts Capacitor | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt Capacitor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts Capacitor** | 3, 3 (Improved voltage stability) | 1, 2 (Farmer 1 bears cost, Farmer 2 free-rides) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt Capacitor** | 2, 1 (Farmer 2 bears cost, Farmer 1 free-rides) | 0, 0 (No improvement in voltage stability) |

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two farmers deciding whether to adopt capacitors to improve voltage stability. Both farmers benefit if both adopt (3, 3), but if only one adopts, the adopter bears the cost while the other free-rides (1, 2 or 2, 1), making unilateral adoption less attractive.

#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction
#### Title: Groundwater Extraction Dilemma
#### Tension: Individual Gain vs. Collective Loss
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Restrains Extraction | Farmer 2 Does Not Restrain Extraction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restrains Extraction** | 3, 3 (Sustainable groundwater use) | 2, 4 (Farmer 1 loses, Farmer 2 gains in short term) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Restrain Extraction** | 4, 2 (Farmer 1 gains in short term, Farmer 2 loses) | 1, 1 (Groundwater depletion, both lose in long term) |

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Farmer-Staff Authorization and Enforcement
#### Tension: Compliance vs. Evasion
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer decides to seek formal authorization or informal access.
2. If Farmer seeks formal authorization, Staff decides to enforce rules or tolerate informal access.
   - If Staff enforces, Farmer faces penalties or exclusion.
   - If Staff tolerates, Farmer may obtain cheaper access, but system records become less reliable.
3. If Farmer seeks informal access, Staff may detect and enforce, leading to penalties for Farmer, or may tolerate, leading to potential system overload.

#### Justification: This sequential action situation captures the interaction between a farmer and staff regarding authorization and enforcement. The farmer's decision to seek formal or informal access influences the staff's decision to enforce or tolerate, leading to various outcomes based on compliance or evasion strategies.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Adoption vs. Non-Adoption Based on Observations
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer observes neighbor's capacitor adoption and outcome.
2. If neighbor's adoption is successful, Farmer decides to adopt based on observed benefits.
3. If neighbor's adoption is unsuccessful or unclear, Farmer may hesitate or decide not to adopt.

#### Justification: This action situation reflects the process of social learning in the context of capacitor adoption. A farmer's decision to adopt a capacitor is influenced by observing the outcomes of neighboring farmers who have already adopted, highlighting the role of social learning in technology diffusion.
