### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization-Enforcement Dilemma
#### Tension: The farmer's decision to seek formal access and the staff's decision to enforce rules or invest in capacity create asymmetric incentives.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer decides to seek formal access or informal access.
2. If formal, staff decide to invest in capacity or withhold.
   - If staff invest, reliability improves, but staff bear effort costs.
   - If staff withhold, farmer bears costs without expected reliability.
3. If informal, staff decide to tolerate or enforce.
   - If tolerate, farmer may obtain cheaper access, but system records become less reliable.
   - If enforce, farmer faces penalties or exclusion.
#### Justification: This sequential action situation captures the complex interaction between farmers and staff regarding authorization, enforcement, and maintenance, with each side's decision affecting the other's incentives and outcomes.

### Action Situation 6: Capacitor Adoption with Social Learning
#### Title: Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful peers, but diffusion is path-dependent and can be discouraged by early failed or isolated adoption.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 decides to adopt a capacitor or not.
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome and decides to adopt or not based on its success.
3. Adoption diffusion continues through the social network, with each farmer's decision influenced by observed outcomes of similar peers.
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the process of social learning in the adoption of capacitors, where the decision to adopt is influenced by the observed success of peers, leading to path-dependent diffusion.
