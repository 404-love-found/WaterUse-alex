### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Coordination Dilemma in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability, with benefits strongest when adoption is coordinated.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Adopts Capacitor | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt Capacitor |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts Capacitor** | 3, 3 (Mutual improvement) | 1, 2 (Farmer 1 bears cost, Farmer 2 free-rides) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt Capacitor** | 2, 1 (Farmer 2 bears cost, Farmer 1 free-rides) | 0, 0 (No improvement) |

#### Justification: The matrix reflects the strategic tension between two farmers deciding on capacitor adoption. Mutual adoption yields the highest payoff (3, 3), while unilateral adoption results in a lower payoff for the adopting farmer.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma
#### Tension: One farmer's authorization or investment in transformer capacity benefits both farmers but costs fall solely on the authorizer.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:

|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 2, 2 (Shared benefit, shared cost) | 1, 3 (Farmer 1 bears cost, Farmer 2 free-rides) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 3, 1 (Farmer 1 free-rides, Farmer 2 bears cost) | 0, 0 (No benefit) |

#### Justification: This matrix represents the asymmetric authorization dilemma, where one farmer's contribution benefits both but creates a free-rider incentive for the other.

#### Title: Groundwater Over-Extraction Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmers must decide how much groundwater to extract, with individual high extraction dominating in the short run but leading to mutual depletion.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Authorization-Enforcement Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmers decide whether to seek formal authorization, while staff decide whether to enforce rules or tolerate informal access.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Request or Informal Access
2. Staff chooses: Enforce or Tolerate
- If Farmer chooses Formal Request and Staff chooses Enforce, outcome is (2, 1)
- If Farmer chooses Formal Request and Staff chooses Tolerate, outcome is (3, 2)
- If Farmer chooses Informal Access and Staff chooses Enforce, outcome is (1, 3)
- If Farmer chooses Informal Access and Staff chooses Tolerate, outcome is (0, 0)

#### Justification: This sequential representation captures the authorization-enforcement dilemma, where farmers and staff make interdependent decisions under formal and informal institutional arrangements.

### Action Situation 6: Capacitor Adoption with Social Learning
#### Title: Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers observe neighbors' capacitor adoption outcomes and decide whether to imitate, with diffusion dependent on visible success.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Neighbor adopts capacitor and observes outcome
2. Farmer observes neighbor's outcome and decides: Adopt or Not Adopt
- If Neighbor's outcome is successful, Farmer adopts with probability p
- If Neighbor's outcome is unsuccessful, Farmer does not adopt

#### Justification: This sequential representation reflects the social learning process in capacitor adoption, where farmers imitate successful neighbors, influencing the diffusion of the technology.
