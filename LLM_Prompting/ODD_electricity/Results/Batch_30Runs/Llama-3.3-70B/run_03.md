### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Coordination Dilemma in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to adopt capacitors to improve voltage stability, with mutual cooperation being beneficial but unilateral adoption providing little benefit.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopt | Farmer 2 Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopt** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two neighboring farmers deciding whether to adopt capacitors. Mutual adoption improves voltage stability, benefiting both, while unilateral adoption offers little benefit, creating a coordination dilemma.

#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contribute | Farmer 2 Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contribute** | 2, 3 | 1, 3 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Contribute** | 3, 2 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation highlights the asymmetric contribution dilemma where one farmer's investment in transformer capacity benefits others, but the contributor incurs the cost, leading to a free-rider problem.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Compliance Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmers must decide whether to seek formal authorization for electricity connections, while staff decide whether to enforce rules, with compliance being beneficial but costly.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Seek Formal Authorization or Not
2. Staff chooses: Enforce or Not
- If Farmer seeks authorization and Staff enforces, both gain (3, 3)
- If Farmer seeks authorization and Staff does not enforce, Farmer loses (1, 2)
- If Farmer does not seek authorization and Staff enforces, Farmer loses (2, 1)
- If Farmer does not seek authorization and Staff does not enforce, both lose (2, 2)
#### Justification: This action situation represents the compliance dilemma where farmers decide whether to seek formal authorization, and staff decide whether to enforce rules. Compliance is beneficial but involves costs, leading to a strategic tension between formal and informal connections.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Sequential Adoption Dilemma
#### Tension: Farmers observe neighbors' adoption of capacitors and decide whether to adopt based on perceived benefits, with early adopters influencing later decisions.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Neighbor adopts capacitor or not
2. Farmer observes outcome and decides: Adopt or Not
- If Neighbor adopts and outcome is successful, Farmer adopts (3)
- If Neighbor adopts and outcome is unsuccessful, Farmer does not adopt (2)
- If Neighbor does not adopt, Farmer does not adopt (2)
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the sequential adoption dilemma where farmers learn from their neighbors' experiences with capacitor adoption and make decisions based on observed outcomes, influencing the diffusion of technology.
