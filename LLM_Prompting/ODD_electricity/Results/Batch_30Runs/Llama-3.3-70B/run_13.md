### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Coordination
#### Title: Mutual Investment in Voltage-Stabilizing Equipment
#### Tension: Coordination problem between two neighboring farmers to invest in capacitors for shared voltage stability improvement.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two farmers deciding whether to invest in capacitors. Mutual investment yields a higher payoff (3, 3) for both, representing improved voltage stability and efficiency. If only one invests, the payoff is lower (1, 2 or 2, 1), indicating reduced private benefit due to lack of coordination.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Asymmetric Authorization and Investment
#### Tension: One farmer’s authorization or investment in transformer capacity benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer, creating a free-rider incentive.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Contributes | Farmer 2 Does Not Contribute |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Contributes** | 2, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Contribute** | 3, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This situation highlights the asymmetric payoff structure where one farmer’s contribution to transformer capacity improves voltage quality for both, but the contributor bears the cost while the non-contributor enjoys the benefit without paying.

### Action Situation 3: Farmer-Staff Mutual Exchange
#### Title: Informal Exchange for Reciprocal Benefit
#### Tension: Farmer and staff engage in informal exchange where reciprocal benefit arises only when both participate.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Staff Offers Exchange | Staff Does Not Offer Exchange |
| --- | --- | --- |

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Title: Individual vs. Collective Interest in Groundwater Use
#### Tension: Farmers face a dilemma between individual short-term gain from high extraction and collective long-term sustainability of the groundwater resource.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restrains | Farmer 2 Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restrains** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the classic prisoner’s dilemma. Mutual restraint (3, 3) is collectively optimal for sustainable groundwater use, but individual high extraction (4, 1 or 1, 4) offers a short-term advantage, leading to potential over-extraction and resource depletion.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Enforcement
#### Title: Formal Compliance and Informal Tolerance
#### Tension: Farmer decides between formal authorization and informal access, while staff decide between enforcement and tolerance.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer chooses: Formal Request or Informal Access
2. Staff responds based on oversight risk and trust:
   - If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff Enforces, payoff (2, 2)
   - If Farmer chooses Formal and Staff Tolerates, payoff (4, 1)
   - If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff Enforces, payoff (1, 3)
   - If Farmer chooses Informal and Staff Tolerates, payoff (3, 2)
#### Justification: This sequential action situation captures the dynamic between a farmer’s choice of access type and a staff member’s response. The payoffs reflect the trade-offs between formal compliance, informal access, enforcement, and tolerance, considering oversight risk and trust between the parties.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning and Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Imitation and Coordination in Technology Adoption
#### Tension: Farmers observe and imitate successful neighbors in adopting capacitors, facing a coordination problem for effective improvement.
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Neighbor adopts capacitor and observes outcome
2. Farmer decides to adopt based on neighbor’s outcome
   - If Neighbor’s outcome is successful and Farmer adopts, payoff (3)
   - If Neighbor’s outcome is unsuccessful or Farmer does not adopt, payoff (1 or 2)
#### Justification: This action situation represents the process of social learning in capacitor adoption. A farmer’s decision to adopt is influenced by the observed outcome of a neighbor’s adoption, reflecting the path-dependent nature of technology diffusion and the importance of coordination for achieving the full benefits of adoption.
