### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Mutual Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination Problem
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopt | Farmer 2 Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopt** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the coordination problem between two neighbouring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilising equipment (capacitors). Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Imitation of Successful Peers
#### Tension: Social Learning Dilemma
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor.
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome.
3. If outcome is successful, Farmer 2 imitates; otherwise, not.
#### Justification: This sequential action situation represents the social-learning process where a farmer observes a peer's outcome and imitates only if that outcome ranks higher, leading to the diffusion of capacitor adoption.

### Action Situation 3: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Uneven Costs and Benefits of Transformer Capacity
#### Tension: Free-Rider Problem
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Authorize | Farmer 2 Not Authorize |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Authorize** | 2, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Not Authorize** | 3, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation captures the asymmetric authorization dilemma between two farmers, where one farmer's authorization or investment benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer, generating a free-rider incentive.

### Action Situation 6: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Title: Unilateral Over-Extraction
#### Tension: Tragedy of the Commons
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restrain | Farmer 2 Over-Extract |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restrain** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
