- **Tension:** Asymmetric free-rider problem: one farmer’s authorization or investment benefits both by raising voltage quality, but the cost falls solely on the authorizer, while the non-authorizer benefits without paying.
- **Matrix/Sequential Representation:** (Sequential game tree)
  ```
  Farmer A (Authorizer):
    Invest → Farmer B:
        Contribute → (3,3)
        Free-ride  → (1,4)
    Not    → (2,2)
  ```
- **Justification:** The tree captures the asymmetric interdependence: Farmer A decides whether to invest in authorization that creates a public good. If A invests, B can contribute to the cost or free-ride. In the unique subgame-perfect equilibrium, B free-rides (4 > 3), so A does not invest (2 > 1). This represents the under-investment trap due to asymmetric costs and benefits.

**AS4: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game**
- **Title:** Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game
- **Tension:** Coordination dilemma in informal exchange: reciprocal benefit arises only when both farmer and staff engage in informal exchange; unilateral offering leads to a loss for the offerer and baseline for the abstainer.
- **Matrix/Sequential Representation:** (Normal-form game)
  ```
        Cooperate  Defect
  Coop   (4,4)     (1,2)
  Def    (2,1)     (2,2)
  ```
- **Justification:** The payoff matrix reflects an assurance game: mutual cooperation yields the highest payoff (4,4); mutual defection gives the baseline (2,2); unilateral cooperation gives the cooperator the worst payoff (1) while the defector gets the baseline (2). Only matched cooperation yields mutual gain, capturing the relational governance between farmer and staff.

**AS5: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game**
- **Title:** Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game
- **Tension:** Asymmetric coordination between legality and opportunism: mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but individual incentives favor informal requests for the farmer and withholding investment for the staff.
- **Matrix/Sequential Representation:** (Normal-form game)
  ```
          Staff: Invest  Withhold
  Farmer: Formal  (3,3)    (1,4)
          Informal (4,1)    (2,2)
  ```
- **Justification:** The matrix captures the asymmetric incentives: (Formal, Invest) is collectively optimal (sum 6), but Farmer prefers (Informal, Invest) where he gains more (4) while Staff bear cost (1); Staff prefer (Formal, Withhold) where they save effort (4) while Farmer bears loss (1). The baseline (Informal, Withhold) gives (2,2). This represents the tension between formal compliance and opportunistic informal exchange.

**AS6: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma**
- **Title:** Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
- **Tension:** Common-pool resource dilemma: mutual restraint sustains groundwater yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain, leading to a dominant strategy to over-extract and a collectively suboptimal outcome.
- **Matrix/Sequential Representation:** (Normal-form game)
  ```
        Restrain  Over-extract
  Restrain (3,3)    (1,4)
  Over     (4,1)    (2,2)
  ```
- **Justification:** The classic PD payoff structure: Temptation (4) > Reward (3) > Punishment (2) > Sucker (1). Over-extraction is dominant, but mutual restraint is Pareto-superior to mutual over-extraction. This captures the depletion dilemma in groundwater use.
