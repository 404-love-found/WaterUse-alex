# Run 11 — deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-R1

Based on the ODD+D description, here are the distinct action situations reflecting unique strategic tensions in electricity governance, presented as 2-player normal form games:

### 1. Capacitor Adoption Assurance  
**Tension**: Coordination problem where mutual investment in capacitors yields Pareto-dominant benefits, but unilateral investment is ineffective due to voltage stability requirements.  
**Payoff Matrix**:  
```
          Farmer 2  
          Adopt    Not Adopt  
Farmer 1 Adopt   (4, 4)    (1, 2)  
         Not Adopt (2, 1)    (2, 2)  
```  
**Justification**: From AS1 (III.iv.a). Mutual adoption improves voltage stability, but unilateral adoption yields no private benefit due to technical interdependencies. Payoffs reflect Pareto-dominant mutual cooperation (4,4), baseline non-adoption (2,2), and asymmetric loss for sole adopters (1/2).  

### 2. Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma  
**Tension**: Asymmetric free-rider problem where one farmer’s investment benefits both, but non-investors gain more.  
**Payoff Matrix**:  
```
          Farmer 2  
          Invest    Free-ride  
Farmer 1 Invest   (3, 3)    (2, 4)  
         Free-ride (4, 2)    (1, 1)  
```  
**Justification**: From AS3 (III.iv.a). Authorization/investment in shared transformer capacity creates collective benefits but uneven costs. Non-investors free-ride (4) while investors bear costs (2). Mutual defection (1,1) is worst.  

### 3. Mutual Exchange Coordination (Farmer-Staff)  
**Tension**: Informal exchanges (e.g., bribes) require mutual engagement; unilateral offers incur losses.  
**Payoff Matrix**:  
```
          Staff  
          Engage    Abstain  
Farmer Engage   (4, 4)    (1, 3)  
       Abstain  (3, 1)    (3, 3)  
```  
**Justification**: From AS4 (III.iv.a). Reciprocal exchanges yield mutual gain (4,4). Unilateral engagement causes loss for the offerer (1) and baseline for the abstainer (3). Mutual abstention (3,3) avoids losses.  

### 4. Authorization-Investment Asymmetric Coordination (Farmer-Staff)  
**Tension**: Formal cooperation is collectively optimal but staff bear investment costs, incentivizing informal shortcuts.  
**Payoff Matrix**:  
```
          Staff  
          Invest    Withhold  
Farmer Formal    (4, 2)    (1, 3)  
       Informal  (5, 1)    (2, 3)  
```  
**Justification**: From AS5 (III.iv.a). Formal authorization with investment (4,2) is efficient but costly for staff. Informal requests reward farmers (5) but burden staff (1). Staff prefer withholding (3) regardless.  

### 5. Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma  
**Tension**: Mutual restraint preserves groundwater, but unilateral over-extraction yields short-term gains.  
**Payoff Matrix**:  
```
          Farmer 2  
          Restrain    Over-extract  
Farmer 1 Restrain   (3, 3)    (1, 4)  
         Over-extract (4, 1)    (2, 2)  
```  
**Justification**: From AS6 (III.iv.a). Mutual restraint sustains resources (3,3). Unilateral over-extraction exploits shared aquifers (4 vs. 1), but mutual over-extraction depletes resources (2,2).  

### Key Notes:  
- **Payoffs** are ordinal ranks (higher = better) derived from ODD+D descriptions (III.iv.b).  
- **Excluded AS2**: Sequential social learning (III.iv.a) is not a simultaneous strategic dilemma and lacks a 2-player matrix.  
- **Bounded rationality & context**: Matrices incorporate empirical constraints (e.g., voltage interdependence, cost asymmetry, collusion norms) from Sections II.i–II.viii.  
- **Linkages**: Actions in one situation (e.g., transformer investment) affect others (e.g., capacitor efficiency) through shared infrastructure.
