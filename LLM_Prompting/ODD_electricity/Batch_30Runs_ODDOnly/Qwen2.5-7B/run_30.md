### Title: Distinct Action Situations in the Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. Farmer-Farmer Coordination on Capacitor Adoption (AS1)
**Tension:** Mutual Cooperation vs. Individualism

**Matrix:**

|            | Farmer 2: Invest | Farmer 2: Do Not Invest |
|------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Invest** | (2, 2)           | (1, 3)                  |
| **Farmer 1: Do Not Invest** | (3, 1)           | (0, 0)                  |

**Justification:** 
- Mutual investment in capacitors improves voltage stability for both farmers, but unilateral investment does not provide any private benefit. This creates a coordination problem where mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant but risky due to the potential for free-riding.

#### 2. Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)
**Tension:** Initial Adoption vs. Waiting for Evidence

**Matrix:**

|            | Farmer 2: Adopt | Farmer 2: Do Not Adopt |
|------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Adopt** | (2, 2)          | (1, 0)                 |
| **Farmer 1: Do Not Adopt** | (0, 1)          | (1, 1)                 |

**Justification:** 
- If both farmers adopt capacitors, they both benefit. However, if one farmer adopts and the other does not, the adopter benefits while the non-adopter does not. The non-adopter waits to see if the adopter's outcome is positive before making a decision.

#### 3. Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)
**Tension:** Farmer Investment vs. Staff Inaction

**Matrix:**

|            | Farmer 2: Invest | Farmer 2: Do Not Invest |
|------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Invest** | (2, 2)           | (1, 0)                  |
| **Farmer 1: Do Not Invest** | (0, 1)           | (1, 1)                  |

**Justification:** 
- Mutual investment in transformer capacity improves voltage stability for both farmers, but the costs fall entirely on the farmer who invests, creating a free-rider dilemma. If only one farmer invests, the non-investing farmer benefits more.

#### 4. Mutual-Exchange Coordination with Staff (AS4)
**Tension:** Informal Exchange vs. Formal Compliance

**Matrix:**

|            | Staff 2: Exchange | Staff 2: No Exchange |
|------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Exchange** | (2, 2)            | (1, 0)               |
| **Farmer 1: No Exchange** | (0, 1)            | (1, 1)               |

**Justification:** 
- Mutual exchange provides reciprocal benefit, but if one party abstains, the offering party incurs a loss while the non-offering party reverts to the baseline. Formal compliance is less costly for staff but may involve a fee.

#### 5. Asymmetric Authorization-Enforcement Game (AS5)
**Tension:** Formal Cooperation vs. Opportunistic Behavior

**Matrix:**

|            | Staff 2: Authorize | Staff 2: Do Not Authorize |
|------------|--------------------|---------------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Formal Request** | (2, 2)             | (1, 0)                    |
| **Farmer 1: Informal Request** | (3, 1)             | (2, 2)                    |

**Justification:** 
- Formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but if the farmer requests formally and staff withhold, the farmer incurs a loss. If the farmer makes an informal request and staff invest, the farmer gains more while the staff bear the cost without the formal fee.

#### 6. Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)
**Tension:** Mutual Restraint vs. Short-Term Gain

**Matrix:**

|            | Farmer 2: Restrain | Farmer 2: Over-Extract |
|------------|--------------------|------------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Restrain** | (2, 2)             | (1, 4)                 |
| **Farmer 1: Over-Extract** | (4, 1)             | (0, 0)                 |

**Justification:** 
- Mutual restraint sustains groundwater yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion. If both farmers restrain, they both benefit, but if one over-extracts, the non-over-extractor bears the loss.
