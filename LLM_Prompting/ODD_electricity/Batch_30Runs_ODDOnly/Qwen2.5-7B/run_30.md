### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)

#### Title
Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game

#### Tension
Mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant, but risky due to the potential for free-riding.

#### Matrix Representation
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}
& \text{Farmer 2 adopts} & \text{Farmer 2 does not adopt} \\
\hline
\text{Farmer 1 adopts} & (3, 3) & (1, 4) \\
\text{Farmer 1 does not adopt} & (4, 1) & (2, 2) \\
\end{array}
\]

#### Justification
This game represents the situation where two neighboring farmers must decide whether to invest in capacitors to improve voltage stability. Mutual cooperation (both adopting capacitors) is Pareto-dominant, but the risk of free-riding (where one farmer benefits without contributing) makes the outcome uncertain. The payoff matrix reflects the benefits of shared improvement versus the risk of unilateral investment.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)

#### Title
Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption

#### Tension
Diffusion of capacitor adoption depends on observing a successful trial, leading to potential delays in adoption.

#### Sequential Representation (Game Tree)
1. **Farmer 1 decides whether to adopt a capacitor (C or NC)**
2. **Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1’s decision and decides whether to adopt a capacitor (C or NC) based on the outcome of Farmer 1**

- If Farmer 1 adopts (C), and the outcome is successful, Farmer 2 imitates with a probability based on observed success.
- If Farmer 1 does not adopt (NC), Farmer 2 may adopt based on the observed outcome of others.

#### Justification
This sequential game represents the process of social learning where farmers observe and imitate the outcomes of their neighbors. The uncertainty in the adoption process and the potential delay in diffusion due to the need for a successful trial are key aspects of this tension.

### Action Situation 5: Asymmetric Authorization and Investment Coordination Game (AS5)

#### Title
Asymmetric Authorization and Investment Coordination Game

#### Tension
Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but there are asymmetric incentives between legality and opportunism.

#### Matrix Representation
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}
& \text{Staff invests} & \text{Staff withholds} \\
\hline
\text{Farmer makes formal request} & (3, 2) & (2, 1) \\
\text{Farmer makes informal request} & (4, 0) & (2, 2) \\
\end{array}
\]

#### Justification
This game captures the tension between formal and informal requests for investment. Mutual formal cooperation is optimal, but there are incentives for staff to withhold investment under formal requests to save effort or cost, and for farmers to make informal requests to gain more benefits.
