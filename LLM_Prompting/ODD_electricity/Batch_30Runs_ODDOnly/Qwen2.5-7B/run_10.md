### Title: Distinct Action Situations in the Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

1. **Title: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)**
   **Tension: Coordination Dilemma**
   **Matrix:**
   ```
      | Farmer 2: Adopt | Farmer 2: Not Adopt
   ---|-----------------|----------------------
   A1 | (3, 3)          | (0, 2)
   A2 | (2, 0)          | (2, 2)
   ```
   **Justification:** Two neighboring farmers decide whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing equipment. Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit, creating a coordination problem with mutual cooperation Pareto-dominant but risky.

2. **Title: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)**
   **Tension: Sequential Dilemma**
   **Sequential Representation:**
   ```
   Farmer 1 decides whether to adopt a capacitor after observing Farmer 2's decision.
   ```
   **Justification:** Each farmer observes a peer’s outcome and imitates only if that outcome ranks higher, so diffusion occurs only after a successful coordinated trial has been observed.

3. **Title: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)**
   **Tension: Free-Rider Dilemma**
   **Matrix:**
   ```
      | Farmer 2: Authorize | Farmer 2: Not Authorize
   ---|---------------------|------------------------
   A1 | (3, 3)              | (3, 1)
   A2 | (1, 3)              | (2, 2)
   ```
   **Justification:** One farmer’s authorization or investment benefits both by raising voltage quality, but costs fall solely on the authorizer, generating a free-rider incentive and uneven payoffs.

4. **Title: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)**
   **Tension: Coordination Dilemma**
   **Matrix:**
   ```
      | Sub-station: Exchange | Sub-station: No Exchange
   ---|-----------------------|-------------------------
   A1 | (3, 3)                | (2, 2)
   A2 | (2, 2)                | (1, 1)
   ```
   **Justification:** Reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage in informal exchange; if either abstains while the other offers exchange, the offerer bears a loss while the abstainer reverts to baseline.

5. **Title: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game (AS5)**
   **Tension: Asymmetric Coordination**
   **Matrix:**
   ```
      | Sub-station: Invest | Sub-station: Withhold
   ---|--------------------|---------------------
   A1 | (3, 2)             | (2, 3)
   A2 | (2, 3)             | (3, 2)
   ```
   **Justification:** Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but if the farmer makes a formal request and the staff withhold, the farmer incurs a loss while the staff save effort or cost.

6. **Title: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)**
   **Tension: Prisoner’s Dilemma**
   **Matrix:**
   ```
      | Farmer 2: Restrain | Farmer 2: Over-Extract
   ---|--------------------|-------------------------
   A1 | (3, 3)             | (2, 4)
   A2 | (4, 2)             | (1, 1)
   ```
   **Justification:** Mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion.

These action situations reflect the core strategic tensions in the irrigation electricity governance model, grounded in the empirical and theoretical background provided.
