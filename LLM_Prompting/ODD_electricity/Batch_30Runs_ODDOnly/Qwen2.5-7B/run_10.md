# Run 10 — Qwen/Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct-Turbo

### Title: Distinct Action Situations in the Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. Farmer-Farmer Coordination: Capacitor Adoption Assurance (AS1)
**Tension:** Mutual Investment vs. Free-Riding

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Free-Rides |
|-----------|------------------|--------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | (B, B)            | (0, B)             |
| **Farmer 1 Free-Rides** | (B, 0)            | (0, 0)             |

**Justification:** Both farmers benefit from improved voltage quality when both invest in capacitors. However, if one farmer invests and the other does not, the investor incurs the full cost without additional benefit. This creates a mutual coordination problem where both must cooperate for mutual benefit, but there is a risk of free-riding.

#### 2. Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)
**Tension:** Diffusion of Successful Practices vs. Risk of Failure

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | (A, A)           | (C, 0)                 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt** | (0, C)           | (0, 0)                 |

**Justification:** Farmers observe each other’s outcomes. If a farmer observes a successful outcome (A), they adopt capacitors. If the observed outcome is unsuccessful (C), they do not adopt capacitors. This process can lead to slow diffusion of successful practices but also carries the risk of failure.

#### 3. Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)
**Tension:** Formal vs. Informal Investment

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
|-----------|------------------|--------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | (F, F)            | (0, I)                   |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | (I, 0)            | (0, 0)                   |

**Justification:** Farmer 1 incurs the full cost of transformer capacity investment. If both invest, the benefit is shared, but if only one invests, the non-investor benefits more. Formal investment incurs a fee, while informal investment incurs no fee but may result in lower enforcement.

#### 4. Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)
**Tension:** Informal vs. Formal Exchange

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Staff Invests | Staff Does Not Invest |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------------|
| **Farmer Invests** | (E, E)        | (F, N)                |
| **Farmer Does Not Invest** | (N, F)        | (0, 0)                |

**Justification:** Both parties benefit from reciprocal exchange. If both engage in formal exchange, both gain. If one engages in informal exchange and the other does not, the informal party gains more. If both abstain, no extra benefit occurs.

#### 5. Asymmetric Coordination Game (AS5)
**Tension:** Formal vs. Informal Request and Investment

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Staff Invests | Staff Does Not Invest |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------------|
| **Farmer Requests Formal** | (C, C)        | (0, D)                |
| **Farmer Requests Informal** | (D, 0)        | (E, E)                |

**Justification:** Formal investment incurs a fee, but both benefit. Informal investment incurs no fee but may result in lower enforcement. The farmer gains more under informal investment, while staff gains modestly under formal investment.

#### 6. Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)
**Tension:** Mutual Restraint vs. Unilateral Over-Extraction

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
|-----------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | (R, R)              | (S, O)                 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | (O, S)              | (0, 0)                 |

**Justification:** Mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion. If both restrain, both benefit. If one over-extracts, the over-extractor gains more, but both face depletion risk.

#### 7. Farmer-Staff Coordination (AS7)
**Tension:** Formal vs. Informal Connection

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Staff Grants Formal Connection | Staff Grants Informal Connection |
|-----------|--------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| **Farmer Requests Formal** | (A, A)                         | (B, C)                          |
| **Farmer Requests Informal** | (C, B)                         | (D, D)                          |

**Justification:** Both parties benefit from formal connections. Informal connections offer a quick fix but may face enforcement risks. The farmer gains more under informal connections, while staff gains more under formal connections.

#### 8. Transformer Capacity Investment (AS8)
**Tension:** Individual vs. Collective Investment

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
|-----------|------------------|--------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | (B, B)            | (0, A)                   |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | (A, 0)            | (0, 0)                   |

**Justification:** Both benefit from improved voltage quality when both invest, but the cost falls unevenly. If only one invests, the non-investor benefits more.

#### 9. Social Learning and Bounded Rationality (AS9)
**Tension:** Heuristic vs. Rational Decision-Making

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Farmer 2 Learns Heuristically | Farmer 2 Learns Rationally |
|-----------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Learns Heuristically** | (H, H)                       | (R, R)                      |
| **Farmer 1 Learns Rationally** | (R, H)                       | (R, R)                      |

**Justification:** Heuristic learning is faster but less accurate. Rational learning is slower but more accurate. Both benefit from rational learning, but heuristics can lead to faster adoption.

#### 10. Informal Exchange vs. Formal Compliance (AS10)
**Tension:** Reciprocal vs. Legal Benefit

**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Staff Engages in Informal Exchange | Staff Enforces Formal Rules |
|-----------|------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Farmer Engages in Informal Exchange** | (I, I)                            | (F, N)                      |
| **Farmer Enforces Formal Rules** | (N, F)                            | (C, C)                      |

**Justification:** Informal exchange offers quick benefits but may face enforcement risks. Formal compliance incurs costs but ensures legal benefits. Both parties benefit from formal compliance, but informal exchange offers quicker gains.
