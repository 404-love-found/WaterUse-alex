### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in the Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. **Farmer-Farmer Coordination for Capacitor Adoption (AS1)**
**Tension:** Mutual Cooperation vs. Free-Rider
**Matrix:**
|               | Farmer B Invests | Farmer B Does Not Invest |
|---------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer A Invests** | (5, 5)           | (1, 4)                  |
| **Farmer A Does Not Invest** | (4, 1)          | (3, 3)                  |

**Justification:** This scenario reflects the coordination dilemma where both farmers benefit from mutual capacitor investment. However, if one farmer invests and the other does not, the investing farmer incurs the cost while the non-investing farmer benefits from the improved voltage quality.

#### 2. **Farmer-Staff Authorization Dilemma (AS3)**
**Tension:** Formal Cooperation vs. Informal Exchange
**Matrix:**
|               | Staff Invests | Staff Does Not Invest |
|---------------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Farmer Requests Formal Connection** | (3, 3)        | (2, 5)               |
| **Farmer Requests Informal Connection** | (4, 2)        | (1, 1)               |

**Justification:** This situation captures the tension between formal and informal connections. Both parties benefit from formal cooperation, but informal connections allow the farmer to receive immediate benefits at the cost of staff effort and potential formal fees.

#### 3. **Farmer-Farmer Groundwater Extraction Dilemma (AS6)**
**Tension:** Mutual Restraint vs. Unilateral Over-Extraction
**Matrix:**
|               | Farmer B Restraints | Farmer B Over-Extracts |
|---------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Farmer Restraints** | (4, 4)             | (3, 5)                 |
| **Farmer Over-Extracts** | (5, 3)             | (1, 1)                 |

**Justification:** This dilemma reflects the common-pool resource extraction problem. Both farmers benefit from mutual restraint, but unilateral over-extraction provides short-term gains at the expense of long-term sustainability.

#### 4. **Farmer-Farmer Sequential Social Learning (AS2)**

#### 6. **Farmer-Farmer Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma (AS3)**
