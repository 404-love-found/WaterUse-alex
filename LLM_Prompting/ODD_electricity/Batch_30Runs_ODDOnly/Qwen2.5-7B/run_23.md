### Title: Distinct Action Situations in the Irrigation Electricity Governance Model

#### 1. Farmer-Farmer Coordination: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)
**Tension:** Mutual cooperation vs. unilateral defection

**Matrix:**

|               | Farmer 2: Adopt Capacitor | Farmer 2: Do Not Adopt Capacitor |
|---------------|---------------------------|---------------------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Adopt Capacitor** | (3, 3)                    | (0, 4)                          |
| **Farmer 1: Do Not Adopt Capacitor** | (4, 0)                    | (2, 2)                          |

**Justification:** Both farmers benefit from mutual capacitor adoption, which stabilizes voltage and reduces pumpset failure. However, unilateral adoption yields no private benefit, creating a coordination problem.

#### 2. Farmer-Staff Asymmetric Authorization Dilemma (AS3)
**Tension:** Farmer invests in transformer capacity vs. Staff enforces formal authorization

**Matrix:**

|               | Farmer: Invest | Farmer: Do Not Invest |
|---------------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Staff: Authorize** | (1, 2)        | (0, 1)               |
| **Staff: Do Not Authorize** | (3, 0)        | (2, 2)               |

**Justification:** Investing in transformer capacity benefits both parties by improving voltage quality. However, the cost of investing falls solely on the farmer, creating a free-rider incentive.

#### 3. Farmer-Staff Mutual-Exchange Coordination (AS4)
**Tension:** Farmer makes formal request vs. Staff invests in capacity vs. Farmer makes informal request vs. Staff invests in capacity

**Matrix:**

|               | Farmer: Formal Request | Farmer: Informal Request |
|---------------|-----------------------|-------------------------|
| **Staff: Invest** | (2, 2)                | (3, 1)                  |
| **Staff: Do Not Invest** | (1, 2)               | (0, 1)                  |

**Justification:** Mutual investment in transformer capacity yields reciprocal benefit, but formal cooperation involves a fee, while informal exchange offers a modest gain for the staff.

#### 4. Farmer-Farmer Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma (AS6)
**Tension:** Mutual restraint vs. Unilateral over-extraction

**Matrix:**

|               | Farmer 2: Restrain | Farmer 2: Over-Extract |
|---------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Restrain** | (2, 2)              | (0, 4)                |
| **Farmer 1: Over-Extract** | (4, 0)              | (1, 1)                |

**Justification:** Mutual restraint sustains groundwater yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain at the cost of long-term depletion.

#### 5. Farmer Social Learning: Capacitor Adoption (AS2)
**Tension:** Imitate successful peer vs. Do not imitate

**Matrix:**

|               | Imitate | Do Not Imitate |
|---------------|---------|----------------|
| **Adopt Capacitor** | (3, 3)  | (2, 2)         |
| **Do Not Adopt Capacitor** | (2, 2)  | (1, 1)         |

**Justification:** Diffusion of capacitor adoption occurs only after a successful coordinated trial is observed, reflecting bounded rationality and social learning.

#### 6. Farmer-Farmer Asymmetric Free-Rider Dilemma (AS3, Part 2)

#### 7. Farmer-Staff Asymmetric Coordination: Formal vs. Informal Requests (AS5)
