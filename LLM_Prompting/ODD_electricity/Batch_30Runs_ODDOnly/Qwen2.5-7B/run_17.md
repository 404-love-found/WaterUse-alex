# Run 17 — Qwen/Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game (AS1)

#### Title
Farmer-Farmer Coordination on Capacitor Adoption

#### Tension
Mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant, but unilateral investment does not yield private benefits, creating a coordination problem.

#### Matrix
|         | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
|---------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | (3, 3) | (2, 1) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | (1, 2) | (2, 2) |

#### Justification
- **Farmer 1 Invests, Farmer 2 Invests:** Both farmers benefit equally from improved voltage stability, leading to a payoff of (3, 3).
- **Farmer 1 Invests, Farmer 2 Does Not Invest:** Farmer 1 incurs the cost without any benefit, leading to a payoff of (2, 1).
- **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest, Farmer 2 Invests:** Farmer 2 incurs the cost without any benefit, leading to a payoff of (1, 2).
- **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest, Farmer 2 Does Not Invest:** Both farmers remain at a lower voltage quality, leading to a payoff of (2, 2).

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)

#### Title
Farmer-Farmer Free-Rider Dilemma on Transformer Upgrades

#### Tension
One farmer’s investment in transformer capacity benefits both, but the costs fall solely on the investor, creating a free-rider incentive.

#### Matrix
|         | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
|---------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | (3, 3) | (1, 3) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | (3, 1) | (2, 2) |

#### Justification
- **Farmer 1 Invests, Farmer 2 Invests:** Both farmers benefit from improved voltage quality, leading to a payoff of (3, 3).
- **Farmer 1 Invests, Farmer 2 Does Not Invest:** Farmer 1 incurs the cost without any additional benefit, leading to a payoff of (1, 3).
- **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest, Farmer 2 Invests:** Farmer 2 incurs the cost without any additional benefit, leading to a payoff of (3, 1).
- **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest, Farmer 2 Does Not Invest:** Both farmers remain at a lower voltage quality, leading to a payoff of (2, 2).

### Action Situation 3: Mutual-Exchange Coordination Game (AS4)

#### Title
Farmer-Staff Informal Exchange

#### Tension
Reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage in informal exchange.

#### Matrix
|         | Staff Invests | Staff Does Not Invest |
|---------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Farmer Invests** | (3, 3) | (1, 1) |
| **Farmer Does Not Invest** | (1, 1) | (2, 2) |

#### Justification
- **Farmer Invests, Staff Invests:** Both farmer and staff benefit from the informal exchange, leading to a payoff of (3, 3).
- **Farmer Invests, Staff Does Not Invest:** Farmer incurs the cost without any benefit, leading to a payoff of (1, 1).
- **Farmer Does Not Invest, Staff Invests:** Staff incurs the cost without any benefit, leading to a payoff of (1, 1).
- **Farmer Does Not Invest, Staff Does Not Invest:** Both revert to a baseline, leading to a payoff of (2, 2).

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)

#### Title
Farmer-Farmer Groundwater Extraction

#### Tension
Mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion.

#### Matrix
|         | Farmer 2 Extracts | Farmer 2 Does Not Extract |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| **Farmer 1 Extracts** | (1, 1) | (3, 0) |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Extract** | (0, 3) | (2, 2) |

#### Justification
- **Farmer 1 Extracts, Farmer 2 Extracts:** Both farmers extract groundwater, leading to a payoff of (1, 1) due to accelerated depletion.
- **Farmer 1 Extracts, Farmer 2 Does Not Extract:** Farmer 1 extracts groundwater, leading to a payoff of (3, 0) due to short-term gain but potential depletion.
- **Farmer 1 Does Not Extract, Farmer 2 Extracts:** Farmer 2 extracts groundwater, leading to a payoff of (0, 3) due to short-term gain but potential depletion.
- **Farmer 1 Does Not Extract, Farmer 2 Does Not Extract:** Both farmers refrain from extraction, leading to a payoff of (2, 2) due to sustained yields.
