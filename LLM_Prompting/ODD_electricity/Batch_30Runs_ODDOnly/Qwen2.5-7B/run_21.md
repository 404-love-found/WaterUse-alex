### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance (AS1)

#### Tension
**Farmer-Farmer Coordination Dilemma**: Mutual investment in voltage-stabilizing capacitors yields shared improvement, but unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

#### Matrix/Sequential Representation
**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|       | Farmer 2: Invest | Farmer 2: Not Invest |
|-------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Invest** | (5, 5) | (1, 0) |
| **Farmer 1: Not Invest** | (0, 1) | (0, 0) |

#### Justification
This represents the mutual coordination between two neighboring farmers to install capacitors. If both invest, they share the benefits equally. If only one invests, the other gains nothing, creating a free-rider problem.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process (AS2)

#### Tension
**Farmer-Farmer Coordination Dilemma**: Diffusion of capacitor adoption occurs only after a successful coordinated trial has been observed, following a sequential social-learning process.

#### Sequential Representation (Game Tree)

```
          (Farmer 1)
             |
         [Choose: Invest/Not Invest]
             |
         [Farmer 2 Observes]
             |
         [Farmer 2: Invest/Not Invest]
```

#### Justification
This sequential process reflects how farmers learn from each other’s outcomes. If a successful trial is observed, the second farmer is more likely to adopt the measure. The process is grounded in the text’s description of social learning.

### Action Situation 3: Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)

#### Tension
**Farmer-Farmer Coordination Dilemma**: One farmer’s authorization or investment benefits both by raising voltage quality, but costs fall solely on the authorizer, creating a free-rider incentive.

#### Matrix/Sequential Representation
**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|       | Farmer 2: Authorize | Farmer 2: Not Authorize |
|-------|---------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Authorize** | (3, 3) | (1, 0) |
| **Farmer 1: Not Authorize** | (0, 1) | (2, 2) |

#### Justification
This matrix shows the dilemma where one farmer bears the cost of improving voltage quality, while the other benefits. If both authorize, they share the benefits, but if only one authorizes, the other reaps the benefits at no cost.

### Action Situation 4: Mutual-Exchange Coordination (AS4)

#### Tension
**Farmer-Staff Coordination Dilemma**: Reciprocal benefit arises only when both engage in informal exchange; if either abstains, the offerer bears a loss.

#### Sequential Representation (Game Tree)

```
          (Farmer)
             |
         [Choose: Exchange/No Exchange]
             |
         [Sub-station Staff Observes]
             |
         [Sub-station Staff: Exchange/No Exchange]
```

#### Justification
This sequential process reflects the mutual exchange between farmers and sub-station personnel. If both parties engage, they benefit; if one party does not, the offerer incurs a loss.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization and Investment Asymmetric Coordination (AS5)

#### Tension
**Farmer-Staff Coordination Dilemma**: Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but unilateral actions create asymmetric incentives.

#### Matrix/Sequential Representation
**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|       | Staff: Invest | Staff: Withhold |
|-------|--------------|----------------|
| **Farmer: Formal Request** | (2, 2) | (0, 3) |
| **Farmer: Informal Request** | (3, 0) | (1, 1) |

#### Justification
This matrix shows the asymmetric incentives between legality and opportunism. Formal cooperation is optimal, but the farmer incurs a cost if the staff withholds, while the staff gains modestly.

#### Tension

#### Matrix/Sequential Representation
**Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|       | Farmer 2: Restrain | Farmer 2: Over-Extract |
|-------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Farmer 1: Restrain** | (3, 3) | (1, 4) |

This matrix reflects the dilemma where mutual restraint is beneficial, but over-extraction offers short-term gains at the cost of long-term depletion.
