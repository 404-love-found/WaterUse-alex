### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance (AS1)
#### Tension
**Coordination Problem**: Mutual investment in capacitors is necessary for effective voltage stabilization, but unilateral investment offers no private benefit, leading to a coordination dilemma.

#### Matrix
|                | Farmer A Invests | Farmer A Does Not Invest |
|----------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer B Invests** | (3, 3)          | (1, 2)                  |
| **Farmer B Does Not Invest** | (2, 1)          | (0, 0)                  |

#### Justification
- **Farmer A Invests**: Both farmers benefit equally from shared voltage stabilization, leading to a mutual benefit of 3.
- **Farmer A Does Not Invest**: If Farmer B invests, Farmer A benefits slightly from the improved voltage (1), but if both do not invest, there is no benefit (0).

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma (AS3)
#### Tension
**Free-rider Problem**: One farmer’s investment in transformer capacity benefits both, but the costs fall entirely on the investor, creating an incentive for free-riding.

#### Matrix
|                | Farmer A Invests | Farmer A Does Not Invest |
|----------------|------------------|-------------------------|
| **Farmer B Invests** | (2, 2)          | (1, 1)                  |
| **Farmer B Does Not Invest** | (1, 1)          | (0, 0)                  |

#### Justification
- **Farmer A Invests**: Both farmers benefit equally from improved voltage quality, with Farmer A bearing the full cost (2).
- **Farmer A Does Not Invest**: If Farmer B invests, both farmers benefit (1), but Farmer A does not bear any cost. If neither invests, there is no benefit (0).

### Action Situation 3: Mutual-Exchange Coordination with Staff (AS4)
#### Tension
**Reciprocity Dilemma**: Mutual benefit arises only if both a farmer and sub-station staff engage in informal exchange; otherwise, one party suffers a loss.

#### Matrix
|                | Farmer Invests | Farmer Does Not Invest |
|----------------|----------------|-----------------------|
| **Staff Invests** | (2, 2)         | (1, 0)                |
| **Staff Does Not Invest** | (0, 1)         | (0, 0)                |

#### Justification
- **Farmer Invests**: Both parties benefit (2) if the staff invests, but the farmer bears the cost. If the staff does not invest, the farmer incurs a loss (0).
- **Farmer Does Not Invest**: If the staff invests, the farmer suffers a loss (0). If the staff does not invest, there is no benefit (0).

### Action Situation 4: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma (AS6)
#### Tension
**Common Pool Resource Problem**: Mutual restraint in groundwater extraction is necessary to sustain yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion.

#### Matrix
|                | Farmer A Extracts | Farmer A Does Not Extract |
|----------------|--------------------|--------------------------|
| **Farmer B Extracts** | (-1, -1)         | (2, 0)                  |
| **Farmer B Does Not Extract** | (0, 2)         | (1, 1)                  |

#### Justification
- **Farmer A Extracts**: Both farmers benefit from mutual restraint (1), but if one extracts and the other does not, the extractor benefits (2) and the non-extractor suffers (0).
- **Farmer A Does Not Extract**: If the other farmer extracts, the non-extractor suffers (0). If both do not extract, there is a modest benefit (1).

### Action Situation 5: Asymmetric Authorization and Investment (AS5)
#### Tension
**Opportunism vs. Formal Cooperation**: Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but the formal fee creates asymmetric incentives between legality and opportunism.

#### Matrix
|                | Farmer Invests (Formal) | Farmer Invests (Informal) | Farmer Does Not Invest |
|----------------|-------------------------|---------------------------|-----------------------|
| **Staff Invests (Formal)** | (2, 2)                | (1, 1.5)                 | (0, 0)                |
| **Staff Invests (Informal)** | (1.5, 1)              | (1, 1)                   | (0, 0)                |
| **Staff Withholds** | (0, 0)                 | (0, 0)                   | (0, 0)                |

#### Justification
- **Farmer Invests (Formal)**: Both benefit from mutual formal cooperation (2), but the staff gains modestly even under formal cooperation (1.5) due to the investment burden.
- **Farmer Invests (Informal)**: Both benefit from mutual informal cooperation (1), but the staff gains more under formal cooperation (1.5).
- **Farmer Does Not Invest**: Staff withholding results in no benefit (0).

### Action Situation 6: Sequential Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption (AS2)
#### Tension
**Social Learning Process**: Diffusion of capacitor adoption occurs only after a successful coordinated trial has been observed.

#### Matrix
|                | Farmer A Imitates | Farmer A Does Not Imitate |
|----------------|--------------------|---------------------------|
| **Farmer B Adopted** | (2, 2)         | (1, 1)                   |
| **Farmer B Did Not Adopt** | (1, 1)         | (0, 0)                   |

#### Justification
- **Farmer A Imitates**: Both farmers benefit (2) if the other has successfully adopted the capacitor, but if the other does not adopt, both benefit (1).
- **Farmer A Does Not Imitate**: If the other farmer does not adopt, there is no benefit (0).
