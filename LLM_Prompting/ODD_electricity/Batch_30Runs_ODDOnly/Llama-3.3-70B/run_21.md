### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination problem between farmers to invest in voltage-stabilising equipment
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two neighbouring farmers deciding whether to invest in capacitor adoption, with mutual investment yielding shared improvement.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Imitation and diffusion of capacitor adoption among farmers
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor and observes outcome.
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome and decides to adopt or not.
   - If Farmer 1's outcome is successful, Farmer 2 adopts.
   - If Farmer 1's outcome is unsuccessful, Farmer 2 does not adopt.
#### Justification: This sequential action situation represents how farmers learn from each other's experiences with capacitor adoption.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game
#### Title: Authorization and Investment
#### Tension: Asymmetric incentives between formal and informal requests
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer makes formal or informal request.
2. Staff decides to invest or withhold capacity.
   - If Farmer makes formal request and Staff invest, outcome is (3, 2).
   - If Farmer makes formal request and Staff withhold, outcome is (1, 3).
   - If Farmer makes informal request and Staff invest, outcome is (4, 1).
   - If Farmer makes informal request and Staff do not invest, outcome is (2, 2).
#### Justification: This sequential action situation captures the asymmetric incentives between farmers and staff in terms of formal and informal requests for capacity investment.

### Action Situation 6: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Title: Groundwater Extraction
#### Tension: Conflict between short-term gain and long-term sustainability
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 3, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation represents the classic prisoner's dilemma, where individual self-interest leads to over-extraction and depletion of the groundwater resource.
