### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination Problem
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 1 Adopt | Farmer 1 Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 2 Adopt** | 3, 3 | 0, 0 |
| **Farmer 2 Not Adopt** | 0, 0 | 1, 1 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the coordination problem between two neighboring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilizing equipment (capacitors). Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Transformer Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Transformer Capacity
#### Tension: Asymmetric Free-Rider Incentive
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 1 Authorize | Farmer 1 Not Authorize |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 2 Authorize** | 2, 2 | 4, 1 |
| **Farmer 2 Not Authorize** | 1, 4 | 0, 0 |
#### Justification: This action situation represents the asymmetric transformer-capacity authorization dilemma between two farmers. One farmer's authorization benefits both but costs fall solely on the authorizer, generating a free-rider incentive.

### Action Situation 4: Authorization and Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game
#### Title: Authorization and Investment
#### Tension: Asymmetric Incentives
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer (Formal/Informal Request)
2. Staff (Invest/Withhold Capacity)
- If Farmer Formal and Staff Invest: Farmer gains 2, Staff gains 1
- If Farmer Formal and Staff Withhold: Farmer loses 1, Staff gains 0
- If Farmer Informal and Staff Invest: Farmer gains 3, Staff loses 1
- If Farmer Informal and Staff Withhold: No extra benefit
#### Justification: This action situation represents the authorization-and-investment asymmetric coordination game between a farmer and staff. Mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but asymmetric incentives exist between legality and opportunism.

### Action Situation 5: Groundwater Extraction Prisoner's Dilemma
#### Title: Groundwater Extraction
#### Tension: Unilateral Over-Extraction
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 1 Restrain | Farmer 1 Over-Extract |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 2 Restrain** | 2, 2 | 4, 1 |
| **Farmer 2 Over-Extract** | 1, 4 | 3, 3 |
#### Justification: This action situation represents the groundwater-extraction prisoner's dilemma between two farmers drawing from the same aquifer. Mutual restraint sustains yields, but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion.

### Action Situation 6: Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Social Learning
#### Tension: Imitation and Coordination
#### Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 Adopt/Not Adopt
2. Farmer 2 Observes Outcome
3. Farmer 2 Imitates/Does Not Imitate
- If Farmer 1 Adopt and Outcome is Successful: Farmer 2 Imitates
- If Farmer 1 Adopt and Outcome is Unsuccessful: Farmer 2 Does Not Imitate
- If Farmer 1 Not Adopt: No Imitation
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the social-learning process in capacitor adoption. Each farmer observes a peer's outcome and imitates only if that outcome ranks higher, leading to diffusion after a successful coordinated trial.
