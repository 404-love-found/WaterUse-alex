### Action Situation 1: Capacitor Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Mutual Investment in Voltage-Stabilising Equipment
#### Tension: Coordination problem where mutual cooperation is Pareto-dominant but risky.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 1 Invests | Farmer 1 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 2 Invests** | 3, 3 | 0, 2 |
| **Farmer 2 Does Not Invest** | 2, 0 | 1, 1 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between two neighbouring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilising equipment (capacitors). Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Imitation of Successful Peers
#### Tension: Diffusion of capacitor adoption through social learning.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor.
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome.
3. If Farmer 1's outcome is successful, Farmer 2 imitates.
#### Justification: This action situation represents the sequential process of social learning in capacitor adoption, where each farmer observes a peer's outcome and imitates only if that outcome ranks higher.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game
#### Title: Asymmetric Incentives between Legality and Opportunism
#### Tension: Staff gain modestly even under formal cooperation due to investment burden.
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer makes a formal request.
2. Staff decide to invest or withhold capacity.
3. Payoffs:
   - Farmer: -1 (loss) if staff withhold, 2 (gain) if staff invest.
   - Staff: 1 (modest gain) if farmer makes a formal request and staff invest, 0 (no gain) if farmer makes an informal request.
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the asymmetric coordination game between a farmer and staff, where mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal, but staff gain modestly even under formal cooperation due to investment burden.
