### Action Situation 1: Capacitor-Adoption Assurance Game
#### Title: Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Coordination Problem
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Adopts | Farmer 2 Does Not Adopt |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Adopts** | 3, 3 | 1, 2 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Adopt** | 2, 1 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the coordination problem between two neighbouring farmers deciding whether to invest in voltage-stabilising equipment (capacitors). Mutual investment yields shared improvement, while unilateral investment yields no added private benefit.

### Action Situation 2: Sequential Social-Learning Process in Capacitor Adoption
#### Title: Social Learning in Capacitor Adoption
#### Tension: Imitation and Coordination
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer 1 adopts capacitor or not.
2. Farmer 2 observes Farmer 1's outcome and decides to adopt or not.
   - If Farmer 1's outcome is successful, Farmer 2 adopts.
   - If Farmer 1's outcome is not successful, Farmer 2 does not adopt.
#### Justification: This sequential action situation represents the social-learning process where a farmer observes a peer's outcome and imitates only if that outcome ranks higher, leading to the diffusion of capacitor adoption.

### Action Situation 3: Asymmetric Transformer-Capacity Authorization Dilemma
#### Title: Transformer Capacity Authorization
#### Tension: Free-Rider Incentive
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Invests | Farmer 2 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Invests** | 2, 4 | 1, 3 |
| **Farmer 1 Does Not Invest** | 3, 2 | 2, 2 |
#### Justification: This action situation represents the asymmetric authorization dilemma between two farmers where one farmer's investment in transformer capacity benefits both but costs fall solely on the investor, generating a free-rider incentive.

### Action Situation 5: Authorization-and-Investment Asymmetric Coordination Game
#### Title: Formal vs. Informal Request and Investment
#### Tension: Asymmetric Incentives
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
1. Farmer decides whether to make a formal or informal request for capacity.
2. Staff decides whether to invest or withhold capacity.
   - If Farmer makes a formal request and Staff invest, both gain modestly.
   - If Farmer makes a formal request and Staff withhold, Farmer incurs a loss.
   - If Farmer makes an informal request and Staff invest, Farmer gains more.
   - If Farmer makes an informal request and Staff withhold, no extra benefit occurs.
#### Justification: This sequential action situation represents the authorization-and-investment game between a farmer and staff, where mutual formal cooperation is collectively optimal but asymmetric incentives exist.

### Action Situation 6: Groundwater-Extraction Prisoner’s Dilemma
#### Title: Groundwater Extraction
#### Tension: Unilateral Over-Extraction
#### Matrix/Sequential Representation:
|  | Farmer 2 Restraints | Farmer 2 Over-Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 Restraints** | 2, 2 | 1, 4 |
| **Farmer 1 Over-Extracts** | 4, 1 | 3, 3 |
#### Justification: This action situation represents the groundwater-extraction prisoner’s dilemma between two farmers drawing from the same aquifer, where mutual restraint sustains yields but unilateral over-extraction offers short-term gain and accelerates depletion.
