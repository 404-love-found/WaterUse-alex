### Action Situation 1: Upstream Farmer's Irrigation Decision
#### Tension: 
Over-Irrigation vs. Conservation

#### Matrix:
|  | Downstream Farmer Conserves | Downstream Farmer Over-Irrigates |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Farmer Conserves** | 8, 8 | 6, 9 |
| **Upstream Farmer Over-Irrigates** | 9, 5 | 5, 5 |

#### Justification: 
In the decentralized case, an upstream farmer's decision to irrigate affects the downstream farmer's water availability. If both conserve, they both get a moderate yield (8). If the upstream farmer over-irrigates and the downstream conserves, the upstream farmer gets a high yield (9) but the downstream farmer's yield decreases (5). If both over-irrigate, they both face reduced yields due to water scarcity (5, 5).

### Action Situation 2: Downstream Farmer's Fishing Decision
#### Tension: 
Over-Fishing vs. Conservation

#### Matrix:
|  | Upstream Farmer Conserves Water | Upstream Farmer Over-Irrigates |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream Farmer Conserves Fish** | 7, 7 | 4, 9 |
| **Downstream Farmer Over-Fishes** | 9, 4 | 5, 5 |

#### Justification: 
The downstream farmer's fishing decision is influenced by the upstream farmer's irrigation choice, affecting fish population. If the upstream farmer conserves water, allowing more fish to survive, and the downstream farmer conserves fish, both get a moderate fishing yield (7). If the downstream farmer over-fishes when the upstream conserves, the downstream gets a high yield (9) but the upstream's future fishing yield decreases (4). If the upstream over-irrigates, reducing fish population, and the downstream over-fishes, both face reduced yields (5, 5).

### Action Situation 3: Farmer's Decision to Invest in Irrigation Efficiency
#### Tension: 
Invest in Efficiency vs. Maintain Status Quo

#### Matrix:
|  | Neighbor Invests in Efficiency | Neighbor Maintains Status Quo |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Invests in Efficiency** | 9, 9 | 8, 6 |
| **Farmer Maintains Status Quo** | 6, 8 | 5, 5 |

#### Justification: 
In the decentralized setting, a farmer's decision to invest in irrigation efficiency can influence and be influenced by their neighbor's choice. If both invest, they both benefit from increased efficiency and yield (9, 9). If one invests and the other does not, the investor benefits more (8) than the non-investor (6). If neither invests, they both face lower yields due to inefficiency (5, 5).

### Action Situation 4: Response to Ecological Thresholds
#### Tension: 
Adapt to Thresholds vs. Ignore Thresholds

#### Matrix:
|  | Other Farmers Adapt to Thresholds | Other Farmers Ignore Thresholds |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Adapts to Thresholds** | 8, 8 | 5, 9 |
| **Farmer Ignores Thresholds** | 9, 5 | 4, 4 |

#### Justification: 
Farmers must decide whether to adapt their irrigation and fishing practices in response to ecological thresholds (e.g., critical water levels or fish populations). If all farmers adapt, they maintain a sustainable yield (8, 8). If one adapts and others do not, the adapter may face short-term losses (5) while non-adapters gain (9), but this is unsustainable. If all ignore thresholds, the ecosystem collapses, and all face significant losses (4, 4).
