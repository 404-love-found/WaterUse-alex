Mathematical Logic & Payoff Structures (The Rules of the Game)

===========================================================
0. TEMPORAL STRUCTURE (Sequence of Events Each Year)
===========================================================
   Phase 1 — PLANNING (Beginning of Season, April):
     Each farmer independently predicts water availability at their
     location using a weighted average of past observed water flows.
     Each farmer decides how many fields to irrigate (0 to 10),
     constrained by their prediction and their available budget.

   Phase 2 — IRRIGATION (Monthly, April to September):
     Water enters the river from upstream.
     Upstream Farmer (UF) extracts water FIRST, proportional to
     his planned number of irrigated fields.
     Remaining water flows downstream.
     Downstream Farmer (DF) extracts water from remaining flow.
     Remaining water flows into the terminal lake.
     Water stress accumulates if received water < demanded water.

   Phase 3 — FISHING (End of Year):
     Downstream Farmer (DF) accesses the fishing lake FIRST.
     Upstream Farmer (UF) accesses the lake AFTER DF.
     Each farmer attempts to catch a fixed target amount of fish
     from adult age classes (age 5+).
     Fish availability depends on lake water inflow during the year.

   Phase 4 — ECOLOGICAL UPDATE & BUDGET:
     Fish population reproduces if lake inflow during May exceeded
     the reproduction threshold. Otherwise larvae recruitment fails.
     Each farmer's budget is updated: Budget = Crop_Yield + Fish_Catch - Irrigation_Costs.

   KEY IMPLICATION: Irrigation decisions (Phase 2) determine how
   much water reaches the lake, which determines fish availability
   (Phase 3) and future fish population (Phase 4). These two
   resource systems are coupled through water flow.

===========================================================
1. STRATEGIC TENSION A: WATER WITHDRAWAL DECISIONS
   (Upstream vs Downstream Asymmetric Commons Dilemma)
===========================================================
   Players: Upstream Farmer (UF) vs Downstream Farmer (DF)
   Decision: How many fields to irrigate (0 to 10)

   PHYSICAL FLOW CONSTRAINT:
     Water_Available_for_DF = Total_Inflow - (UF_Fields * Water_Per_Field)
     Water_to_Lake = Water_Available_for_DF - (DF_Fields * Water_Per_Field)
     Water_Per_Field = 1 unit per field per month

   CROP YIELD FUNCTION:
     Yield_j = Y_max * N_fields_j * (Sum of Water_Received / Water_Demanded) / 6
     If a farmer plants more fields than available water can support,
     water stress accumulates and yield is proportionally reduced.

   SPATIAL ASYMMETRY:
     - UF extracts water FIRST and always receives the full inflow.
       UF's crop yield is NOT affected by DF's decisions.
     - DF receives only the RESIDUAL water after UF's extraction.
       DF's crop yield IS affected by UF's decisions.
     - This creates a sequential advantage: UF can guarantee his own
       crop yield regardless of DF's choice, but DF cannot.

   PAYOFF STRUCTURE:
     Each farmer's crop payoff = Yield - Irrigation_Cost
     UF payoff depends ONLY on UF's own extraction and total inflow.
     DF payoff depends on BOTH UF's extraction and DF's own extraction.

===========================================================
2. STRATEGIC TENSION B: FISH EXTRACTION
   (Common Pool Resource Game)
===========================================================
   Players: Downstream Farmer (DF) vs Upstream Farmer (UF)
   Decision: How much fish to harvest from the lake

   ACCESS ASYMMETRY:
     - DF accesses the fishing lake FIRST (closer to lake).
     - UF accesses the lake AFTER DF, and can only catch remaining fish.
     - This is the REVERSE of the water asymmetry: DF has priority
       in fishing, while UF had priority in water.

   FISH POPULATION DYNAMICS (Non-Linear):
     The fish population follows an age-structured model (12 age classes).
     Only adult fish (age 5+) can be harvested.

     REPRODUCTION THRESHOLD:
       IF lake water inflow during May >= Threshold:
          Larvae can migrate into lake, fish population is sustained.
       IF lake water inflow during May < Threshold:
          Larvae recruitment FAILS, juvenile population collapses,
          future adult fish stock declines toward zero.

     This creates a NON-LINEAR tipping point: the combined water
     extraction decisions from Tension A directly determine whether
     the fish population in Tension B can sustain itself.

   PAYOFF STRUCTURE:
     Each farmer's fish payoff = Fish_Catch (from available adult stock)
     DF tends to catch more fish (priority access).
     UF catches only what remains after DF.
     Both farmers benefit from a healthy fish stock in future years.

===========================================================
3. COUPLING BETWEEN THE TWO TENSIONS
===========================================================
   The two action situations are NOT independent. They are linked
   through the water flow:

     High total water extraction (Tension A)
       → Less water reaches the lake
       → Fish reproduction threshold may not be met
       → Fish stock declines in subsequent years (Tension B)
       → Both farmers lose future fish income

   This coupling means that a farmer's optimal irrigation strategy
   must account for the indirect effect on fish income. However,
   because farmers use past water flows (not future fish stock)
   as the basis for their decisions, the ecological feedback is
   delayed and imperfect.

===========================================================
4. BUDGET AND DECISION CONSTRAINTS
===========================================================
   - Maximum fields per farmer: 10
   - Irrigation cost: Fixed cost per field per season
   - Budget constraint: A farmer cannot irrigate more fields than
     their budget allows.
   - Decision heuristic (DV): Farmers adjust field count based on:
     (a) Whether past income was below a survival threshold
     (b) Whether past water demand was met
     (c) Available budget
   - Memory: Farmers estimate future water using a weighted average
     of past observations, with memory parameter delta controlling
     how strongly recent years are weighted.
