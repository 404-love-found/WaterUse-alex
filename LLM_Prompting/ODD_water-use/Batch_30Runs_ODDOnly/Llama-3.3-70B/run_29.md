### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations

#### 1. Upstream Farmer's Irrigation Decision
**Title:** Upstream Irrigation Dilemma
**Tension:** Balancing individual water usage with potential downstream impacts
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream Farmer Cooperates (Conserves Water) | Downstream Farmer Defects (Over-uses Water) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Farmer Cooperates (Conserves Water)** | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 3, Downstream: 6 |
| **Upstream Farmer Defects (Over-uses Water)** | Upstream: 6, Downstream: 3 | Upstream: 4, Downstream: 4 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, an upstream farmer's decision to irrigate more fields affects the water available downstream. If both farmers conserve water, they both get a moderate benefit (5 units). If the upstream farmer over-uses water and the downstream farmer conserves, the upstream farmer gets more benefit (6 units) at the expense of the downstream farmer (3 units). If both over-use water, they both suffer from reduced water availability (4 units each).

#### 2. Downstream Farmer's Response to Upstream Water Usage
**Title:** Downstream Response Dilemma
**Tension:** Deciding whether to adapt to reduced water flow or to over-use the remaining water
**Matrix:**

|  | Upstream Farmer Cooperates (Conserves Water) | Upstream Farmer Defects (Over-uses Water) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream Farmer Adapts (Conserves Water)** | Downstream: 5, Upstream: 5 | Downstream: 2, Upstream: 6 |
| **Downstream Farmer Over-uses Water** | Downstream: 6, Upstream: 3 | Downstream: 3, Upstream: 4 |

**Justification:** The downstream farmer must respond to the upstream farmer's water usage. If the upstream farmer conserves water and the downstream farmer also conserves, both get a moderate benefit (5 units). If the upstream farmer over-uses water, the downstream farmer must decide whether to adapt to the reduced flow (getting 2 units) or to over-use the remaining water (getting 3 units).

#### 3. Farmer's Decision to Invest in Irrigation vs. Fishing
**Title:** Irrigation vs. Fishing Dilemma
**Tension:** Balancing investment in irrigation with potential fishing benefits
**Matrix:**

|  | Farmer Invests in Fishing | Farmer Invests in Irrigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Invests in Fishing** | Fishing: 4, Irrigation: 2 | Fishing: 3, Irrigation: 5 |
| **Farmer Invests in Irrigation** | Fishing: 2, Irrigation: 5 | Fishing: 1, Irrigation: 6 |

**Justification:** Farmers must decide how to allocate their resources between irrigation and fishing. Investing in fishing provides a moderate benefit (4 units) if the other farmer also invests in fishing, but a lower benefit (3 units) if the other farmer invests in irrigation. Investing in irrigation provides a higher benefit (5-6 units) but may lead to over-reliance on a single resource.

#### 4. Ecological Threshold Dilemma
**Title:** Water Usage and Ecological Threshold
**Tension:** Balancing water usage with the risk of exceeding ecological thresholds
**Matrix:**

|  | Farmer Conserves Water (Below Threshold) | Farmer Exceeds Threshold |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Conserves Water (Below Threshold)** | Farmer: 5, Environment: 5 | Farmer: 2, Environment: 0 |
| **Farmer Exceeds Threshold** | Farmer: 6, Environment: 0 | Farmer: 0, Environment: 0 |

**Justification:** Farmers must balance their water usage with the risk of exceeding ecological thresholds, which can have catastrophic consequences for the environment and their own livelihoods. Conserving water and staying below the threshold provides a moderate benefit (5 units) for both the farmer and the environment. Exceeding the threshold leads to severe consequences (0 units) for both.
