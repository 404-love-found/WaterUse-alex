### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations

#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may irrigate more fields, reducing water availability for downstream farmers.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream Cooperates | Downstream Defects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Cooperates** | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 3 |
| **Upstream Defects** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 2 | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 1 |

**Justification:** The matrix reflects the strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers. If both cooperate (irrigate fewer fields), they both receive a moderate payoff (5). If the upstream farmer defects (irrigates more fields), they receive a higher payoff (8), while the downstream farmer's payoff decreases (2 or 1).

#### 2. Fishing Exploitation Dilemma
**Tension:** Farmers may overfish, reducing the fish population and affecting future catches.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers Cooperate | Other Farmers Defect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Cooperates** | Farmer: 4, Other: 4 | Farmer: 2, Other: 6 |
| **Farmer Defects** | Farmer: 6, Other: 2 | Farmer: 3, Other: 3 |

**Justification:** The matrix reflects the strategic tension between farmers regarding fishing. If all farmers cooperate (catch fewer fish), they receive a moderate payoff (4). If a farmer defects (catches more fish), they receive a higher payoff (6), while other farmers' payoffs decrease (2).

#### 3. Water Conservation vs. Field Expansion Dilemma
**Tension:** Farmers must balance water conservation with the desire to expand their irrigated fields.
**Matrix:**

|  | Conserve Water | Expand Fields |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Conserve Water** | Farmer: 5, Environment: 5 | Farmer: 3, Environment: 2 |
| **Expand Fields** | Farmer: 8, Environment: 1 | Farmer: 6, Environment: 0 |

**Justification:** The matrix reflects the strategic tension between farmers' economic interests and environmental concerns. If a farmer conserves water, they receive a moderate payoff (5), and the environment also benefits (5). If a farmer expands their fields, they receive a higher payoff (8), but the environment suffers (1).

#### 4. Ecological Threshold Dilemma
**Tension:** Farmers' actions may push the ecosystem beyond ecological thresholds, affecting the fish population and water quality.
**Matrix:**

|  | Respect Thresholds | Ignore Thresholds |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Respect Thresholds** | Farmer: 4, Ecosystem: 4 | Farmer: 2, Ecosystem: 1 |
| **Ignore Thresholds** | Farmer: 6, Ecosystem: 0 | Farmer: 0, Ecosystem: 0 |

**Justification:** The matrix reflects the strategic tension between farmers' short-term gains and long-term ecological sustainability. If a farmer respects ecological thresholds, they receive a moderate payoff (4), and the ecosystem remains healthy (4). If a farmer ignores thresholds, they may receive a higher payoff (6) in the short term, but the ecosystem collapses (0), affecting future payoffs.
