### Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Version (DV) of the Water Use Model

#### Title: Strategic Dilemma of Water Allocation and Fish Harvesting

#### Tension: Balancing Water Allocation and Fish Harvesting

**Justification:**
In the decentralized version (DV), each farmer must decide how many fields to irrigate and how much to fish, balancing their immediate financial gains with the long-term sustainability of the water and fish resources. This situation captures the strategic tension between short-term economic interests and the need to preserve resources for future use.

#### 2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|                | Downstream Farmer (Fish Harvesting) | Upstream Farmer (Fish Harvesting) |
|----------------|-------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Upstream Farmer (Water Allocation)** | Downstream Farmer: Low Harvest, Upstream Farmer: Low Allocation | Downstream Farmer: High Harvest, Upstream Farmer: Low Allocation |
| **Upstream Farmer: Low Allocation**  | (5, 5)                               | (3, 7)                            |
| **Upstream Farmer: High Allocation** | (7, 3)                               | (4, 4)                            |

**Justification:**
- **Low Allocation, Low Harvest**: If both farmers allocate fewer fields for irrigation, they can focus more on fishing. Both benefit from high fish harvests, leading to a payoff of (5, 5).
- **Low Allocation, High Harvest**: If the upstream farmer allocates fewer fields for irrigation, they can allow more water to flow downstream, enabling the downstream farmer to harvest more fish. The downstream farmer benefits, but the upstream farmer gains less from fishing, leading to a payoff of (3, 7).
- **High Allocation, Low Harvest**: If the upstream farmer allocates more fields for irrigation, they reduce the amount of water available for fishing. The downstream farmer benefits less from fishing, but the upstream farmer can still allocate more fields, leading to a payoff of (7, 3).
- **High Allocation, High Harvest**: If both farmers allocate more fields for irrigation, they reduce the amount of water available for fishing. Both benefit from moderate fishing, leading to a payoff of (4, 4).

#### Tension: Water Allocation and Ecological Sustainability

**Justification:**
