### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Version (DV) Using the IAD Framework

#### 1. Strategic Tension: Spatial Asymmetry (Upstream vs Downstream)

**Title:** Spatial Asymmetry in Irrigation Decisions

**Tension:** Upstream farmers can benefit from higher water availability, but downstream farmers are more constrained by water scarcity.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Upstream Irrigates 10 | Upstream Irrigates 9 |
|----------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Downstream Irrigates 10** | (10, 10) | (10, 9) |
| **Downstream Irrigates 9**  | (9, 10) | (9, 9) |

**Justification:** In the decentralized version, each farmer decides independently on the number of fields to irrigate. Upstream farmers, being closer to the water source, have higher water availability. Downstream farmers are more constrained. If both irrigate 10 fields, both get the maximum benefit. However, if one irrigates 9 fields and the other 10, the downstream farmer is at a disadvantage due to reduced water availability. The matrix reflects the strategic trade-off between maximizing individual benefit and considering the downstream impact.

#### 2. Strategic Tension: Ecological Thresholds (Tipping Points)

**Title:** Ecological Thresholds in Fish Population Dynamics

**Tension:** The fish population is vulnerable to overfishing and water stress, leading to potential collapse if not managed sustainably.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Upstream Catches 20 Fish | Upstream Catches 10 Fish |
|----------------|-------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Downstream Catches 20 Fish** | (20, 20) | (20, 10) |
| **Downstream Catches 10 Fish**  | (10, 20) | (10, 10) |

**Justification:** The fish population is modeled with age-structured dynamics. If both upstream and downstream farmers overfish, the fish population may reach a tipping point and collapse. If one farmer catches 20 fish and the other 10, the total catch is sustainable, ensuring the fish population remains stable. However, if both overfish, the fish population may collapse, leading to a negative payoff for both. The matrix reflects the strategic decision to balance individual fishing yield with the ecological health of the fish population.

#### 3. Strategic Tension: Resource Scarcity and Budget Constraints

**Title:** Resource Scarcity and Budget Constraints in Irrigation Decisions

**Tension:** Farmers must balance the number of fields to irrigate with their budget constraints, considering the uncertainty of water availability.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Upstream Irrigates 8 Fields | Upstream Irrigates 7 Fields |
|----------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Downstream Irrigates 8 Fields** | (8, 8) | (8, 7) |
| **Downstream Irrigates 7 Fields**  | (7, 8) | (7, 7) |

**Justification:** In the decentralized version, farmers decide independently on the number of fields to irrigate, constrained by their budget and the uncertainty of water availability. If both farmers irrigate 8 fields, they can achieve a balanced outcome. However, if one irrigates 8 fields and the other 7, the downstream farmer is at a disadvantage due to reduced water availability. The matrix reflects the strategic decision to optimize the number of irrigated fields within budget constraints while considering the impact on water availability.

These action situations capture the unique strategic tensions in the decentralized version of the model, reflecting spatial asymmetry, ecological thresholds, and resource scarcity.
