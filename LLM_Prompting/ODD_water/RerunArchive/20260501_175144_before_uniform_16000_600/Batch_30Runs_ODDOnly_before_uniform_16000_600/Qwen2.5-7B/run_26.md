### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Case (DV)

#### Title: Strategic Tensions in a Decentralized Water Use Model

#### Tension 1: Risk and Reward in Water Allocation

**Justification:**
In the decentralized version, farmers have to decide whether to take a risk and increase the number of irrigated fields based on their income situation and water flow predictions. Downstream farmers have a greater risk due to limited water availability, while upstream farmers have more flexibility.

**Action Situations:**
- Upstream Farmer (U) decides to irrigate 9 fields.
- Upstream Farmer (U) decides to irrigate 10 fields.
- Downstream Farmer (D) decides to irrigate 9 fields.
- Downstream Farmer (D) decides to irrigate 10 fields.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Downstream Farmer (D) Irrigates 9 Fields | Downstream Farmer (D) Irrigates 10 Fields |
|----------------|-----------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Upstream Farmer (U) Irrigates 9 Fields** | (5, 5)                                  | (4, 6)                                    |
| **Upstream Farmer (U) Irrigates 10 Fields** | (6, 4)                                  | (3, 3)                                    |

**Explanation:**
- If both farmers irrigate 9 fields, they both benefit from a balanced water distribution, resulting in a payoff of (5, 5).
- If both farmers irrigate 10 fields, the water distribution is strained, leading to a lower payoff for both, (3, 3).
- If the downstream farmer irrigates 10 fields and the upstream farmer irrigates 9 fields, there is a higher risk for the downstream farmer, leading to a lower payoff for both, (4, 6).
- If the upstream farmer irrigates 10 fields and the downstream farmer irrigates 9 fields, the upstream farmer takes a risk and ends up with a lower payoff for both, (6, 4).
