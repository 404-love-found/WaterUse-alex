### Analysis of Strategic Tensions in the Decentralized Version (DV) of the Water Use Model Using the IAD Framework

#### 1. Strategic Tension: Spatial Asymmetry (Upstream vs Downstream)

**Title:** Spatial Asymmetry in Water Allocation

**Tension:** Upstream farmers have less water availability and thus may face more water stress compared to downstream farmers. However, upstream farmers have the first opportunity to allocate water, which can lead to a strategic advantage in securing more water resources.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Downstream Farmer Irrigates Fewer Fields | Downstream Farmer Irrigates More Fields |
|-----------|------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Upstream Farmer Irrigates Fewer Fields** | (1, 2)                                   | (0.5, 1.5)                             |
| **Upstream Farmer Irrigates More Fields** | (1.5, 0.5)                               | (0, 0)                                  |

**Justification:**
- **Upstream Farmer Irrigates Fewer Fields:**
  - If both farmers irrigate fewer fields, the upstream farmer has a slight advantage in securing more water for their fields due to the sequential allocation process. Hence, they get a payoff of 1, while the downstream farmer gets 2.
  - If the downstream farmer irrigates more fields and the upstream farmer irrigates fewer fields, the downstream farmer will face water stress, and the upstream farmer will get a higher payoff of 1.5 due to having the first opportunity to allocate water.
- **Upstream Farmer Irrigates More Fields:**
  - If both farmers irrigate more fields, they both face water stress, leading to a payoff of 0 for both.
  - If the downstream farmer irrigates fewer fields and the upstream farmer irrigates more fields, the downstream farmer will get a payoff of 0.5, while the upstream farmer will get 0 due to water stress.

#### 2. Strategic Tension: Ecological Thresholds (Tipping Points)

**Title:** Ecological Thresholds in Fish Population Dynamics

**Tension:** The fish population growth is sensitive to the water inflow, which can lead to a tipping point where the fish population collapses if the water inflow falls below a critical threshold.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Downstream Farmer Catches Fewer Fish | Downstream Farmer Catches More Fish |
|-----------|-------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Upstream Farmer Catches Fewer Fish** | (1, 1)                               | (0.5, 0.5)                        |
| **Upstream Farmer Catches More Fish** | (0.5, 0.5)                           | (0, 0)                            |

**Justification:**
- **Upstream Farmer Catches Fewer Fish:**
  - If both farmers catch fewer fish, the fish population is more likely to remain stable, leading to a payoff of 1 for both.
  - If the downstream farmer catches more fish and the upstream farmer catches fewer fish, the fish population is more likely to be stressed, leading to a lower payoff of 0.5 for both.
- **Upstream Farmer Catches More Fish:**
  - If both farmers catch more fish, the fish population is likely to be stressed, leading to a payoff of 0 for both.
  - If the downstream farmer catches fewer fish and the upstream farmer catches more fish, the fish population is more likely to be stable, leading to a lower payoff of 0.5 for both.

#### 3. Strategic Tension: Water Stress and Yield

**Title:** Water Stress and Yield

**Tension:** Water stress due to insufficient water allocation can lead to lower yields for farmers, but over-irrigation can also lead to water stress downstream, creating a trade-off.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Downstream Farmer Irrigates Fewer Fields | Downstream Farmer Irrigates More Fields |
|-----------|------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| **Upstream Farmer Irrigates Fewer Fields** | (2, 2)                                   | (1, 1)                                 |
| **Upstream Farmer Irrigates More Fields** | (1, 1)                                   | (0.5, 0.5)                             |

**Justification:**
- **Upstream Farmer Irrigates Fewer Fields:**
  - If both farmers irrigate fewer fields, the water stress is lower, leading to higher yields for both, with a payoff of 2 for both.
  - If the downstream farmer irrigates more fields and the upstream farmer irrigates fewer fields, the downstream farmer will face water stress, leading to a lower payoff of 1 for both.
- **Upstream Farmer Irrigates More Fields:**
  - If both farmers irrigate more fields, the water stress is higher, leading to lower yields for both, with a payoff of 0.5 for both.
  - If the downstream farmer irrigates fewer fields and the upstream farmer irrigates more fields, the downstream farmer will face lower water stress, leading to a higher payoff of 1 for both.

These matrices reflect the strategic tensions and the unique interactions between the farmers in the decentralized version of the water use model, considering spatial asymmetry, ecological thresholds, and water stress.
