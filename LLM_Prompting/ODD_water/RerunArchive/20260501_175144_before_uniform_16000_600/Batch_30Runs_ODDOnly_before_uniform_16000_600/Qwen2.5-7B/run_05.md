### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Case (DV)

#### Title: Decentralized Water Use Model - Strategic Tensions

#### 1. Action Situation: Upstream vs Downstream Farmers' Irrigation Decisions

**Tension:** **Spatial Asymmetry vs Ecological Thresholds**

**Justification:**
- **Spatial Asymmetry:** Upstream farmers have more water available initially but face the risk of water stress as the water flow decreases downstream.
- **Ecological Thresholds:** There is an ecological threshold on water flow below which fish survival and yield are severely affected.

**Explanation:**
- **Payoff (U, D):** The first number is the payoff for Upstream (U), and the second is for Downstream (D).
- **Payoff Structure:** Upstream farmers gain more if they irrigate fewer fields, reducing the downstream water requirement. Downstream farmers benefit from more fields irrigated upstream, but face ecological thresholds if water is too low.
- **Strategic Tension:** Upstream farmers must balance their immediate gains with the potential ecological damage downstream, while downstream farmers must balance their need for more fields with the risk of ecological collapse.
