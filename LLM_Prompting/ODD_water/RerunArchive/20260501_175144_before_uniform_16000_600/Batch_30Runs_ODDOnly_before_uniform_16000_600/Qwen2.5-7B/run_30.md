### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Version (DV) of the Water Use Model Using the IAD Framework

#### 1. Strategic Tension: Spatial Asymmetry (Upstream vs Downstream)

**Title: Spatial Asymmetry - Upstream vs Downstream**

**Tension:** 
Upstream and downstream farmers have different incentives and constraints due to their varying access to water resources and the cumulative effects of water withdrawal. Upstream farmers are first to withdraw water, potentially facing water stress if water is insufficient, while downstream farmers may have more water available but face reduced yields if upstream farmers have already withdrawn a significant amount of water.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| Upstream Farmer | Downstream Farmer |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Irrigate 1 Field** | **Irrigate 1 Field** | **Irrigate 2 Fields** |
| **Irrigate 1 Field** | (10, 10) | (8, 12) |
| **Irrigate 2 Fields** | (12, 8) | (9, 9) |

**Justification:**
- **Upstream Farmer (U):** Irrigating 1 field yields a higher payoff (10) if the downstream farmer also irrigates 1 field, as there is more water available. However, if the downstream farmer irrigates 2 fields, the payoff drops to 8 due to reduced water availability.
- **Downstream Farmer (D):** Irrigating 1 field yields a higher payoff (12) if the upstream farmer irrigates 2 fields, as there is more water available. However, if the upstream farmer irrigates 1 field, the payoff drops to 8 due to reduced water availability.

The matrix reflects the spatial asymmetry where both farmers face a strategic dilemma in managing their water resources based on their location.

#### 2. Strategic Tension: Ecological Thresholds - Tipping Points

**Title: Ecological Thresholds - Tipping Points**

**Tension:** 
The fish population growth is modeled with ecological thresholds, where the survival of fish in juvenile age classes is density-dependent, and adult fish are subject to density-independent mortality. The ecological thresholds can be reached if the water inflow is insufficient or excessive, leading to a tipping point in the fish population.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| Upstream Farmer | Downstream Farmer |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Irrigate 1 Field** | **Irrigate 1 Field** | **Irrigate 2 Fields** |
| **Irrigate 1 Field** | (10, 10) | (5, 15) |
| **Irrigate 2 Fields** | (15, 5) | (8, 8) |

**Justification:**
- **Upstream Farmer (U):** Irrigating 1 field yields a higher payoff (10) if the downstream farmer also irrigates 1 field, as there is more water available. However, if the downstream farmer irrigates 2 fields, the payoff drops to 5 due to the tipping point in the fish population.
- **Downstream Farmer (D):** Irrigating 1 field yields a higher payoff (15) if the upstream farmer irrigates 2 fields, as there is more water available. However, if the upstream farmer irrigates 1 field, the payoff drops to 5 due to the tipping point in the fish population.

The matrix reflects the ecological thresholds where both farmers face a strategic dilemma in managing their water resources to avoid the tipping point in the fish population.

#### 3. Strategic Tension: Budget Constraints

**Title: Budget Constraints**

**Tension:** 
Farmers have a limited budget to irrigate their fields, and they must balance their water use with their financial resources. Each farmer has a critical income threshold below which they are more willing to risk increasing their water use.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| Upstream Farmer | Downstream Farmer |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Irrigate 1 Field** | **Irrigate 1 Field** | **Irrigate 2 Fields** |
| **Irrigate 1 Field** | (10, 10) | (8, 12) |
| **Irrigate 2 Fields** | (12, 8) | (9, 9) |

**Justification:**
- **Upstream Farmer (U):** Irrigating 1 field yields a higher payoff (10) if the downstream farmer also irrigates 1 field, as there is more water available. However, if the downstream farmer irrigates 2 fields, the payoff drops to 8 due to the budget constraints.
- **Downstream Farmer (D):** Irrigating 1 field yields a higher payoff (12) if the upstream farmer irrigates 2 fields, as there is more water available. However, if the upstream farmer irrigates 1 field, the payoff drops to 8 due to the budget constraints.

The matrix reflects the budget constraints where both farmers face a strategic dilemma in managing their water use to avoid exceeding their financial resources.
