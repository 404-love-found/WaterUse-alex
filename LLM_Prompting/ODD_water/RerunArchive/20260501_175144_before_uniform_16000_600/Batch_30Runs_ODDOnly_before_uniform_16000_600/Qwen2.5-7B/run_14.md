### Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Version (DV) of the Model

#### 1. **Strategic Tension: Risk-Avoidance vs. Risk-Taking**

**Title:** Farmer's Decision to Increase or Maintain Irrigated Fields

**Tension:** Downstream farmers have a higher risk tolerance compared to upstream farmers. Downstream farmers can afford to take more risks and increase the number of irrigated fields, hoping for more water availability, while upstream farmers prefer to maintain or reduce the number of irrigated fields to avoid potential water stress and maintain stability.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                   | Upstream Farmer (Maintain) | Upstream Farmer (Increase) |
|-------------------|----------------------------|----------------------------|
| Downstream Farmer (Maintain) | (10, 10)                   | (8, 12)                    |
| Downstream Farmer (Increase) | (12, 8)                    | (9, 9)                     |

**Justification:**
- **Downstream Farmer (Maintain):** If both farmers maintain, they both achieve a stable outcome.
- **Downstream Farmer (Increase):** If the downstream farmer increases while the upstream farmer maintains, the downstream farmer benefits from increased yields, but the upstream farmer suffers from reduced water availability.
- **Upstream Farmer (Maintain):** If the upstream farmer maintains and the downstream farmer increases, the downstream farmer benefits, but the upstream farmer avoids potential water stress.
- **Upstream Farmer (Increase):** If both farmers increase, both face a higher risk of water stress, leading to a lower but shared outcome.

#### 2. **Strategic Tension: Water Stress vs. Optimal Irrigation**

**Title:** Farmer's Response to Water Stress

**Tension:** Farmers face a decision on whether to reduce the number of irrigated fields to avoid water stress or to maintain the current number of fields and risk reduced yields.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                   | Reduce Fields | Maintain Fields |
|-------------------|---------------|-----------------|
| No Water Stress   | (10, 10)      | (7, 7)          |
| Water Stress      | (5, 5)        | (8, 8)          |

**Justification:**
- **No Water Stress:** If both farmers manage to avoid water stress, they both achieve a stable outcome.
- **Water Stress:** If water stress occurs, both farmers face reduced yields, but maintaining the fields may result in a slightly higher yield compared to reducing them.

#### 3. **Strategic Tension: Ecological Thresholds vs. Economic Stability**

**Title:** Fish Population Impact on Farmer's Decision

**Tension:** The health of the fish population can lead to ecological thresholds where the fish population becomes too low to sustain fishing, forcing farmers to reduce their reliance on fishing.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                   | High Fish Pop. | Low Fish Pop. |
|-------------------|----------------|---------------|
| High Fishing      | (15, 15)       | (5, 5)        |
| Low Fishing       | (10, 10)       | (12, 12)      |

**Justification:**
- **High Fish Pop.:** If the fish population is healthy, both farmers can benefit from high yields from fishing.
- **Low Fish Pop.:** If the fish population is low, both farmers face reduced yields from fishing, but they can still benefit from agricultural activities. Reducing fishing can help the fish population recover, allowing for higher yields in the future.

### Conclusion
The above action situations reflect the strategic tensions faced by farmers in the decentralized version of the model, considering spatial asymmetry, ecological thresholds, and the max fields constraint.
