### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations for the Decentralized Case (DV) Using the IAD Framework

#### 1. Strategic Tension: Water Allocation vs. Economic Safety

**Title:** Water Allocation vs. Economic Safety

**Tension:** Downstream farmers have to decide whether to risk increasing the number of irrigated fields based on uncertain water availability, or to play it safe and irrigate fewer fields to ensure a stable income.

**Justification:** This tension reflects the spatial asymmetry where downstream farmers have less water availability and thus need to be more cautious with their water use to maintain their income. Upstream farmers, having more water available, can afford to take more risks.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Downstream Farmer (Risk) | Downstream Farmer (Safe) |
|----------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Upstream Farmer (Risk)** | (3, 3) | (4, 2) |
| **Upstream Farmer (Safe)** | (2, 4) | (5, 5) |

**Explanation:**
- **(3, 3):** Both farmers take the risk and irrigate more fields, but due to limited water, both suffer a moderate loss.
- **(4, 2):** Upstream farmer takes the risk, downstream farmer plays it safe. Upstream farmer gains more due to better water availability, downstream farmer loses.
- **(2, 4):** Upstream farmer plays it safe, downstream farmer takes the risk. Downstream farmer gains more due to better water availability, upstream farmer loses.
- **(5, 5):** Both farmers play it safe, both maintain a stable income.

#### 2. Strategic Tension: Water Allocation vs. Ecological Sustainability

**Title:** Water Allocation vs. Ecological Sustainability

**Tension:** Farmers must balance their need for water to sustain their crops and livelihoods with the ecological threshold of the fish population, which requires a minimum water flow to survive.

**Justification:** This tension highlights the ecological threshold where too much water extraction can lead to the collapse of the fish population, affecting the downstream farmers who depend on fishing.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Downstream Farmer (Risk) | Downstream Farmer (Safe) |
|----------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Upstream Farmer (Risk)** | (3, 3) | (4, 2) |
| **Upstream Farmer (Safe)** | (2, 4) | (5, 5) |

**Explanation:**
- **(3, 3):** Both farmers take the risk and irrigate more fields, but due to over-extraction, both suffer a moderate loss.
- **(4, 2):** Upstream farmer takes the risk, downstream farmer plays it safe. Upstream farmer gains more due to better water availability, but downstream farmer's fish population suffers.
- **(2, 4):** Upstream farmer plays it safe, downstream farmer takes the risk. Downstream farmer gains more due to better water availability, but upstream farmer's fish population suffers.
- **(5, 5):** Both farmers play it safe, both maintain a stable income and the fish population is sustainable.

#### 3. Strategic Tension: Water Allocation vs. Spatial Asymmetry

**Title:** Water Allocation vs. Spatial Asymmetry

**Tension:** Farmers at different locations along the river stretch face different water availability, leading to different decisions on water allocation that affect their yields and the downstream farmers.

**Justification:** This tension reflects the spatial asymmetry where farmers closer to the source have more water available and can afford to irrigate more fields, while downstream farmers have less water and must be more cautious.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Downstream Farmer (Risk) | Downstream Farmer (Safe) |
|----------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Upstream Farmer (Risk)** | (3, 3) | (4, 2) |
| **Upstream Farmer (Safe)** | (2, 4) | (5, 5) |

**Explanation:**
- **(3, 3):** Both farmers take the risk and irrigate more fields, but due to limited water, both suffer a moderate loss.
- **(4, 2):** Upstream farmer takes the risk, downstream farmer plays it safe. Upstream farmer gains more due to better water availability, downstream farmer loses.
- **(2, 4):** Upstream farmer plays it safe, downstream farmer takes the risk. Downstream farmer gains more due to better water availability, upstream farmer loses.
- **(5, 5):** Both farmers play it safe, both maintain a stable income.

These action situations reflect the strategic dilemmas faced by farmers in the decentralized version of the model, considering the spatial asymmetry and ecological thresholds.
