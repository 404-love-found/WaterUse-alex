### Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Case (DV)

#### 1. Strategic Tension: **Upstream vs Downstream Farmers' Irrigation Decisions**

**Title:** Upstream-Downstream Conflict in Irrigation Decisions

**Tension:** Upstream farmers have to balance their irrigation needs with the downstream farmers' access to water, which is crucial for the downstream farmers' ability to fish. If upstream farmers irrigate too much, it can lead to a water shortage for downstream farmers, affecting their fishing activities and income.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Downstream Irrigates Less | Downstream Irrigates More |
|----------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Upstream Irrigates Less** | (3, 3) | (1, 5) |
| **Upstream Irrigates More** | (5, 1) | (2, 2) |

**Justification:**
- **(Upstream Irrigates Less, Downstream Irrigates Less):** Both farmers have a moderate income, but neither is maximizing their potential.
- **(Upstream Irrigates Less, Downstream Irrigates More):** Downstream farmers have more income, but upstream farmers have less.
- **(Upstream Irrigates More, Downstream Irrigates Less):** Upstream farmers have more income, but downstream farmers have less.
- **(Upstream Irrigates More, Downstream Irrigates More):** Both farmers have a low income due to water scarcity.

#### 2. Strategic Tension: **Risk Aversion vs Risk Tolerance in Irrigation Decisions**

**Title:** Risk Aversion vs Risk Tolerance in Irrigation

**Tension:** Farmers with lower income are more likely to be risk-averse and avoid increasing their irrigated fields, while those with higher income may be more willing to take risks in the hope of increasing their income.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Higher Income Farmer Irrigates More | Higher Income Farmer Irrigates Less |
|----------------|-------------------------------------|------------------------------------|
| **Lower Income Farmer Irrigates More** | (2, 2) | (4, 1) |
| **Lower Income Farmer Irrigates Less** | (1, 4) | (3, 3) |

**Justification:**
- **(Lower Income Farmer Irrigates More, Higher Income Farmer Irrigates More):** Both farmers have a moderate income, but neither is maximizing their potential.
- **(Lower Income Farmer Irrigates More, Higher Income Farmer Irrigates Less):** Lower income farmer has a low income, while higher income farmer has a moderate income.
- **(Lower Income Farmer Irrigates Less, Higher Income Farmer Irrigates More):** Higher income farmer has a high income, while lower income farmer has a moderate income.
- **(Lower Income Farmer Irrigates Less, Higher Income Farmer Irrigates Less):** Both farmers have a moderate income, but neither is maximizing their potential.

#### 3. Strategic Tension: **Ecological Tipping Points in Fish Population Dynamics**

**Title:** Environmental Tipping Point in Fish Population

**Tension:** The fish population growth is highly dependent on the amount of water inflow into the lake. If the water inflow is below a critical threshold, the fish population can collapse, leading to a significant loss of income for the farmers.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Water Inflow Exceeds Threshold | Water Inflow Below Threshold |
|----------------|-------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Fish Population Survives** | (4, 4) | (2, 2) |
| **Fish Population Dies** | (1, 1) | (3, 3) |

**Justification:**
- **(Water Inflow Exceeds Threshold, Fish Population Survives):** Both farmers have a high income due to successful fish population growth.
- **(Water Inflow Exceeds Threshold, Fish Population Dies):** Both farmers have a low income due to the collapse of the fish population.
- **(Water Inflow Below Threshold, Fish Population Survives):** Both farmers have a low income, but the fish population survives.
- **(Water Inflow Below Threshold, Fish Population Dies):** Both farmers have a very low income, as the fish population collapses.

These action situations capture the strategic tensions in the decentralized water use model, reflecting the spatial asymmetry and ecological thresholds.
