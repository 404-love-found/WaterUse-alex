### Title: Strategic Tensions in Decentralized Water Use Model (DV)

#### 1. Strategic Tension: **Water Allocation vs. Fish Population Balance**

**Tension:**
In the decentralized version (DV), each farmer must decide whether to allocate more water to their fields (to potentially increase yields and income) or to maintain water levels for the fish population (to ensure sustainable fish growth and avoid ecological thresholds).

**Justification:**
Upstream farmers have a direct impact on downstream farmers' access to water and the fish population. If upstream farmers use more water, it can lead to water stress for downstream farmers and reduce fish populations, which can affect downstream farmers' fishing yields.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                  | Upstream Farmer Allocates More Water | Upstream Farmer Allocates Less Water |
|------------------|--------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Downstream Farmer Allocates More Water** | (5, 5) - Both farmers benefit from higher water levels, but the fish population may be stressed. | (7, 3) - Upstream farmer benefits more as they have more water for irrigation, but downstream farmer's fish population is at risk. |
| **Downstream Farmer Allocates Less Water** | (3, 7) - Downstream farmer benefits more as they preserve water for the fish population, but upstream farmer's yields are reduced. | (4, 4) - Both farmers benefit from balanced water use, but fish population may still face stress. |

#### 2. Strategic Tension: **Income Threshold vs. Risk Tolerance**

**Tension:**
Farmers must decide whether to risk increasing the number of irrigated fields based on their income from the previous year. If their income was below a critical threshold, they are more likely to take a risk and increase their fields, hoping for better water availability. If their income was above the threshold but they did not meet their water demands, they are less likely to take a risk.

**Justification:**
This tension reflects the trade-off between financial security and potential higher yields. Upstream farmers have a greater impact on downstream farmers' access to water, and the risk of water stress increases as they increase their water demands.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                  | Upstream Farmer Risks Increasing Fields | Upstream Farmer Does Not Risk Increasing Fields |
|------------------|----------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| **Downstream Farmer Risks Increasing Fields** | (6, 6) - Both farmers benefit from higher yields, but there is a risk of water stress. | (8, 4) - Upstream farmer benefits more as they have more fields, but downstream farmer's water stress is reduced. |
| **Downstream Farmer Does Not Risk Increasing Fields** | (4, 8) - Downstream farmer benefits more by preserving water, but upstream farmer's yields are reduced. | (5, 5) - Both farmers benefit from balanced water use, but there is a risk of lower yields. |

#### 3. Strategic Tension: **Water Stress vs. Yield**

**Tension:**
Farmers must decide how many fields to irrigate based on their water demand and available budget. If the water demand exceeds the available water, fields will experience water stress, leading to lower yields. Conversely, if they irrigate too few fields, they may not maximize their potential yields.

**Justification:**
This tension reflects the trade-off between water use and agricultural productivity. Upstream farmers' decisions directly affect downstream farmers' access to water and their ability to irrigate their fields.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                  | Upstream Farmer Irrigates More Fields | Upstream Farmer Irrigates Fewer Fields |
|------------------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| **Downstream Farmer Irrigates More Fields** | (7, 7) - Both farmers benefit from higher yields, but there is a risk of water stress. | (9, 5) - Upstream farmer benefits more as they have more irrigated fields, but downstream farmer's water stress is reduced. |
| **Downstream Farmer Irrigates Fewer Fields** | (5, 9) - Downstream farmer benefits more by preserving water, but upstream farmer's yields are reduced. | (6, 6) - Both farmers benefit from balanced water use, but there is a risk of lower yields. |

#### 4. Strategic Tension: **Fish Population Survival vs. Water Allocation**

**Tension:**
Farmers must consider the survival of the fish population when allocating water to their fields. If water levels are too low, the fish population may face ecological thresholds and die off, leading to reduced fishing yields for downstream farmers.

**Justification:**
This tension reflects the trade-off between economic benefits from water use and ecological sustainability. Upstream farmers' decisions directly impact the fish population and downstream farmers' access to fish.
