### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Version (DV) Using the IAD Framework

#### 1. **Title: Spatial Asymmetry in Water Allocation Between Upstream and Downstream Farmers**

#### 2. **Tension:**
   - **Spatial Asymmetry:** Upstream farmers have more water availability due to their location, whereas downstream farmers have less. This creates a strategic tension where upstream farmers may risk more fields knowing they have a higher water availability, potentially depleting the water resource for downstream farmers.

#### 3. **2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                   | Downstream Farmer (D) - Risk Fields (11) | Downstream Farmer (D) - Risk Fields (10) |
|-------------------|------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| **Upstream Farmer (U) - Risk Fields (11)** | (10, 10)                                 | (15, 5)                                  |
| **Upstream Farmer (U) - Risk Fields (10)** | (5, 15)                                  | (10, 10)                                 |

#### 4. **Justification:**

- **Upstream Farmer (U) - Risk Fields (11):** If the upstream farmer risks 11 fields, they are taking a significant risk. If the downstream farmer also risks 11 fields, both will likely face water stress, resulting in low yields for both (10, 10). However, if the downstream farmer only risks 10 fields, the upstream farmer may benefit more by having more fields irrigated (15, 5).
- **Upstream Farmer (U) - Risk Fields (10):** If the upstream farmer risks 10 fields, they are taking a moderate risk. If the downstream farmer risks 11 fields, the upstream farmer will likely suffer more water stress (5, 15). If the downstream farmer only risks 10 fields, both can achieve a balanced outcome (10, 10).

#### 5. **Title: Ecological Thresholds in Fish Population Dynamics**

#### 6. **Tension:**
   - **Ecological Thresholds:** The fish population is sensitive to water inflow, and there is a critical threshold for fish larvae to survive. This creates a strategic tension where farmers must balance their water use to prevent the fish population from collapsing.

#### 7. **2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                   | Downstream Farmer (D) - Irrigate Max (10) | Downstream Farmer (D) - Irrigate Max (9) |

| **Upstream Farmer (U) - Irrigate Max (9)**  | (5, 15)                                  | (10, 10)                                 |

#### 8. **Justification:**

- **Upstream Farmer (U) - Irrigate Max (10):** If the upstream farmer irrigates 10 fields, they are at a high risk of depleting the water resource, which could cause the fish population to drop below the survival threshold. If the downstream farmer also irrigates 10 fields, both will likely face water stress, resulting in low yields for both (10, 10). However, if the downstream farmer irrigates 9 fields, the upstream farmer may benefit more by having more fields irrigated and a higher chance of the fish population surviving (15, 5).
- **Upstream Farmer (U) - Irrigate Max (9):** If the upstream farmer irrigates 9 fields, they are taking a moderate risk. If the downstream farmer irrigates 10 fields, the upstream farmer will likely suffer more water stress (5, 15). If the downstream farmer irrigates 9 fields, both can achieve a balanced outcome (10, 10).
