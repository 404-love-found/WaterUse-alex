### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Version (DV) Using the IAD Framework

#### Title: Strategic Tensions in Decentralized Water Use Decision-Making

#### 1. Strategic Tension: Balancing Budget and Water Availability

**Tension:**
Farmers must balance their budget constraints with the uncertain availability of water. This tension arises from the uncertainty of water flows and the limited budget for irrigation.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                   | Upstream Farmer (U) | Downstream Farmer (D) |
|-------------------|---------------------|-----------------------|
| **Increase Fields** | (2, 2)               | (1, 3)                 |
| **Maintain Fields** | (3, 1)               | (2, 2)                 |

**Justification:**
- **Increase Fields:** If both farmers increase their fields, they both face water stress, leading to lower yields (2, 2).
- **Downstream Farmer (D) Increases Fields:** If the downstream farmer increases fields and the upstream farmer maintains them, the downstream farmer has a higher potential yield due to better water availability (1, 3).
- **Upstream Farmer (U) Increases Fields:** If the upstream farmer increases fields and the downstream farmer maintains them, the upstream farmer has a higher potential yield due to better water availability (3, 1).
- **Maintain Fields:** If both farmers maintain their fields, they both achieve a balanced outcome (2, 2).

#### 2. Strategic Tension: Risk vs. Reward

**Tension:**
Farmers must decide whether to take a risk by increasing their fields or maintain their current number of fields based on past water availability and budget.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                   | Upstream Farmer (U) | Downstream Farmer (D) |
|-------------------|---------------------|-----------------------|
| **Increase Fields** | (3, 3)               | (2, 4)                 |
| **Maintain Fields** | (4, 2)               | (3, 3)                 |

**Justification:**
- **Increase Fields:** If both farmers increase their fields, they both benefit from higher yields due to better water availability (3, 3).
- **Downstream Farmer (D) Increases Fields:** If the downstream farmer increases fields and the upstream farmer maintains them, the downstream farmer has higher yields (2, 4).
- **Upstream Farmer (U) Increases Fields:** If the upstream farmer increases fields and the downstream farmer maintains them, the upstream farmer has higher yields (4, 2).
- **Maintain Fields:** If both farmers maintain their fields, they both achieve a balanced outcome (3, 3).

#### 3. Strategic Tension: Ecological Thresholds and Tipping Points

**Tension:**
Farmers must consider the ecological thresholds that can lead to tipping points in the fish population, affecting their fishing yields.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                   | Upstream Farmer (U) | Downstream Farmer (D) |
|-------------------|---------------------|-----------------------|
| **Reduce Fishing** | (5, 5)               | (3, 6)                 |
| **Increase Fishing** | (6, 3)               | (4, 4)                 |

**Justification:**
- **Reduce Fishing:** If both farmers reduce fishing, the fish population is less stressed, leading to higher yields for both (5, 5).
- **Downstream Farmer (D) Increases Fishing:** If the downstream farmer increases fishing and the upstream farmer reduces it, the downstream farmer has higher yields (3, 6).
- **Upstream Farmer (U) Increases Fishing:** If the upstream farmer increases fishing and the downstream farmer reduces it, the upstream farmer has higher yields (6, 3).
- **Increase Fishing:** If both farmers increase fishing, the fish population is more stressed, leading to lower yields for both (4, 4).

#### 4. Strategic Tension: Spatial Asymmetry (Upstream vs Downstream)

**Tension:**
Upstream farmers have the advantage of better water availability compared to downstream farmers, leading to different strategic decisions.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                   | Upstream Farmer (U) | Downstream Farmer (D) |
|-------------------|---------------------|-----------------------|
| **Increase Fields** | (4, 2)               | (2, 4)                 |
| **Maintain Fields** | (3, 3)               | (3, 3)                 |

**Justification:**
- **Increase Fields:** If both farmers increase their fields, the upstream farmer has a higher yield due to better water availability (4, 2).
- **Downstream Farmer (D) Increases Fields:** If the downstream farmer increases fields and the upstream farmer maintains them, the downstream farmer has a higher yield (2, 4).
- **Upstream Farmer (U) Increases Fields:** If the upstream farmer increases fields and the downstream farmer maintains them, both achieve a balanced outcome (3, 3).
- **Maintain Fields:** If both farmers maintain their fields, they both achieve a balanced outcome (3, 3).

These action situations reflect the unique strategic tensions in the decentralized version of the water use model, considering the budget constraints, water availability, ecological thresholds, and spatial asymmetry.
