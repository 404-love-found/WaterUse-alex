### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Water Withdrawal
#### Tension: Water Scarcity vs. Irrigation Needs
#### Matrix:

|  | Upstream (Cooperate) | Upstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream (Cooperate)** | 5, 5 | 2, 8 |
| **Downstream (Defect)** | 8, 2 | 3, 3 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer decides on the number of fields to irrigate based on their prediction of water flows and available budget. The upstream farmer's decision affects the downstream farmer's water availability. If both cooperate (irrigate fewer fields), they both get a moderate payoff (5). If the upstream farmer defects (irrigates more fields), they get a higher payoff (8), while the downstream farmer gets a lower payoff (2). If both defect, they both get a low payoff (3) due to water scarcity.

#### Justification:
The fish population growth depends on the water inflow, which affects the number of larvae transported into the lake. Farmers can choose to fish at a low or high level. If the water inflow is high, and the farmer fishes at a low level, they both get a moderate payoff (6). If the farmer fishes at a high level, they get a higher payoff (8), but the fish population suffers. If the water inflow is low, the payoffs are lower, and the farmer's fishing decision has a greater impact on the fish population.
