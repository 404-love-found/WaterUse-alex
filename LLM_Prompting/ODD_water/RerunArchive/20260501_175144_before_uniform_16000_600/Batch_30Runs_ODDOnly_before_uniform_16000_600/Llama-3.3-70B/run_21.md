### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations

#### 1. Upstream/Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may irrigate more, reducing water availability for downstream farmers.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream Cooperates | Downstream Defects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Cooperates** | 5, 5 | 3, 6 |
| **Upstream Defects** | 6, 3 | 4, 4 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, upstream farmers have an incentive to irrigate more, as they have first access to the water. Downstream farmers, on the other hand, may suffer from reduced water availability. The matrix reflects the strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers, where cooperation means irrigating fewer fields (5) and defection means irrigating more fields (6). The payoffs are based on the assumption that each farmer can irrigate up to 10 fields, and the water availability decreases as more farmers irrigate.

The payoffs are based on the assumption that the fish population is affected by the total catch, and the ecological threshold is reached when the catch exceeds a certain limit.
