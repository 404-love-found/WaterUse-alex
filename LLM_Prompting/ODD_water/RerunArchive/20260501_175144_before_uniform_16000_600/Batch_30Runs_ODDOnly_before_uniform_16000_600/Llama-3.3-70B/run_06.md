# Run 6 — meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Water Extraction
#### Tension: 
Upstream farmers may extract more water, affecting downstream farmers' irrigation and fishing activities.

#### Matrix:
|  | Upstream Cooperates | Upstream Defects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream Cooperates** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 6 | Upstream: 9, Downstream: 3 |
| **Downstream Defects** | Upstream: 7, Downstream: 7 | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 4 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer decides on the number of fields to irrigate based on their prediction of water flows and available budget. Upstream farmers have an advantage in terms of water extraction, which can lead to a strategic tension with downstream farmers. The matrix reflects the spatial asymmetry, where upstream farmers can defect by extracting more water, affecting downstream farmers' payoffs.

### Action Situation 2: Fishing Resource Competition
#### Tension: 
Farmers compete for the fishing resource, with upstream farmers having priority access.

#### Matrix:
|  | Upstream Conserves | Upstream Overfishes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream Conserves** | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 4 | Upstream: 6, Downstream: 2 |
| **Downstream Overfishes** | Upstream: 4, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 3 |

#### Justification:
The fishing lake is a common-pool resource, and farmers have different access priorities based on their location. Upstream farmers can conserve or overfish, affecting downstream farmers' payoffs. The matrix reflects the ecological threshold, where overfishing can lead to a decline in the fish population, affecting both farmers' payoffs.

### Action Situation 3: Irrigation Investment
#### Tension: 
Farmers decide how many fields to irrigate, balancing the risk of water stress with the potential for increased yields.

#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer 1 Invests | Farmer 1 Does Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 2 Invests** | Farmer 1: 7, Farmer 2: 6 | Farmer 1: 4, Farmer 2: 8 |
| **Farmer 2 Does Not Invest** | Farmer 1: 9, Farmer 2: 3 | Farmer 1: 5, Farmer 2: 5 |

#### Justification:
Farmers in the decentralized case make individual decisions about irrigation investments, which can lead to strategic tensions. The matrix reflects the ecological threshold, where water stress can affect yields, and the spatial asymmetry, where upstream farmers have an advantage in terms of water extraction. The payoffs are based on the assumption that investing in irrigation can lead to increased yields, but also increases the risk of water stress.
