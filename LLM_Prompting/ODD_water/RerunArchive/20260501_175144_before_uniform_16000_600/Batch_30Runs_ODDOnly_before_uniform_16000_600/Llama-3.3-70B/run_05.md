### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
#### Tension: 
Upstream farmers may irrigate more fields, reducing water availability for downstream farmers, who must then decide whether to irrigate fewer fields or risk water stress.

#### Matrix:
|  | Downstream Irrigates 5 fields | Downstream Irrigates 10 fields |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Irrigates 5 fields** | Upstream: 20, Downstream: 15 | Upstream: 20, Downstream: 5 |
| **Upstream Irrigates 10 fields** | Upstream: 30, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 30, Downstream: 0 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, upstream farmers have an incentive to irrigate more fields, as this increases their yield. However, this reduces the water available for downstream farmers, who must then decide whether to irrigate fewer fields or risk water stress. The matrix reflects the strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers, with upstream farmers preferring to irrigate 10 fields regardless of the downstream farmer's action, and downstream farmers preferring to irrigate 5 fields if the upstream farmer irrigates 10 fields.

### Action Situation 2: Fishing and Irrigation Trade-off
#### Tension: 
Farmers must decide whether to allocate their resources to fishing or irrigation, given the ecological threshold of fish population growth and the spatial asymmetry of water availability.

#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer A (Upstream) Fishes | Farmer A (Upstream) Irrigates |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer B (Downstream) Fishes** | Farmer A: 10, Farmer B: 5 | Farmer A: 20, Farmer B: 10 |
| **Farmer B (Downstream) Irrigates** | Farmer A: 15, Farmer B: 0 | Farmer A: 25, Farmer B: 15 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, farmers must decide whether to allocate their resources to fishing or irrigation. The matrix reflects the strategic tension between farmers, with upstream farmers preferring to irrigate due to higher yields, and downstream farmers preferring to fish due to limited water availability. The ecological threshold of fish population growth is reflected in the reduced payoff for downstream farmers if they fish when the upstream farmer irrigates.

### Action Situation 3: Risking Water Stress
#### Tension: 
Farmers must decide whether to risk water stress by irrigating more fields, given the uncertainty of water flows and the spatial asymmetry of water availability.

#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer A (Upstream) Takes Risk | Farmer A (Upstream) Plays Safe |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer B (Downstream) Takes Risk** | Farmer A: 25, Farmer B: 0 | Farmer A: 20, Farmer B: 10 |
| **Farmer B (Downstream) Plays Safe** | Farmer A: 30, Farmer B: 5 | Farmer A: 25, Farmer B: 15 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, farmers must decide whether to risk water stress by irrigating more fields. The matrix reflects the strategic tension between farmers, with upstream farmers preferring to take the risk due to potential higher yields, and downstream farmers preferring to play safe due to limited water availability. The uncertainty of water flows is reflected in the reduced payoff for downstream farmers if they take the risk and the upstream farmer also takes the risk.
