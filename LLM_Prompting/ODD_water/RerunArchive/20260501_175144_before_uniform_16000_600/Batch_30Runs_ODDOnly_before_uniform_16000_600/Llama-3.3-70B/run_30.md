### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations
#### 1. Upstream/Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may irrigate more fields, reducing water availability for downstream farmers.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream Cooperates | Downstream Defects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Cooperates** | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 6, Downstream: 2 |
| **Upstream Defects** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 1 | Upstream: 7, Downstream: 3 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, upstream farmers have an incentive to irrigate more fields, as they have first access to the water. However, this reduces the water available for downstream farmers, creating a strategic tension. The matrix reflects the trade-off between cooperating (irrigating fewer fields) and defecting (irrigating more fields).

#### 2. Fishing Exploitation Dilemma
**Tension:** Farmers may overfish, reducing the fish population and affecting future catches.

|  | Other Farmers Cooperate | Other Farmers Defect |

However, overfishing reduces the fish population, affecting future catches. The matrix reflects the trade-off between cooperating (catching fewer fish) and defecting (catching more fish).
