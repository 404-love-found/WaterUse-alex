### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
#### Tension: 
Upstream farmers may irrigate more fields, reducing water availability for downstream farmers, who must decide whether to irrigate more fields, potentially exceeding their budget, or reduce their irrigation, ensuring budget sustainability but potentially reducing yields.

#### Matrix:
|  | Downstream Irrigates 5 fields | Downstream Irrigates 10 fields |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Irrigates 5 fields** | Upstream: 20, Downstream: 15 | Upstream: 20, Downstream: 10 |
| **Upstream Irrigates 10 fields** | Upstream: 30, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 30, Downstream: 0 |

#### Justification:
This action situation reflects the strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers in the decentralized case. The upstream farmer's decision to irrigate more fields directly affects the downstream farmer's water availability, creating a dilemma. The payoff matrix illustrates the potential outcomes, with the upstream farmer's decision to irrigate 10 fields resulting in higher yields but significantly reducing the downstream farmer's yields.

#### Matrix:

#### Justification:
This action situation highlights the trade-off between fishing and irrigation for a farmer. The ecological threshold is reflected in the fish population's decline as the farmer's irrigation increases.
