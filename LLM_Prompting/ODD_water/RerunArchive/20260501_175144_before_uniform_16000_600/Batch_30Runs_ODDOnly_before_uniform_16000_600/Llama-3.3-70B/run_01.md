### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
#### Tension: 
The upstream farmer may choose to irrigate more fields, reducing the water available for the downstream farmer, who must then decide whether to irrigate fewer fields or risk water stress.

#### Matrix:
|  | Downstream: Irrigate 5 fields | Downstream: Irrigate 10 fields |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream: Irrigate 5 fields** | Upstream: 20, Downstream: 15 | Upstream: 20, Downstream: 5 |
| **Upstream: Irrigate 10 fields** | Upstream: 30, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 30, Downstream: 0 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer decides independently. The upstream farmer's decision affects the downstream farmer's water availability. If the upstream farmer irrigates more fields (10), the downstream farmer faces water stress, regardless of their decision. This matrix reflects the spatial asymmetry and ecological threshold where excessive water withdrawal by the upstream farmer tips the balance, severely impacting the downstream farmer.

### Action Situation 3: Water Conservation vs. Expansion
#### Tension: 
A farmer decides whether to conserve water by irrigating fewer fields or to expand irrigation to more fields, considering the memory of past water flows and the risk of water stress.

#### Matrix:
|  | Conserve Water | Expand Irrigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Past Water Flow: Above Average** | Farmer: 22, Future Water Availability: 80 | Farmer: 28, Future Water Availability: 60 |
| **Past Water Flow: Below Average** | Farmer: 18, Future Water Availability: 90 | Farmer: 20, Future Water Availability: 40 |

#### Justification:
This situation reflects the farmer's decision-making process based on the memory of past water flows. If past water flows were above average, the farmer might be inclined to expand irrigation. However, this could lead to water stress if future water availability is below expectations. The matrix captures the strategic tension between conserving water for future security and expanding irrigation for immediate gain, considering the uncertainty and ecological thresholds of water availability.
