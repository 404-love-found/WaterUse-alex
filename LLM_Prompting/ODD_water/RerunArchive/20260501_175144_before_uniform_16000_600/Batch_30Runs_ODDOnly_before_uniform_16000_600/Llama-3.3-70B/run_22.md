### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations

#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
**Title:** Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Conflict
**Tension:** Strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers over water allocation for irrigation.
**Matrix:**

|  | Upstream (Irrigate 10) | Upstream (Irrigate 5) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream (Irrigate 10)** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 2 | Upstream: 6, Downstream: 6 |
| **Downstream (Irrigate 5)** | Upstream: 9, Downstream: 1 | Upstream: 7, Downstream: 5 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, upstream farmers have priority access to water. The matrix reflects the strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers. If both choose to irrigate 10 fields, the upstream farmer gets 8 units of water, while the downstream farmer only gets 2 units due to water scarcity. If the upstream farmer chooses to irrigate 5 fields, the downstream farmer can irrigate 10 fields and get 6 units of water.

If one farmer chooses a low fishing quota, the other farmer can exploit the fish population more, but the first farmer gets a higher payoff in the long run due to sustainable fishing practices.
