### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Water Extraction
#### Tension: 
Spatial asymmetry in water extraction leads to a prisoner's dilemma, where upstream farmers have an incentive to extract more water, potentially harming downstream farmers.

#### Matrix:
|  | Upstream (Cooperate) | Upstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream (Cooperate)** | 5, 5 | 2, 8 |
| **Downstream (Defect)** | 8, 2 | 3, 3 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each! farmer decides on the number of fields to irrigate. Upstream farmers have priority access to water, creating a spatial asymmetry. If both upstream and downstream farmers cooperate (irrigate fewer fields), they both receive a moderate payoff (5). If the upstream farmer defects (irrigates more fields), they receive a higher payoff (8), while the downstream farmer receives a lower payoff (2). If both defect, they both receive a low payoff (3) due to water scarcity and ecological thresholds.
