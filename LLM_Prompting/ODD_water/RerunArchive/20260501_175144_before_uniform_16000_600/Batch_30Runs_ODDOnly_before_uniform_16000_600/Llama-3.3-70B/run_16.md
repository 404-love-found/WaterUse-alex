### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations
#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Water Withdrawal Tension
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may withdraw more water, affecting downstream farmers' irrigation needs.
**Matrix:**

|  | Upstream (Cooperate) | Upstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream (Cooperate)** | 5, 5 | 2, 8 |
| **Downstream (Defect)** | 8, 2 | 3, 3 |

**Justification:** The matrix reflects the strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers. If both cooperate (withdraw less water), they both get a moderate payoff (5 fields irrigated). If the upstream farmer defects (withdraws more water), they get a higher payoff (8 fields), while the downstream farmer gets a lower payoff (2 fields). If both defect, they both get a low payoff (3 fields) due to water scarcity.

#### 2. Fishing Resource Competition Tension
**Tension:** Farmers compete for the fishing resource, with upstream farmers having priority access.
**Matrix:**

|  | Upstream (Cooperate) | Upstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream (Cooperate)** | 3, 3 | 1, 5 |
| **Downstream (Defect)** | 5, 1 | 2, 2 |

**Justification:** The matrix reflects the strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers competing for the fishing resource. If both cooperate (limit fishing), they both get a moderate payoff (3 fish). If the upstream farmer defects (overfish), they get a higher payoff (5 fish), while the downstream farmer gets a lower payoff (1 fish). If both defect, they both get a low payoff (2 fish) due to resource depletion.

#### 3. Ecological Threshold Tension
**Tension:** Farmers' water withdrawal and fishing activities may exceed ecological thresholds, affecting the fish population.
**Matrix:**

|  | Farmer (Sustainable) | Farmer (Overexploit) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Fish Population (Resilient)** | 6, 6 | 2, 8 |
| **Fish Population (Depleted)** | 8, 2 | 1, 1 |

**Justification:** The matrix reflects the strategic tension between farmers' activities and the fish population's resilience. If farmers act sustainably, the fish population remains resilient, and both get a moderate payoff (6 fields/fish). If farmers overexploit, the fish population depletes, and farmers get a lower payoff (2 fields), while the fish population gets a very low payoff (1 fish). If the fish population is already depleted, both farmers and the fish population get a very low payoff (1 field/fish).

#### 4. Budget Constraint Tension
**Tension:** Farmers must balance their irrigation and fishing activities with their budget constraints.
**Matrix:**

|  | Farmer (Irrigation) | Farmer (Fishing) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Budget (Adequate)** | 7, 7 | 3, 3 |
| **Budget (Constrained)** | 3, 3 | 1, 1 |

**Justification:** The matrix reflects the strategic tension between farmers' activities and their budget constraints. If farmers have an adequate budget, they can choose between irrigation and fishing, both getting a moderate payoff (7 fields/fish). If farmers' budget is constrained, they must prioritize one activity, getting a lower payoff (3 fields/fish). If both activities are constrained, farmers get a very low payoff (1 field/fish).
