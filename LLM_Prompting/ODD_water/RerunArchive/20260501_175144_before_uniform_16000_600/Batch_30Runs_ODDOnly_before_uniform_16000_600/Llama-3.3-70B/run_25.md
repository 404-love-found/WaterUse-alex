### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Water Extraction
#### Tension: 
Water extraction by upstream farmers affects downstream farmers' access to water, creating a strategic tension between individual gain and collective sustainability.

#### Matrix:
|  | Upstream Cooperates | Upstream Defects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream Cooperates** | 8, 8 | 2, 10 |
| **Downstream Defects** | 10, 2 | 5, 5 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer decides on the number of fields to irrigate. Upstream farmers have priority access to water, which may lead them to extract more water, affecting downstream farmers. The matrix reflects the strategic tension: if both cooperate (extract less water), they both get a moderate payoff (8). If one defects (extracts more water) while the other cooperates, the defector gets a high payoff (10), while the cooperator gets a low payoff (2). If both defect, they both get a moderate payoff (5), as the water stress affects both.
