### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations
#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may irrigate more, reducing water availability for downstream farmers.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream (Cooperate) | Downstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream (Cooperate)** | 5, 5 | 5, 3 |
| **Upstream (Defect)** | 8, 2 | 6, 4 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, upstream farmers have an incentive to irrigate more, as they have first access to the water. However, this reduces the water available for downstream farmers. The matrix reflects the strategic tension, where cooperation means irrigating a moderate amount (5 fields), and defection means irrigating more (8 fields). The payoffs are based on the number of fields irrigated, with upstream farmers having an advantage.
