### Action Situation 1: Upstream Farmer's Irrigation Decision
#### Title: Irrigation Dilemma
#### Tension: The upstream farmer must decide whether to irrigate their fields, potentially reducing the water available for downstream farmers, while considering their own water needs and the potential impact on their yields.
#### Matrix:

|  | Downstream Farmer Cooperates (Irrigates Less) | Downstream Farmer Defects (Irrigates More) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Farmer Cooperates (Irrigates Less)** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 7 | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 9 |
| **Upstream Farmer Defects (Irrigates More)** | Upstream: 9, Downstream: 4 | Upstream: 6, Downstream: 6 |

#### Justification: The upstream farmer has the advantage of accessing the water first, but their decision affects the downstream farmer's water availability. The matrix reflects the strategic tension between cooperating (irrigating less) and defecting (irrigating more), considering the potential yields and water availability.
