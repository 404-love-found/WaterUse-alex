### Decentralized Water Management Action Situations

#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
**Tension:** Over-extraction of water by upstream farmers, affecting downstream farmers' irrigation needs.
**Matrix:**

|  | Upstream (Cooperate) | Upstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream (Cooperate)** | 5, 5 | 2, 8 |
| **Downstream (Defect)** | 8, 2 | 3, 3 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, each farmer decides on the number of fields to irrigate. Upstream farmers have priority access to water, creating a strategic tension. If both cooperate (irrigate fewer fields), they both receive a moderate payoff (5). If the upstream farmer defects (irrigates more fields), they receive a high payoff (8), while the downstream farmer receives a low payoff (2). If both defect, they both receive a low payoff (3).

#### 2. Fishing Exploitation Dilemma
**Tension:** Overfishing by downstream farmers, affecting the fish population and future fishing yields.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream (Cooperate) | Downstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream (Cooperate)** | 4, 6 | 3, 9 |
| **Upstream (Defect)** | 7, 3 | 5, 5 |

**Justification:** Downstream farmers have priority access to the fishing lake. If they cooperate (catch fewer fish), they receive a moderate payoff (6), while upstream farmers receive a lower payoff (4). If downstream farmers defect (catch more fish), they receive a high payoff (9), while upstream farmers receive a low payoff (3). If both defect, they both receive a moderate payoff (5).

#### 3. Water Conservation vs. Irrigation Expansion Dilemma
**Tension:** Balancing water conservation with irrigation expansion, considering ecological thresholds.
**Matrix:**

|  | Cooperate (Conserve) | Defect (Expand) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Cooperate (Conserve)** | 6, 6 | 4, 8 |
| **Defect (Expand)** | 8, 4 | 2, 2 |

**Justification:** Farmers must balance water conservation with irrigation expansion. If both cooperate (conserve water), they receive a moderate payoff (6). If one defects (expands irrigation), they receive a high payoff (8), while the other receives a low payoff (4). If both defect, they both receive a low payoff (2), reflecting the ecological threshold where over-extraction leads to severe consequences.

#### 4. Information Sharing Dilemma
**Tension:** Sharing information about water flows and fishing yields, considering the potential for free-riding.
**Matrix:**

|  | Share Information | Withhold Information |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Share Information** | 5, 5 | 3, 7 |
| **Withhold Information** | 7, 3 | 4, 4 |

**Justification:** Farmers must decide whether to share information about water flows and fishing yields. If both share information, they receive a moderate payoff (5). If one shares information while the other withholds it, the sharing farmer receives a low payoff (3), while the withholding farmer receives a high payoff (7). If both withhold information, they both receive a moderate payoff (4), reflecting the lack of cooperation.
