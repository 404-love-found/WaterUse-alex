### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Water Allocation
#### Title: Water Allocation Dilemma
#### Tension: Strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers over water allocation, considering the spatial asymmetry and ecological thresholds.
#### Matrix:

|  | Downstream Cooperates | Downstream Defects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Cooperates** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 6 | Upstream: 9, Downstream: 3 |
| **Upstream Defects** | Upstream: 10, Downstream: 2 | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 4 |

#### Justification: In the decentralized case, upstream farmers have priority access to water. The matrix reflects the tension between upstream and downstream farmers. If both cooperate (allocate water fairly), they receive moderate payoffs (8, 6). If the upstream farmer defects (allocates most water to themselves), they receive a higher payoff (10), while the downstream farmer receives a lower payoff (2). The downstream farmer's decision to cooperate or defect affects the upstream farmer's payoff, as defecting reduces the upstream farmer's payoff (8, 4).
