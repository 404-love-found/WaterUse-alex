### Action Situation 1: Upstream Farmer's Irrigation Decision
#### Tension: 
Over-irrigation by upstream farmers may reduce water availability for downstream farmers, creating a strategic tension between individual gain and collective well-being.

#### Matrix:
|  | Downstream Farmer Cooperates (Irrigates Less) | Downstream Farmer Defects (Irrigates More) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Farmer Cooperates (Irrigates Less)** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 8 | Upstream: 9, Downstream: 6 |
| **Upstream Farmer Defects (Irrigates More)** | Upstream: 10, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 4 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer predicts water availability and decides on the number of fields to irrigate. Upstream farmers have priority access to water, creating a spatial asymmetry. If an upstream farmer irrigates more, they may increase their yield (up to 10 fields), but this reduces water availability for downstream farmers, potentially decreasing their yield. The matrix reflects this tension, where cooperation (irrigating less) leads to a more balanced outcome, while defection (irrigating more) prioritizes individual gain over collective well-being.

#### Matrix:

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, downstream farmers have priority access to the fishing lake. The matrix reflects this tension, where cooperation (fishing less) leads to a more sustainable outcome, while defection (fishing more) prioritizes short-term gain over long-term sustainability.

#### Matrix:
