#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may over-irrigate, leaving downstream farmers with insufficient water.
**Matrix:**

|  | Upstream (High Irrigation) | Upstream (Low Irrigation) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream (High Irrigation)** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 2 | Upstream: 6, Downstream: 6 |
| **Downstream (Low Irrigation)** | Upstream: 9, Downstream: 1 | Upstream: 7, Downstream: 7 |

**Justification:** The upstream farmer has an incentive to irrigate heavily, as it increases their yield. However, this reduces the water available to the downstream farmer, creating a strategic tension. The matrix reflects the spatial asymmetry, where the upstream farmer's actions have a greater impact on the downstream farmer's payoff.
