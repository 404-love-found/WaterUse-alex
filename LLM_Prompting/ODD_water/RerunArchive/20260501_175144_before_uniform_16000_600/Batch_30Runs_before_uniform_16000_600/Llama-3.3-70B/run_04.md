### Decentralized Water Management Action Situations

#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may irrigate more, reducing water availability for downstream farmers.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream (Cooperate) | Downstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream (Cooperate)** | 5, 5 | 6, 3 |
| **Upstream (Defect)** | 3, 6 | 4, 4 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, upstream farmers have priority access to water. If both upstream and downstream farmers cooperate (irrigate fewer fields), they both receive a moderate payoff (5). If the upstream farmer defects (irrigates more fields), they receive a higher payoff (6), but the downstream farmer receives a lower payoff (3). If both defect, they both receive a lower payoff (4) due to ecological thresholds and reduced water availability.

#### 2. Fishing Quota Dilemma
**Tension:** Farmers may overfish, depleting the fish population and affecting future catches.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers (Cooperate) | Other Farmers (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer (Cooperate)** | 4, 4 | 2, 6 |
| **Farmer (Defect)** | 6, 2 | 3, 3 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, each farmer decides their fishing quota independently. If all farmers cooperate (catch fewer fish), they all receive a moderate payoff (4). If one farmer defects (catches more fish), they receive a higher payoff (6), but other farmers receive a lower payoff (2) due to depleted fish populations. If all defect, they all receive a lower payoff (3) due to ecological thresholds and overfishing.

#### 3. Water Conservation vs. Irrigation Dilemma
