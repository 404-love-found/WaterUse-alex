#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may over-irrigate, leaving insufficient water for downstream farmers, who may then over-fish to compensate for lost agricultural income.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream Conserves Fish | Downstream Over-fishes |
| --- | --- | --- |

| **Upstream Conserves Water** | Upstream: 6, Downstream: 6 | Upstream: 6, Downstream: 5 |

**Justification:** The matrix reflects the strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers. Upstream farmers have an incentive to over-irrigate (max fields = 10), as it increases their yield, but this reduces the water available for downstream farmers, who may then over-fish to compensate for lost agricultural income. If both conserve, they can maintain a balance, but if one defects, the other may suffer.

**Tension:** Farmers must balance their water allocation for irrigation with their fishing effort to maximize their overall income, considering the ecological threshold of fish population sustainability.
**Matrix:**

|  | Farmer Increases Fishing Effort | Farmer Decreases Fishing Effort |

**Justification:** The matrix highlights the trade-off between irrigation and fishing effort. Increasing irrigation may boost agricultural income but harm the fish population, while increasing fishing effort may provide short-term gains but threaten the long-term sustainability of the fish population. Decreasing irrigation and fishing effort can help maintain ecological balance, but may reduce income.

**Tension:** Upstream farmers have priority access to water, which may lead to over-extraction, affecting downstream farmers' agricultural income and fishing opportunities.

**Justification:** The matrix illustrates the spatial asymmetry in water use, where upstream farmers have priority access. If the upstream farmer over-extracts, the downstream farmer suffers, regardless of their own actions. If the upstream farmer conserves, the downstream farmer can maintain a reasonable income, but if they over-extract as well, the downstream farmer's income decreases.
