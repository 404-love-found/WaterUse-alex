#### 1. Strategic Tension: Upstream vs Downstream Farmers' Water Allocation Choices

**Tension:**
Upstream farmers have limited water access, while downstream farmers have access to a shared common pool resource (the fishing lake). This creates a trade-off between the upstream farmers' need for water for irrigation and the downstream farmers' need for fish from the lake.

**Justification:**
- Upstream farmers face more water stress and may need to allocate more fields to ensure sufficient water supply.
- Downstream farmers can access the fishing lake and may prioritize fish catches over more water allocation.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| Upstream \ Downstream | No Risk (Increase Fields) | Risk (Maintain Fields) |
|-----------------------|----------------------------|------------------------|
| No Risk (Increase Fields) | (2, 1) | (1, 2) |
| Risk (Maintain Fields) | (1, 1) | (2, 2) |

**Explanation:**
- **(2, 1):** Upstream farmer increases fields, downstream farmer does not risk and maintains fields. Upstream farmer benefits more from increased water allocation, while downstream farmer benefits from the shared resource.
- **(1, 2):** Upstream farmer does not risk and maintains fields, downstream farmer increases fields. Downstream farmer benefits more from increased fish catch, while upstream farmer benefits from reduced water stress.
- **(1, 1):** Both farmers maintain fields. This results in a balanced but lower payoff for both.
- **(2, 2):** Both farmers increase fields. This results in a balanced but lower payoff for both, reflecting the ecological threshold where excessive water extraction can lead to a tipping point.
