### Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Version (DV) of the Water Use Model

#### 1. **Strategic Tension: Risk Aversion vs. Risk Taking**

**Title:** Risk Aversion vs. Risk Taking

**Tension:** Farmers at different locations along the river face a strategic tension between risk-averse and risk-taking strategies when deciding how many fields to irrigate.

**Justification:** Upstream farmers have less water access and are more risk-averse, while downstream farmers have more water access and are more willing to take risks. This creates a strategic tension where upstream farmers may choose to irrigate fewer fields to avoid water stress, while downstream farmers may choose to irrigate more fields to maximize their yields, potentially leading to water stress downstream.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|          | Upstream Farmer Irrigates 8 Fields | Upstream Farmer Irrigates 6 Fields |
|----------|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Downstream Farmer Irrigates 9 Fields** | (50, 40)                          | (60, 30)                          |
| **Downstream Farmer Irrigates 7 Fields** | (45, 35)                          | (55, 32)                          |

**Explanation:**
- **Upstream Farmer Irrigates 8 Fields (High Risk):** If the downstream farmer irrigates 9 fields, the upstream farmer faces water stress and yields 50. If the downstream farmer irrigates 7 fields, the upstream farmer's yields are 45.
- **Upstream Farmer Irrigates 6 Fields (Low Risk):** If the downstream farmer irrigates 9 fields, the upstream farmer's yields are 60. If the downstream farmer irrigates 7 fields, the upstream farmer's yields are 55.

### Thought Process:
- **Spatial Asymmetry:** Upstream and downstream farmers have different water access and therefore different risk and gain profiles.
- **Ecological Thresholds:** Ensuring that the fish population does not face tipping points due to excessive water stress.
- **Max Fields:** Ensuring that the number of fields irrigated does not exceed the maximum (10 fields).

These action situations capture the strategic tensions that arise in the decentralized version of the water use model, reflecting the complex interactions between water allocation, fish population dynamics, and farmer decision-making.
