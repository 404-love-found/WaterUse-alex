### Distinct Action Situations for the Decentralized Case (DV) Reflecting Strategic Tensions

#### 1. Strategic Tension: Information Asymmetry and Water Stress
**Title:** Upstream vs Downstream Farmers' Water Stress and Information Asymmetry

**Tension:** Upstream farmers have less accurate information about water availability due to their location and the spatial distribution of water stress. Downstream farmers face more water stress but have better information about water availability.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| Upstream Farmer | Downstream Farmer |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Reduce Irrigation** | **Reduce Irrigation** | (2, 2) |
| **Reduce Irrigation** | **Increase Irrigation** | (1, 4) |
| **Increase Irrigation** | **Reduce Irrigation** | (4, 1) |
| **Increase Irrigation** | **Increase Irrigation** | (3, 3) |

**Justification:**
- **Upstream Farmer**: If both farmers reduce irrigation, they both face moderate water stress and have more accurate information (2, 2).
- **Upstream Farmer**: If they both increase irrigation, they both face high water stress but have better information (3, 3).
- **Upstream Farmer**: If the upstream farmer reduces irrigation and the downstream increases, the upstream gets more accurate information but faces less stress (1, 4).
- **Upstream Farmer**: If the upstream farmer increases irrigation and the downstream reduces, the upstream faces high stress and less accurate information (4, 1).

#### 2. Strategic Tension: Ecological Threshold and Fish Population Viability
**Title:** Upstream vs Downstream Farmers' Impact on Fish Population Viability

**Tension:** Upstream farmers' decisions on water use can affect the fish population, which is crucial for downstream farmers' fishing activities. Downstream farmers rely on the fish population for their income and food security.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| Upstream Farmer | Downstream Farmer |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Reduce Irrigation** | **Reduce Irrigation** | (3, 3) |
| **Reduce Irrigation** | **Increase Irrigation** | (2, 1) |
| **Increase Irrigation** | **Reduce Irrigation** | (1, 2) |
| **Increase Irrigation** | **Increase Irrigation** | (2, 2) |

**Justification:**
- **Upstream Farmer**: If both farmers reduce irrigation, they both face moderate water stress but the fish population remains viable (3, 3).
- **Upstream Farmer**: If they both increase irrigation, they both face high water stress but the fish population remains viable (2, 2).
- **Upstream Farmer**: If the upstream farmer reduces irrigation and the downstream increases, the upstream gets more accurate information but the fish population might be stressed (2, 1).
- **Upstream Farmer**: If the upstream farmer increases irrigation and the downstream reduces, the upstream faces high stress and the fish population might be stressed (1, 2).

#### 3. Strategic Tension: Risk Aversion and Income Threshold
**Title:** Upstream vs Downstream Farmers' Risk Aversion and Income Threshold

**Tension:** Upstream farmers are more risk-averse and base their decisions on past income. Downstream farmers are less risk-averse and are more willing to take risks to increase their income.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| Upstream Farmer | Downstream Farmer |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Reduce Irrigation** | **Reduce Irrigation** | (2, 2) |
| **Reduce Irrigation** | **Increase Irrigation** | (1, 4) |
| **Increase Irrigation** | **Reduce Irrigation** | (4, 1) |
| **Increase Irrigation** | **Increase Irrigation** | (3, 3) |

**Justification:**
- **Upstream Farmer**: If both farmers reduce irrigation, they both face moderate water stress and have more accurate information (2, 2).
- **Upstream Farmer**: If they both increase irrigation, they both face high water stress but have better information (3, 3).
- **Upstream Farmer**: If the upstream farmer reduces irrigation and the downstream increases, the upstream gets more accurate information but faces less stress (1, 4).
- **Upstream Farmer**: If the upstream farmer increases irrigation and the downstream reduces, the upstream faces high stress and less accurate information (4, 1).

### Thought Process
- **Spatial Asymmetry**: Upstream and downstream farmers face different levels of water stress and information accuracy.
- **Ecological Thresholds**: Decisions can affect the fish population, which is a critical resource for downstream farmers.
- **Risk Aversion**: Upstream farmers are more cautious, while downstream farmers are more willing to take risks to increase their income.
