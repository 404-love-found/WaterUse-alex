### Strategic Tension Analysis for Decentralized Version (DV)

#### 1. Strategic Tension: Spatial Asymmetry vs. Ecological Thresholds

**Title:** Spatial Asymmetry vs. Ecological Thresholds

**Tension:** Upstream farmers have a first-mover advantage in accessing water, but the downstream farmers' fish population is more sensitive to ecological thresholds, leading to potential over-extraction of water by upstream farmers.

**Justification:** In the decentralized version, each farmer independently decides on the number of fields to irrigate based on their local water flow predictions. Upstream farmers can access water first, but over-extraction can lead to ecological thresholds being breached downstream, affecting the fish population and, consequently, downstream farmers' livelihoods.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Downstream Irrigates 10 | Downstream Irrigates 9 |
|----------------|------------------------|------------------------|
| **Upstream Irrigates 10** | (10, 10) | (10, 9) |
| **Upstream Irrigates 9**  | (9, 10)  | (9, 9)  |

**Thought Process:**
- If both upstream and downstream irrigate 10 fields, both face medium ecological and economic costs due to potential over-extraction.
- If upstream irrigates 10 fields and downstream irrigates 9 fields, downstream suffers less ecological impact but still faces some costs.
- If both irrigate 9 fields, the ecological threshold is maintained, and both benefit, but at a slightly lower yield.
- The matrix reflects the trade-off between maximizing short-term gains (irrigating more) and maintaining long-term sustainability (irrigating less).

#### 2. Strategic Tension: Risk Aversion vs. Risk Taking

**Title:** Risk Aversion vs. Risk Taking

**Tension:** Downstream farmers must decide whether to take risks by increasing the number of irrigated fields, hoping for more water, or play it safe by irrigating fewer fields, avoiding potential losses.

**Justification:** Downstream farmers' decision is influenced by their past income and the memory capacity, leading to a risk-averse or risk-taking behavior.

|                | Downstream Irrigates 10 | Downstream Irrigates 9 |
|----------------|------------------------|------------------------|
| **Upstream Irrigates 10** | (10, 10) | (10, 9) |
| **Upstream Irrigates 9**  | (9, 10)  | (9, 9)  |

**Thought Process:**
- If both upstream and downstream irrigate 10 fields, downstream faces higher risk but potentially higher yields.
- If upstream irrigates 10 fields and downstream irrigates 9 fields, downstream faces less risk but lower potential yields.
- If both irrigate 9 fields, downstream is safer but faces lower yields.
- The matrix reflects the trade-off between short-term gains (taking more risks) and long-term stability (playing it safe).

#### 3. Strategic Tension: Local vs. Global Optima

**Title:** Local vs. Global Optima

**Tension:** Each farmer's decision is influenced by their local water flow predictions, leading to a potential conflict between individual optimal strategies and the collective well-being of the entire system.

**Justification:** In the decentralized version, each farmer makes decisions based on local water flow predictions, which may not align with the overall water resource management.

**Thought Process:**
- If both upstream and downstream irrigate 10 fields, the system faces potential over-extraction, but each farmer benefits from higher yields.
- If upstream irrigates 10 fields and downstream irrigates 9 fields, the downstream farmer benefits from lower risk, while the upstream farmer faces some costs.
- If both irrigate 9 fields, the system operates more sustainably, but yields are lower for both farmers.

### Summary

The decentralized version of the model presents several strategic tensions, reflecting the complex interactions between spatial asymmetry, ecological thresholds, risk aversion, and local vs. global optima. These tensions highlight the challenges in managing water resources in a decentralized manner, where individual decisions can have significant impacts on the entire system.
