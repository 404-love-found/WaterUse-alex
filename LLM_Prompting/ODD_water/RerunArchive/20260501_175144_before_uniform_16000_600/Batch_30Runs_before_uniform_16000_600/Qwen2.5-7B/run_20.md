### Title: Strategic Tensions in the Decentralized Version of the Water Use Model

#### Tension 1: Upstream vs. Downstream Farmer Water Allocation

**Strategic Tension:**
Downstream farmers have less water available for irrigation due to upstream water withdrawals. This creates a dilemma between upstream and downstream farmers over water allocation.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|          | Downstream Farmer Irrigates 1 Field | Downstream Farmer Irrigates 2 Fields |
|----------|-------------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| **Upstream Farmer Irrigates 1 Field** | (8, 8)                              | (5, 10)                             |
| **Upstream Farmer Irrigates 2 Fields** | (10, 5)                             | (6, 6)                              |

**Justification:**
- **(8, 8):** Both farmers irrigate 1 field. Upstream has more water and benefits from a higher yield, while downstream has enough water for 1 field.
- **(5, 10):** Upstream irrigates 1 field, and downstream irrigates 2 fields. Upstream has less water for 2 fields, resulting in a lower yield, while downstream has a higher yield due to more water availability.
- **(10, 5):** Upstream irrigates 2 fields, and downstream irrigates 1 field. Upstream has a higher yield, but downstream has limited water for 2 fields.
- **(6, 6):** Both farmers irrigate 2 fields. Upstream has a moderate yield, and downstream has a moderate yield, but both have limited water.

**Thought Process:**
- The upstream farmer has more control over water allocation and can choose to irrigate fewer fields, ensuring downstream farmers get enough water.
- The downstream farmer is more dependent on upstream water and has to balance between taking risks to irrigate more fields or ensuring sufficient water for fewer fields.

#### Tension 2: Risk vs. Caution in Water Allocation

**Strategic Tension:**
Farmers decide whether to risk increasing the number of irrigated fields based on uncertain water availability. This creates a tension between taking risks and being cautious.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|          | Farmer Irrigates 2 Fields | Farmer Irrigates 3 Fields |
|----------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Irrigates 2 Fields** | (7, 7)                    | (5, 10)                   |
| **Irrigates 3 Fields** | (10, 5)                   | (8, 8)                    |

**Justification:**
- **(7, 7):** Both farmers irrigate 2 fields. They take a moderate risk and have a balanced yield.
- **(5, 10):** One farmer irrigates 2 fields, and the other irrigates 3 fields. The farmer with 3 fields takes a higher risk and gets a higher yield, while the other farmer has a lower yield due to water stress.
- **(10, 5):** One farmer irrigates 3 fields, and the other irrigates 2 fields. The farmer with 3 fields has a higher yield, while the other farmer has a lower yield.
- **(8, 8):** Both farmers irrigate 3 fields. They take a higher risk and have a balanced yield.

**Thought Process:**
- The farmer with 3 fields takes a higher risk in hopes of a higher yield, while the other farmer is more cautious and takes a moderate risk.
- The downstream farmer, being more dependent on water availability, might take more risks compared to the upstream farmer who has more water security.

#### Tension 3: Fishing vs. Irrigation

**Strategic Tension:**
Farmers decide between prioritizing irrigation and fishing based on water availability and budget constraints. This creates a tension between using water for agriculture and using it for subsistence.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|          | Farmer Prioritizes Fishing | Farmer Prioritizes Irrigation |
|----------|-----------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **Irrigates 2 Fields** | (6, 6)                      | (8, 4)                        |
| **Irrigates 3 Fields** | (4, 8)                      | (5, 5)                        |

**Justification:**
- **(6, 6):** Both farmers irrigate 2 fields and fish. They have a balanced yield and catch.
- **(8, 4):** One farmer irrigates 2 fields and the other prioritizes fishing. The farmer with 2 fields has a higher yield, while the other has a higher catch.
- **(4, 8):** One farmer irrigates 3 fields and the other prioritizes fishing. The farmer with 3 fields has a lower yield, while the other has a higher catch.
- **(5, 5):** Both farmers irrigate 3 fields and fish. They have a balanced yield and catch, but lower than when both focus on irrigation.

**Thought Process:**
- The farmer with 2 fields can balance between irrigation and fishing, ensuring a moderate yield and catch.
- The farmer with 3 fields prioritizes irrigation, ensuring a higher yield but potentially lower catch due to water stress.

### Summary
The decentralized version of the water use model presents several strategic tensions, including water allocation between upstream and downstream farmers, risk-taking in water allocation, and the trade-off between irrigation and fishing. These tensions reflect the spatial asymmetry and ecological thresholds, highlighting the complexities in managing water resources in a decentralized setting.
