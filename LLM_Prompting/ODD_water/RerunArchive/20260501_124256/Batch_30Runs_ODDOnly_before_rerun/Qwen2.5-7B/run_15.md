### Title: Decentralized Water Use Model - Distinct Action Situations

### Tension 1: Spatial Asymmetry vs Ecological Thresholds

#### Justification:
In the decentralized model (DV), farmers make decisions based on their location along the river stretch, leading to spatial asymmetry. Additionally, the ecological threshold for fish larvae survival introduces a critical point where the amount of water inflow must exceed a certain threshold. Downstream farmers might face more severe ecological impacts if the water inflow is insufficient, creating a strategic tension.

#### 2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:

|                | Downstream Farmer (D) - Irrigate 9 Fields | Downstream Farmer (D) - Irrigate 10 Fields |
|----------------|-------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|
| **Upstream Farmer (U) - Irrigate 9 Fields** | (2, 2)                                    | (1, 3)                                     |
| **Upstream Farmer (U) - Irrigate 10 Fields** | (3, 1)                                    | (2, 2)                                     |

**Explanation:**
- If both farmers irrigate 9 fields, the resource is still sufficient for both to meet their water demands without risking ecological thresholds.
- If both farmers irrigate 10 fields, the water demand exceeds the available resource, but the downstream farmer is more at risk due to the ecological threshold for fish larvae survival.
- If one farmer irrigates 9 fields and the other irrigates 10 fields, the downstream farmer benefits from the additional water, but the upstream farmer faces a higher risk of meeting the ecological threshold.
