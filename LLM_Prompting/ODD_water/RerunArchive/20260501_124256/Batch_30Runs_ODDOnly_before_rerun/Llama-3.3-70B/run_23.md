### Action Situation 1: Upstream Farmer's Irrigation Decision
#### Tension: 
Over-Irrigation vs. Conservation, considering the impact on downstream farmers and the fish population.

#### Matrix:
|  | Downstream Farmer Conserves | Downstream Farmer Over-Irrigates |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Farmer Conserves** | 5, 5 | 3, 6 |
| **Upstream Farmer Over-Irrigates** | 6, 3 | 2, 2 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, an upstream farmer's decision to conserve or over-irrigate affects both their own yield and that of the downstream farmer, as well as the fish population. If both conserve, they both get a moderate yield (5). If one over-irrigates while the other conserves, the over-irrigating farmer gets a higher yield (6) at the expense of the conserving farmer's yield (3). If both over-irrigate, they both suffer from reduced yields (2) due to water scarcity and potential ecological thresholds being crossed.
