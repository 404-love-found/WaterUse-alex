### Action Situation 1: Upstream Farmer's Irrigation Decision
#### Tension: 
Over-irrigation by upstream farmers may reduce water availability for downstream farmers, creating a strategic tension between individual gain and collective sustainability.

#### Matrix:
|  | Downstream Farmer Cooperates (I) | Downstream Farmer Defects (D) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Farmer Cooperates (I)** | 5, 5 | 7, 2 |
| **Upstream Farmer Defects (D)** | 2, 7 | 4, 4 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer decides on the number of fields to irrigate. An upstream farmer may choose to cooperate (irrigate fewer fields) or defect (irrigate more fields). Similarly, a downstream farmer may cooperate (accept reduced water flow) or defect (try to access more water). The payoff matrix reflects the strategic tension between individual gain (irrigating more fields) and collective sustainability (ensuring sufficient water flow for all farmers). The numbers in the matrix represent the number of fields each farmer can irrigate, with the upstream farmer's payoff first.
