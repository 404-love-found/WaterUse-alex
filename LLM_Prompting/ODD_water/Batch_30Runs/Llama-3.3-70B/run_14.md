### Action Situation 1: Upstream Farmer's Irrigation Decision
#### Title: Irrigation Dilemma
#### Tension: The upstream farmer must decide whether to irrigate more fields, potentially reducing the water available for downstream farmers.
#### Matrix:

|  | Downstream Farmer Cooperates (Irrigates Less) | Downstream Farmer Defects (Irrigates More) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Farmer Cooperates (Irrigates Less)** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 8 | Upstream: 6, Downstream: 9 |
| **Upstream Farmer Defects (Irrigates More)** | Upstream: 9, Downstream: 6 | Upstream: 7, Downstream: 7 |

#### Justification: The upstream farmer has an advantage in terms of water access, but over-irrigation can lead to ecological thresholds being crossed, affecting both farmers. The downstream farmer's cooperation is crucial for the upstream farmer's long-term success.
