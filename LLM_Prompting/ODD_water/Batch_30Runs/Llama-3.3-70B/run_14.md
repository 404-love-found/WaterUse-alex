### Action Situation 1: Upstream Farmer's Irrigation Decision
#### Tension: Over-extraction of Water
In the decentralized case, an upstream farmer's decision to irrigate more fields can lead to reduced water availability for downstream farmers, creating a strategic tension.

#### Matrix:

|  | Downstream Farmer Cooperates (Irrigates Less) | Downstream Farmer Defects (Irrigates More) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Farmer Cooperates (Irrigates Less)** | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 3, Downstream: 7 |
| **Upstream Farmer Defects (Irrigates More)** | Upstream: 7, Downstream: 3 | Upstream: 4, Downstream: 4 |

#### Justification:
The upstream farmer has an incentive to defect and irrigate more fields, as it increases their yield. However, this reduces the water available for the downstream farmer, who may also choose to defect and irrigate more fields, leading to a suboptimal outcome for both.
