### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations

#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may irrigate more, reducing water availability for downstream farmers.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream (Cooperate) | Downstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream (Cooperate)** | 5, 5 | 6, 3 |
| **Upstream (Defect)** | 3, 6 | 4, 4 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, upstream farmers have priority access to water. If both upstream and downstream farmers cooperate (irrigate moderately), they both get a moderate yield (5). If the upstream farmer defects (irrigates more), they get a higher yield (6), but the downstream farmer gets a lower yield (3). If the downstream farmer defects (tries to irrigate more), but the upstream farmer has already taken most of the water, the downstream farmer gets a lower yield (3 or 4).

#### 2. Fishing Competition Dilemma
**Tension:** Farmers may overfish, reducing the fish population and future catches.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers (Cooperate) | Other Farmers (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer (Cooperate)** | 4, 4 | 2, 6 |
| **Farmer (Defect)** | 6, 2 | 3, 3 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, farmers can choose to cooperate (catch a moderate amount of fish) or defect (catch as many fish as possible). If all farmers cooperate, they each get a moderate catch (4). If one farmer defects, they get a higher catch (6), but the other farmers get a lower catch (2). If all farmers defect, the fish population declines, and they all get a lower catch (3).

#### 3. Water Conservation vs. Irrigation Dilemma
**Tension:** Farmers must balance water conservation with irrigation needs to maintain ecological thresholds.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers (Conserve) | Other Farmers (Irrigate) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer (Conserve)** | 4, 4 | 3, 6 |
| **Farmer (Irrigate)** | 6, 3 | 5, 5 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, farmers must decide whether to conserve water or irrigate their fields. If all farmers conserve water, they each get a moderate yield (4). If one farmer irrigates, they get a higher yield (6), but the other farmers may face water scarcity (3). If all farmers irrigate, they all get a moderate yield (5), but the ecological threshold may be exceeded, leading to negative consequences.

#### 4. Spatial Asymmetry Dilemma
**Tension:** Upstream farmers have priority access to water, while downstream farmers have priority access to fish.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream (Prioritize Fish) | Downstream (Prioritize Water) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream (Prioritize Water)** | 6, 4 | 5, 5 |
| **Upstream (Prioritize Fish)** | 4, 6 | 3, 3 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, upstream farmers have priority access to water, while downstream farmers have priority access to fish. If the upstream farmer prioritizes water and the downstream farmer prioritizes fish, the upstream farmer gets a higher yield (6), and the downstream farmer gets a moderate catch (4). If both prioritize their respective resources, they both get a moderate yield/catch (5). If they both prioritize the other resource, they both get a lower yield/catch (3).
