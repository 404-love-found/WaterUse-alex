### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Water Usage
#### Tension: 
Strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers regarding water usage, where upstream farmers have priority access to water, but downstream farmers have priority access to fish.

#### Matrix:
|  | Upstream (Cooperate) | Upstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream (Cooperate)** | 5, 5 | 3, 7 |
| **Downstream (Defect)** | 7, 3 | 2, 2 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer predicts water availability and decides on the number of fields to irrigate. Upstream farmers have priority access to water, which may lead to downstream farmers facing water scarcity. The matrix reflects the strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers, where cooperation means using water sustainably, and defection means using as much water as possible. The payoffs are based on the number of fields that can be irrigated, with a maximum of 10 fields.

#### Tension: 
Strategic tension between farmers regarding fishing and water usage, where fishing affects the fish population, and water usage affects the available water for irrigation.

#### Matrix:

| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 2 (Sustainable Water Usage)** | 5, 5 | 4, 6 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer tries to catch a fixed target amount of fish, and fishing is not costly. However, overfishing can deplete the fish population, affecting future catches. The matrix reflects the strategic tension between farmers regarding fishing and water usage, where sustainable fishing and water usage lead to higher payoffs. The payoffs are based on the number of fields that can be irrigated and the fish catch, with ecological thresholds affecting the fish population.
