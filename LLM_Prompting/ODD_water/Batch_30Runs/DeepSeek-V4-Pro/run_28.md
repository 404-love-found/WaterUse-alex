U:U  (40,10)    (40,-20)

This is the same as before but with F=10 and irrigation profits adjusted. Now, the Nash equilibrium is (U,S) with payoffs (40,10). But is (U,S) really an equilibrium? If U chooses U, D's best response is S (10 > -20). If D chooses S, U's best response is U (40 > 10). So yes, (U,S) is Nash. (S,U) is not Nash because U would deviate to U (40 > 10). (S,S) is not Nash because U would deviate. (U,U) is not Nash because D would deviate. So the equilibrium is (U,S). This shows the spatial asymmetry: Upstream exploits his position, Downstream is forced to limit irrigation, and the fish collapse.

But wait: in (S,U), U gets 10 and D gets 40. That's better for D than (U,S) where D gets 10. So D would prefer (S,U) but cannot force U to choose S. U will choose U regardless. So the equilibrium is (U,S). That's the tragedy: the fish collapse, and Downstream suffers.
