### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations

#### 1. Upstream vs Downstream Irrigation
**Tension:** Over-extraction of water by upstream farmers affects downstream farmers' irrigation capabilities.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream Cooperates | Downstream Defects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Cooperates** | 5, 5 | 8, 2 |
| **Upstream Defects** | 2, 8 | 4, 4 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, upstream farmers have an incentive to extract more water, potentially leaving less for downstream farmers. This creates a strategic tension where each farmer must decide whether to cooperate (irrigate fewer fields) or defect (irrigate more fields). The payoff matrix reflects the spatial asymmetry, where upstream farmers can extract more water, but downstream farmers are more vulnerable to water scarcity.
