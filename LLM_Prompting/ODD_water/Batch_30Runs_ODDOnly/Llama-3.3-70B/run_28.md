### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Irrigation Dilemma
#### Tension: 
The strategic tension arises between an upstream farmer and a downstream farmer in the decentralized case, where each farmer decides on the number of fields to irrigate based on their prediction of water flows and available budget. The upstream farmer's decision affects the water availability for the downstream farmer.

#### Matrix:
|  | Downstream Farmer (Cooperate: Irrigate 5 fields, Defect: Irrigate 10 fields) |
| --- | --- |
| **Upstream Farmer (Cooperate: Irrigate 5 fields)** | Upstream: 20, Downstream: 15 | Upstream: 25, Downstream: 0 |
| **Upstream Farmer (Defect: Irrigate 10 fields)** | Upstream: 15, Downstream: 10 | Upstream: 20, Downstream: 5 |

#### Justification:
In this action situation, both farmers have the option to cooperate (irrigate 5 fields) or defect (irrigate 10 fields). If both cooperate, they receive moderate payoffs (20, 15). If the upstream farmer defects and the downstream farmer cooperates, the upstream farmer receives a higher payoff (25), while the downstream farmer receives nothing (0) due to water scarcity. If both defect, they both receive lower payoffs (20, 5) due to over-extraction of water.

### Action Situation 2: Fishing and Irrigation Trade-off
#### Tension: 
The strategic tension arises between two farmers, where one farmer prioritizes fishing and the other prioritizes irrigation. The farmer who prioritizes fishing may catch more fish, but the farmer who prioritizes irrigation may receive higher returns from agriculture.

#### Matrix:

| --- | --- |

| **Farmer 1 (Defect: Prioritize Irrigation)** | Farmer 1: 15, Farmer 2: 9 | Farmer 1: 18, Farmer 2: 15 |

#### Justification:
In this action situation, both farmers have the option to cooperate (prioritize fishing) or defect (prioritize irrigation). If both cooperate, they receive moderate payoffs (10, 12). If one farmer defects and prioritizes irrigation, they receive a higher payoff (18 or 20), while the other farmer receives a lower payoff (8 or 9) due to reduced fishing opportunities.

### Action Situation 3: Ecological Threshold and Water Extraction
#### Tension: 
The strategic tension arises between two farmers, where one farmer extracts water above the ecological threshold, causing a decline in fish population and affecting the other farmer's fishing opportunities.

#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer 2 (Cooperate: Extract water below threshold, Defect: Extract water above threshold) |
| --- | --- |
| **Farmer 1 (Cooperate: Extract water below threshold)** | Farmer 1: 12, Farmer 2: 10 | Farmer 1: 8, Farmer 2: 15 |
| **Farmer 1 (Defect: Extract water above threshold)** | Farmer 1: 15, Farmer 2: 5 | Farmer 1: 10, Farmer 2: 10 |

#### Justification:
In this action situation, both farmers have the option to cooperate (extract water below the ecological threshold) or defect (extract water above the threshold). If both cooperate, they receive moderate payoffs (12, 10). If one farmer defects and extracts water above the threshold, they receive a higher payoff (15), while the other farmer receives a lower payoff (5 or 8) due to the decline in fish population. However, if both defect, they both receive lower payoffs (10, 10) due to the degradation of the ecosystem.
