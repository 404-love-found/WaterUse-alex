### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Water Usage
#### Tension: 
Water usage by upstream farmers affects downstream farmers' access to water, creating a strategic tension between individual interests and collective well-being.

#### Matrix:
|  | Upstream (Cooperate) | Upstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream (Cooperate)** | 5, 5 | 2, 8 |
| **Downstream (Defect)** | 8, 2 | 3, 3 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer decides on the number of fields to irrigate. Upstream farmers have priority access to water, which can lead to downstream farmers facing water scarcity. The matrix reflects the payoff for each possible combination of actions, where cooperation means irrigating a moderate number of fields (5) and defection means irrigating the maximum number of fields (10). The payoffs are based on the assumption that excessive water usage by upstream farmers will harm downstream farmers.

The payoffs are based on the assumption that the fish population is sensitive to water inflow and fishing pressure.
