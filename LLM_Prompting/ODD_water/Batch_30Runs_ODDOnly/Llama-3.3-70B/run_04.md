### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Water Usage
#### Tension: 
Water usage by an upstream farmer affects the water available to a downstream farmer, creating a strategic tension between maximizing individual irrigation needs and ensuring sufficient water flow downstream.

#### Matrix:

|  | Downstream Conserves | Downstream Irrigates |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Conserves** | 5, 3 | 2, 6 |
| **Upstream Irrigates** | 6, 1 | 3, 4 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer decides on the number of irrigated fields independently. An upstream farmer has more control over the water flow, potentially affecting the downstream farmer's access to water. The matrix reflects the strategic tension where both farmers must decide whether to conserve water (potentially leading to lower yields but ensuring water for the other) or irrigate maximally (potentially maximizing yields but risking water scarcity for the other). The numbers represent the payoff in terms of fields irrigated, with higher numbers indicating better outcomes for the farmer.
