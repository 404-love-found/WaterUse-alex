### Action Situation 1: Upstream Farmer's Irrigation Decision
#### Tension: 
Over-irrigation by upstream farmers may reduce water availability for downstream farmers, creating a strategic tension between individual gain and collective sustainability.

#### Matrix:
|  | Downstream Farmer Cooperates (Conserves Water) | Downstream Farmer Defects (Over-irrigates) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Farmer Cooperates (Conserves Water)** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 8 | Upstream: 9, Downstream: 5 |
| **Upstream Farmer Defects (Over-irrigates)** | Upstream: 10, Downstream: 4 | Upstream: 6, Downstream: 3 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer predicts water availability and decides on the number of fields to irrigate. Upstream farmers have an advantage in accessing water, but over-irrigation can harm downstream farmers. The matrix reflects the tension between individual gain (irrigating more fields) and collective sustainability (conserving water for downstream farmers). The numbers represent the payoff in terms of the number of fields irrigated, with a maximum of 10 fields.
