### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations
#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Water Withdrawal
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may withdraw more water, affecting downstream farmers' irrigation and fishing.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream Conserves | Downstream Withdraws |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Conserves** | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 3 |
| **Upstream Withdraws** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 2 | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 1 |

**Justification:** The matrix reflects the strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers. If both conserve water, they both get a moderate payoff (5 fields irrigated). If the upstream farmer withdraws more water, the downstream farmer's payoff decreases, regardless of their action.

#### 2. Fishing Resource Management
**Tension:** Farmers may overfish, depleting the resource and affecting their own and others' future catches.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers Conserve | Other Farmers Overfish |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Conserve** | You: 3, Other: 3 | You: 2, Other: 4 |

#### 3. Ecological Threshold Management
**Tension:** Farmers may exceed the ecological threshold, causing a tipping point that affects all farmers' water availability and fish catches.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers Stay Below Threshold | Other Farmers Exceed Threshold |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Stay Below** | You: 6, Other: 6 | You: 4, Other: 2 |
| **Exceed** | You: 8, Other: 4 | You: 0, Other: 0 |

**Justification:** The matrix reflects the strategic tension around exceeding the ecological threshold. If all farmers stay below the threshold, they each get a high payoff (6 fields irrigated). If one farmer exceeds the threshold, their payoff increases, but the other farmers' payoffs decrease. If all farmers exceed the threshold, the ecosystem collapses, and all payoffs are zero.
