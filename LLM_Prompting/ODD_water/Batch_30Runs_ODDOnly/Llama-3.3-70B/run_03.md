### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations

#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Water Extraction Tension
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may extract more water, reducing downstream farmers' access.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream Cooperates | Downstream Defects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Cooperates** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 6 | Upstream: 9, Downstream: 3 |
| **Upstream Defects** | Upstream: 10, Downstream: 2 | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 4 |

**Justification:** In the decentralized case, upstream farmers have an incentive to extract more water, as it directly benefits their crops. However, this reduces the water available to downstream farmers, creating a strategic tension. The matrix reflects the spatial asymmetry, where upstream farmers have more control over water extraction.

#### 2. Fishing Resource Competition Tension
**Tension:** Farmers may overfish, depleting the resource and affecting future catches.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers Cooperate | Other Farmers Defect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Cooperates** | Farmer: 5, Other Farmers: 5 | Farmer: 3, Other Farmers: 7 |
| **Farmer Defects** | Farmer: 7, Other Farmers: 3 | Farmer: 5, Other Farmers: 5 |

**Justification:** The fish population is a common-pool resource, and farmers may have an incentive to overfish, depleting the resource. The matrix reflects the ecological threshold, where overfishing can lead to a tipping point, reducing future catches.

#### 3. Irrigation Investment Tension
**Tension:** Farmers may invest in more fields, increasing their water demands and potentially exceeding ecological thresholds.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers Invest | Other Farmers Do Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Invests** | Farmer: 9, Other Farmers: 5 | Farmer: 10, Other Farmers: 6 |
| **Farmer Does Not Invest** | Farmer: 6, Other Farmers: 9 | Farmer: 8, Other Farmers: 8 |

**Justification:** Farmers may invest in more fields to increase their yields, but this increases their water demands. If multiple farmers invest, they may exceed the ecological threshold, reducing water availability and affecting future yields. The matrix reflects the strategic tension, where farmers must balance their investment decisions with the potential risks of exceeding ecological thresholds.

#### 4. Water Conservation vs. Crop Yield Tension
**Tension:** Farmers may conserve water, reducing their crop yields, or use more water, potentially exceeding ecological thresholds.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers Conserve | Other Farmers Do Not Conserve |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Conserves** | Farmer: 7, Other Farmers: 7 | Farmer: 5, Other Farmers: 9 |
| **Farmer Does Not Conserve** | Farmer: 9, Other Farmers: 5 | Farmer: 8, Other Farmers: 8 |

**Justification:** Farmers must balance their water use with their crop yields. Conserving water may reduce yields, but using more water may exceed ecological thresholds, reducing future water availability. The matrix reflects the strategic tension, where farmers must weigh the benefits of conserving water against the potential costs to their crop yields.
