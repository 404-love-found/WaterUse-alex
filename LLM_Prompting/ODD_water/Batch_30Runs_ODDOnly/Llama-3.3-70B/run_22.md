### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Water Allocation
#### Tension: 
The strategic tension arises between an upstream farmer and a downstream farmer in the decentralized case, where each farmer decides on the number of fields to irrigate based on their prediction of water flows and available budget. The upstream farmer's decision affects the downstream farmer's water availability, creating a spatial asymmetry.

#### Matrix:
|  | Downstream Farmer Cooperates (Irrigates Less) | Downstream Farmer Defects (Irrigates More) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Farmer Cooperates (Irrigates Less)** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 6 | Upstream: 7, Downstream: 5 |
| **Upstream Farmer Defects (Irrigates More)** | Upstream: 9, Downstream: 4 | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 3 |

#### Justification:
In this action situation, both farmers have to decide how many fields to irrigate. If both cooperate (irrigate less), they both get a moderate payoff (8 for the upstream farmer, 6 for the downstream farmer), reflecting a balance between water usage and availability. If the upstream farmer defects (irrigates more) and the downstream farmer cooperates, the upstream farmer gets a higher payoff (9), but the downstream farmer's payoff decreases (4) due to reduced water availability. If both defect, they both suffer from reduced payoffs (8 for the upstream, 3 for the downstream) due to over-irrigation and water scarcity.

### Action Situation 2: Fishing Resource Management
#### Tension: 
The strategic tension arises among farmers regarding the management of the fishing resource. Each farmer tries to catch a fixed target amount of fish, but the fishing activity is not costly and does not affect agricultural activities. The ecological threshold of fish population growth and the spatial asymmetry of access to the fishing lake create a dilemma.

#### Matrix:
|  | Other Farmers Cooperate (Catch Less) | Other Farmers Defect (Catch More) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Cooperates (Catches Less)** | Farmer: 5, Others: 4 | Farmer: 3, Others: 6 |
| **Farmer Defects (Catches More)** | Farmer: 7, Others: 2 | Farmer: 5, Others: 4 |

#### Justification:
In this action situation, each farmer must decide how much to catch. If all farmers cooperate (catch less), they all get a moderate payoff (5 for the farmer, 4 for others), ensuring the sustainability of the fish population. If a farmer defects (catches more) while others cooperate, the defecting farmer gets a higher payoff (7), but others suffer (2) due to overfishing. If all defect, they all suffer reduced payoffs (5 for the farmer, 4 for others) due to the depletion of the fish population, reflecting the ecological threshold.

### Action Situation 3: Water Flow Prediction and Irrigation Strategy
#### Tension: 
The strategic tension arises for a farmer in deciding how to predict water flows and choose an irrigation strategy. The farmer must balance the risk of over-irrigation or under-irrigation based on water flow predictions, considering the spatial asymmetry and ecological thresholds.

#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer Predicts Low Water Flow | Farmer Predicts High Water Flow |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Irrigates Less** | Farmer: 6, Yield: 5 | Farmer: 4, Yield: 8 |
| **Farmer Irrigates More** | Farmer: 3, Yield: 9 | Farmer: 8, Yield: 6 |

#### Justification:
In this action situation, the farmer must decide on an irrigation strategy based on predicted water flows. If the farmer predicts low water flow and irrigates less, they get a moderate payoff (6) with a yield of 5. If they predict high water flow and irrigate more, they get a higher payoff (8) with a yield of 6, reflecting efficient water usage. However, incorrect predictions lead to lower payoffs (3 if predicting low but irrigating more, 4 if predicting high but irrigating less), highlighting the risk of misjudging water availability.

### Action Situation 4: Budget Allocation Between Irrigation and Fishing
#### Tension: 
The strategic tension arises for a farmer in allocating the budget between irrigation for agricultural activities and fishing for subsistence or investment. The spatial asymmetry and ecological thresholds influence this decision.

#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer Allocates More to Irrigation | Farmer Allocates More to Fishing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer Prioritizes Agriculture** | Farmer: 7, Fishing: 2 | Farmer: 5, Fishing: 5 |
| **Farmer Prioritizes Fishing** | Farmer: 4, Fishing: 6 | Farmer: 6, Fishing: 7 |

#### Justification:
In this action situation, the farmer must allocate the budget between irrigation and fishing. If the farmer prioritizes agriculture and allocates more to irrigation, they get a higher payoff from agriculture (7) but a lower payoff from fishing (2). If they prioritize fishing, they get a higher payoff from fishing (7) but a moderate payoff from agriculture (6), reflecting the balance between subsistence and investment needs. Incorrect allocations lead to reduced payoffs in both activities, highlighting the importance of balancing budget allocation.
