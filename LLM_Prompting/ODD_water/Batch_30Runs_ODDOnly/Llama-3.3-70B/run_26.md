### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations
#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Water Extraction Tension
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may extract more water, reducing downstream farmers' access.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream Conserves | Downstream Extracts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Conserves** | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 3 |
| **Upstream Extracts** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 2 | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 1 |

**Justification:** This matrix reflects the strategic tension between upstream and downstream farmers. If both conserve water, they both get a moderate amount (5 fields). If the upstream farmer extracts more water, the downstream farmer's access is reduced, regardless of their own extraction decision.

#### 2. Fishing Resource Competition Tension
**Tension:** Farmers may overfish, depleting the resource and affecting their own and others' future catches.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers Conserve | Other Farmers Overfish |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Conserve** | Catch: 10, Other Catch: 10 | Catch: 5, Other Catch: 15 |
| **Overfish** | Catch: 15, Other Catch: 5 | Catch: 8, Other Catch: 8 |

**Justification:** This matrix represents the competition for the fishing resource. If all farmers conserve, they all get a high catch (10). If one farmer overfishes while others conserve, they get a higher catch, but the others' catch is reduced.

#### 3. Ecological Threshold Tension
**Tension:** Farmers' water extraction may exceed ecological thresholds, affecting fish population and their own future water availability.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers Stay Below Threshold | Other Farmers Exceed Threshold |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Stay Below Threshold** | Yield: 8, Water Availability: 10 | Yield: 5, Water Availability: 5 |
| **Exceed Threshold** | Yield: 10, Water Availability: 0 | Yield: 2, Water Availability: 0 |

**Justification:** This matrix reflects the tension between staying below ecological thresholds and exceeding them. If all farmers stay below the threshold, they maintain a moderate yield and high water availability. If one farmer exceeds the threshold, their yield may increase, but water availability is reduced for all.

#### 4. Budget and Investment Tension
**Tension:** Farmers must balance their budget and investment decisions, considering the uncertainty of water availability and crop yields.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers Invest | Other Farmers Do Not Invest |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Invest** | Yield: 12, Budget: 5 | Yield: 10, Budget: 8 |
| **Do Not Invest** | Yield: 8, Budget: 10 | Yield: 5, Budget: 12 |

**Justification:** This matrix represents the tension between investing in irrigation and not investing. If all farmers invest, they get a high yield, but their budget is reduced. If one farmer invests while others do not, they get a moderate yield and a higher budget.

#### 5. Memory Capacity and Water Prediction Tension
**Tension:** Farmers with different memory capacities may make different water extraction decisions, affecting their own and others' yields.
**Matrix:**

|  | Other Farmers Have High Memory | Other Farmers Have Low Memory |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **High Memory** | Yield: 10, Water Extraction: 8 | Yield: 12, Water Extraction: 5 |
| **Low Memory** | Yield: 8, Water Extraction: 10 | Yield: 5, Water Extraction: 12 |

**Justification:** This matrix reflects the tension between farmers with high and low memory capacities. If all farmers have high memory, they extract less water and get a moderate yield. If one farmer has high memory while others have low memory, they extract less water and get a higher yield.
