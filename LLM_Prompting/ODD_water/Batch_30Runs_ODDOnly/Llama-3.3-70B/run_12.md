### Action Situation 1: Upstream-Downstream Water Allocation
#### Tension: 
Water usage by upstream farmers affects downstream farmers' access to water, creating a strategic tension between individual interests and collective well-being.

#### Matrix:
|  | Upstream (Cooperate) | Upstream (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Downstream (Cooperate)** | 5, 5 | 2, 8 |
| **Downstream (Defect)** | 8, 2 | 3, 3 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, each farmer predicts water availability and decides on the number of fields to irrigate. Upstream farmers have more control over water usage, potentially affecting downstream farmers. The matrix reflects the spatial asymmetry, where upstream farmers can defect (use more water) and receive a higher payoff (8), while downstream farmers cooperate (use less water) and receive a lower payoff (2). If both cooperate, they receive a moderate payoff (5, 5). If both defect, they both receive a low payoff (3, 3) due to ecological thresholds (water scarcity).

### Action Situation 2: Fishing Resource Management
#### Tension: 
Farmers' fishing activities impact the fish population, creating a strategic tension between individual fishing interests and collective sustainability of the resource.

#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer 1 (Cooperate) | Farmer 1 (Defect) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 2 (Cooperate)** | 4, 4 | 1, 6 |
| **Farmer 2 (Defect)** | 6, 1 | 2, 2 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, farmers access the fishing lake and try to catch a fixed target amount of fish. The matrix reflects the tension between individual fishing interests and collective sustainability. If both farmers cooperate (limit their catch), they receive a moderate payoff (4, 4). If one defects (overfishes) and the other cooperates, the defector receives a higher payoff (6), while the cooperator receives a lower payoff (1). If both defect, they both receive a low payoff (2, 2) due to ecological thresholds (fish population depletion).

### Action Situation 3: Irrigation Investment
#### Tension: 
Farmers' decisions to invest in irrigation affect their individual yields and the overall water availability, creating a strategic tension between individual investment interests and collective water management.

#### Matrix:
|  | Farmer 1 (Invest) | Farmer 1 (Not Invest) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Farmer 2 (Invest)** | 6, 6 | 4, 8 |
| **Farmer 2 (Not Invest)** | 8, 4 | 5, 5 |

#### Justification:
In the decentralized case, farmers decide whether to invest in irrigation based on their predicted water availability and budget. The matrix reflects the tension between individual investment interests and collective water management. If both farmers invest, they receive a high payoff (6, 6). If one invests and the other does not, the investor receives a higher payoff (8), while the non-investor receives a lower payoff (4). If both do not invest, they both receive a moderate payoff (5, 5) due to reduced water usage. The spatial asymmetry and ecological thresholds are reflected in the payoffs, where investment in irrigation can lead to higher yields but also increased water usage, affecting downstream farmers.
