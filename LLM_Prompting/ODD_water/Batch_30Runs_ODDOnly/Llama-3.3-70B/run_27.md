### Decentralized Water Use Action Situations
#### 1. Upstream-Downstream Water Withdrawal
**Tension:** Upstream farmers may withdraw more water, reducing downstream water availability.
**Matrix:**

|  | Downstream Cooperates | Downstream Defects |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Upstream Cooperates** | Upstream: 5, Downstream: 5 | Upstream: 6, Downstream: 2 |
| **Upstream Defects** | Upstream: 8, Downstream: 1 | Upstream: 7, Downstream: 3 |

**Justification:** Upstream farmers have an incentive to withdraw more water, as it increases their yield. However, this reduces the water available to downstream farmers, creating a strategic tension. The matrix reflects the spatial asymmetry, where upstream farmers have more control over water withdrawal.

#### 3. Irrigation Investment
**Tension:** Farmers may invest in more fields, increasing their water demand and potentially exceeding the available water supply.
**Matrix:**

#### 4. Water Conservation vs. Yield Maximization
**Tension:** Farmers may choose to conserve water or maximize their yield, affecting their own and others' water availability.

|  | Other Farmers Conserve | Other Farmers Maximize Yield |

The matrix reflects the strategic tension between individual yield maximization and collective water conservation.
