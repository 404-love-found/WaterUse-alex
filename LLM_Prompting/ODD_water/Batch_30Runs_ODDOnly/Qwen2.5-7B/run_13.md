### Title: Strategic Tensions in Decentralized Water Use Model (DV)

#### 1. Strategic Tension: Upstream vs. Downstream Farmers' Irrigation Decisions

**Tension:**
Upstream and downstream farmers face a trade-off between maximizing their individual yields and ensuring sufficient water flow for downstream farmers. If upstream farmers irrigate too many fields, it can lead to insufficient water reaching downstream farmers, affecting their yields and potentially causing a collapse in the fish population.

**Action Situations:**
- **Upstream:** Irrigate 6-10 fields.
- **Downstream:** Irrigate 6-10 fields.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Downstream Irrigates 6 | Downstream Irrigates 7 | Downstream Irrigates 8 | Downstream Irrigates 9 | Downstream Irrigates 10 |
|----------------|-----------------------|-----------------------|-----------------------|-----------------------|------------------------|
| **Upstream**   | (3, 3)                | (4, 3)                | (5, 3)                | (6, 3)                | (7, 3)                 |
| **Irrigates 6**| (3, 3)                | (3, 4)                | (3, 5)                | (3, 6)                | (3, 7)                 |
| **Irrigates 7**| (3, 4)                | (4, 4)                | (4, 5)                | (4, 6)                | (4, 7)                 |
| **Irrigates 8**| (3, 5)                | (4, 5)                | (5, 5)                | (5, 6)                | (5, 7)                 |
| **Irrigates 9**| (3, 6)                | (4, 6)                | (5, 6)                | (6, 6)                | (6, 7)                 |
| **Irrigates 10**| (3, 7)                | (4, 7)                | (5, 7)                | (6, 7)                | (7, 7)                 |

**Justification:**
- **Spatial Asymmetry:** Upstream farmers have more control over water flow and can potentially over-irrigate, leading to less water reaching downstream farmers.
- **Ecological Thresholds:** If too many fields are irrigated, the water flow can drop below a critical threshold, affecting the fish population and downstream farmers' yields.
- **Strategic Tension:** Upstream farmers must balance their own yield with the need to maintain sufficient water for downstream farmers to avoid a collapse in the fish population.

#### 2. Strategic Tension: Income Threshold and Risk-Taking

**Tension:**
Farmers who have not met their income threshold in the past year are more likely to take risks and increase the number of irrigated fields, while those who have met their threshold are more cautious.

**Action Situations:**
- **Risk-Taking Farmers:** Irrigate 8-10 fields.
- **Cautious Farmers:** Irrigate 6-7 fields.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                | Cautious (6-7) | Cautious (6-7) | Cautious (6-7) | Cautious (6-7) | Cautious (6-7) |
|----------------|----------------|----------------|----------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Risk-Taking**| (6, 5)         | (7, 5)         | (8, 5)         | (9, 5)         | (10, 5)        |
| **Irrigates 8**| (5, 6)         | (6, 6)         | (7, 6)         | (8, 6)         | (9, 6)         |
| **Irrigates 9**| (5, 7)         | (6, 7)         | (7, 7)         | (8, 7)         | (9, 7)         |
| **Irrigates 10**| (5, 8)         | (6, 8)         | (7, 8)         | (8, 8)         | (9, 8)         |

**Justification:**
- **Spatial Asymmetry:** Different farmers have varying incomes based on their location and water availability.
- **Ecological Thresholds:** Over-irrigation can lead to ecological tipping points, such as reduced fish populations.
- **Strategic Tension:** Risk-taking farmers must balance the potential for higher yields with the risk of over-irrigating and causing ecological damage.

**Tension:**
Farmers must balance their water flow predictions with their budget constraints to determine the optimal number of fields to irrigate.

**Action Situations:**

**Justification:**

- **Strategic Tension:** Farmers must balance their water flow predictions with their budget constraints to avoid over-irrigating and causing ecological damage.
