### Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Case (DV) Using the IAD Framework

#### 1. **Spatial Asymmetry: Upstream vs Downstream**
**Tension:**
- **Upstream Farmers vs Downstream Farmers**
- **Strategic Tension:** Upstream farmers have an advantage in accessing water resources earlier, which can lead to a strategic advantage in irrigating more fields or catching more fish, potentially depleting resources for downstream farmers.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|                  | Upstream Farmers Irrigate 8 Fields | Upstream Farmers Irrigate 9 Fields |
|------------------|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Downstream Farmers Irrigate 8 Fields** | (8, 8)                             | (7, 9)                            |
| **Downstream Farmers Irrigate 9 Fields** | (9, 7)                             | (8, 8)                            |

**Justification:**
- **Upstream Farmers Irrigate 8 Fields:**
  - Downstream farmers also irrigate 8 fields. Both farmers have a moderate yield, but upstream farmers have a slight advantage due to their earlier access to water.
  - Downstream farmers irrigate 9 fields. Upstream farmers have a higher yield, but downstream farmers have a lower yield due to potential water stress.
- **Upstream Farmers Irrigate 9 Fields:**
  - Downstream farmers irrigate 8 fields. Upstream farmers have a higher yield, but downstream farmers have a lower yield due to potential water stress.
  - Downstream farmers irrigate 9 fields. Both farmers have an equal yield, but upstream farmers risk depleting water resources for downstream farmers.
