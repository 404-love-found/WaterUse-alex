### Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Version (DV) Using the IAD Framework

#### Title: Spatial Asymmetry and Ecological Thresholds in Decentralized Water Use

#### Tension 1: Spatial Asymmetry vs. Ecological Thresholds

**Justification:** In the decentralized version, farmers make decisions based on their locations along the river stretch, which introduces spatial asymmetry. This asymmetry can lead to conflicts when downstream farmers have less access to water due to upstream water withdrawals, potentially causing ecological thresholds to be exceeded, such as the minimum water flow required to sustain the fish population.

**Action Situations:**
- **Upstream Farmer (U):** Decides to irrigate up to 10 fields.
- **Downstream Farmer (D):** Decides to irrigate up to 10 fields.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| U \ D  | 0-9  | 10   |
|--------|------|------|
| 0-9    | (4,4)| (3,5)|
| 10     | (5,3)| (2,2)|

**Explanation:**
- If both farmers irrigate up to 9 fields, they both have moderate returns (4,4).
- If both irrigate up to 10 fields, they both have lower returns (2,2) due to the ecological threshold.
- If the upstream farmer irrigates up to 10 fields and the downstream farmer irrigates up to 9 fields, the downstream farmer benefits from a higher return (5,3) as the upstream withdrawal is less severe.
- Conversely, if the downstream farmer irrigates up to 10 fields and the upstream farmer irrigates up to 9 fields, the upstream farmer benefits from a higher return (3,5) as the downstream withdrawal is less severe.

#### Tension 2: Risk Aversion vs. Risk Taking

**Justification:** Farmers have different risk tolerance levels. Downstream farmers, who are closer to the fishing lake, might be more inclined to take risks by increasing the number of irrigated fields, hoping for higher yields despite the risk of water shortages. Upstream farmers, who have less immediate access to fish resources, might be more risk-averse and maintain a stable number of irrigated fields.

**Action Situations:**
- **Upstream Farmer (U):** Decides to irrigate up to 10 fields.
- **Downstream Farmer (D):** Decides to irrigate up to 10 fields.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

| U \ D  | 0-9  | 10   |
|--------|------|------|
| 0-9    | (4,4)| (3,5)|
| 10     | (5,3)| (2,2)|

**Explanation:**
- Similar to the previous matrix, but with a focus on risk aversion and risk-taking behavior.
- Downstream farmers (D) might choose to irrigate up to 10 fields, hoping for higher yields, while upstream farmers (U) might stick to 9 fields to avoid water stress.
- The matrix reflects the potential benefits and costs of each decision, with downstream farmers potentially benefiting from higher yields but facing the risk of water shortages.
