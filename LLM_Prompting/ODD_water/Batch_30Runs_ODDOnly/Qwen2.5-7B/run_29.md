### Distinct Action Situations in the Decentralized Version (DV)

#### Title: Spatially Asymmetric Water Allocation and Yield Trade-offs

#### Tension: 
**Upstream vs. Downstream Farmers' Water Allocation Decisions and Their Impact on Yields**

In the decentralized version, each farmer makes decisions independently based on their location along the river. Upstream farmers have less access to water and thus face more severe water stress compared to downstream farmers. This creates a strategic tension between maximizing yields and ensuring sustainable water use, particularly when considering the ecological thresholds and spatial asymmetry.

#### 2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|           | Upstream (U) | Downstream (D) |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| **U**     | (4, 6)       | (3, 3)         |
| **D**     | (6, 4)       | (5, 5)         |

**Justification:**
1. **Spatial Asymmetry and Ecological Thresholds:**
   - **Upstream (U):** Farmers in the upstream region have less water availability and thus face higher water stress. They have to decide whether to allocate water to fewer but higher-yielding fields or to more fields with lower yields to ensure sustainable water use.
   - **Downstream (D):** Farmers in the downstream region have better access to water and can allocate water to more fields with higher yields without facing severe water stress.

2. **Payoff Interpretation:**
   - **(4, 6):** Upstream farmer allocates water to fewer fields, resulting in lower yields (4). Downstream farmer allocates water to more fields, resulting in higher yields (6). This reflects the trade-off where upstream farmers prioritize sustainability over maximum yield.
   - **(3, 3):** Both upstream and downstream farmers allocate water to fewer fields, resulting in lower yields (3 each). This reflects a scenario where both farmers are constrained by water stress.
   - **(6, 4):** Upstream farmer allocates water to more fields, resulting in higher yields (6). Downstream farmer allocates water to fewer fields, resulting in lower yields (4). This reflects a scenario where upstream farmers prioritize higher yield over sustainability.
   - **(5, 5):** Both upstream and downstream farmers allocate water to a moderate number of fields, resulting in moderate yields (5 each). This reflects a balanced approach to water allocation.

#### Title: Ecological Tipping Points and Fish Population Dynamics

#### Tension: 
**Fish Population Survival and Farmer Yields in Response to Water Inflows**

The fish population is sensitive to water inflows, and there are ecological thresholds that can lead to population collapse if these thresholds are not met. Farmers need to balance their water allocation decisions to ensure both fish population survival and their own yields.

#### 2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix

|           | High Inflow (H) | Low Inflow (L) |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **H**     | (7, 7)          | (4, 4)         |
| **L**     | (4, 4)          | (6, 6)         |

**Justification:**
1. **Ecological Tipping Points:**
   - **High Inflow (H):** The fish population can survive if the water inflow is high, allowing for sufficient migration and survival of age classes. Both farmers benefit from higher yields (7).
   - **Low Inflow (L):** The fish population is at risk of collapse if the water inflow is low, leading to lower yields for both farmers (4).

2. **Payoff Interpretation:**
   - **(7, 7):** High water inflow ensures fish population survival, and both farmers benefit from higher yields (7).
   - **(4, 4):** Low water inflow results in fish population collapse, and both farmers face lower yields (4).
   - **(4, 4):** Low water inflow results in fish population collapse, and both farmers face lower yields (4).
   - **(6, 6):** Low water inflow is sufficient for fish population survival, and both farmers benefit from moderate yields (6).

#### Title: Economic Constraints and Yield Optimization

#### Tension: 
**Farming Budget Constraints and Optimal Water Allocation**

Farmers must balance their budget constraints with the need to allocate water to maximize yields. There is a trade-off between spending more on irrigation to increase yields and maintaining sustainable water use.

#### 2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix
