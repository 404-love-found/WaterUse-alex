# Run 1 - Qwen/Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Resident vs. Non-Resident Charging Conflict
**Tension:** A non-resident and a resident both need to charge their EVs, but only one charger is available. The resident has a discounted rate, creating a potential conflict over who should use the charger.
**Matrix:**
| Player A / Player B | Resident Charges | Non-Resident Charges |
|---|---|---|
| Resident Charges | (Discounted Cost, Higher Cost) | (Discounted Cost, Higher Cost + Waiting Time) |
| Non-Resident Charges | (Higher Cost + Waiting Time, Discounted Cost) | (Higher Cost + Waiting Time, Higher Cost + Waiting Time) |
**Justification:** This situation highlights the conflict between a resident and a non-resident over the limited charger. The resident has a financial incentive to use the charger, while the non-resident faces a higher cost and potential waiting time.

### Action Situation 2: Early Bird Charging Strategy
**Tension:** A resident wants to ensure they have a charger for their EV before returning home, leading to potential overuse or early charging.
**Matrix:**
| Player A / Player B | Early Charge | Normal Charge |
|---|---|---|
| Early Charge | (Early Access, Potential Overuse) | (No Early Access, Normal Usage) |
| Normal Charge | (No Early Access, Normal Usage) | (No Early Access, Normal Usage) |
**Justification:** This situation involves a resident who might use the charger early to secure it for their return, potentially at the expense of other residents or non-residents who need it later.

### Action Situation 3: Queueing for Chargers
**Tension:** Multiple residents are waiting to use a charger, and the queueing system is not clearly defined, leading to disputes.
**Matrix:**
| Player A / Player B | Queue First | Queue Later |
|---|---|---|
| Queue First | (First Access, Slightly Longer Wait) | (Second Access, Longer Wait) |
| Queue Later | (Second Access, Longer Wait) | (Third Access, Very Long Wait) |
**Justification:** This situation arises from the uncertainty of the queueing system, leading to potential disputes over who should be first in line, especially if the queue is not managed fairly.

### Action Situation 4: Charging Time Limits
**Tension:** A resident needs to charge their EV for a longer duration than allowed, potentially cutting off a non-resident who arrives later.
**Matrix:**
| Player A / Player B | Exceed Time Limit | Follow Time Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Exceed Time Limit | (Full Charge, Disruption to Others) | (Partial Charge, No Disruption) |
| Follow Time Limit | (Partial Charge, No Disruption) | (Partial Charge, No Disruption) |
**Justification:** This situation involves a resident who might exceed the charging time limit, potentially disrupting the charging plans of other users, including non-residents who arrive later.

### Action Situation 5: Priority Charging System
**Tension:** Implementing a priority system (e.g., residents first, then non-residents) could lead to disputes over fairness and adherence to the system.
**Matrix:**
| Player A / Player B | Prioritize Residents | Prioritize Non-Residents |
|---|---|---|
| Prioritize Residents | (Residents First, Fairness) | (Residents Wait, Unfairness) |
| Prioritize Non-Residents | (Non-Residents First, Unfairness) | (Non-Residents Wait, Fairness) |
**Justification:** This situation involves the implementation of a priority system, which could lead to disputes if not adhered to or if residents believe the system is unfair.
