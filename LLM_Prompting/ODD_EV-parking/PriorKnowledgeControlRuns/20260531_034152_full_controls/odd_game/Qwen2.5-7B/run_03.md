# Run 3 - Qwen/Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct-Turbo

### Action Situation 1: Queue Bypassing
**Tension:** A resident or non-resident user may bypass the queue to gain immediate access to a charging bay, creating a conflict with the posted rules and fair queue order.
**Matrix:**
| Player A / Player B | Resident Bypasses Queue | Non-Resident Bypasses Queue |
|---|---|---|
| Resident Bypasses Queue | (Higher Wait for Others, Higher Cost for Others) | (Higher Wait for Others, Higher Cost for Others) |
| Non-Resident Bypasses Queue | (Higher Wait for Others, Higher Cost for Others) | (Higher Wait for Others, Higher Cost for Others) |
**Justification:** This situation arises when a user decides to bypass the queue to gain immediate access, which conflicts with the posted rules and affects the waiting time and cost for other users.

### Action Situation 2: Prompt Move-Out After Charging
**Tension:** A user may choose to remain in a charging bay after completing their energy requirement, which delays the next user's access to the charger.
**Matrix:**
| Player A / Player B | Prompt Move-Out | Delayed Move-Out |
|---|---|---|
| Prompt Move-Out | (Shorter Wait for Next User, Lower Cost for Next User) | (Higher Wait for Next User, Higher Cost for Next User) |
| Delayed Move-Out | (Higher Wait for Next User, Higher Cost for Next User) | (Shorter Wait for Next User, Lower Cost for Next User) |
**Justification:** This situation highlights the tension between a user's desire to complete their charging and move out promptly versus the need to respect queue order and avoid delaying the next user.
