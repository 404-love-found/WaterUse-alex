### Action Situation 1: Queue Entry Compliance
**Tension:** Individual drivers can jump the queue to reduce their wait time, but if many do so, the queue system collapses and everyone waits longer.
**Matrix:**
| User A / User B | Respect Queue | Bypass Queue |
|---|---|---|
| Respect Queue | (3, 3) | (1, 4) |
| Bypass Queue | (4, 1) | (2, 2) |
**Justification:** The ODD+D states that users decide “whether to join or bypass a queue” (II.ii.a). The scenario describes that bypassing gives the individual a shorter wait but imposes longer waits on others, a classic social dilemma (Prisoner’s Dilemma). Payoffs are inferred ordinal rankings (4=best, 1=worst).
