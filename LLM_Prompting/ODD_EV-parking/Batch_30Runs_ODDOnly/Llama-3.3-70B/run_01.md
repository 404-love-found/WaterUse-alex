### Action Situation 1: Queue Fairness
#### Tension: User Compliance vs. Queue Bypassing
#### Matrix:
|  | User Complies | User Bypasses |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Staff Enforces** | 3, 3 | 0, 5 |
| **Staff Doesn't Enforce** | 2, 4 | 1, 1 |
#### Justification: This action situation reflects the strategic tension between users deciding whether to comply with the queue or bypass it and staff deciding whether to enforce queue rules or not. The payoff matrix shows that when both users comply and staff enforce, both receive a moderate payoff (3, 3). If staff doesn't enforce and users comply, users receive a lower payoff (2) due to potential frustration, while staff receives a higher payoff (4) from less conflict. If users bypass the queue and staff enforces, the bypassing user receives a high payoff (5) for saving time but staff receives a low payoff (0) due to the effort in enforcing. If neither enforces nor complies, both receive low payoffs (1, 1) due to chaos and mistrust.

| **Staff Intervenes** | 4, 4 | 1, 2 |
| **Staff Doesn't Intervene** | 3, 5 | 0, 0 |
#### Justification: This situation involves the tension between users deciding how long to occupy a charger and staff deciding whether to intervene in cases of overstay. The matrix shows that when users move promptly and staff intervenes, both receive a high payoff (4, 4) due to efficient charger use. If staff doesn't intervene and users move promptly, users receive a moderate payoff (3) from easy access, while staff receives a high payoff (5) from less work. If neither moves promptly nor intervenes, both receive low payoffs (0, 0) due to wasted resources and potential conflicts.

### Action Situation 4: Capacity Governance
#### Tension: Management Expansion vs. User Demand
#### Matrix:
|  | Management Expands | Management Doesn't Expand |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **High User Demand** | 4, 5 | 1, 2 |
| **Low User Demand** | 2, 4 | 3, 3 |
#### Justification: This situation involves the strategic tension between management deciding whether to expand charger capacity and users having varying levels of demand for charging services. The matrix shows that when management expands capacity and user demand is high, management receives a moderate payoff (4) from increased costs, and users receive a high payoff (5) from improved access. If management doesn't expand and demand is high, management receives a low payoff (1) from unmet demand, and users receive a low payoff (2) from limited access. If demand is low and management expands, management receives a moderate payoff (2) from underutilized expansion, and users receive a high payoff (4) from easy access. If neither expands nor is there high demand, both receive moderate payoffs (3, 3) from a stable, unchanged situation.
