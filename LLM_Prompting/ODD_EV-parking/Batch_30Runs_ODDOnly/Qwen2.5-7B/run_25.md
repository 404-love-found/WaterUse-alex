### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in the Shared EV Charging Model

#### 1. **Resident vs. Non-Resident Charging Priority**

**Title:** Resident vs. Non-Resident Charging Priority

**Tension:** Residents and non-residents have different incentives regarding queue fairness and charging access.

**Justification:** Residents receive a discount and may expect priority access, while non-residents pay full price and may not. This creates a strategic tension over queue order and fairness.

**Matrix:**

|              | Resident (R) follows rules | Resident (R) bypasses queue |
|--------------|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Non-Resident (NR) follows rules** | NR gets fair access, R gets fair access | NR gets fair access, R gets unfair access |
| **Non-Resident (NR) bypasses queue** | NR gets unfair access, R gets fair access | NR gets unfair access, R gets unfair access |

#### 2. **User vs. Staff Queue Enforcement**

**Title:** User vs. Staff Queue Enforcement

**Tension:** Users decide whether to follow queue rules, while staff decide whether to enforce them.

**Justification:** Users may choose to bypass the queue to save time, while staff may choose to enforce the queue to maintain order and fairness.

**Matrix:**

|              | User (U) follows queue | User (U) bypasses queue |
|--------------|-----------------------|------------------------|
| **Staff (S) enforces queue** | User gets fair access, Staff gets fair order | User gets unfair access, Staff gets fair order |
| **Staff (S) ignores queue** | User gets unfair access, Staff gets fair order | User gets unfair access, Staff gets fair order |

#### 3. **Resident vs. Resident Charging Fairness**

**Title:** Resident vs. Resident Charging Fairness

**Tension:** Residents may compete for charger access, especially during peak times.

**Justification:** Residents may try to reserve chargers or charge during off-peak times to ensure they get access, while others may try to use them during peak times.

**Matrix:**

|              | Resident (R1) charges during off-peak | Resident (R1) charges during peak |
|--------------|--------------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Resident (R2) charges during off-peak** | R1 and R2 get fair access | R1 gets unfair access, R2 gets fair access |
| **Resident (R2) charges during peak** | R1 gets fair access, R2 gets unfair access | R1 and R2 get unfair access |

#### 4. **User vs. Staff Charging Duration**

**Title:** User vs. Staff Charging Duration

**Tension:** Users decide how long to charge, while staff may enforce move-out rules.

**Justification:** Users may charge longer than necessary, while staff may ask them to leave to make space for others.

**Matrix:**

|              | User (U) charges appropriately | User (U) charges excessively |
|--------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Staff (S) enforces move-out** | User gets fair access, Staff gets fair order | User gets unfair access, Staff gets fair order |
| **Staff (S) does not enforce move-out** | User gets unfair access, Staff gets fair order | User gets unfair access, Staff gets fair order |

#### 5. **Non-Resident vs. Resident Charging Fairness**

**Title:** Non-Resident vs. Resident Charging Fairness

**Tension:** Non-residents may perceive unfair access to chargers compared to residents.

**Justification:** Non-residents may try to access chargers during off-peak times, while residents may try to reserve them for themselves.

**Matrix:**

|              | Non-Resident (NR) charges during off-peak | Non-Resident (NR) charges during peak |
|--------------|------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| **Resident (R) charges during off-peak** | NR gets fair access, R gets fair access | NR gets unfair access, R gets fair access |
| **Resident (R) charges during peak** | NR gets unfair access, R gets fair access | NR gets unfair access, R gets unfair access |

These action situations highlight the strategic tensions and decision-making processes among different actors in the shared EV charging model.
