# Run 3 - Qwen/Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct-Turbo

### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in the Shared EV Charging Model

#### 1. Resident vs. Non-Resident Queue Priority

**Title:** Resident vs. Non-Resident Queue Priority

**Tension:** Residents vs. Non-Residents

**Matrix:**

|                | Non-Residents Wait | Non-Residents Bypass Queue |
|----------------|--------------------|----------------------------|
| **Residents Wait** | 2, 2               | 1, 3                        |
| **Residents Bypass Queue** | 3, 1             | 0, 0                        |

**Justification:** Residents and non-residents face a strategic tension over queue priority. If both wait, the queue remains fair but residents may perceive unfairness. If residents bypass the queue, they gain quicker access but risk escalating disputes. Non-residents bypassing the queue may cause immediate queue chaos and dissatisfaction.

#### 2. User vs. Staff Queue Enforcement

**Title:** User vs. Staff Queue Enforcement

**Tension:** User vs. Staff

**Matrix:**

|                | User Complies | User Bypasses Queue |
|----------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Staff Enforces** | 2, 2         | 1, 3                |
| **Staff Tolerates** | 3, 1         | 0, 0                |

**Justification:** Users and staff face a strategic tension over queue enforcement. If both comply, the queue order is maintained, but users may perceive staff as ineffectual. If users bypass the queue, they gain quicker access but risk staff intervention. Staff tolerating queue bypassing can lead to further violations but may improve user satisfaction in the short term.

#### 3. Resident vs. Resident Charging Norms

**Title:** Resident vs. Resident Charging Norms

**Tension:** Residents vs. Residents

**Matrix:**

|                | Resident Charges During Off-Peak | Resident Charges During Peak |
|----------------|----------------------------------|------------------------------|
| **Resident Charges During Off-Peak** | 2, 2                          | 1, 3                         |
| **Resident Charges During Peak** | 3, 1                          | 0, 0                         |

**Justification:** Residents face a strategic tension over when to charge their EVs. Charging during off-peak hours can reduce costs and improve overall queue fairness, but some residents may prefer to charge during peak hours to avoid the queue. If many residents charge during peak hours, it can lead to longer wait times for others.
