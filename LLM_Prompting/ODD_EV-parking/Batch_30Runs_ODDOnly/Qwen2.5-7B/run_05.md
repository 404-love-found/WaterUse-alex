### Analysis of Distinct Action Situations in the Shared EV Charging Model

#### 1. **Resident vs. Non-Resident Charging Fairness**
**Title:** Resident vs. Non-Resident Charging Fairness

**Tension:** Residents and non-residents face a strategic dilemma over charging fairness. Residents seek to maintain perceived fairness and priority, while non-residents aim to have equal access without being unfairly disadvantaged.

**Matrix:**

|                  | Non-Resident Adheres to Queue | Non-Resident Bypasses Queue |
|------------------|------------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Resident Adheres to Queue** | (Fair, Fair) | (Unfair, Unfair) |
| **Resident Bypasses Queue**   | (Unfair, Unfair) | (Fair, Unfair) |

**Justification:** Residents may adhere to queue rules to maintain perceived fairness, but non-residents may bypass the queue to access the charger faster. The payoff matrix reflects the potential for unfairness in either case, where adhering to the queue benefits the resident but may disadvantage the non-resident, and bypassing the queue can provide immediate access for the non-resident but at the cost of fairness.

---

#### 2. **User vs. Management Charging Enforcement**
**Title:** User vs. Management Charging Enforcement

**Tension:** Users and management staff face a strategic dilemma over charging enforcement. Users may attempt to bypass the queue to avoid waiting, while management staff must decide whether to enforce queue rules to maintain order.

**Matrix:**

|                  | Management Enforces Queue | Management Bypasses Queue |
|------------------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| **User Adheres to Queue** | (Compliant, Orderly) | (Compliant, Disorderly) |
| **User Bypasses Queue**   | (Non-compliant, Disorderly) | (Non-compliant, Orderly) |

**Justification:** Users may adhere to queue rules to avoid conflict with management, but may also choose to bypass the queue to access the charger faster. Management staff can either enforce the queue to maintain order or bypass it to expedite charging for users. The payoff matrix reflects the potential for order or disorder in the system, depending on whether the queue is enforced or bypassed.

---

#### 3. **Resident vs. Management Resident Discount Policy**
**Title:** Resident vs. Management Resident Discount Policy

**Tension:** Residents and management staff face a strategic dilemma over the resident discount policy. Residents may push for a resident discount to ensure priority access, while management may resist to avoid unfairness and maintain order.

**Matrix:**

|                  | Management Maintains Discount | Management Removes Discount |
|------------------|------------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Resident Supports Discount** | (Priority, Fairness) | (No Priority, Unfairness) |
| **Resident Opposes Discount**   | (No Priority, Fairness) | (Priority, Unfairness) |

**Justification:** Residents may support a resident discount to ensure priority access, but management may remove it to avoid perceived unfairness. The payoff matrix reflects the potential for fairness or perceived unfairness in the system, depending on whether the discount is maintained or removed.

---

#### 4. **User vs. Management Charger Maintenance**
**Title:** User vs. Management Charger Maintenance

**Tension:** Users and management staff face a strategic dilemma over charger maintenance. Users may complain about charger outages, while management must decide whether to prioritize maintenance to ensure reliability.

**Matrix:**

|                  | Management Prioritizes Maintenance | Management Ignores Maintenance |
|------------------|-----------------------------------|------------------------------|
| **User Reports Outage** | (Quick Fix, Reliability) | (Delayed Fix, Reliability) |
| **User Ignores Outage**   | (Delayed Fix, Reliability) | (Quick Fix, Reliability) |

**Justification:** Users may report charger outages to ensure quick fixes, but management may ignore them to prioritize other tasks. The payoff matrix reflects the potential for quick or delayed fixes, depending on whether the outages are reported or ignored.

---

#### 5. **User vs. User Queue Bypassing**
**Title:** User vs. User Queue Bypassing

**Tension:** Users face a strategic dilemma over queue bypassing. Users may attempt to bypass the queue to access the charger faster, but may face conflict with other users.

**Matrix:**

|                  | Other User Adheres to Queue | Other User Bypasses Queue |
|------------------|-----------------------------|---------------------------|
| **User Adheres to Queue** | (Compliant, Orderly) | (Non-compliant, Orderly) |
| **User Bypasses Queue**   | (Non-compliant, Disorderly) | (Non-compliant, Disorderly) |

**Justification:** Users may adhere to the queue to maintain order, but may also choose to bypass it to access the charger faster. The payoff matrix reflects the potential for order or disorder in the system, depending on whether the queue is adhered to or bypassed.

---

These action situations capture the core strategic tensions in the shared EV charging model.
