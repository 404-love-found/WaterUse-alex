### Analysis: Distinct Action Situations in Shared EV Charging Model

#### 1. **Resident vs. Non-Resident Charging Access**
**Title:** Resident Discount vs. Fair Queue Access

**Tension:** Residents have a discount but non-residents pay the regular rate. This creates a tension between the perceived fairness of the resident discount and the need to maintain a fair queue for all users.

**Matrix:**
|           | Resident Charges | Non-Resident Charges |
|-----------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Resident** | Fair Queue (Wait) | Take Priority (Wait) |
| **Non-Resident** | Fair Queue (Pay) | Fair Queue (Pay) |

**Justification:** Residents may try to take priority access to the charger to save money, while non-residents want to ensure that the queue is fair and respects the resident discount. The matrix captures the strategic tension between fairness and financial incentives.

#### 2. **User vs. Management Queue Enforcement**
**Title:** Queue Compliance vs. Overstay

**Tension:** Users must comply with queue rules, but management may choose to enforce or tolerate overstay.

**Matrix:**
|           | Comply | Overstay |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| **User** | Wait (Low Overstay) | Leave (High Overstay) |
| **Management** | Enforce (Compliant) | Tolerate (Overstay) |

**Justification:** Users may choose to overstay to save time or avoid delays, while management must decide whether to enforce rules strictly or allow some flexibility. The matrix shows the trade-off between fairness and operational efficiency.

#### 3. **User vs. User Queue Bypassing**
**Title:** Queue Bypass vs. Fair Queue Order

**Tension:** Users may try to bypass the queue to save time, but this undermines the fairness of the queue system.

**Matrix:**
|           | Bypass Queue | Follow Queue |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **User A** | Save Time (Bypass) | Wait (Follow) |
| **User B** | Wait (Follow) | Save Time (Bypass) |

**Justification:** Users who bypass the queue gain access to a charger more quickly, but this disrupts the fairness of the queue system. The matrix captures the strategic tension between personal convenience and collective fairness.

#### 4. **Management vs. User Complaint Resolution**
**Title:** Compliant Behavior vs. Overstay Tolerance

**Tension:** Management must decide whether to resolve complaints by enforcing rules or tolerating overstay.

**Matrix:**
|           | Enforce | Tolerate |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| **User** | Resolve Complaint (Compliant) | Ignore Complaint (Overstay) |
| **Management** | Resolve (Compliant) | Ignore (Overstay) |

**Justification:** Users may complain about overstay, and management must decide whether to resolve the issue by enforcing rules or allowing some tolerance. The matrix shows the strategic tension between enforcing rules and maintaining user satisfaction.

#### 5. **User vs. User Charger Occupation**
**Title:** Charger Occupation vs. Charging Efficiency

**Tension:** Users may choose to remain in a charger bay after useful charging to save time, but this reduces the efficiency of charger use.

**Matrix:**
|           | Stay in Bay | Leave Bay |
|-----------|------------|-----------|
| **User A** | Save Time (Stay) | Efficient (Leave) |
| **User B** | Efficient (Leave) | Save Time (Stay) |

**Justification:** Users who stay in the charger bay after useful charging save time but reduce the availability of the charger for others. The matrix captures the strategic tension between personal efficiency and system efficiency.

#### 6. **Management vs. User Informal Requests**
**Title:** Formal vs. Informal Charging Access

**Tension:** Management may have to decide whether to allow informal requests for charging access or enforce formal rules.

**Matrix:**
|           | Allow Informal | Enforce Rules |

**Justification:** Users may ask for informal access, and management must decide whether to grant it or enforce formal rules. The matrix shows the strategic tension between flexibility and fairness.

#### 7. **User vs. User Charging Urgency**
**Title:** Urgent Charging vs. Fair Queue Order

**Matrix:**

#### 8. **User vs. Management Charging Session Duration**
