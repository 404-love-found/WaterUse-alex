### Title: Strategic Tensions in Shared EV Charging

### 1. Resident vs. Non-Resident Access
**Tension:**
Residents may attempt to maintain priority access to chargers, while non-residents seek fair access based on posted rules.

**Matrix:**

|                | Non-Residents Follow Rules | Non-Residents Bypass Queue |
|----------------|----------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Residents Follow Rules** | (Fair Access, Fair Access) | (Priority Access, Displacement) |
| **Residents Bypass Queue** | (Displacement, Priority Access) | (Priority Access, Priority Access) |

**Justification:**
Residents may attempt to maintain priority access by not following posted rules, while non-residents may bypass the queue to ensure fair access. The payoff matrix reflects the dilemma faced by both groups.

### 2. Queue Bypass vs. Queue Compliance
**Tension:**
Users must decide whether to join the queue or bypass it based on perceived fairness and enforcement likelihood.

**Matrix:**

|                | Queue Compliance | Queue Bypass |
|----------------|------------------|--------------|
| **Queue Compliance** | (Fair Wait, Fair Wait) | (Short Wait, Long Wait) |
| **Queue Bypass** | (Long Wait, Short Wait) | (Short Wait, Short Wait) |

**Justification:**
Users must decide whether to follow queue rules or bypass the queue based on the perceived fairness and enforcement likelihood. The dilemma is whether to follow posted rules or take advantage of perceived lax enforcement.

### 3. Staff Enforcement vs. Informal Requests

**Justification:**
Management staff must balance enforcing posted rules with the need to maintain resident satisfaction and operational order. The dilemma is whether to strictly enforce rules or grant informal requests.
