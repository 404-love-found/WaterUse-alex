### Title: Strategic Tensions in Shared EV Charging Governance

#### 1. Resident vs. Non-Resident Queue Access
**Tension:** Residents vs. Non-Residents in Queue Access
**Justification:** The resident discount and fair queue access create a strategic tension between residents and non-residents. Residents may feel entitled to prioritize access, while non-residents may feel unfairly treated.

**Matrix:**

|           | Non-Residents Follow Queue | Non-Residents Bypass Queue |
|-----------|----------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Residents Follow Queue** | (X, X) | (X, Y) |
| **Residents Bypass Queue** | (Y, X) | (Y, Y) |

**Explanation:** 
- (X, X): Both follow the queue, leading to stable fairness.
- (X, Y): Residents follow the queue, but non-residents bypass it, leading to unfair access for residents.
- (Y, X): Residents bypass the queue, but non-residents follow it, leading to unfair access for non-residents.
- (Y, Y): Both bypass the queue, leading to chaotic and unfair access.

#### 2. User vs. Management in Queue Enforcement
**Tension:** User vs. Management in Queue Enforcement
**Justification:** Users may choose to follow or bypass the queue, while management decides whether to enforce or tolerate queue violations. This creates a strategic tension.

**Matrix:**

|           | Management Enforces Queue | Management Tolerates Queue |
|-----------|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Users Follow Queue** | (X, X) | (X, Y) |
| **Users Bypass Queue** | (Y, X) | (Y, Y) |

**Explanation:**

- (Y, X): Users bypass the queue, but management enforces it, leading to conflict.
- (Y, Y): Both bypass the queue, leading to chaotic and unfair access.
