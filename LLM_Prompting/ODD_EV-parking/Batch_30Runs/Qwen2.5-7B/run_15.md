### Strategic Dilemma 1: Queue Bypassing vs. Queue Compliance

#### Title
Queue Bypassing vs. Queue Compliance

#### Tension
Residents and non-residents face a strategic dilemma over queue order. Residents may bypass the queue to access chargers more quickly, while non-residents may comply with the posted rules to ensure fair access.

#### Matrix
|             | Non-resident Complies | Non-resident Bypasses |
|-------------|-----------------------|-----------------------|
| **Resident Complies** | (-1, -1) | (0, 1) |
| **Resident Bypasses** | (1, 0) | (0, 0) |

#### Justification
- **Resident Complies, Non-resident Complies**: Both users experience a wait, leading to a negative outcome for both.
- **Resident Complies, Non-resident Bypasses**: The non-resident gets immediate access, while the resident waits, leading to a positive outcome for the non-resident and a negative outcome for the resident.
- **Resident Bypasses, Non-resident Complies**: The resident gets immediate access, while the non-resident waits, leading to a positive outcome for the resident and a negative outcome for the non-resident.
- **Resident Bypasses, Non-resident Bypasses**: Both users get immediate access, leading to a neutral outcome for both.

The resident has a strategic incentive to bypass the queue to avoid waiting, while the non-resident has an incentive to comply to ensure fairness.
