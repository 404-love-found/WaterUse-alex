### Title: Strategic Tensions in Shared EV Charging

#### 1. Resident vs. Non-Resident Charging Access

**Tension:**
Residents and non-residents compete for limited charging access. Residents have a discount and may believe they have stronger entitlement due to their housing relationship, while non-residents pay full price and may believe in equal treatment once admitted.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|            | Non-Resident Follows Queue | Non-Resident Bypasses Queue |
|------------|----------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Resident Follows Queue** | (公平, 公平) | (不公平, 公平) |
| **Resident Bypasses Queue** | (公平, 不公平) | (不公平, 不公平) |

**Justification:**
- Both residents and non-residents prefer a fair queue system.
- If a resident follows the queue, it maintains fairness but may delay their own access.
- If a resident bypasses the queue, it gives immediate access but violates fairness.
- Non-residents prefer to follow the queue for fair treatment, but may bypass if enforcement is weak.
- The matrix captures the trade-off between immediate access and fairness.

#### 2. Management vs. User Charging Behavior

**Tension:**
Parking-lot management must balance user compliance with queue order and the need for low complaint volume and orderly operations against users' desire for predictable access and low conflict.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|            | User Follows Queue | User Bypasses Queue |
|------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Management Enforces Queue** | (低投诉, 高秩序) | (高投诉, 高秩序) |
| **Management Tolerates Bypassing** | (低投诉, 低秩序) | (低投诉, 低秩序) |

**Justification:**
- Management prefers low complaint volume and orderly operations.
- Users prefer predictable access and low conflict.
- Enforcing the queue reduces complaints but may create conflict.
- Tolerating bypassing reduces conflict but increases complaints.

#### 3. Resident vs. Staff Informal Requests

**Tension:**
Residents may seek informal priority from staff, while staff may receive such requests and choose to honor them or enforce posted rules.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|            | Staff Honors Request | Staff Enforces Rules |
|------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Resident Seeks Informal Priority** | (不公平, 不公平) | (公平, 公平) |
| **Resident Follows Rules** | (公平, 公平) | (公平, 公平) |

**Justification:**
- Residents prefer informal priority for faster access.
- Staff prefer to enforce rules to maintain order and fairness.
- Honoring requests reduces conflict but violates posted rules.
- Enforcing rules maintains order but may upset residents.

#### 4. Resident vs. Non-Resident Charging Demand

**Tension:**
Residents and non-residents compete for limited charging capacity. Residents have a discount and may have higher demand due to home proximity, while non-residents pay full price and may have lower demand.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|            | Non-Resident Uses Fewer Bays | Non-Resident Uses More Bays |
|------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Resident Uses Fewer Bays** | (公平, 公平) | (不公平, 公平) |
| **Resident Uses More Bays** | (不公平, 公平) | (不公平, 不公平) |

**Justification:**
- Residents prefer to use fewer bays to maintain fairness and avoid conflict.
- Non-residents prefer to use fewer bays to avoid violating posted rules.
- Using more bays by residents can lead to unfair treatment of non-residents.
- Balancing usage ensures fair access for both groups.

#### 5. Resident vs. Management Capacity Expansion

**Tension:**
Residents may pressure management to expand charger capacity, while management must balance resident requests with the need for manageable costs and visitor parking.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|            | Management Expands Capacity | Management Does Not Expand |
|------------|------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| **Resident Supports Expansion** | (公平, 公平) | (不公平, 公平) |
| **Resident Opposes Expansion** | (公平, 公平) | (公平, 公平) |

**Justification:**
- Residents prefer expanded capacity for fair and convenient access.
- Management prefers manageable costs and maintaining visitor parking.
- Expanding capacity improves fairness but may increase costs.
- Not expanding capacity maintains current fairness but may reduce resident satisfaction.
