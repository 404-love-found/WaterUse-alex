### Title: Strategic Tensions in Shared EV Charging Governance

#### 1. Resident vs. Non-Resident Access to Charging Bays

**Tension:**
Residents and non-residents have different perceptions of fairness and entitlement to charging bays. Residents believe they have a stronger claim because of their housing relationship, while non-residents believe full-price payment entitles them to equal treatment.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Non-Resident Follows Queue | Non-Resident Bypasses Queue |
|-----------|----------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Resident Follows Queue** | (1, 1) | (0, 2) |
| **Resident Bypasses Queue** | (2, 0) | (0, 0) |

**Justification:**
- Both follow the queue: Both get fair access, leading to a stable outcome.
- Resident bypasses queue: Resident gains immediate access, but non-resident incurs a loss.
- Non-resident bypasses queue: Non-resident gains immediate access, but resident incurs a loss.
- Both bypass the queue: Both gain immediate access, but this undermines the fairness and can lead to conflict.

#### 2. User vs. Staff on Queue Enforcement

**Tension:**
Users and staff have different incentives regarding queue enforcement. Users may benefit from lax enforcement, while staff face the trade-off between complaint volume and enforcement effort.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Staff Enforces Queue | Staff Tolerates Violations |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| **User Follows Queue** | (1, 1) | (2, 0) |
| **User Bypasses Queue** | (0, 2) | (0, 0) |

**Justification:**
- Both follow the queue: Both benefit from a fair system.
- User bypasses queue: User gains immediate access, but staff incurs a loss.
- Staff tolerates violations: Staff saves effort but increases complaints.
- Both bypass the queue: Both gain immediate access, but this undermines the system's fairness and can lead to increased complaints.

#### 4. User vs. Staff on Charger Maintenance

**Tension:**
Users and staff have different incentives regarding charger maintenance. Users may benefit from quick repair, while staff face the trade-off between quick response and maintenance costs.

**2-Player Normal Form Payoff Matrix:**

|           | Staff Responds Quickly | Staff Delays Response |
|-----------|------------------------|-----------------------|
| **User Reports Fault** | (1, 1) | (2, 0) |
| **User Uses Faulty Charger** | (0, 2) | (0, 0) |

**Justification:**
- Both respond quickly: Both benefit from a functional system.
- User reports fault: User gains by having a functional charger, but staff incurs a cost.
- Staff delays response: Staff saves cost but increases user inconvenience.
- User uses faulty charger: User incurs inconvenience, but staff saves cost.
