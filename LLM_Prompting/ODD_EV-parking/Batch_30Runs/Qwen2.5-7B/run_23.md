### Strategic Tension 1: Queue Bypassing vs. Queue Compliance

#### Title: Queue Bypassing vs. Queue Compliance

#### Tension: 
Residents and non-residents face a strategic dilemma where they can either comply with the queue order or bypass it to secure charging access. The decision is influenced by the perceived fairness and legitimacy of the queue system.

#### Normal Form Payoff Matrix:

|                     | Non-Resident Bypasses | Non-Resident Complies |
|---------------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Resident Bypasses** | (Shorter Wait, Conflict) | (Longer Wait, No Conflict) |
| **Resident Complies** | (Longer Wait, Conflict) | (Shorter Wait, No Conflict) |

#### Justification:
1. **If Both Bypass**: This results in higher wait times for all users, leading to conflict and dissatisfaction.
2. **If Both Comply**: Users follow the posted queue order, which is fair but may lead to longer wait times.
3. **If Resident Bypasses, Non-Resident Complies**: The resident may secure access faster, causing conflict and dissatisfaction among non-residents.
4. **If Resident Complies, Non-Resident Bypasses**: The non-resident may secure access faster, causing conflict and dissatisfaction among residents.

The tension arises because both groups have an incentive to bypass the queue to reduce wait times, but doing so undermines the fairness and legitimacy of the system.

### Strategic Tension 2: Informal Priority vs. Formal Queue Order

#### Title: Informal Priority vs. Formal Queue Order

#### Tension: 
Parking-lot management staff and users face a strategic dilemma where users can either follow the formal queue order or seek informal priority from staff. The decision is influenced by staff discretion and enforcement.

#### Normal Form Payoff Matrix:

|                     | Staff Enforces Queue | Staff Grants Informal Priority |
|---------------------|---------------------|--------------------------------|
| **User Follows Queue** | (Shorter Wait, Conflict) | (Shorter Wait, No Conflict) |
| **User Seeks Informal Priority** | (Longer Wait, No Conflict) | (Shorter Wait, Conflict) |

#### Justification:
1. **If Staff Enforces Queue, User Follows Queue**: The user waits longer but maintains the fairness and legitimacy of the system.
2. **If Staff Enforces Queue, User Seeks Informal Priority**: The user gets faster access but risks conflict and reduced legitimacy.
3. **If Staff Grants Informal Priority, User Follows Queue**: The user gets faster access but risks conflict and reduced legitimacy.
4. **If Staff Grants Informal Priority, User Seeks Informal Priority**: Both get faster access, but the system loses legitimacy and fairness.

The tension arises because users have an incentive to seek informal priority to reduce wait times, but doing so undermines the formal queue system and may lead to conflict and reduced legitimacy.

### Strategic Tension 3: Resident Discount vs. Non-Resident Fairness

#### Title: Resident Discount vs. Non-Resident Fairness

#### Tension: 
Residents and non-residents face a strategic dilemma where residents can either use the resident discount or non-residents can demand equal treatment. The decision is influenced by the resident discount and the perceived fairness of the system.

#### Normal Form Payoff Matrix:

|                     | Non-Resident Demands Equal Treatment | Non-Resident Accepts Discount |
|---------------------|-------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Resident Uses Discount** | (Conflict, Resident Favors Discount) | (No Conflict, Resident Favors Discount) |
| **Resident Accepts Discount** | (No Conflict, Non-Resident Favors Fairness) | (Conflict, Non-Resident Favors Fairness) |

#### Justification:
1. **If Both Use Discount**: The resident may prioritize the discount, causing conflict with non-residents who demand equal treatment.
2. **If Both Accept Discount**: Users accept the discount, which is fair but may reduce resident satisfaction.
3. **If Resident Uses Discount, Non-Resident Demands Equal Treatment**: The resident may prioritize their discount, causing conflict with non-residents.
4. **If Resident Accepts Discount, Non-Resident Demands Equal Treatment**: Users accept the discount, but non-residents may feel the system is unfair.

The tension arises because residents may prioritize their discount, causing conflict with non-residents who demand equal treatment, and undermining the fairness of the system.

### Strategic Tension 4: Off-Peak Charging vs. Peak Charging

#### Title: Off-Peak Charging vs. Peak Charging

#### Tension: 
Residents and non-residents face a strategic dilemma where they can either charge off-peak or peak. The decision is influenced by the desired energy demand and the perceived fairness of the system.

#### Normal Form Payoff Matrix:

|                     | Non-Resident Charges Off-Peak | Non-Resident Charges Peak |
|---------------------|-------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Resident Charges Off-Peak** | (Shorter Wait, No Conflict) | (Longer Wait, Conflict) |
| **Resident Charges Peak** | (Longer Wait, Conflict) | (Shorter Wait, No Conflict) |

#### Justification:
1. **If Both Charge Off-Peak**: Users charge during off-peak periods, reducing wait times and conflict.
2. **If Both Charge Peak**: Users charge during peak periods, increasing wait times and conflict.
3. **If Resident Charges Off-Peak, Non-Resident Charges Peak**: The resident charges off-peak, reducing their wait time, while the non-resident charges peak, increasing wait times and conflict.
4. **If Resident Charges Peak, Non-Resident Charges Off-Peak**: The resident charges peak, increasing wait times, while the non-resident charges off-peak, reducing wait times and conflict.

The tension arises because charging off-peak reduces wait times, but residents may prefer to charge peak to avoid conflict, while non-residents may prefer off-peak to reduce wait times and conflict.
