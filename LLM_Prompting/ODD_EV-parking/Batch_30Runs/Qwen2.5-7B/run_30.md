### Title: Strategic Tensions in Shared EV Charging

#### Tension 1: Queue Bypass vs. Compliance
**Tension:** Residents vs. Non-Residents

**Justification:** Residents may perceive that their housing relationship gives them priority to use the chargers, while non-residents may believe that paying the regular rate entitles them to equal treatment. This creates a conflict over queue fairness.

**Matrix:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}
 & \text{Comply} & \text{Bypass} \\
\hline
\text{Comply} & \text{Fairness, } +\text{ Wait} & \text{Conflict, } +\text{ Wait} \\
\text{Bypass} & \text{Conflict, } -\text{ Wait} & \text{Priority, } -\text{ Wait} \\
\end{array}
\]

#### Tension 2: Informal Priority vs. Formal Queue
**Tension:** Residents vs. Management

**Justification:** Residents may request informal priority from management, which can undermine the formal queue system and create conflict with non-residents.

**Matrix:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}
 & \text{Enforce Queue} & \text{Grant Informal Priority} \\
\hline
\text{Enforce Queue} & \text{Legitimacy, } +\text{ Order} & \text{Conflict, } -\text{ Order} \\
\text{Grant Informal Priority} & \text{Conflict, } -\text{ Order} & \text{Conflict, } +\text{ Order} \\
\end{array}
\]

#### Tension 3: Off-Peak Charging vs. Peak Charging
**Tension:** Residents vs. Non-Residents

**Justification:** Residents may learn to use off-peak charging to avoid long waits, while non-residents may prefer peak charging when they visit.

**Matrix:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}
 & \text{Off-Peak} & \text{Peak} \\
\hline
\text{Off-Peak} & \text{Shorter Wait, } +\text{ Cost} & \text{Longer Wait, } +\text{ Cost} \\
\text{Peak} & \text{Longer Wait, } +\text{ Cost} & \text{Shorter Wait, } -\text{ Cost} \\
\end{array}
\]

#### Tension 4: Staff Enforcement vs. User Tolerance
**Tension:** Management vs. Users

**Justification:** Management may need to enforce posted rules to maintain fairness, but users may tolerate violations to avoid conflict.

**Matrix:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}
 & \text{Enforce Rules} & \text{Tolerate Violations} \\
\hline
\text{Enforce Rules} & \text{Fairness, } +\text{ Compliance} & \text{Conflict, } -\text{ Compliance} \\
\text{Tolerate Violations} & \text{Conflict, } -\text{ Compliance} & \text{Conflict, } +\text{ Compliance} \\
\end{array}
\]

#### Tension 5: Capacity Expansion vs. Resident Pressure
**Tension:** Residents vs. Management

**Justification:** Residents may pressure management to add more chargers, but management may need to balance this with public parking access and costs.

**Matrix:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}

\hline

\end{array}
\]

#### Tension 6: Informal Requests vs. Fairness
**Tension:** Management vs. Users

**Justification:** Management may receive informal requests from residents to hold charging bays, which can undermine the fairness of the queue system.

**Matrix:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}

\]

#### Tension 7: Resident Discount vs. Non-Resident Perception
**Tension:** Residents vs. Non-Residents

#### Tension 8: Charger Faults vs. Queue Fairness
**Tension:** Users vs. Management

**Justification:** Faulty chargers can disrupt the queue order and create fairness issues. Users may report faults, while management decides how quickly to repair them.

**Matrix:**
\[
\begin{array}{c|cc}

\]
