# Run 1 - deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro

**Action Situation 1: Queue Bypassing Dilemma**  
**Tension:** Individual incentive to bypass the queue for immediate charger access versus collective interest in maintaining fair order.  
**Matrix:**  
| User A \ User B | Wait (Comply) | Bypass (Defect) |
|------------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Wait (Comply)    | (3,3)         | (1,4)           |
| Bypass (Defect)  | (4,1)         | (2,2)           |
**Justification:** The ODD states that users can bypass the queue, causing others to wait longer. This is a social dilemma where defection is dominant but mutual cooperation yields a better collective outcome.

**Action Situation 2: Move‑out Promptness Dilemma**  
**Tension:** Individual convenience of overstaying in a charging bay after charging versus the collective need for bay turnover.  
**Matrix:**  
| User A \ User B | Move (Comply) | Overstay (Defect) |
|------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Move (Comply)    | (3,3)         | (1,4)             |
| Overstay (Defect)| (4,1)         | (2,2)             |
**Justification:** The ODD describes that overstaying blocks the bay without paying additional energy costs, creating a temptation to overstay. This is a social dilemma similar to queue bypassing but focused on post‑charging behavior.

**Action Situation 3: Staff Enforcement Game**  
**Tension:** Staff trade‑off between enforcing rules (costly effort) and tolerating violations (saving effort but risking disorder); users decide to comply or violate based on expected enforcement.  
**Matrix:**  
| Staff \ User   | Comply | Violate |
|----------------|--------|---------|
| Enforce        | (3,3)  | (2,1)   |
| Not Enforce    | (4,3)  | (1,4)   |
**Justification:** The ODD highlights the strategic interaction between staff enforcement effort and user compliance. Staff face a trade‑off between maintaining order and minimizing effort, while users decide to comply based on expected enforcement.

**Action Situation 4: Informal Priority Game**  
**Tension:** User’s desire for preferential treatment versus staff’s dilemma between maintaining rule legitimacy and accommodating requests.  
**Matrix:**  
| User \ Staff     | Accommodate | Deny |
|------------------|-------------|------|
| Seek Priority    | (4,2)       | (1,3)|

**Action Situation 5: Resident vs Non‑resident Priority Game**  
**Tension:** Conflict over who gets priority when both resident and non‑resident claim entitlement to a charging bay.  
**Matrix:**  
| Resident \ Non‑resident | Insist | Yield |
|------------------------|--------|-------|
| Insist                 | (2,2)  | (4,2) |
| Yield                  | (2,4)  | (3,3) |
**Justification:** The ODD notes that residents may feel entitled due to discount, while non‑residents feel entitled due to full price payment. This Hawk‑Dove game models the conflict over priority.

**Matrix:**

**Action Situation 7: Capacity Expansion Collective Action**  
**Tension:** Residents’ decision to support or free‑ride on the funding of additional chargers.  
**Matrix:**  
| Resident A \ Resident B | Support | Free‑ride |
|------------------------|---------|-----------|
| Support                | (3,3)   | (2,4)     |
| Free‑ride              | (4,2)   | (1,1)     |
**Justification:** The ODD mentions that residents can pressure management to add chargers, and some may support higher fees while others prefer to wait. This is a public goods dilemma where individual defection is tempting but mutual defection leads to the worst outcome.

**Action Situation 8: Reservation vs Live Queue Game**  
**Tension:** Individual advantage of reserving a slot versus the collective need for a balanced queue system.  
**Matrix:**  
| User A \ User B | Reserve | Join Live |
|------------------|---------|-----------|
| Reserve          | (2,2)   | (4,1)     |
| Join Live        | (1,4)   | (3,3)     |
**Justification:** The ODD highlights the choice between reserving a slot and joining the live queue. This creates a tension where reserving gives individual advantage but if too many reserve, the live queue becomes unpredictable, leading to a “reservation congestion” dilemma.
